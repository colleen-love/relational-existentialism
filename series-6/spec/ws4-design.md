# WS4 — The arrow: self-knowing gives directionality

**Design doc. Series 6, payoff workstream. Owns: the proof that the order `≺` (README §4 `prec`) is strictly directional and endogenous — the self-survey `trunc` is a lossy, deterministic, many-to-one map (the past), the residue-opening (WS3's `Δ`) is one-to-many (the open future), the generated order strict (no return), and the arrow carries no imported time axis and no first moment. `≺` is defined once in README §4 and shared with WS5; WS4 proves it a strict directional order, WS5 proves total-or-partial. WS4 does not redefine `≺`.**

*Series 6 is standalone; upstream names transcribed into `series-6/formal/ws4.lean`, re-namespaced `Series6.WS4` (see `spec/README.md` §5). Shared objects (`Approx`, `trunc`, `Proc`, `Δ`, `Moment`, `prec`, `faceAt`, `Ω_n`) are defined in `spec/README.md` §§2–4 and only cited here.*

## The object at stake

The charter (§5.3) separates two things WS3 does not distinguish. Fixed-point-freeness buys **perpetuity** — no moment is at rest — but a frictionless pendulum never rests and still returns; perpetuity is not yet an **arrow**. Something must forbid return, and self-knowing is that something: to know yourself is to survey yourself, and the diagonal makes the survey a *proper* part of the whole, so self-knowing is lossy and lossy *in one direction only*. Surveying coarsens — `trunc`, a deterministic many-to-one map — while generating the missed detail is one-to-many, a choice, not a function. That asymmetry is the arrow: the past is the surveyable direction where self-knowledge is a well-defined lossy map, the future is the residue direction where it fails to fix a unique answer, and time points *away from the surveyable*, strictly, because the survey is *properly* contained (fixed-point-freeness again, now forbidding return).

The design question is how to cash out "directionality" so that (a) `≺` is a genuine **strict** order (irreflexive, transitive, no return), (b) the direction is carried by the **proper-subobject order on self-surveys** (`trunc`/`faceAt`), not by an external counter, and (c) the arrow is **endogenous** — no imported ℕ, no first moment — surviving the **strip test** (charter §7): delete the external time index from every statement and a direction must remain. If direction survives only through an external `ℕ` order on depths, the arrow is imposed, not earned, and WS4 reports honest **Partial**.

**Ambient theory.** `spec/README.md` §4: `Moment κ` = a pair `(x, n)` (a process `x : Proc κ` observed through survey-depth `n`), equivalently a finite founded history-prefix; `prec : Moment κ → Moment κ → Prop`, `m ≺ m'` iff `m'` opens a residue of a survey available at `m` — the extension order on founded histories generated by `Δ` (README §3). The survey/truncation `trunc` (README §2) and the two-sided face `faceAt` (README §4) are the proper-subobject structure the strip test falls back on. `Δ`'s fixed-point-freeness on moments (no moment equals its own successor) is WS3's `ws6_lawvere_incomplete` transported; WS4 consumes it, does not reprove it.

## Candidates (for cashing out directionality)

### C1 — The arrow as the proper-prefix order on founded histories (the lead; survives the strip)

```lean
-- m ≺ m'  iff  the founded history of m' properly extends that of m by opening a Δ-residue.
-- This IS README §4 `prec`, unfolded as a PROPER-containment order via `trunc`, with NO ℕ<ℕ.
def IsResidueExt {κ} (m m' : Moment κ) : Prop :=
  ∃ d, Opened (m'.hist) d ∧ IsResidue (survey m) d ∧ trunc_prefix m m'   -- m' = m + one opened residue
```
Directionality is the asymmetry of `trunc`: forward is the deterministic coarsening (past), its converse the multivalued residue-opening (future).

- **Ambient / fpf map:** `Δ` on moments (README §3); the survey is `trunc`-image, non-surjective by the finite Cantor diagonal (`ws6_lawvere_incomplete`).
- **Success condition:** `prec` is a strict order; strictness = the survey is *properly* contained (Cantor residue nonempty); the past-map `trunc` is a function, its converse a relation.
- **Failure mode (guarded against):** if "extends" were read as `n < n'` on the depth index, the order would be an external-ℕ fact wearing history-vocabulary — the C2 trap. Avoided by defining extension through `trunc`-properness (residue *genuinely new*), not through the depth label.

**Paper triage.** *The lead, and strip-survivable by construction.* Delete the depth index `n` from `Moment` and keep only the founded state plus its `trunc`-image: the direction remains, because `trunc` is still a non-injective coarsening and its converse still multivalued — the proper-subobject order on self-surveys is intact. **Winner**, subject to routing strictness to WS3's properness (`ws4_arrow_from_properness`) rather than to a counter.

### C2 — The arrow as an external ℕ-index on moments (the STOPWATCH trap; rejected, fails the strip)

```lean
def precStopwatch {κ} (m m' : Moment κ) : Prop := m.depth < m'.depth   -- ℕ carries the arrow
```
Order moments by their depth coordinate: `t ↦ x_t`, `t ∈ ℕ`. Looks dynamic, directional, strict for free.

- **Ambient / fpf map:** none needed — `<` on `ℕ` does all the work.
- **Success condition:** would be that `<` on depths is a strict order (trivially true).
- **Failure mode:** **imported axis with a first moment.** `precStopwatch` is `Nat.lt` relabelled; it has a least element `depth = 0` — a first moment, a state relationless-before-it, the tower-with-a-bottom cheat of charter §4.1 relocated to time. And it **fails the strip**: delete the external `ℕ` and nothing remains — the direction was the counter, not the self-survey.

**Paper triage.** The named trap (charter §4.1, §5.6, §9). Decidable-on-paper and decided against: strip the `ℕ` and `precStopwatch` has no residual content, so the arrow is imposed not earned. **Reject.** Retained only as the negative the strip test exists to catch; WS7 checks every WS4 statement against exactly this.

### C3 — The arrow as strict monotone of a genuinely-internal accumulated-distinction measure

```lean
-- μ(m) = the total distinction accumulated in m's founded history (opened residues, counted
-- by the state, not by a stopwatch). m ≺ m' ⇒ μ strictly increases.
def accDist {κ} (m : Moment κ) : Ordinal      -- read OFF the founded state, not an imported clock
theorem accDist_strict_mono : prec m m' → accDist m < accDist m'
```
Directionality = a strictly increasing internal measure of accumulated distinction: each residue is *genuinely new* (fixed-point-freeness, WS3), so distinction strictly accrues and never returns.

- **Ambient / fpf map:** `Δ`; the residue is new because the survey is non-surjective (Cantor), so `accDist` cannot repeat.
- **Success condition:** `accDist` is definable from the founded state alone and strictly monotone along `≺`, certifying *no return* without a counter.
- **Failure mode:** **the measure is a counter in disguise.** If `accDist m` is secretly `m.depth` (or any order-embedding of `ℕ` stipulated rather than derived from the state's distinction content), C3 collapses to C2 and fails the strip.

**Paper triage.** *Not a rival carrier — the strictness certificate C1 needs.* `accDist` must be **derived from the founded state's distinction content** (which buds have opened to genuinely-new residues), so that its strict increase is a *theorem from Cantor-properness*, not a stipulated tally. Then it survives the strip (delete `n`; `accDist` still increases, because properness still holds). **Fold into C1 as the no-return witness**, with the load-bearing check that `accDist` factors through `faceAt`/`trunc`-properness, not through the depth label — this is the coincidence duty, `ws4_arrow_from_properness`.

### C4 — The metric / discounted version (asymmetric behavioural distance)

```lean
-- put the behavioural quasimetric on moments; the arrow = strictly decreasing distance to the
-- refused limit, asymmetric because generating ≠ forgetting.
def bdistᵃ {κ} (m m' : Moment κ) : ℝ≥0∞      -- asymmetric: bdistᵃ m m' ≠ bdistᵃ m' m
```
The arrow is a strictly monotone (discounted) approach along an **asymmetric** quasimetric; asymmetry `bdistᵃ m m' ≠ bdistᵃ m' m` encodes forgetting ≠ generating.

- **Ambient / fpf map:** the diagonal as a fixed-point-free contraction on an incomplete space (Series 6 WS1 C4 fallback).
- **Success condition:** an asymmetric distance whose non-symmetry *is* the survey/residue asymmetry, strictly monotone along `≺`.
- **Failure mode:** **the pun does the work.** Risk that "directional" (distance asymmetry) is proved of a quasimetric with no faithful tie back to `trunc`-properness — the strip fails because the direction lives in the metric, not the self-survey.

**Paper triage.** Strong and Lean-tractable (Mathlib quasimetrics), and it prefigures WS5's asymmetry lever, but it changes the primitive from *relating* to *distance* and makes the residue-as-past/future asymmetry harder to render literal. **Pre-registered alternative:** if C1's strictness cannot be tied to properness on the thread encoding (`ws4_arrow_from_properness` fails the strip), escalate to C4, where the contraction's asymmetry is manifest. Typed fallback, not the lead.

## Winning candidate: C1 (proper-prefix order = README §4 `prec`), with C3's accumulated-distinction as the no-return certificate and C4 as typed fallback

The decisive moves, converting directionality to decidable facts on `prec` (not redefining it):

1. **The arrow is `trunc`'s asymmetry, read on the shared `prec`.** `trunc` is a *function* one way (deterministic, many-to-one — the past is settled) and its converse is a *relation* one way (one-to-many — the future is open). The order `prec` is the transitive generation of that single asymmetry; strictness is inherited from properness, not from a counter.
2. **Strictness = properness, not depth.** Irreflexivity and no-return come from the survey being *properly* contained (Cantor residue nonempty, WS3), so no moment is a residue of itself and none returns — provable with the depth label stripped.

### The obligations, each: Lean signature + strategy + paper-decidable note + strip annotation

**`ws4_survey_lossy` — the survey is deterministic and many-to-one (the past).**
```lean
theorem ws4_survey_lossy {κ} (hinf : ℵ₀ ≤ κ) :
    -- deterministic: `trunc` is a genuine total function (automatic by type)…
    ∀ n, ∃ s t : A κ (n+1), s ≠ t ∧ trunc κ n s = trunc κ n t          -- …and many-to-one
```
*Strategy:* exhibit, at each stage, two stage-`(n+1)` approximations differing only in a deepest layer `trunc` forgets — a bud opened two admissible ways over the same stage-`n` state. Non-injectivity of `PkMap (trunc …)` from a nontrivial fibre. *Paper-decidable:* yes — a fibre-cardinality computation on the transcribed `trunc`; `trunc` being a function is definitional. *Strip:* survives — "coarsening forgets detail" mentions no `ℕ`-order, only the map `trunc`.

**`ws4_residue_one_to_many` — the residue-opening is a relation, not a function (the open future).**
```lean
theorem ws4_residue_one_to_many {κ} (hinf : ℵ₀ ≤ κ) :
    ∃ x : A κ n, 2 ≤ Cardinal.mk { s : A κ (n+1) // trunc κ n s = x }    -- ≥ 2 futures over one past
```
*Strategy:* the fibre of `trunc` over a state that admits a genuine residue has ≥ 2 elements — the same non-injectivity of `ws4_survey_lossy` read *backwards*: forgetting is a function, generating is its multivalued converse. The two extensions are two admissible residue-openings (`Δ` is a choice among them, not a function on moments). *Paper-decidable:* yes — same fibre, opposite reading. *Strip:* survives — the multivaluedness is a fact about `trunc`'s converse, not about a counter. **This is the arrow's core asymmetry: `ws4_survey_lossy` (function one way) ∧ `ws4_residue_one_to_many` (relation the other).**

**`ws4_arrow_strict` — `≺` is a strict order with no return.**
```lean
theorem ws4_arrow_strict {κ} (hinf : ℵ₀ ≤ κ) :
    Irreflexive (prec (κ := κ)) ∧ Transitive (prec (κ := κ))
theorem ws4_no_return {κ} (hinf : ℵ₀ ≤ κ) :
    ∀ m m' : Moment κ, prec m m' → ¬ prec m' m                          -- asymmetry: no cycles
```
*Strategy:* transitivity from composing residue-extensions (`Δ`-orbits compose). Irreflexivity from **properness**: `m ≺ m` would make a moment the opening of a residue of *its own* survey, i.e. a `Δ`-fixed moment; `ws6_lawvere_incomplete` (Cantor) forbids the survey from surjecting, so the residue is a *proper* new part and no moment is its own successor. No-return from the strict monotone `accDist` (C3): `prec m m'` gives `accDist m < accDist m'`, and `<` on the internal distinction measure is asymmetric, so `prec m' m` would give `accDist m' < accDist m` — contradiction. *Paper-decidable:* yes — irreflexivity is one application of the transcribed diagonal; no-return is monotonicity of `accDist`. *Strip:* survives — strictness is charged to properness (Cantor) and to `accDist` *derived from state*, neither mentioning the depth order. **This is the frictionless-pendulum guard: perpetuity (WS3) plus properness (here) = no return.**

**`ws4_arrow_endogenous` — no imported axis, no first moment; survives the strip.**
```lean
theorem ws4_arrow_endogenous {κ} (hinf : ℵ₀ ≤ κ) :
    -- (i) no first moment: every moment on a productive orbit has a ≺-predecessor…
    (∀ m : Moment κ, Productive m.1 → ∃ m₀, prec m₀ m)
    -- …(ii) no imported axis: `prec` is generated by `Δ`/`trunc`-properness, not by `Nat.lt`.
  ∧ (∀ m m', prec m m' ↔ IsResidueExt m m')                            -- def-independent of depth<depth
```
*Strategy:* **(i)** a `≺`-minimal moment `m₀` would be the opening of a residue of *nothing* — the residue of a survey of a closed prior state (a rest state / `Δ`-fixed moment). WS3 (Cantor) forbids a closed prior: no state surveys itself completely, so no moment is generated by surveying a rest state, so no moment is minimal. (The depth-0 bud `PUnit` is the *unopened* terminal — pre-temporal, not a moment: it carries no proper survey, hence opens no residue, so it is not a `≺`-least moment either.) **(ii)** the second conjunct *is* the coincidence with the forced form (next obligation): `prec` unfolds to `IsResidueExt`, which references `trunc`/`Δ` and never `Nat.lt`. *Paper-decidable:* (i) is a diagonal argument (minimality ⇒ closed prior ⇒ Cantor contradiction); (ii) is definitional unfolding checked against README §4. *Strip:* this is the strip test itself — delete `Moment`'s depth field and (ii) still types, because `IsResidueExt` is stated over founded states and `trunc`; **if (ii) failed the strip, WS4 reports Partial (imported axis).**

**`ws4_arrow_from_properness` — the coincidence: directionality comes from properness, not a counter.**
```lean
theorem ws4_arrow_from_properness {κ} (hinf : ℵ₀ ≤ κ) :
    -- the DEFINITIONAL order (README `prec`) equals the FORCED order (residue of a properly-
    -- contained survey), and its strictness is EXACTLY the properness of self-knowledge.
    (∀ m m', prec m m' ↔ IsResidueExt m m')
  ∧ (Irreflexive (prec (κ := κ)) ↔ SurveyProperlyContained (κ := κ))   -- strictness ⇔ Cantor-properness
```
*Strategy:* first conjunct as in `ws4_arrow_endogenous` (ii). Second is the load-bearing coincidence: irreflexivity of `≺` holds *iff* the survey is properly contained (the Cantor residue is nonempty) — forward, properness gives no self-successor; backward, if some moment were its own successor the survey would be surjective, negating properness. So the arrow's strictness is not an extra stipulation and not a counter — it is `ws6_lawvere_incomplete` read temporally. *Paper-decidable:* both directions are the transcribed diagonal used as an iff. *Strip:* the whole statement is counter-free; deleting the depth index leaves properness ⇔ strictness intact. **Coincidence duty discharged iff both directions prove; if only `prec ⇒ IsResidueExt` (definitional) holds, report Partial — definitional-only, forced form open.**

## Outcome classes (per charter §7), with pre-registered honest alternatives

- **Discharged:** `ws4_survey_lossy`, `ws4_residue_one_to_many`, `ws4_arrow_strict` + `ws4_no_return`, `ws4_arrow_endogenous`, and the coincidence `ws4_arrow_from_properness` — the arrow is strict, directional, endogenous, and charged to properness, every statement counter-free and strip-surviving.
- **Impossibility proved (success):** if a `≺`-minimal moment provably *must* exist on every productive orbit (a forced first moment), that is a sharp negative — the endogenous arrow is unearnable on the thread encoding — escalating to C4 (metric), where the incomplete space has no least point.
- **Partial — imported axis (the pre-registered risk, charter §9):** if `ws4_arrow_endogenous` (ii) or `ws4_arrow_from_properness`'s second conjunct **fails the strip** — directionality survives only via `Nat.lt` on depth — then the arrow is Series 5's no-first-level relocated to time, not earned; reported **Partial**, forced (properness) form named open, C4 flagged as the escalation. *Not a redefinition of the target.*
- **Partial — definitional-only:** the strict order `prec` delivered, but the coincidence `strictness ⇔ Cantor-properness` proved in one direction only; forced form is the open obligation.
