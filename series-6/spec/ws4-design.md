# WS4 ‚Äî The arrow: self-knowing gives directionality

**Design doc. Series 6, payoff workstream. Owns: the proof that the order `‚â∫` (README ¬ß4 `prec`) is strictly directional and endogenous ‚Äî the self-survey `trunc` is a lossy, deterministic, many-to-one map (the past), the residue-opening (WS3's `Œî`) is one-to-many (the open future), the generated order strict (no return), and the arrow carries no imported time axis and no first moment. `‚â∫` is defined once in README ¬ß4 and shared with WS5; WS4 proves it a strict directional order, WS5 proves total-or-partial. WS4 does not redefine `‚â∫`.**

*Series 6 is standalone; upstream names transcribed into `series-6/formal/ws4.lean`, re-namespaced `Series6.WS4` (see `spec/README.md` ¬ß5). Shared objects (`Approx`, `trunc`, `Proc`, `Œî`, `Moment`, `prec`, `faceAt`, `Œ©_n`) are defined in `spec/README.md` ¬ß¬ß2‚Äì4 and only cited here.*

## The object at stake

The charter (¬ß5.3) separates two things WS3 does not distinguish. Fixed-point-freeness buys **perpetuity** ‚Äî no moment is at rest ‚Äî but a frictionless pendulum never rests and still returns; perpetuity is not yet an **arrow**. Something must forbid return, and self-knowing is that something: to know yourself is to survey yourself, and the diagonal makes the survey a *proper* part of the whole, so self-knowing is lossy and lossy *in one direction only*. Surveying coarsens ‚Äî `trunc`, a deterministic many-to-one map ‚Äî while generating the missed detail is one-to-many, a choice, not a function. That asymmetry is the arrow: the past is the surveyable direction where self-knowledge is a well-defined lossy map, the future is the residue direction where it fails to fix a unique answer, and time points *away from the surveyable*, strictly, because the survey is *properly* contained (fixed-point-freeness again, now forbidding return).

The design question is how to cash out "directionality" so that (a) `‚â∫` is a genuine **strict** order (irreflexive, transitive, no return), (b) the direction is carried by the **proper-subobject order on self-surveys** (`trunc`/`faceAt`), not by an external counter, and (c) the arrow is **endogenous** ‚Äî no imported ‚Ñï, no first moment ‚Äî surviving the **strip test** (charter ¬ß7): delete the external time index from every statement and a direction must remain. If direction survives only through an external `‚Ñï` order on depths, the arrow is imposed, not earned, and WS4 reports honest **Partial**.

**Ambient theory.** `spec/README.md` ¬ß4: `Moment Œ∫` = a pair `(x, n)` (a process `x : Proc Œ∫` observed through survey-depth `n`), equivalently a finite founded history-prefix; `prec : Moment Œ∫ ‚Üí Moment Œ∫ ‚Üí Prop`, `m ‚â∫ m'` iff `m'` opens a residue of a survey available at `m` ‚Äî the extension order on founded histories generated by `Œî` (README ¬ß3). The survey/truncation `trunc` (README ¬ß2) and the two-sided face `faceAt` (README ¬ß4) are the proper-subobject structure the strip test falls back on. `Œî`'s fixed-point-freeness on moments (no moment equals its own successor) is WS3's `ws6_lawvere_incomplete` transported; WS4 consumes it, does not reprove it.

## Candidates (for cashing out directionality)

### C1 ‚Äî The arrow as the proper-prefix order on founded histories (the lead; survives the strip)

```lean
-- m ‚â∫ m'  iff  the founded history of m' properly extends that of m by opening a Œî-residue.
-- This IS README ¬ß4 `prec`, unfolded as a PROPER-containment order via `trunc`, with NO ‚Ñï<‚Ñï.
def IsResidueExt {Œ∫} (m m' : Moment Œ∫) : Prop :=
  ‚àÉ d, Opened (m'.hist) d ‚àß IsResidue (survey m) d ‚àß trunc_prefix m m'   -- m' = m + one opened residue
```
Directionality is the asymmetry of `trunc`: forward is the deterministic coarsening (past), its converse the multivalued residue-opening (future).

- **Ambient / fpf map:** `Œî` on moments (README ¬ß3); the survey is `trunc`-image, non-surjective by the finite Cantor diagonal (`ws6_lawvere_incomplete`).
- **Success condition:** `prec` is a strict order; strictness = the survey is *properly* contained (Cantor residue nonempty); the past-map `trunc` is a function, its converse a relation.
- **Failure mode (guarded against):** if "extends" were read as `n < n'` on the depth index, the order would be an external-‚Ñï fact wearing history-vocabulary ‚Äî the C2 trap. Avoided by defining extension through `trunc`-properness (residue *genuinely new*), not through the depth label.

**Paper triage.** *The lead, and strip-survivable by construction.* Delete the depth index `n` from `Moment` and keep only the founded state plus its `trunc`-image: the direction remains, because `trunc` is still a non-injective coarsening and its converse still multivalued ‚Äî the proper-subobject order on self-surveys is intact. **Winner**, subject to routing strictness to WS3's properness (`ws4_arrow_from_properness`) rather than to a counter.

### C2 ‚Äî The arrow as an external ‚Ñï-index on moments (the STOPWATCH trap; rejected, fails the strip)

```lean
def precStopwatch {Œ∫} (m m' : Moment Œ∫) : Prop := m.depth < m'.depth   -- ‚Ñï carries the arrow
```
Order moments by their depth coordinate: `t ‚Ü¶ x_t`, `t ‚àà ‚Ñï`. Looks dynamic, directional, strict for free.

- **Ambient / fpf map:** none needed ‚Äî `<` on `‚Ñï` does all the work.
- **Success condition:** would be that `<` on depths is a strict order (trivially true).
- **Failure mode:** **imported axis with a first moment.** `precStopwatch` is `Nat.lt` relabelled; it has a least element `depth = 0` ‚Äî a first moment, a state relationless-before-it, the tower-with-a-bottom cheat of charter ¬ß4.1 relocated to time. And it **fails the strip**: delete the external `‚Ñï` and nothing remains ‚Äî the direction was the counter, not the self-survey.

**Paper triage.** The named trap (charter ¬ß4.1, ¬ß5.6, ¬ß9). Decidable-on-paper and decided against: strip the `‚Ñï` and `precStopwatch` has no residual content, so the arrow is imposed not earned. **Reject.** Retained only as the negative the strip test exists to catch; WS7 checks every WS4 statement against exactly this.

### C3 ‚Äî The arrow as strict monotone of a genuinely-internal accumulated-distinction measure

```lean
-- Œº(m) = the total distinction accumulated in m's founded history (opened residues, counted
-- by the state, not by a stopwatch). m ‚â∫ m' ‚áí Œº strictly increases.
def accDist {Œ∫} (m : Moment Œ∫) : Ordinal      -- read OFF the founded state, not an imported clock
theorem accDist_strict_mono : prec m m' ‚Üí accDist m < accDist m'
```
Directionality = a strictly increasing internal measure of accumulated distinction: each residue is *genuinely new* (fixed-point-freeness, WS3), so distinction strictly accrues and never returns.

- **Ambient / fpf map:** `Œî`; the residue is new because the survey is non-surjective (Cantor), so `accDist` cannot repeat.
- **Success condition:** `accDist` is definable from the founded state alone and strictly monotone along `‚â∫`, certifying *no return* without a counter.
- **Failure mode:** **the measure is a counter in disguise.** If `accDist m` is secretly `m.depth` (or any order-embedding of `‚Ñï` stipulated rather than derived from the state's distinction content), C3 collapses to C2 and fails the strip.

**Paper triage.** *Not a rival carrier ‚Äî the strictness certificate C1 needs.* `accDist` must be **derived from the founded state's distinction content** (which buds have opened to genuinely-new residues), so that its strict increase is a *theorem from Cantor-properness*, not a stipulated tally. Then it survives the strip (delete `n`; `accDist` still increases, because properness still holds). **Fold into C1 as the no-return witness**, with the load-bearing check that `accDist` factors through `faceAt`/`trunc`-properness, not through the depth label ‚Äî this is the coincidence duty, `ws4_arrow_from_properness`.

### C4 ‚Äî The metric / discounted version (asymmetric behavioural distance)

```lean
-- put the behavioural quasimetric on moments; the arrow = strictly decreasing distance to the
-- refused limit, asymmetric because generating ‚âÝ forgetting.
def bdist·µÉ {Œ∫} (m m' : Moment Œ∫) : ‚Ñù‚â•0‚àû      -- asymmetric: bdist·µÉ m m' ‚âÝ bdist·µÉ m' m
```
The arrow is a strictly monotone (discounted) approach along an **asymmetric** quasimetric; asymmetry `bdist·µÉ m m' ‚âÝ bdist·µÉ m' m` encodes forgetting ‚âÝ generating.

- **Ambient / fpf map:** the diagonal as a fixed-point-free contraction on an incomplete space (Series 6 WS1 C4 fallback).
- **Success condition:** an asymmetric distance whose non-symmetry *is* the survey/residue asymmetry, strictly monotone along `‚â∫`.
- **Failure mode:** **the pun does the work.** Risk that "directional" (distance asymmetry) is proved of a quasimetric with no faithful tie back to `trunc`-properness ‚Äî the strip fails because the direction lives in the metric, not the self-survey.

**Paper triage.** Strong and Lean-tractable (Mathlib quasimetrics), and it prefigures WS5's asymmetry lever, but it changes the primitive from *relating* to *distance* and makes the residue-as-past/future asymmetry harder to render literal. **Pre-registered alternative:** if C1's strictness cannot be tied to properness on the thread encoding (`ws4_arrow_from_properness` fails the strip), escalate to C4, where the contraction's asymmetry is manifest. Typed fallback, not the lead.

## Winning candidate: C1 (proper-prefix order = README ¬ß4 `prec`), with C3's accumulated-distinction as the no-return certificate and C4 as typed fallback

The decisive moves, converting directionality to decidable facts on `prec` (not redefining it):

1. **The arrow is `trunc`'s asymmetry, read on the shared `prec`.** `trunc` is a *function* one way (deterministic, many-to-one ‚Äî the past is settled) and its converse is a *relation* one way (one-to-many ‚Äî the future is open). The order `prec` is the transitive generation of that single asymmetry; strictness is inherited from properness, not from a counter.
2. **Strictness = properness, not depth.** Irreflexivity and no-return come from the survey being *properly* contained (Cantor residue nonempty, WS3), so no moment is a residue of itself and none returns ‚Äî provable with the depth label stripped.

### The obligations, each: Lean signature + strategy + paper-decidable note + strip annotation

**`ws4_survey_lossy` ‚Äî the survey is deterministic and many-to-one (the past).**
```lean
theorem ws4_survey_lossy {Œ∫} (hinf : ‚Ñµ‚ÇÄ ‚â§ Œ∫) :
    -- deterministic: `trunc` is a genuine total function (automatic by type)‚Ä¶
    ‚àÄ n, ‚àÉ s t : A Œ∫ (n+1), s ‚âÝ t ‚àß trunc Œ∫ n s = trunc Œ∫ n t          -- ‚Ä¶and many-to-one
```
*Strategy:* exhibit, at each stage, two stage-`(n+1)` approximations differing only in a deepest layer `trunc` forgets ‚Äî a bud opened two admissible ways over the same stage-`n` state. Non-injectivity of `PkMap (trunc ‚Ä¶)` from a nontrivial fibre. *Paper-decidable:* yes ‚Äî a fibre-cardinality computation on the transcribed `trunc`; `trunc` being a function is definitional. *Strip:* survives ‚Äî "coarsening forgets detail" mentions no `‚Ñï`-order, only the map `trunc`.

**`ws4_residue_one_to_many` ‚Äî the residue-opening is a relation, not a function (the open future).**
```lean
theorem ws4_residue_one_to_many {Œ∫} (hinf : ‚Ñµ‚ÇÄ ‚â§ Œ∫) :
    ‚àÉ x : A Œ∫ n, 2 ‚â§ Cardinal.mk { s : A Œ∫ (n+1) // trunc Œ∫ n s = x }    -- ‚â• 2 futures over one past
```
*Strategy:* the fibre of `trunc` over a state that admits a genuine residue has ‚â• 2 elements ‚Äî the same non-injectivity of `ws4_survey_lossy` read *backwards*: forgetting is a function, generating is its multivalued converse. The two extensions are two admissible residue-openings (`Œî` is a choice among them, not a function on moments). *Paper-decidable:* yes ‚Äî same fibre, opposite reading. *Strip:* survives ‚Äî the multivaluedness is a fact about `trunc`'s converse, not about a counter. **This is the arrow's core asymmetry: `ws4_survey_lossy` (function one way) ‚àß `ws4_residue_one_to_many` (relation the other).**

**`ws4_arrow_strict` ‚Äî `‚â∫` is a strict order with no return.**
```lean
theorem ws4_arrow_strict {Œ∫} (hinf : ‚Ñµ‚ÇÄ ‚â§ Œ∫) :
    Irreflexive (prec (Œ∫ := Œ∫)) ‚àß Transitive (prec (Œ∫ := Œ∫))
theorem ws4_no_return {Œ∫} (hinf : ‚Ñµ‚ÇÄ ‚â§ Œ∫) :
    ‚àÄ m m' : Moment Œ∫, prec m m' ‚Üí ¬¨ prec m' m                          -- asymmetry: no cycles
```
*Strategy:* transitivity from composing residue-extensions (`Œî`-orbits compose). Irreflexivity from **properness**: `m ‚â∫ m` would make a moment the opening of a residue of *its own* survey, i.e. a `Œî`-fixed moment; `ws6_lawvere_incomplete` (Cantor) forbids the survey from surjecting, so the residue is a *proper* new part and no moment is its own successor. No-return from the strict monotone `accDist` (C3): `prec m m'` gives `accDist m < accDist m'`, and `<` on the internal distinction measure is asymmetric, so `prec m' m` would give `accDist m' < accDist m` ‚Äî contradiction. *Paper-decidable:* yes ‚Äî irreflexivity is one application of the transcribed diagonal; no-return is monotonicity of `accDist`. *Strip:* survives ‚Äî strictness is charged to properness (Cantor) and to `accDist` *derived from state*, neither mentioning the depth order. **This is the frictionless-pendulum guard: perpetuity (WS3) plus properness (here) = no return.**

**`ws4_arrow_endogenous` ‚Äî no imported axis, no first moment; survives the strip.**
```lean
theorem ws4_arrow_endogenous {Œ∫} (hinf : ‚Ñµ‚ÇÄ ‚â§ Œ∫) :
    -- (i) no first moment: every moment on a productive orbit has a ‚â∫-predecessor‚Ä¶
    (‚àÄ m : Moment Œ∫, Productive m.1 ‚Üí ‚àÉ m‚ÇÄ, prec m‚ÇÄ m)
    -- ‚Ä¶(ii) no imported axis: `prec` is generated by `Œî`/`trunc`-properness, not by `Nat.lt`.
  ‚àß (‚àÄ m m', prec m m' ‚Üî IsResidueExt m m')                            -- def-independent of depth<depth
```
*Strategy:* **(i)** a `‚â∫`-minimal moment `m‚ÇÄ` would be the opening of a residue of *nothing* ‚Äî the residue of a survey of a closed prior state (a rest state / `Œî`-fixed moment). WS3 (Cantor) forbids a closed prior: no state surveys itself completely, so no moment is generated by surveying a rest state, so no moment is minimal. (The depth-0 bud `PUnit` is the *unopened* terminal ‚Äî pre-temporal, not a moment: it carries no proper survey, hence opens no residue, so it is not a `‚â∫`-least moment either.) **(ii)** the second conjunct *is* the coincidence with the forced form (next obligation): `prec` unfolds to `IsResidueExt`, which references `trunc`/`Œî` and never `Nat.lt`. *Paper-decidable:* (i) is a diagonal argument (minimality ‚áí closed prior ‚áí Cantor contradiction); (ii) is definitional unfolding checked against README ¬ß4. *Strip:* this is the strip test itself ‚Äî delete `Moment`'s depth field and (ii) still types, because `IsResidueExt` is stated over founded states and `trunc`; **if (ii) failed the strip, WS4 reports Partial (imported axis).**

**`ws4_arrow_from_properness` ‚Äî the coincidence: directionality comes from properness, not a counter.**
```lean
theorem ws4_arrow_from_properness {Œ∫} (hinf : ‚Ñµ‚ÇÄ ‚â§ Œ∫) :
    -- the DEFINITIONAL order (README `prec`) equals the FORCED order (residue of a properly-
    -- contained survey), and its strictness is EXACTLY the properness of self-knowledge.
    (‚àÄ m m', prec m m' ‚Üî IsResidueExt m m')
  ‚àß (Irreflexive (prec (Œ∫ := Œ∫)) ‚Üî SurveyProperlyContained (Œ∫ := Œ∫))   -- strictness ‚áî Cantor-properness
```
*Strategy:* first conjunct as in `ws4_arrow_endogenous` (ii). Second is the load-bearing coincidence: irreflexivity of `‚â∫` holds *iff* the survey is properly contained (the Cantor residue is nonempty) ‚Äî forward, properness gives no self-successor; backward, if some moment were its own successor the survey would be surjective, negating properness. So the arrow's strictness is not an extra stipulation and not a counter ‚Äî it is `ws6_lawvere_incomplete` read temporally. *Paper-decidable:* both directions are the transcribed diagonal used as an iff. *Strip:* the whole statement is counter-free; deleting the depth index leaves properness ‚áî strictness intact. **Coincidence duty discharged iff both directions prove; if only `prec ‚áí IsResidueExt` (definitional) holds, report Partial ‚Äî definitional-only, forced form open.**

## Outcome classes (per charter ¬ß7), with pre-registered honest alternatives

- **Discharged:** `ws4_survey_lossy`, `ws4_residue_one_to_many`, `ws4_arrow_strict` + `ws4_no_return`, `ws4_arrow_endogenous`, and the coincidence `ws4_arrow_from_properness` ‚Äî the arrow is strict, directional, endogenous, and charged to properness, every statement counter-free and strip-surviving.
- **Impossibility proved (success):** if a `‚â∫`-minimal moment provably *must* exist on every productive orbit (a forced first moment), that is a sharp negative ‚Äî the endogenous arrow is unearnable on the thread encoding ‚Äî escalating to C4 (metric), where the incomplete space has no least point.
- **Partial ‚Äî imported axis (the pre-registered risk, charter ¬ß9):** if `ws4_arrow_endogenous` (ii) or `ws4_arrow_from_properness`'s second conjunct **fails the strip** ‚Äî directionality survives only via `Nat.lt` on depth ‚Äî then the arrow is Series 5's no-first-level relocated to time, not earned; reported **Partial**, forced (properness) form named open, C4 flagged as the escalation. *Not a redefinition of the target.*
- **Partial ‚Äî definitional-only:** the strict order `prec` delivered, but the coincidence `strictness ‚áî Cantor-properness` proved in one direction only; forced form is the open obligation.
