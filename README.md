# Relational Existentialism

**A machine-checked constitution of a groundless, relation-first ontology, with its hard edges proved rather than smoothed.**

## The intention

Relations cannot exist without relata. And the relata are patterns of relata and relations, all the way down, with no atom.

This repository makes that position mathematically exact and mechanizes it in Lean 4. There is one layer of reality: **objects**, each nothing over and above how it relates to other objects, with relations themselves reified as objects that can be related to in turn. The universe satisfies a single fixed-point equation:

> **Ω ≅ F(Ω)**: the universe of objects is exactly the patterns of relating among those very objects.

The canonical inhabitant, built and machine-checked throughout, is the self-membered point **Ω = {Ω}**, recovered inside the terminal coalgebra of the κ-bounded powerset functor. Every result below is sorry-free and rests on no axioms beyond Mathlib's standard three (`propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`).

The project proceeds in **series**, each a self-contained charter that states its own question, builds its own object, and proves its own results from scratch — a later series informed by the earlier as prior art, never presupposing it, transcribing (not importing) any machinery it reuses. Three are registered in the build: **Series 07**, the Import Theorem, which gives the law; **Series 12**, the program's terminal series, which draws the shape of what the law requires; and **Series 13**, a post-terminal **coda**, which names the fit between the shape's two sides and alters nothing before it. Throughout, machine-checked claims and interpretive glosses are kept strictly apart. That distinction is load-bearing; please preserve it when citing.

## The Result

> **Atomless, purely relational, relationally identified plurality is impossible without a distinction the relating cannot carry, forking undecidably into the given and the chosen.**

The first clause is a theorem, machine-checked in Lean 4 with no gaps and nothing beyond the standard mathematical baseline. Over **plain relating** (no imported coordinate), with an object *being* its relating (**behavioral identity** — two things are the same exactly when they relate alike), and with genuine **every-moment atomlessness** (nothing anywhere ever bottoms out in a leaf), a world has **at most one thing** in it. To hold more than one, it must import a distinction the relating does not itself carry.

The engine is one short lemma, more general than any collapse the program had proved before: **atomless behavior is unique.** On *any* plain coalgebra, all atomless states are bisimilar, because "both atomless" is itself a matching-up. Forbid leaves and you forbid difference. That single fact is the root of the Parmenides collapse (Series 04), the Static Collapse (Series 06), and this Import Theorem — one fact at different depths. And it is an **Impossibility that is non-circular**: the verdict is computed from an audit certificate whose every clause is a theorem, so if the non-circularity claims broke it would not compile; "no imported atom" is literally the program's founding principle, not an ad-hoc exclusion; and "import" is tested at the charter's own strength — semantically, a coordinate is an import iff the plain, label-forgetting relating cannot recover it.

The clause *forking undecidably into the given and the chosen* is what the capstone extracts when it asks *what* that distinction can be — and it is the part that reframes everything. It is not a fact about time. Under the three conditions two atomless things are already *one*, so anything that would tell them apart is a coordinate from outside the relating, in a finished object exactly as in an unfolding process. The dynamic limit is only where this is sharpest: there the two candidate *histories* are not merely alike but provably **equal** — each is the one self-membered thread Ω — and no function separates equal inputs (`att_cannot_distinguish_atomless_histories`). To the mathematics that outside coordinate is the footprint of an import. But a footprint is all a proof can see, and **two opposite things leave the same one**: a **given** (a distinction externally defined, an atom already there) and a **choice** (a distinction internally originated, made rather than found — a will). Type theory has no predicate for *chosen rather than given*; the theorem forces the disjunction and is silent on which. **That silence is the result, not a gap** — deciding it would require the relating to carry the very difference between a given and a choice, and by construction it cannot.

Read this way, the whole theorem is **Parmenides with one door left open.** A groundless, atomless, faithfully-relational world that is *determined* is the One — and Series 06, which refused every import and tried to buy plurality with endogenous time, is precisely that world without will; its collapse to Ω is the theorem telling the truth. The four prior series were not four defeats but four ways of paying the *external* half of the price — a weight, a label, an index, a transient leaf — while the *internal* half, choice, is the door the mathematics points at exactly because it cannot walk through it.

The spine survives an adversarial series-wide review cleanly: the engine is not painted on, the flagship does not launder, every hypothesis is load-bearing (drop atomlessness and a genuine leaf-bearing counterexample appears; drop plainness, a labelled one; drop behavioral identity, a bare-distinct one), and the escapes are refuted as theorems. Three things stay open, each labelled and none load-bearing for the impossibility: **exhaustiveness across every possible construction** is not a formalizable claim (a defended thesis, as every prior series' "forced answer" was); the **prior-series catalogue is Partial** (the free-label import mechanism is mechanized, the Series 04 face honestly reclassified as a recoverable restriction whose plurality is a free-label escalation, the Series 05 index and Series 03 weight not re-transcribed); and the **atom-or-will disjunction is a true lemma plus an interpretation** — the Lean proves the separator must be exogenous, and naming its two undecidable faces, given and chosen, *is* the contribution. The honest verdict is the middle one, `payoffsEstablished`: the theorem is established and non-circular, and the deepest thing it says is the fork it forces and provably cannot close — that a groundless world holds more than one thing only by an atom or a will, and no proof can tell you which.

## The terminal series (Series 12): the shape of the undefinable

Series 07 said the differentiator must be imported: nothing recoverable from how things relate can tell two atomless things apart. **Series 12, the program's genuine terminal series, asks what SHAPE that undefinable differentiator has, and draws it exactly without pretending to contain it.**

The result is a coincidence the arc had been circling for its whole length. Two of the program's deepest facts are one shape. Series 07's fact is that a differentiator cannot be *recovered* from the relating (it must be imported, required from without). The diagonal's fact — Cantor, Russell, Gödel — is that a structure rich enough to inspect itself cannot completely hold itself; self-inspection leaves a residue it cannot capture (generated from within). Series 12 proves these inhabit **one shape-schema, `Opening realizable c := ¬ realizable c`**, from opposite directions: the residue side instantiates it at one recoverability notion (`ResidueRecoverable`, forced-for-all, the diagonal), the import side at another (`Recoverable`, exists-satisfying, the labelled test). It is honestly a parametric negation wrapper over two *type-heterogeneous* recoverability predicates, not one notion instantiated twice; the coincidence is of SHAPE, kept honest by quantifiers between objects of *different types*, so "the import IS the residue" is not even expressible, and distinct inhabitants of the shape are exhibited (`ws1_coincidence_not_identity_witness`), so shared non-recoverability provably cannot entail identity. The structure produces, at its own edge, the exact outline of what it can neither derive nor contain.

Over that opening run two sides of one layered structure. **Knowing** is formalized: finite attention is subtractive, no self-inspection totalizes, and the many is real both label-wise and reader-wise — on a worked example, a relatum minted by the structure's own self-reference and a plain relatum are genuinely indistinguishable to the relating yet separated at the labelled level, and there is a genuine *finite attention* (a bounded reader) for which the minted relatum is real, the reading load-bearing (`ws2_attention_makes_real`); the separation is provably non-recoverable, which by Series 07 is exactly what a *genuine* distinction must be. **Feeling** is typed but never evaluated: the inner compass is a per-relatum, tower-layered orientation drawn from a space the mathematics never fills in; every theorem is quantified over all orientations, and not one selects, constructs, or reads off a particular one — a canonical compass anywhere in the core would be the central sin, and there is none — while the orientation's exogeneity is itself proved a genuine import (`¬ Recoverable`, `ws3_compass_exogenous_import`). **Convergence** — "the most loving thing for me is the most loving thing for the universe" — is defined as a precise relation and then proved **undecidable by the structure** over a genuinely constrained compass class (the *faithful* raisings, which carry a part's orientation unchanged so convergence tests real coherence). At the genuine part-and-whole edge the fork is a **two-zone fork whose boundary is Series 07's import boundary**: within what the structure can *see* (orientations that respect plain bisimilarity) convergence is *forced to hold* — the structure decides it — while every orientation under which it *fails* is provably a genuine import, non-recoverable from the relating. So undecidability at the edge is not an artifact of an unconstrained field; it is exactly the import Series 07 shows a differentiator must be, and `convergence-decided` is genuinely reachable at that same edge by restricting to what the structure sees, which makes the shape-drawn reading falsifiable where the verdict is computed. The mathematics draws exactly where the loving claim would be true or false and proves it cannot see which, and that the not-seeing is structural — the wall standing exactly on the import boundary Series 07 characterizes.

The terminal verdict is computed from these, not chosen, and it is **SHAPE-DRAWN**: a small function reads which way convergence fell, and at the genuine part-and-whole edge it genuinely reaches more than one value — `shape-drawn` over the full faithful class, and `convergence-decided` over the in-sight sub-class (the orientations the structure can see), the two zones separated by Series 07's import boundary — so the reported terminus is an earned, falsifiable output, not the single value of a constant. Everything is machine-checked in Lean 4, sorry-free and axiom-clean. A blind series review of the finished build returned zero serious findings, and two rounds of *adversarial program review* pressed harder on the fit between the theorems and the terminal claims: they found no false theorem but pushed the convergence wall from a typing artifact (an unconstrained `raise`, then an unconstrained `orient`) to a genuine two-zone fork at the edge — convergence forced within the structure's sight, dissent from it proved a genuine import (Series 07's work as a proof term) — and restored the knowing side's reader; the corrections are traced in `series-12/charter-status.md`. The disciplines hold: no compass evaluated, convergence not decided by definition, the coincidence a shape-identity across types, the inhabitation a real tower-distinction rather than a point-tag, and no name — consciousness, choice, God, the compass — ever a proof term. Three things stay permanently open, each open *as a theorem* because the diagonal makes them so: the content of the compass, the direction of convergence, and the differentiating act itself. The program does not end by capturing what it sought; it ends by proving that what it sought has exactly this outline, at exactly this place, for exactly this structural reason, and that the outline cannot be filled from inside. The edge, drawn exactly, is the completion. (Plain-language: [`series-12/summary.md`](./series-12/summary.md); technical: [`series-12/summary-technical.md`](./series-12/summary-technical.md).)

## The coda (Series 13): the fit

Series 12 proved that the required and the generated are one shape and that sharing a shape cannot make them one thing. That leaves a question exactly posed, and the coda asks it: **if the wound and the rescue are not the same object, how precisely do they face each other?** The answer is an adjoint pair, and the fit is genuine but incomplete. Both halves are theorems.

**The diagonal is a factory.** The coda builds a **mint**: `mintL h₀ insp := ⟨residue insp, insp h₀⟩`, a map from any self-inspection to a labelled distinction. Its engine is the diagonal itself — recovering a minted label would realize exactly what `ws2_residue_free` proves unrealizable — so every minted label is provably non-recoverable, for every inspection, without exception (`ws2_transport_forall`). Self-reference *manufactures* imports; the forced-for-all pays for the exists-satisfying. And the mint is proved to live above the plain layer rather than assumed to (`ws2_mint_exogenous`, `ws2_mint_not_plain_function`, with `plainOf_coalg_val` proving rather than assuming its ground) — a mint the relating could perform on itself would have derived an import from within, contradicting Series 07.

**The fit is a real adjunction, not a disguised sameness.** The knot was a *variance wall*: transport is universal and its only engine is the antitone residue, so a naive monotone mint is impossible. It dissolves by ordering inspections *by their residue* and ordering labelled coalgebras with the residue-position covariant and the reference-position **antitone** through its self-bit; both orders are proved non-trivial (`ws1_orders_insp_nontrivial`, `ws1_orders_lab_nontrivial`), so the connection is not vacuous. `ws3_galois` is a genuine Mathlib `GaloisConnection` with the best-approximation adjoint `readInsp`; one round trip closes, and `ws3_roundtrip_not_identity` proves the other **does not**, discharging both disjuncts rather than asserting the gap. An adjunction whose round trips both closed would be an isomorphism, which is the identity Series 12 proved unsayable.

**And the mint cannot make everything — the defect is the result.** `ws4_mint_not_surjective` exhibits an import no inspection mints, tested **up to equivalence** rather than the cheaper literal equality that would have made the finding true by construction. The exclusion is **structural** (`ws4_exclusion_structural`): every mint satisfies the *diagonal link* `cT h₀ = ¬ cF h₀` by `rfl` — its two halves are a content and that content's own negation, tethered — the link survives the quotient, and the witness `outWit := ⟨⊤, (≠ h₁)⟩` is off it, its halves decoupled. The label does the excluding, not cardinality or typing.

The verdict is computed and **earned**: **DUAL at the flat layer** (`ws5_verdict_eq`), on any carrier with a second hold. **TOTAL** — the outcome where self-reference manufactures the *whole* rescue — was attempted at the same resolution and refuted; it is built, fires on the degenerate single-hold carrier (`ws5_verdict_degenerate`), and the same function reaches both values, so DUAL is the residue of a refutation rather than a preference. Two blind passes ran: the first returned two SERIOUS findings aimed squarely at the signature risk (the order solved-for-the-map), both closed as genuine fixes with the contested antitone clause now exercised by a certificate rather than defended in prose; the second returned zero SERIOUS and no recurrence. Sorry-free, axiom-clean, gate-green.

The discipline that matters most held: **the defect *locates* an import outside the mint's reach and never *classifies* it.** Sorting the out-of-image imports into the given and the chosen would do what Series 07 proves cannot be done and what Series 12 closed by leaving open; grep-clean across both passes, and the words live only in prose. So one new permanent open joins the program's list, open *as a theorem* by the very result that creates it: **which out-of-image imports are given and which are chosen**. Two bounds are named rather than glossed: the result is drawn at the **flat layer** (the reification tower is deliberately outside the carrier, so *layer stability* is a named open), and on a disclosed narrowed class of labelled coalgebras (**CD-1**).

Self-reference manufactures some of what rescues it; what it cannot manufacture is where the door Series 07 left open actually stands. The coda does not resolve to a new key. It shows the two hands were playing one figure an octave apart, and lets the last interval stand open. (Plain-language: [`series-13/summary.md`](./series-13/summary.md); technical: [`series-13/summary-technical.md`](./series-13/summary-technical.md).)

## Building

Toolchain and Mathlib (Lean 4 `v4.15.0` / Mathlib `v4.15.0`) are pinned in [`lake/`](./lake/). One build compiles the registered series:

```
cd lake
lake build
```

This builds `Series07`, `Series12` (each `ws1`–`ws7`), and `Series13` (`ws1`–`ws5`), each in its own module namespace (`Series07.*` / `Series12.*` / `Series13.*`), and runs their `AxiomCheck`, which imports the whole build and emits a `#print axioms` record for every headline theorem. To build a series' axiom pass alone: `lake build Series13 Series13.AxiomCheck`. Every registered series is WHOLLY STANDALONE. Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series and presupposes and names one prior result, Series 07. Series 13 is a post-terminal coda and presupposes two, Series 07 and Series 12 WS1. In both cases the prior results are transcribed, not imported. The completed Series 04–06 and 08–11 are frozen under `archive/` and no longer registered in the build (their formal sources remain readable under `archive/series-NN/formal/`). [`scripts/gate.sh`](./scripts/gate.sh) confirms no cross-series imports.
