# T3.01 — Two faces of one generator

> *Node `T3.01` of the proof DAG ([`../../theory/spec/NODES.md`](../../theory/spec/NODES.md)).* Lean:
> `Paper3.OneGenerator` (`paper-3/formal/Paper3/OneGenerator.lean`); imports **T0.04** (`Theory.ModularFlow`)
> and **T0.01** (`Theory.RotatingSpectrum`).

The open-system (GKLS) generator `𝓛(M) = -i[(1/β)K, M] + 𝒟(M)` has two faces — a **unitary** clock and a
**dissipative** arrow — and at equilibrium they are **one** generator, not two clocks. The unitary face is
the modular flow `σ_s` (the modular Hamiltonian `K = -log ρ`, via the finite-dimensional KMS identity
`K = β·H + log Z`, so `-i[K,·] = β·(-i[H,·])` runs the modular clock at rate `1/β`). The dissipative face is
paper one's dephasing semigroup `e^{t𝒟} = schur(μ^t)`, decaying each coherence at rate
`genReal μ = Re log μ` (the arrow). In the preferred basis (`B1 = eigenbasis(ρ)`) **both are Schur
multipliers**, so they **commute**, and the joint flow `combinedFlow t = σ_{t/β} ∘ e^{t𝒟}` is a genuine
one-parameter semigroup — its law `combinedFlow_add` holding *because* the faces commute. "Two faces of one
generator," earned at equilibrium.

**Boundary, named.** The commutation rests on `B1 = eigenbasis(ρ)` (equilibrium einselection). Out of
equilibrium — that modular time *is* physical time generally — is the inherited Connes–Rovelli frontier, not
claimed (see [`../../theory/spec/modular-frontier.md`](../../theory/spec/modular-frontier.md)).
