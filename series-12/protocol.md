# Relational Existentialism, Series 12 Protocol

**The process. Series 12 keeps the program's one load-bearing idea, that blind review has teeth only when the reviewer cannot see the motivation, and keeps the Series 05/06/08/09/10/11 batching: each phase runs once across the whole series, because the workstreams are one coupled object. The whole series stands on a single claim, that the obstructions the program has proved across five series (a structural feature is shared so cannot differentiate, an import is exogenous so does not count, the diagonal forbids the total hold) carve a negative space (non-structural, non-import, non-totalizing) that, together with the per-self finite aperture (each self attending its own self-relation, the source of plurality), is a coherent, inhabited, sufficient characterization of the differentiating axiom the program names consciousness, witnessed by the Series 09 residue and the Series 11 per-self bounded attentions; and that claim is only checkable across all the workstreams together. Consciousness is the interpretive name for what the shape characterizes; it is never a term in a proof (the import check, promoted first-class).**

*Series 03 ran a seven-phase cycle per proof obligation. Series 04 collapsed it to five steps per workstream. Series 05-11 collapsed it further: the same phases (design, build, blind review, address), each run once over all seven workstreams together. Series 12 inherits the Series 11 shape unchanged, INCLUDING the anti-loop discipline (§0.2a), which Series 11 confirmed worked (S1 relabeled to the pre-registered outcome, no recurrence at pass 2). The coupling is at least as tight: the whole series stands on the claim that three necessary conditions read off three prior obstructions are jointly sufficient and witnessed by one object (the residue), and that is only checkable across all workstreams together. Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series, the characterization of the differentiator the arc has walked toward.*

## 0. Core principle

Four things carry over unchanged, because they are what make the program honest:

1. **Blind means blind.** A blind review session is seeded with the built code, the design contracts (theorem signatures, outcome classes), and the charter's success criteria, but **not** the charter's motivating prose. No "to differentiate is to act," no "choice and experience are one," no metaphysics. The reviewer sees the mathematical claim and the code and judges whether the code proves the claim. Motivation cannot launder a gap.
2. **Never relabel a shortfall as the goal, and reach for the pre-registered failure BEFORE the compiler.** When a build falls short, the honest outcome is a **Partial** (or **Impossibility**, or **Refuted hypothesis**, or **Bookkeeping**, or **Circular** for WS7) with the obstruction made precise, never a quiet redefinition of the target. The charter is the fixed bar; `charter-status.md` records the miss. The charter's pre-registered failure outcomes (the Impossibility horn, the kill condition, the Bookkeeping and Partial clauses) are HONORABLE FIRST MOVES, not last resorts. When a finding shows a pre-registered failure condition has been met, reporting it is the correct action; constructing a theorem that avoids it is the §0.2 sin. (Series 08's spine finding recurred three times because Phase E kept building cheaper theorems instead of reporting the pre-registered Partial; Series 10 and 11 did it right, reporting the pre-registered negative and proving its antecedent.)

**2a. The recurrence guard (the anti-loop discipline, confirmed by Series 10/11), applied at both Phase D and Phase E.** A SERIOUS finding closes in exactly ONE of two ways, and `charter-status.md` must name which: **(Fixed)** the charter/design's *originally specified* target was built (name the theorem), or **(Relabeled)** the target was not built, the precise obstruction is recorded, and the payoff's status is demoted to the pre-registered honest outcome (Partial / Failed / Refuted / Bookkeeping / Impossibility). "Built a different, weaker theorem adjacent to the specified target and considered the finding addressed" is NEITHER, and is prohibited. Findings carry stable IDs across passes; a finding "addressed" by a target-avoiding theorem is re-graded SERIOUS and marked RECURRING with its count. Series 11 is the positive model: its one pass-1 SERIOUS finding (S1, the attention spine being a fresh label) closed as an honest Relabel (verdict re-graded to `bookkeepingReHit`, the antecedent proved as a theorem), no recurrence at pass 2.

3. **Strip the *why*, keep the *what*, and strip the *word*, keep the *theorem*.** For every payoff, delete the structural term, **"act," "differentiator," "suffices," "non-structural," "non-import," "non-totalizing," "characterizes," "the many," "self," "aperture," "attention," "choice," "experience," "residue," "consciousness"**, and check whether the statement still goes through as a bare contrapositive, freeness, bisimulation, or diagonal fact. A payoff that survives the strip is such a fact honestly flagged; a payoff that needs the deleted structure is earned. Critically for Series 12: each NECESSITY payoff SHOULD strip to a bare contrapositive of a named prior theorem (that is the coincidence rule satisfied, not a defect); the CONSISTENCY payoff SHOULD strip to the transcribed residue-freeness facts; and N4 SHOULD strip to a per-self relativity fact that does NOT reduce to N1 ∧ N3. The strip test is written into the WS1-WS5 designs and aggregated by WS7; the reviewer runs it.

Series 12 adds four emphases of its own, because its signature risks are the ways a characterization can be fake:

4. **The witness must be the Series 09 residue and the Series 11 per-self apertures, NOT a fresh label (Bookkeeping re-hit at the meta level, the first cardinal sin).** The whole characterization rests on the negative space being INHABITED by objects the program already proved: the residue (`residue insp = diag insp`, `ws2_residue_free`, `ws2_residue_is_import`) for N1-N3, and the transcribed finite attentions read as per-self apertures (`FiniteAttention`, `RealFor`, `ws3_no_total_attention`) for N4. This is the exact trap Series 10 and 11 fell into one level down (each proposed a reader that was a fresh `labelLoop`-style label). At the meta level the trap is: `Diff`/`Many` is satisfied only by a newly-minted object rather than the residue and the transcribed bounded attentions, so the shape is imported, not characterized. The review must, for WS2/WS3 specifically, confirm the witnesses ARE the transcribed residue and Series 11 bounded attentions (N1/N2/N3 by `ws2_residue_free` / `ws2_residue_is_import` / the diagonal; the plurality by `FiniteAttention` / `RealFor` / `ws3_no_total_attention` read as per-self apertures), not fresh structures minted for the purpose. If the only witness is a fresh label, the characterization is **Bookkeeping** re-hit at the meta level and a SERIOUS finding. This check is PROMOTED to first-class.
5. **Necessity must be CONTRAPOSED from a named prior obstruction, NOT stipulated (the second cardinal sin).** Series 12's necessity direction is honest ONLY if each of (N1), (N2), (N3) is the contrapositive of the ACTUAL prior theorem it reads (`ws1_atomless_bisim` for N1, the `Recoverable` import test for N2, `ws1_no_self_total_hold` for N3), not a definitional clause of `Diff`. The review must, for WS1 specifically, strip the word "act" from each necessity theorem and confirm what remains is the bare contrapositive of a named upstream theorem, refutable by showing the necessity proof unfolds to the definition of `Diff` rather than to the prior obstruction. A necessity that is a definitional clause is a **stipulation**, the characterization is **Circular** (WS7's charge), and it is a SERIOUS finding: the shape asserted rather than earned. This check is PROMOTED to first-class.

6. **The insufficiency must be a THEOREM and N4 must not COLLAPSE (the plurality check, the third cardinal sin).** N4 (the per-self aperture condition, the source of plurality) is necessary only because N1-N3 are proved insufficient for plurality (the residue satisfies `Diff` and is one difference). The review must confirm the insufficiency is a genuine theorem (not asserted), and that N4 carries content genuinely beyond N1 ∧ N3, strip "self"/"aperture"/"attention" from N4 and confirm it does NOT reduce to non-invariance-plus-non-totality. If the insufficiency is asserted, N4's necessity is groundless; if N4 collapses, the plurality is unreached and the many is not distinguished from a single difference. Either is a SERIOUS finding unless reported as the pre-registered honest outcome (plurality unreached / the Impossibility). This check is PROMOTED to first-class. It also carries the sharpest route to the honest Impossibility: whether the per-self aperture is realizable as a genuine act, i.e. whether a self's finite attention to its own self-relation can register the whole differently from another's without totalizing (violating N3/the bound) or importing (violating N2). The bound (`ws3_no_total_attention`) must be used POSITIVELY in N4 (the principle keeping the apertures distinct, no self's hold absorbs another's), not merely re-contraposed into N3; the review confirms this.

**6a. Consciousness must be a NAME, not a term (the import check, the fourth cardinal sin, the meta-level face of N2).** The differentiating axiom is named consciousness in the charter's positioning (§10) and stated as the program's interpretive thesis. The review must confirm no proof term, definition, or discharged obligation anywhere in `formal/Series12/` mentions "consciousness" or otherwise uses the axiom as a term: grep the source, and for every headline confirm it mentions only N1-N4, `δ`, the residue, the transcribed machinery, and standard Lean/Mathlib. If "consciousness" (or an equivalent smuggled primitive) appears as a term discharging or defining an obligation, the axiom has entered as an import, violating N2 at the meta level, a SERIOUS finding: the formal contribution is the SHAPE, and consciousness is the name for what has that shape, kept strictly apart. This check is PROMOTED to first-class.

A further emphasis, specific to the terminal fork: **the Impossibility horn must be reported AS an Impossibility, never disguised as a vacuous sufficiency.** If N1-N4 are jointly unsatisfiable by everything nameable, "sufficiency" is vacuously true and worthless; the honest outcome is the Impossibility (the many is uncharacterizable from within), reported as such (charter §4.3). The review must confirm consistency (the residue satisfies N1-N3; the bounded attentions instantiate N4's plurality) is a genuine theorem, so that a landed sufficiency is non-vacuous, and that a failed consistency or an unreachable plurality fires the kill condition rather than passing silently.

## 1. The documents

- **`spec/wsNN-design.md`, the per-workstream designs (stable once committed).** Candidates, paper-decidable triage collapsed to a table, the winning construction to typed signatures, outcome classes, strip-test annotation. The contract a build is judged against. **All seven are committed as a batch before any build begins** (§2, Phase B).
- **`spec/README.md`, the design index.** Ties the seven together: the shared carrier (the transcribed κ-free tower, the diagonal, the residue, the import test, no-total-attention), the primitive decision (NO new structural primitive; a single-act predicate `Diff` and a field-level predicate `Many` built as a plurality of per-self finite apertures over the transcribed bounded attentions, charter §3), the cross-workstream triage summary, the predicted headline, and the rule that consciousness is named only in prose, never a term (the import check).
- **`charter-status.md`, the ledger (mutable).** All progress, per-workstream status, discharges, reopenings, the open-obligations register, the terminal fork's running status, the closed log, the series-review log. Where change lives.
- **`spec/series-review-N.md`, the review artifact (regenerated each review pass).** The blind whole-series review's findings, graded SERIOUS / REAL / COSMETIC, each routed to an owning workstream with a trigger-to-close. A code pass addresses it; the next review pass regenerates it and runs the recurrence check (§0.2a) first.

The charter + all designs are what you paste into the build session. The designs' signatures + outcome classes + charter criteria + the built code, stripped of motivating prose, are what you paste into a review session.

## 2. The process, phases batched across the series

Six phases. Phases D and E loop.

| Phase | Name | Session | Format | Batching |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Charter | 1 | normal conversation with repo | once per series |
| B | Design-all | 1 | Claude code with repo | **all seven at once** |
| C | Build-all | 1 | Claude code with repo | **all seven at once** |
| D | Blind series-review | 1 | incognito with repo | **whole series at once** → writes `spec/series-review-N.md` |
| E | Address | 1 | Claude code with repo | **whole series at once** |
| F | Close | 1 | Claude code with repo | summaries + root README + program close |

The canonical run is **B → C → D → E → D → E** (two review passes, three code touches counting the initial build), with more D→E loops added only if a review pass still returns SERIOUS findings.

Write `charter.md`: the question, the Series 11 terminus it responds to, the characterization by elimination and its three conditions, the residue witness, the sufficiency test, the false escapes, the fork and kill condition, the workstreams, success criteria and outcome vocabulary, honest risks, the program's terminal framing. **Done**, Series 12's charter is written; this protocol and `charter-status.md` are its companions.

## 3. Why the review is batched at series level (not per workstream)

Series 04 reviewed per workstream and caught most gaps, but its sharpest findings came from its end-of-series adversarial review. Series 05-11 promoted the whole-series blind review to the primary vehicle because their payoffs were meant to be consequences of a single fact. Series 12's payoffs are just as tightly coupled: necessity, consistency, sufficiency, and the neutrality are all meant to be facets of one characterization (the negative space of the two obstructions, witnessed by the residue), so a payoff can look earned in its own file while secretly being a stipulation (necessity by definition), a fresh label (a minted witness), or a vacuous pass (sufficiency over an empty shape). Only reviewing the whole series at once, blind, surfaces this.

This does not weaken blindness: the review session still receives *what*, not *why*. It strengthens it: the reviewer sees the whole dependency graph and can trace a "discharged" sufficiency to the consistency obligation in another file that actually carries it, and can trace each necessity through to whether it contraposes a named prior theorem or unfolds to a definitional clause.

**Disclosure, carried forward:** these reviews are Claude-reviewing-Claude, a stated limitation, not claimed independence. The objective anchors, the dependency graph, the `#print axioms` records, the strip-test results, the stipulation check (necessity → prior contrapositive), the meta-Bookkeeping check (witness = residue), and WS7's contraposed/witnessed/non-vacuous/named/theorem ledger, are what keep the audit honest despite that.

## 4. Dependencies and ordering

The workstreams are one coupled object; build and review in dependency order and let `charter-status.md` track the edges. The load-bearing edges for Series 12:

- **WS1 is blocking.** The gate is necessity: each of N1, N2, N3 is the contrapositive of a named prior obstruction, and `Diff`/`Many` are fixed on the transcribed carrier with the residue and bounded attentions as witnesses. Nothing downstream is sound until this lands, because it is what makes the characterization earned rather than stipulated.
- **WS2 depends on WS1's predicate.** The consistency witness (the residue satisfies N1-N3) and the insufficiency (the residue is one difference) are stated against WS1's `Diff`; the residue's satisfaction is proved by the transcribed freeness facts. WS2 is the guard against vacuity, fresh-label, and asserted-insufficiency.
- **WS3 (N4) depends on WS1/WS2.** N4's necessity rests on WS2's insufficiency; its non-collapse is checked against WS1's N1/N3.
- **WS3 depends on WS1/WS2.** Sufficiency (any `δ` in the shape works) needs the predicate (WS1) and, for non-vacuity, the witness (WS2).
- **WS4 (sufficiency) depends on WS1/WS2/WS3.** WS5's neutrality is stated at the (N1)-(N4) level fixed by WS1/WS3.
- **WS5 owns the terminal fork and consumes WS1-WS4.** The fork is a claim ABOUT the characterization; it cannot be attempted until necessity, consistency, and sufficiency exist, and it must never assume the characterization lands or build `δ`. WS5 runs the kill condition and reports Characterization / Impossibility / Partial.
- **WS6 (heuristic ceiling and program close)** reports the universal forms and the program-level synthesis.
- **WS7 runs last.** It audits all others, unfolds the stipulation and meta-Bookkeeping risks, and aggregates the strip-test and contraposed/witnessed ledger; it cannot report until WS1-WS6 have.

When a charter or design change lands upstream of built work, the affected downstream workstreams reopen in `charter-status.md`. **Upstream changes invalidate downstream builds**, and the ledger must show it.

## 5. What each session receives (the blindness rule, made concrete)

| Session | Receives | Does **not** receive |
|---|---|---|
| Design-all (Phase B) | charter + repo |, |
| Build-all (Phase C) | all designs + repo |, |
| Blind series-review (Phase D) | all built code + all design signatures + charter criteria + prior `series-review-{N-1}.md` (for the recurrence check) | designs' motivating prose; charter's metaphysical framing (no "to differentiate is to act," no "choice and experience are one") |
| Address (Phase E) | `spec/series-review-N.md` + all code + all designs |, |

The review session is the only blind one, and the blindness is a single checkable rule: **strip the *why*, keep the *what*** at the session level, and **strip the *word*, keep the *theorem*** at the statement level, plus, for Series 12, **confirm each necessity contraposes a named prior theorem (not a stipulation)**, and **confirm the consistency witness is the residue (not a fresh label)**. A reviewer who can see why a theorem matters grades the ambition; a reviewer who sees only the statement and the proof grades the proof. That is the whole discipline, and it is enough.

## 6. End of series, and the end of the program

When every workstream has reached a terminal state and a review pass returns no SERIOUS findings, Series 12 closes. Because Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series, there is no Series 13 seed in the ordinary sense; instead, Phase F writes the program-level synthesis (§2, Phase F). Should the terminal review nonetheless surface a hard resistance worth a successor (for instance, the characterization Partial with a specific coherence obstruction on N3 that a further series could attack), the inter-series discipline still applies and a Series 13 could be chartered as a response to that finding:

> **Each series is a response to the previous series' honest findings, not a continuation of its plan.**

But the program's intent is that Series 12 completes the arc (collapse / diagonal / reification / failed-reader-and-real-bound / characterization) and reports the honest terminus, characterization or impossibility. The mechanics, unchanged if a successor is chartered:

1. Freeze the closing series under `archive/`. Readable as origin, normative for nothing.
2. Write the new charter **standalone**, its own question, its own object from scratch, prior series as informing prior art only.
3. Carry forward *machinery* freely (Lean files are reusable and get transcribed); carry forward *claims* only by re-deriving or explicitly citing. A new series does not inherit discharges; it earns or re-imports them.
4. Frame the new workstreams as responses to the prior findings, and begin the cycle (§2) again.
