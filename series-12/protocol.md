# Relational Existentialism, Series 12 Protocol

**The process. Series 12 keeps the program's one load-bearing idea, that blind review has teeth only when the reviewer cannot see the motivation, and keeps the batching used since Series 05: each phase runs once across the whole series, because the workstreams are one coupled object. The whole series stands on a single constructive question, the SHAPE of the attention-directing mechanism `A` that makes a self-referencing, relation-reifying, finitely-attending structure PLURAL. `A` maps a basepoint (an SHNE point) to a finite horizon (a `FiniteAttention` grounded in `SReaches`), and three shape conditions fix it: (M1) `A` is a SECTION not a projection (its selection is `¬ Recoverable`); (M2) `A` is NON-CONSTANT across basepoints and its variation FACTORS THROUGH the tower order (`prec`, `SReaches`, `ws3_order_endogenous`), NOT a carrier coordinate; (M3) FINITUDE INDIVIDUATES (the bound `ws3_no_total_attention` used positively, since a total horizon collapses the many). The single fact that makes `A` possible or impossible is tower NON-HOMOGENEITY (distinct SHNE basepoints with non-plain-bisimilar finite neighborhoods), the day-one blocking check. The key fact that makes the honest/dishonest line DECIDABLE: endogeneity is a property of `A`'s VARIATION across basepoints (does basepoint-dependence factor through `prec`/`SReaches` or through a carrier coordinate), not of any reading in isolation. That claim is only checkable across all the workstreams together. Consciousness is the interpretive name for what `A` realizes; it is never a term in a proof (the import check, promoted first-class).**

*The program's phase cycle collapsed over its history: from a seven-phase cycle per proof obligation, to five steps per workstream, to the current shape, the same phases (design, build, blind review, address) each run once over all seven workstreams together, with the anti-loop discipline (§0.2a) that keeps a shortfall from being quietly relabeled as the goal. Series 12 uses this shape unchanged. The coupling is tight: the whole series stands on whether the structure's own geometry, read from many finite basepoints, makes it plural, and that is only checkable across all workstreams together. Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series, the mechanism the arc has walked toward.*

## 0. Core principle

Three things carry the program's honesty, and are unchanged:

1. **Blind means blind.** A blind review session is seeded with the built code, the design contracts (theorem signatures, outcome classes), and the charter's success criteria, but **not** the charter's motivating prose. No "to attend is to become," no metaphysics, no "consciousness." The reviewer sees the mathematical claim and the code and judges whether the code proves the claim. Motivation cannot launder a gap.

2. **Never relabel a shortfall as the goal, and reach for the pre-registered failure BEFORE the compiler.** When a build falls short, the honest outcome is a **Partial** (or **No-mechanism**, or **Refuted**, or **Circular** for WS7) with the obstruction made precise, never a quiet redefinition of the target. The charter is the fixed bar; `charter-status.md` records the miss. The charter's pre-registered failure outcomes (the geometric Impossibility / no-mechanism, the Partial clauses) are HONORABLE FIRST MOVES, not last resorts. When a finding shows a pre-registered failure condition has been met, reporting it is the correct action; constructing a theorem that avoids it is the §0.2 sin.

**2a. The recurrence guard (the anti-loop discipline), applied at both Phase D and Phase E.** A SERIOUS finding closes in exactly ONE of two ways, and `charter-status.md` must name which: **(Fixed)** the charter/design's *originally specified* target was built (name the theorem), or **(Relabeled)** the target was not built, the precise obstruction is recorded, and the payoff's status is demoted to the pre-registered honest outcome (Partial / No-mechanism / Refuted). "Built a different, weaker theorem adjacent to the specified target and considered the finding addressed" is NEITHER, and is prohibited. Findings carry stable IDs across passes; a finding "addressed" by a target-avoiding theorem is re-graded SERIOUS and marked RECURRING with its count.

3. **Strip the *why*, keep the *what*, and strip the *word*, keep the *theorem*.** For every payoff, delete the structural term, **"mechanism," "section," "basepoint," "horizon," "variation," "non-homogeneous," "perspectival," "finite," "endogenous," "exogenous," "self," "choice," "experience," "residue," "consciousness"**, and check whether the statement still goes through as a bare reachability (`SReaches`), order (`prec`), bisimulation, or diagonal fact. A payoff that survives the strip is such a fact honestly flagged; a payoff that needs the deleted structure is earned. Critically: the NON-HOMOGENEITY payoff SHOULD strip to "two SHNE basepoints with non-plain-bisimilar finite neighborhoods" (a `plainOf`/`prec` fact); the VARIATION payoff (M2) SHOULD strip to "`A p ≠ A q` witnessed by a `SReaches`/`prec` difference" and must NOT strip to a carrier-component difference; the FINITUDE payoff (M3) SHOULD strip to "totality would force `A p = A q`." The strip test is written into the WS1-WS5 designs and aggregated by WS7; the reviewer runs it.

Series 12 adds five emphases of its own, because its signature risks are the ways the plural-making mechanism can be faked:

4. **Non-homogeneity must be a `plainOf`-visible geometric fact, not asserted (WS1, the blocking check, PROMOTED to first-class).** WS1's blocking obligation is to exhibit distinct SHNE basepoints `p`, `q` (`SHNE dest p`, `SHNE dest q`) whose finite reification neighborhoods are NOT plain-bisimilar. The review must confirm the witness is a genuine `plainOf`/`prec` difference of local geometry, not an asserted hypothesis and not a carrier artifact. If non-homogeneity is assumed to unblock the build, `A`'s variation has nothing real to inherit and the plurality is manufactured, a SERIOUS finding (the §0.2 sin). If non-homogeneity is refuted, the mechanism is impossible and the terminus is the geometric Impossibility (no-mechanism), reported as such.

5. **`A`'s variation must FACTOR THROUGH the tower order, not the carrier (M2, WS3, the first cardinal sin, PROMOTED to first-class).** The mechanism is endogenous ONLY if `A p ≠ A q` is witnessed by a `SReaches`/`prec` fact, the finite neighborhoods genuinely differ through the structure, NOT by a carrier-component difference. The review must unfold the witness of `A`'s non-constancy and confirm it is a reachability/order fact, refutable by showing it is a carrier-label difference (a `labelLoop`-style coordinate indexed by basepoints, exogenous plurality, the structure did not make itself many, a coordinate did). This is the endogenous/exogenous line, checkable on the mechanism's VARIATION rather than on a reading in isolation, which is exactly what makes it decidable: two readings can look identically free in isolation (both non-`Recoverable`) yet their DIFFERENCE be endogenous or exogenous.

6. **`A` must be a genuine SECTION (M1, WS2).** `A`'s selection of a finite horizon per basepoint must be `¬ Recoverable` (not the plain quotient, which merges basepoints). The review confirms `A` is not a constant map dressed as a section (that is one reading, not plurality), and not a carrier-indexed selection (that is exogenous). If `A` is constant or carrier-indexed, it is not the plural-making mechanism, a SERIOUS finding.

**6a. Finitude must INDIVIDUATE, not merely restrict (M3, WS3, the positive-bound check, PROMOTED to first-class).** The review must confirm the bound `ws3_no_total_attention` is used POSITIVELY: that a total horizon would force `A p = A q` for all basepoints (one reading, the One), so finitude is what GENERATES the difference across basepoints. A merely-prohibitive use of the bound (keeping horizons apart without showing finitude generates the difference) is a Partial, not the mechanism, and must be reported as such, not as MECHANISM-EXISTS.

**6b. Plurality must be witnessed endogenously (WS4, the payoff).** The `Many` payoff (`RealFor (A p) x ∧ ¬ RealFor (A q) x`) must be witnessed by a `prec`/`SReaches` fact inherited from WS1's non-homogeneity through WS2-WS3's section, NOT by a carrier label. If the only available witness is exogenous, plurality is retracted and the terminus is the geometric Impossibility (NO-MECHANISM), a SERIOUS finding if reported otherwise.

**6c. Consciousness must be a NAME, not a term (the import check).** The review must confirm no proof term, definition, or discharged obligation anywhere in `formal/Series12/` mentions "consciousness" or otherwise uses the axiom as a term: grep the source, and confirm every headline mentions only the mechanism `A`, basepoints, finite horizons, reachability/order, the transcribed machinery, and standard Lean/Mathlib. If "consciousness" appears as a term, the axiom has entered as an import, a SERIOUS finding: the core proves whether the plural-making mechanism has the shape, and consciousness is the name for what would run it, kept strictly apart.

- **`spec/README.md`, the design index.** Ties the seven together: the shared carrier (the transcribed κ-free tower `towerN`/`reifyStep`/`prec`, the diagonal, the residue, the import test `Recoverable`/`plainOf`, `SReaches`/`SHNE`, `FiniteAttention`/`RealFor`/`Many`, `ws3_no_total_attention`, `ws3_order_endogenous`), the primitive decision (NO new structural primitive and NO new monadic predicate; ONE object, the mechanism `A : basepoint → FiniteAttention`, whose shape M1-M3 is the target, charter §3), the cross-workstream triage summary, the three outcomes (mechanism-exists / no-mechanism / partial), and the rule that consciousness is named only in prose, never a term (the import check).

- **`spec/series-review-N.md`, the review artifact (regenerated each review pass).** The blind whole-series review's findings, graded SERIOUS / REAL / COSMETIC, each routed to an owning workstream with a trigger-to-close. The charter + all designs are what you paste into the build session. The designs' signatures + outcome classes + charter criteria + the built code, stripped of motivating prose, are what you paste into a review session.

## 2. The process, phases batched across the series

Six phases. Phases D and E loop.

| Phase | Name | Session | Format | Batching |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Charter | 1 | normal conversation with repo | once per series |
| B | Design-all | 1 | Claude code with repo | **all seven at once** |
| C | Build-all | 1 | Claude code with repo | **all seven at once** |
| D | Blind series-review | 1 | incognito with repo | **whole series at once** → writes `spec/series-review-N.md` |
| E | Address | 1 | Claude code with repo | **whole series at once** |
| F | Close | 1 | Claude code with repo | summaries + root README + program close |

The canonical run is **B → C → D → E → D → E** (two review passes, three code touches counting the initial build), with more D→E loops added only if a review pass still returns SERIOUS findings.

## 3. Why the review is batched at series level (not per workstream)

Earlier series reviewed per workstream and caught most gaps, but their sharpest findings came from end-of-series adversarial review, so the whole-series blind review is the primary vehicle. Series 12's payoffs are tightly coupled: non-homogeneity, the section `A`, the factor-through-the-order variation, and the plurality witness are all facets of one question (does the structure's own geometry make it plural), so a payoff can look earned in its own file while secretly resting on an asserted non-homogeneity (WS1), a carrier-factored variation (WS3), or an exogenous plurality witness (WS4). Only reviewing the whole series at once, blind, surfaces this.

This does not weaken blindness: the review session still receives *what*, not *why*. It strengthens it: the reviewer sees the whole dependency graph and can trace a "settled" plurality to the non-homogeneity witness and the section in other files that actually carry it, and can trace `A`'s variation through to whether it factors through `prec`/`SReaches` or silently through a carrier label.

**Disclosure, carried forward:** these reviews are Claude-reviewing-Claude, a stated limitation, not claimed independence. ## 4. Dependencies and ordering

The workstreams are one coupled object; build and review in dependency order and let `charter-status.md` track the edges. The load-bearing edges:

- **WS1 is blocking.** The gate is tower NON-HOMOGENEITY: distinct SHNE basepoints with non-plain-bisimilar finite neighborhoods, a `plainOf`/`prec` fact. Nothing downstream is sound until this lands, because it is the variation `A` inherits, and if it is refuted the terminus is the geometric Impossibility (no-mechanism).
- **WS2 depends on WS1's witness.** The mechanism `A` (a basepoint-indexed finite section, M1) is built from the non-homogeneity witness. WS2 is the guard against a constant or carrier-indexed `A`.
- **WS3 depends on WS1/WS2.** M2 (variation factors through `prec`/`SReaches`, not a carrier) and M3 (finitude individuates) are proved against WS1's witness and WS2's section.
- **WS4 depends on WS1/WS2/WS3.** Plurality (`Many` via `A`, witnessed endogenously) needs the witness (WS1), the section (WS2), and the endogenous variation (WS3).
- **WS5 owns the verdict and consumes WS1-WS4.** The verdict is a FUNCTION of the mechanism's existence and the locus of its variation, never hand-set; it cannot be computed until WS4 settles, and its witness must be endogenous (a `prec`/`SReaches` fact). WS5 also carries the standing neutrality consequence.
- **WS6 (program close)** reports the terminus (mechanism-exists / no-mechanism / partial) and the program-level synthesis.
- **WS7 runs last.** It audits all others, unfolds the non-homogeneity and factor-through risks, and aggregates the strip-test ledger; it cannot report until WS1-WS6 have.

When a charter or design change lands upstream of built work, the affected downstream workstreams reopen in `charter-status.md`. **Upstream changes invalidate downstream builds**, and the ledger must show it.

## 6. End of series, and the end of the program

When every workstream has reached a terminal state and a review pass returns no SERIOUS findings, Series 12 closes. Because Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series, there is no Series 13 seed in the ordinary sense; instead, Phase F writes the program-level synthesis (§2, Phase F). Should the terminal review nonetheless surface a hard resistance worth a successor (for instance, a no-mechanism finding whose homogeneity a richer geometry could attack), the inter-series discipline still applies and a Series 13 could be chartered as a response to that finding:

> **Each series is a response to the previous series' honest findings, not a continuation of its plan.**

But the program's intent is that Series 12 completes the arc (collapse / diagonal / reification / self-bounded-tower / the plural-making mechanism) and reports the honest terminus, mechanism-exists / no-mechanism / partial. The mechanics, unchanged if a successor is chartered:

1. Freeze the closing series under `archive/`. Readable as origin, normative for nothing.
2. Write the new charter **standalone**, its own question, its own object from scratch, prior series as informing prior art only.
3. Carry forward *machinery* freely (Lean files are reusable and get transcribed); carry forward *claims* only by re-deriving or explicitly citing. A new series does not inherit discharges; it earns or re-imports them.
4. Frame the new workstreams as responses to the prior findings, and begin the cycle (§2) again.
