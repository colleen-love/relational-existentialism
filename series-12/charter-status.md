# Relational Existentialism, Series 12: Charter Status

**The mutable companion to `charter.md`. All progress, revisions, per-workstream status, discharges, and open items live here; the charter stays clean.**

*Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series. Its question: what is the SHAPE of an exogenous, finite, attention-directing mechanism `A` that makes a self-referencing, relation-reifying structure PLURAL? The premise, proved by Series 07: in an atomless world any distinct pair failing behavioral identity is an import (`ws2_import_theorem`, `ws3_atomless_distinct_is_import`), and atomless loci with no imported atom are plain-bisimilar (`ws1_atomless_bisim`), so endogenous plurality is impossible and the differentiator is NECESSARILY exogenous. `A` assigns to each relatum a finite attention (a bounded reading of the reified structure) directed by an adjoined parameter not recoverable from the relating; three conditions fix it: (M1) exogenous (an open import, certified necessary by Series 07); (M2) finite (each reading bounded, so no self-definition closes); (M3) labels self-relations (directs a relatum's finite attention into its own reified self-relation). The payoff is two-sided: with `A`, plurality holds via distinct free readings; without any import, the One (Series 07). The genuinely uncertain obligation is whether the exogenous direction has PURCHASE (genuinely makes distinct readings) or is degenerate (every reading coincides), which is the honest no-mechanism if it fails. The verdict is a function of the import's purchase. The identification of `A`'s exogenous finite attending AS consciousness is the program's central interpretive thesis, walled off from the machine-checked core, which proves the shape and never takes consciousness as a term. Phase A (charter, charter-status, protocol) is the current state; Phases B-F have not been run.*

**Recurring lesson carried across the program:** the sharpest result is a proved impossibility, and an honestly-reported Partial beats a smuggled success. A theorem can hold and still fail to be the fact you claimed. A payoff can be genuine yet thin. And, the specific lesson Series 12 turns on: a distinction that is free/non-recoverable is NOT thereby a failure. In a reader-question (can the structure see the many unaided) a non-recoverable distinction is disqualified as an import. The whole series rests on getting this sign right: reporting mechanism-exists only if the import is adjoined openly and has genuine purchase (distinct readings that genuinely differ under the direction), and reporting no-mechanism only if the import has no purchase (readings coincide), never by re-deriving the Series 07 collapse (the import's absence), which is not the question.
