**The mutable companion to `charter.md`. All progress, revisions, per-workstream status, discharges, and open items live here; the charter stays clean.**

*Series 12 is the program's genuine terminal series. Its question: what is the SHAPE of the attention-directing mechanism `A` that makes a self-referencing, relation-reifying, finitely-attending structure PLURAL? The arc has built the parts (self-reference and the diagonal residue, Series 09; reification into a tower, Series 10; the tower self-bounded, Series 11) but never the mechanism that assembles a many. Series 12's claim is that the mechanism, if it exists, is perspectival: the many is the many finite first-person horizons onto one shared structure, genuinely many only if the structure is not the same seen from every basepoint. `A` maps a basepoint (an SHNE point) to a finite horizon (a `FiniteAttention` grounded in `SReaches`), and three shape conditions fix it: (M1) a section not a projection (`¬ Recoverable`); (M2) non-constant across basepoints with its variation factoring through the tower order (`prec`, `SReaches`, `ws3_order_endogenous`), not a carrier coordinate; (M3) finitude as the individuator (the bound `ws3_no_total_attention` used positively, since a total horizon collapses the many). The blocking obligation is tower NON-HOMOGENEITY (distinct SHNE basepoints with non-plain-bisimilar finite neighborhoods). The verdict is a function of the mechanism's existence and the locus of its variation. The identification of `A`'s output AS consciousness is the program's central interpretive thesis, walled off from the machine-checked core, which proves the shape and never takes consciousness as a term. Phase A (charter, charter-status, protocol) is the current state; Phases B-F have not been run.*
