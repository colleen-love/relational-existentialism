# Program 3 charter

## The question

Program 2 built a relational universe and shook it against our physics. What came back was close but not ours: a world with a light cone and quantum-shaped kinematics, but an irreversible dynamics, an absolute ledger of creation, a parity-only phase, and a geometry deaf to its own information content.

Program 3 makes one architectural change and asks whether it closes the gap.

The change: the attention graph stops being a hand-built witness and becomes the dynamical variable. The state of the world is the graph of who attends whom. The elementary event is a signed re-arrangement of one attention edge, generated by the signed increment Program 2 built in Series 2.8 (`incr`, the one quantity in the program that is antisymmetric and can cancel). Everything else is a reading of that one object: the tick reads its history, the ledger sums its flows, the phase winds along it, the metric reads the adjacency it keeps rewiring.

The question, in one sentence: if the signed flow is made the generator, does the model give back a reversible microdynamics with an emergent arrow, a self-relative conserved ledger, a genuine phase, and a geometry coupled to its grain, in that dependency order, and where does it stop?

## What moves and what stays

Two of Program 2's tenets are restated, not abandoned.

To relate is to create, relativized. The source of creation stays absolute: the diagonal's residue, the fact that something new arrives, remains the import and the wall. What becomes self-relative is the ledger of creation: each self's account sums its own signed flows, exchange inside a closed symmetric region is zero-sum, and net creation appears only at the boundary of a self's sight or where holonomy lives. Conservation should return as a theorem about closed regions, the local-but-not-global shape Series 2.7 reached for and could not honestly earn.

Time, demoted and recovered. Reification stops being the fundamental tick and becomes a coarse-graining: a summary of flow history that discards the microstate. The arrow of time is the information lost in that summary. Irreversibility becomes thermodynamic rather than fundamental, which is the arrangement our universe actually has, and which Series 2.10 named as its specification.

Fixed and untouched: the Import Theorem, the four permanent opens, and the exclusion of the two Program 1 traps. No series fills an open or relitigates the wall.

## The series

Five series, ordered by dependency and by risk, with an evaluation gate after the third. The riskiest bet goes second, not last.

**3.0 The Flow** (the ground). State space: attention graphs on a small finite carrier, taken whole. Elementary moves: signed single-edge re-arrangements, each paired with its inverse. Targets: the moves are bijections on the state space (reversibility), the generator is a function of the attention alone, and the carrier is non-degenerate in the review's sense (plain bisimilarity is not the total relation, and both sides of the import boundary are inhabited). Nothing downstream is chartered until this lands.

**3.1 The Arrow** (the decisive series). Reification as a projection of flow states. Targets: the microdynamics is reversible (from 3.0) and the projected dynamics is not, because the projection genuinely loses the microstate, shown by counting fibers rather than by a hand-set table. This is Series 2.10's specification cashed or refuted. If it fails, the program stops here and says so; that outcome is pre-registered and honorable.

**3.2 The Ledger**. Per-self accounts summing signed flows. Targets: exchange is zero-sum on closed symmetric regions (conservation as a theorem), and net creation appears only at the boundary of sight or at non-zero holonomy. With a non-degenerate carrier this is the first time the conservation question is put to a quotient that could refute it.

**The gate.** After 3.2 the program holds an evaluable object: a reversible, quantized, locally conserved world with an emergent arrow. Program review runs here, before anything further is chartered. The remaining two series are paid for only if the review accepts the core.

**3.3 The Phase**. Winding along flow histories; the parity sign upgraded to a genuine phase group. Targets: interference restated on histories, and the weight question re-run against a declared family of candidate measures, inheriting Program Review 2-1's finding that a two-candidate test forces nothing. The honest target is forced-within-a-family, not uniqueness.

**3.4 The Curve** (the capstone). Flow moves the adjacency; the metric reads the adjacency; so the coupling question is finally a dynamics question. Target: whether grain concentration induces a non-trivial law of distance change. This is the question Series 2.13 structurally could not ask.

Not built as series: the cone (Series 2.9's rate bound transfers to the flow with one bridge theorem); Gleason uniqueness and the area law (disclosed forward-notes, out of scope).

## Step zero

Before the 3.0 outline is finalized, a scratch de-risk: on the full state space of attention graphs over a three-element carrier (512 states, fully decidable), check that a generator set exists which is bijective per move, has the signed increment as its conserved charge, and projects under reification onto something tick-like and non-invertible. If no such generator exists, the design is falsified for the price of a spec document, and this charter is revised rather than executed.

## Disciplines

These are charter law, inherited from Program Review 2-1. A series that violates one has not landed, whatever its verdict says.

1. Non-degenerate carriers. Plain bisimilarity must not be the total relation on any witness; both sides of the import boundary must be inhabited somewhere in the series.
2. Derived quantities. Ranks, tick-hood, and charges are computed from the dynamics, never painted onto a table and read back by `decide`. A `decide` over the whole state space is fine; a `decide` over a co-designed witness is a consistency check and must be labeled as one.
3. Tied verdicts. Every verdict flag is bound to its justifying proposition by a tying theorem (the `PR2R1` pattern). No literal booleans standing in for theorems; no meta-flags without disclosure.
4. Bridges for reuse. Anything reused from Program 1 or 2 enters with a bridge theorem connecting it to the built object, or it is named as a fresh object. No re-seat by prose.
5. No vacuous audits. An audit clause is a theorem with content or it is not written.
6. Prose matches Lean. A claim in the README, an outline, or a docstring that exceeds its theorem is a finding, not a flourish.

## Outcomes

Pre-registered at program level, read off the series as they land:

- **Recovered core** — 3.0 through 3.2 land: reversible flow, emergent arrow, relative ledger. The gate opens.
- **Recovered** — the full arc lands, including a genuine phase and a non-trivial coupling law.
- **Arrow fails** — 3.1 cannot derive irreversibility from the projection. The program stops at two series and reports the failure as the design's refutation.
- **Flat** — the core lands but 3.3 and 3.4 both return not-recovered. The flow model is a cleaner restatement of Program 2's world, not a step toward ours; reported as such.
- **Obstructed** — step zero or 3.0 fails; the charter is revised, not executed around.

The verdict is read off the landed series. It is never asserted ahead of them.
