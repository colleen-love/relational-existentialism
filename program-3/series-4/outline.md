# Series 3.4 — The Curve

The capstone. One question: now that the metric and the grain are both readings of one dynamical state, does attention flux obey an exact law coupling the two — does concentration curve distance?

Series 2.13 asked the static question and honestly answered INERT: with the adjacency and the grain held in separate slots of a product type, the model's distance could not read the grain. The flow dissolves that separation: a transport changes the state, and the state is what both the metric and the charge read. The coupling question becomes a question about the dynamics, which is where gravity actually lives.

## The objects

- The dynamical metric: `flowDist g x y`, the shortest directed attention-path length in the state `g`, with a sentinel for unreachable pairs. A fresh definition at this carrier (Series 2.13's `pathDist` lives on four relata and cannot be imported; the shape is restated and named fresh, per the bridge discipline).
- The grain: the charge of Series 3.0, unchanged.

Workstream 4, the flux-curvature law (the heart):

An exact, universal law coupling grain flux to metric change — concentration of the conserved quantity contracts distance. The shape of gravity, in the model's own key, as a theorem about the dynamics.
