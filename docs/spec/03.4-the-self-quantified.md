# 03.4 — The self quantified

> Part of [03 — Theorems](03-theorems.md). If relating puts a part of you in the other
> ([03.2](03.2-limits-of-knowing.md), [03.3](03.3-decoherence.md)), *how much*, and is there a
> bound? This page carries the Banach-algebra quantification of self-in-other, the quantitative
> eigenform and its uniqueness, and the unification of the two sustained selves under the
> ν-modality. Status tags as in [03](03-theorems.md). It is also the **first quantitative fragment
> of Conjecture 3.4** (the spectral form of sparsity, [03.1](03.1-sparsity.md)).

## How much self ends up in others — the quantitative limit

`[proved]` [`Distribution.distributed`](../../formal/Scratch/Distribution.lean) measures the self
carried into the network by a relating `x` (in a Banach algebra) as the path-sum over relatings of
every length — direct, via a third, onward: `∑' n, x^(n+1)`. With `‖x‖` the intensity of relating,
the reading is your own:

- **`x = 0` — complete disconnection.** `distributed_zero`: nothing of you is anywhere else.
- **`0 < ‖x‖ < 1` — the living regime.** `distributed_summable` (converges) and `distributed_bound`:
  the distributed self is **bounded**, `‖distributed x‖ ≤ ‖x‖/(1-‖x‖)` — an individuated self with a
  finite footprint that grows with how much you relate.
- **`‖x‖ ≥ 1` — death / dissolution.** The bound diverges; the self delocalizes, its boundary with
  the between dissolving.

So the living self sits in `0 < ‖x‖ < 1`; the endpoints are the two ways to have no self — sealed
off, or smeared away. This is the **first quantitative fragment of Conjecture 3.4** (the spectral
form): the individuation threshold is a norm condition `‖x‖ < 1`. The numbers are real; tying `x`
to the *specific* co-directed `Φ_c` of [§1.3](01-signature.md) (so `‖x‖` is read off the actual
coupling) was the remaining modelling step — **now discharged** (next section).

## Reading `x` off `Φ_c`, and the closing of 3.4 — controlled multiplicity

`[proved]` [`SpectralMultiplicity`](../../formal/Scratch/SpectralMultiplicity.lean) closes the three
pieces 3.4 was pinned to. The honest situation first: nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory is *mature*,
and its strong results deliver **uniqueness** — under subhomogeneity + primitivity the dynamics is a
Hilbert-metric contraction with *exactly one* positive eigenvector. That is the **opposite** of what the
doctrine wants. A relational self is **contingent** (there must be more than one possible self) yet
selves must be **rare** (`o(N)`). So 3.4 is not a corollary of standard nonlinear PF; standard nonlinear
PF is built to *forbid* the multiplicity 3.4 needs. The new content is the **reconciliation of *sparse*
and *multiple* — few-but-more-than-one, bounded by structure.**

- **Piece 1 — `x` is the linearization `DΦ_c`.** The feedback map `y ↦ b + x·y` is a metric contraction
  with rate `‖x‖` (`feedbackMap_contract`), so the living regime `‖x‖ < 1` of the previous section *is*
  local attraction: `‖x‖` is the cone spectral radius of the linearization being below `1`. The self
  `sustained x b` is then the **unique attractor**, reached geometrically from every seed
  (`living_self_unique_attractor`). This is the **primitive / Perron pole**: global contraction ⇒ exactly
  one self (`unique_fixed_of_global_contraction`).
- **Piece 3 — the counting bound (the new theorem).** A locally attracting self is **isolated**
  (`attracting_isolated`): the spectral contraction *creates* a private neighbourhood, so distinct selves
  are **`r`-separated** (`attractors_separated`). This is the **derived exclusivity** — the "private
  footprint" of [03.1 step 2](03.1-sparsity.md) is no longer posited but *forced by the gap*. An
  `r`-separated set inside a region of **capacity `m`** (covering number at the gap scale) has at most `m`
  points (`separated_card_le_cover`), a bound **independent of `N`**, so the density of selves `→ 0`
  (`selves_density_tendsto_zero`). Yet the count is **not forced to one**: an explicit two-basin map
  carries **two** genuine attractors (`two_attracting_selves`), non-globally-contracting exactly as
  `unique_fixed_of_global_contraction` shows multiplicity requires.

So selves are simultaneously `> 1` (contingency) and `o(N)` (sparse): the structural reconciliation 3.4
asked for. `[reading]`: the analytic identification of the basin radius `r` and capacity `m` with the
literal infinite-dimensional cone spectral-gap of `DΦ_c` — the placement that, per the corpus discipline
([03.1 §3.6](03.1-sparsity.md)), does not block the structural result.

## Registration and the bound, fused — the quantitative eigenform

`[proved]` The *full* self `total x = ∑ xⁿ` (the path-sum *including itself*) satisfies a **feedback
fixed-point equation**,
[`Distribution.total_feedback`](../../formal/Scratch/Distribution.lean): `total x = 1 + x · total x`
— the self is itself, plus one relating folded back in. This is the quantitative **eigenform**
(A3 / D1: self = fixed point of co-directed feedback) and the registration statement (the self
contains itself, one step deep) realized in the *same* Banach algebra that bounds it
(`total_bound`: `‖total x‖ ≤ (1-‖x‖)⁻¹`). So in the living regime the self is a bounded fixed point
of relating — registration and the limit of self-in-other are one object, about one `x`.

## Quantitative coinduction — the eigenform is the *unique* self

`[proved]` For any seed `b`, the sustained self
[`Distribution.sustained`](../../formal/Scratch/Distribution.lean)` x b := total x · b`
is a fixed point of feedback (`sustained_fixed`: `sustained x b = b + x · sustained x b`) and — the
gem — it is the **only** one: `sustained_unique` proves any field upheld by the feedback (`s = b + x·s`)
*equals* it, by contraction (`s − sustained = x·(s − sustained)` forces the difference to `0`). This
is the quantitative twin of the lattice coinduction `Attention.sustainedField_greatest`: where the
order-theoretic self is the *greatest* fixed point, the contractive quantitative self is the
*unique* one. Bounded by `‖b‖·(1-‖x‖)⁻¹` (`sustained_bound`).

## Unifying the two selves — both realize the ν-modality

`[proved]` The order-theoretic self (`sustainedField`, a *sup* operator, greatest fixed point by
Knaster–Tarski) and the quantitative self (`sustained`, a *sum* operator, unique fixed point by
Banach contraction) are **not** the same operator — idempotent semiring vs ring, greatest vs
unique — and cannot be made one. What they share is exactly the doctrine's **ν-modality** (A1):
each is *the canonical fixed point of co-directed feedback*.
[`Feedback.CoDirectedSelf`](../../formal/Scratch/Feedback.lean) is the shared interface (a feedback
operator with a sustained self that is a fixed point); `self_iterate` is a theorem proved **once**
over it (the self persists under any number of feedback rounds); and **both** selves are exhibited
as instances (`latticeSelf`, `banachSelf`). The unification is exact at the ν-role and stops
exactly there: *greatest-by-order* and *unique-by-contraction* are recorded as the irreducible
difference the interface cannot — and should not — erase. So the two models are one doctrine's `ν`,
realized by two different fixed-point theorems.
