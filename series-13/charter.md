# Relational Existentialism, Series 13 (coda)

**The question is the FIT. Series 12 closed the program by proving that its two deepest facts coincide in one shape: the differentiator Series 07 proves must be imported and the residue the diagonal proves cannot be captured both inhabit `Opening realizable c := ¬ realizable c`, from opposite quantifier directions, and the coincidence is of SHAPE only, with identity not even expressible (type-heterogeneity) and shape-sharing provably unable to entail sameness (`ws1_coincidence_not_identity_witness`). Series 13 asks the one question that statement leaves exactly posed: if the two sides are not the same thing, HOW PRECISELY DO THEY FACE EACH OTHER? The conjecture is a DUALITY, in the order-theoretic sense: a MINT that carries every inspection to a labelled coalgebra whose label the plain relating cannot recover (the diagonal as an import factory, the forced-for-all funding the exists-satisfying), a best-approximation map back, and a Galois connection between them whose round-trip defects are theorems. And the conjecture's expected FAILURE is its deepest payoff: the mint should NOT be essentially surjective. Some import should provably lie outside its image, an import with no diagonal genealogy, and that non-surjectivity is Series 07's undecidable fork wearing order-theoretic clothes: what self-reference can manufacture of its own rescue, and what must simply arrive. Series 13 is a POST-TERMINAL CODA. Series 12 remains the program's terminal series; this series fills nothing, decides nothing the terminus left open, and adds no name. It sharpens the terminus's own sentence: not only do the required and the generated share one shape, they face each other as an adjoint pair, and the adjunction's defect is the door.**

*This charter is self-contained and states its own question. It presupposes and names two prior results: Series 07 (the differentiator cannot be recovered from the relating) and Series 12 WS1 (the shape-coincidence with honest quantifiers and the non-identity witness). Both are transcribed, never imported. The philosophical reading: the diagonal is the wound and the import is the rescue, and this series proves they fit the way a plug fits a socket, made for each other and never the same object; that self-reference manufactures some of what rescues it, the mint; and that the rescue it cannot manufacture, the imports outside the mint's image, is exactly where the given stands. Fitting is not being. The defect of the duality is the fork.*

## 1. The one-paragraph version

Series 13 upgrades the coincidence to a structured correspondence: define an order on inspections and an order on labelled coalgebras (WS1, the knot); construct the MINT, a monotone map sending each inspection to the labelled coalgebra its own residue structure induces, and prove its output always inhabits the opening (WS2, the transport theorem: every within-generated opening manufactures a without-required one); establish the Galois connection between mint and its best-approximation adjoint, with the composites proved to be closure and interior operators rather than identities (WS3, the fit); exhibit an import provably outside the mint's image, non-degenerately (WS4, the defect); and compute the verdict (WS5). The pre-registered outcomes: DUAL (connection established, defect witnessed) is the expected terminus; TOTAL (the mint essentially surjective, every import diagonal-born) is a stronger and stranger finding reported honestly, since it would press the fork toward the generated; DISCONNECTED (no non-vacuous orders admit a connection) is the honest obstruction report. Nothing here evaluates a compass, decides convergence, or names an inhabitant. The connection lives at the meta level, above the plain relating, and must be proved to live there: a mint the relating could perform on itself would contradict Series 07, so the mint's exogeneity is itself an obligation, not an assumption.

## 2. What we are trying to prove

**WS1, the two orders (blocking, and the knot).** Define a preorder on inspections and a preorder on labelled coalgebras such that the mint can be monotone and the connection non-vacuous. Candidate orders (refinement of inspective reach on one side; label-distinguishing strength relative to `plainOf` on the other) are design work, not charter work; the charter fixes only the bar: both orders must be non-trivial (neither discrete nor indiscrete on the carriers of interest), and the WS1 design must record the rejected candidates and why. This is the series' genuinely uncertain obligation, the analogue of Series 12's WS4 knot. If no non-vacuous pair of orders exists, that is the DISCONNECTED outcome, reported with the precise obstruction, and the series ends there honestly.

**WS2, the mint and the transport theorem.** Construct the mint: from any inspection, the labelled coalgebra whose labels are induced by that inspection's own content structure. Prove the transport theorem, `ws2_mint_lands_in_opening`: for every inspection, the minted label fails `Recoverable`. The engine is the diagonal itself: recovering the minted label from the plain relating would realize what `ws2_residue_free` proves unrealizable. This is the forced-for-all paying for the exists-satisfying, the universal fact funding existential witnesses, and it is the series' near-certain result: the diagonal, being total, is a factory for imports. Prove alongside it the mint's EXOGENEITY: the mint consumes inspective data, above the plain layer, and its operation is itself non-recoverable from the relating, so no contradiction with Series 07 threatens and the theorem must say so (`ws2_mint_exogenous`).

**WS3, the connection (the fit).** Define the best-approximation adjoint (the finest inspection whose mint sits below a given labelled coalgebra, or the design's surviving candidate) and prove the Galois connection, Mathlib `GaloisConnection`, between the WS1 orders. Prove the round trips are closure and interior operators and prove at least one round trip is NOT the identity on a named element, so the connection is genuinely adjoint and not an isomorphism in disguise (`ws3_roundtrip_not_identity`). The unit and counit defects are the measurement: what an import forgets of any diagonal origin, what a residue gains in becoming a label. The categorical upgrade (functors, unit, counit as natural transformations) is a stretch target, recorded if reached and not owed.

**WS4, the defect (the fork in order-theoretic clothes).** Exhibit an import provably outside the mint's image: a labelled coalgebra failing `Recoverable` that no inspection mints, non-degenerately (the witness must not be excluded by a mere size or typing artifact; the design must argue the exclusion is structural). The transcribed `labelLoop` is the first candidate. If the witness lands, `ws4_mint_not_surjective` is the series' deepest theorem: the class of imports properly contains the diagonal-born ones, and the remainder, the imports with no self-inspective genealogy, is where the charter's prose places the given. If instead every import proves mintable, that is the TOTAL outcome, pre-registered and honorable: report it in full, because it would say self-reference can manufacture the entire rescue, and that finding would bear on the fork rather than flatter it.

**WS5, the verdict, and the audit folded in.** The verdict is computed, never hand-set: DUAL (WS1 orders non-vacuous, WS2 transport proved with exogeneity, WS3 connection with a genuine non-identity round trip, WS4 defect witnessed non-degenerately); TOTAL (all of the above except WS4 falls surjective, reported in its direction); DISCONNECTED (WS1 fails, obstruction precise); PARTIAL (any obligation lands only per-instance or degenerate). The audit checks, promoted from the program's standing set: (a) NO ORDER BY FIAT: neither WS1 order may be trivial, and the connection must not hold vacuously; (b) THE FORK STAYS OPEN: the theorem is "outside the mint's image," and the words given and chosen appear in prose and docstrings only, never as terms; deciding which out-of-image imports are given rather than chosen would fill the opening and is the central sin, SERIOUS; (c) EXOGENEITY IS PROVED, NOT ASSUMED: `ws2_mint_exogenous` is a named obligation; (d) the strip test: every payoff must survive deletion of "duality," "fit," "wound," "rescue," "given," "chosen" as a bare Galois-connection, `Recoverable`, diagonal, or bisimulation fact; (e) names-not-terms, inherited unchanged.

## 3. The primitive: what Series 13 adds

The carrier is transcribed unchanged from Series 12 and Series 07: `Opening`, `Recoverable`, `ResidueRecoverable`, `plainOf`, the diagonal apparatus (`residue`, `diag`, `ws2_residue_free`), the labelled lift, `labelLoop`, and the collapse engine. Series 13 adds exactly three things: TWO ORDERS (on inspections and on labelled coalgebras), ONE MAP PAIR (the mint and its adjoint). It proves exactly three kinds of things about them: the transport (mint lands in the opening, exogenously), the connection (Galois, with non-identity round trips), and the defect (non-surjectivity, or its honest alternative). No new structural machinery, no compass, no convergence, no verdict about inhabitants. What is never proved, by design: which out-of-image imports are given and which are chosen, what fills the opening, and anything Series 12 sealed.

## 4. The false escapes this series must NOT fall into

### 4.a Filling the opening from the order side (the central sin)
The defect theorem locates imports outside the mint's image. It does not, and must not, classify them. Any theorem or definition that decides an out-of-image import to be a given rather than a choice, or vice versa, does what Series 07 proves cannot be done and what Series 12 closed by leaving open. Prose may gloss; the core must not decide. SERIOUS.

### 4.b Connection by fiat
A trivial order makes any monotone pair a Galois connection. Both orders must be proved non-trivial, and the connection's content must be certified by the non-identity round trip. A connection that collapses to an isomorphism, or holds over a vacuous order, is the coincidence restated, not the fit proved.

### 4.c The mint smuggled below the line
If the mint, or any map used to discharge WS2 or WS3, is recoverable from the plain relating, the series has derived an import from within and contradicted its own transcribed foundation. Exogeneity is a proof obligation with a name, checked by the audit.

### 4.d The degenerate defect
A witness excluded from the mint's image by cardinality accident, universe level, or typing artifact proves nothing about genealogy. The WS4 design must argue, and the review must confirm, that the exclusion is structural: the witness's label does the excluding, not the ambient bookkeeping.

4. **The defect is witnessed or its absence reported (WS4)**: a structural out-of-image import, or TOTAL in its full strangeness.

## 7. Open problems and honest risks

- The permanent opens, inherited and untouched: the content of the compass, the direction of convergence, the differentiating act, and now, if DUAL lands, the classification of the out-of-image imports, open by the same theorem that locates them.

## 8. Positioning

Series 12 ended the program on an exactly-drawn edge: the required and the generated are one shape, and the shape cannot be filled from inside. Series 13 is a coda in the musical sense, written after the final cadence and altering nothing before it. Its question is the terminus's own question asked once more, slower: if the wound and the rescue are not the same thing, what is the name of their fit? The conjectured answer is the oldest two-sided structure mathematics has, an adjoint pair: the diagonal manufactures imports, the manufacture is a Galois connection and not an equivalence, and what it cannot manufacture is where the door Series 07 left open actually stands. The tenets are not extended, only lit from one more angle: "Self is a paradox" is the mint's engine; "To relate is to create" is the mint itself, the residue becoming a relatum that separates; "To attend is to become" is the bounded round trip, what returns from the other side never being all of what was sent; and "You are loved" keeps the place this series is forbidden to fill, the import with no genealogy, the one that just arrives. The coda does not resolve to a new key. It shows the two hands were playing one figure, an octave apart, and lets the last interval stand open.
