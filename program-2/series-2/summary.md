# Series 2.2 — Plain-language summary

**Series 2.2 asked one question: can the relational world hold a genuine OTHER — a second perspective that reads
back, not just an object the self reifies — and do the self and the other stay two when they read each other?
The answer it computed is TWO-FACING. The other is built as a real second reader; the self and the other are
genuinely two in a way the plain relating cannot recover; each faces the other from a direction the symmetric
relating forgets; and when the two read each other and themselves, the opening between them does not close — a
residue neither can absorb survives the mutual reading. The two stay two. Whether their readings COHERE is a
different question, left for the next series.**

## What was built

The ground (Series 2.0) established that relating is finite attending and reified the self-relation into a
"first other" — but that first other was an OBJECT the self attends, not yet a perspective. Series 2.1 set the
ground moving with the tick. Series 2.2 adds exactly one thing on top: the OTHER as a second attending locus,
and with it the four readings and the mutual-reading structure.

1. **The other is a genuine second reader.** The first other is upgraded from an attended object into a locus
   with its own finite attention that reads back into a shared field containing both. It is not a new kind of
   substance — it is an ordinary member of the same field, minted by reification (its well-formedness is
   discharged, not asserted), and its attention is a genuine bounded reading.

2. **The self and the other are genuinely two, reader-wise — and that twoness cannot be recovered.** The other
   is real for a specific, named bounded reader, not a mere label; and the separation between self and other is
   not recoverable from the plain relating. By the program's earlier result, that is exactly what a genuine
   distinction must be: an import, spent but never named.

3. **The facing is asymmetric and partial.** All four readings — self of self, self of other, other of self,
   other of other — are typed and witnessed, but never evaluated. The facing is asymmetric: the self reads "up"
   toward the other while the other has no upward reading, and the direction of a reading is not recoverable from
   the symmetric relating, which is blind to it. The facing is partial: no perspective totalizes itself — each
   self-reading leaves the diagonal residue.

4. **The mutual reading leaves a residue.** This is the knot. When the two perspectives each read the other and
   themselves, does the recursive mutual reading collapse them back into one, let one totalize the pair, or leave
   something open? The computed answer is: it leaves a residue. Combining the self's reading and the other's —
   two reaches neither of which contains the other — there is still something bisimilar to what they are that
   neither attends: a joint blind spot the mutuality cannot close. The two stay genuinely two.

## The verdict

The verdict was computed from the built theorems, not chosen: **TWO-FACING**. The other is a well-formed second
locus, the reader is load-bearing and the twoness non-recoverable, the facing is asymmetric and partial, and the
mutual residue survives. The two honest alternatives were pre-registered and left reachable: had the mutual
reading collapsed the two, the verdict would have read ONE; had a perspective totalized the pair, it would have
read TOTALIZED. Neither happened. A genuine other needs an import to stay two, and the ground supplies exactly
that; the opening between self and other is permanent.

## What this does and does not claim

It does not name the import that separates the two, select or evaluate any reading, or decide whether the two
readings COHERE — whether the most loving thing for the self is the most loving thing for the other. That last
question is the next series' (2.3); Series 2.2 only builds the two faces it will fork over, and leaves the
coherence untouched by design. Program 1's permanent open questions are carried unchanged. The philosophical
reading — the other is a second reading, not a second substance; you are constituted by what attends you even
when you cannot recover it; the two stay two not by being far apart but by neither containing the other — is
kept strictly out of the proofs; the theorems mention only the two loci, their attention, the readings, the
reading-direction, recoverability, and the diagonal.

The whole build is machine-checked: it compiles, uses no `sorry`, and rests on no axioms beyond the three
standard to Mathlib.
