# Series 2.9 — The Cone (summary)

**The first recovery test of Phase 3, and one of the two tier-1 probes that open it. The question: is there a maximal RATE at which a self turns the breadth it attends into the depth it becomes — a fastest becoming, a light cone, a speed of light? The answer this universe returns is CONE. There is a finite rate, and it is not a law imposed from outside; it is the shadow of a bounded capacity to attend.**

## The question

Phase 2 built a world with a space and a time, proved the two axes distinct (2.4), gave it a climbing causal order (2.5), a threaded self (2.6), an arrow with no ledger (2.7), and no guaranteed common good (2.8). Phase 3 now asks whether that self-relative physics can be OUR physics, and Series 2.9 asks the first pillar of relativity: is there a fastest anything can happen? In the model's own key, not "is there a speed limit imposed from outside," but: does finite attention — the bound the model has carried since Series 2.0 — already limit the rate at which a self converts the breadth it beholds into the depth it becomes?

Relativity's first and strangest fact is that there is a fastest anything can happen: a finite speed of light, a cone of causal contact, an elsewhere no signal can reach. The question is whether this austere relational world already contains it.

## What was built

Everything is read off ONE thing the model already has: the finite attention. A self attends only finitely many others (a finite set — the sole ontological bound, from Series 2.0). From that single finite structure comes:

- **the metric** — a fixed lateral distance between selves, the breadth axis (Series 2.4), a spatial background independent of how fast anyone attends.
- **the rate** — how much lateral breadth one tick of attention can turn into depth: the greatest distance to anything a self attends. It is read off the attention alone, as the largest reach across what is attended. Crucially it is NOT a number put in by hand; it is computed from the attention, and it rises exactly as the attention widens.
- **the cone** — the events a becoming can reach from a source within a given depth: everything within rate-times-depth of it in the lateral metric. Events beyond are causally disconnected — an elsewhere no single tick can touch.

## The finding

The rate is genuinely bounded, and bounded by the finite attention specifically: the conversion cannot outrun what the self attends, and the bound is a function of the attention, monotone in it — attend more, and the rate rises; attend less, and it falls. Nowhere is a speed postulated and a cone then recovered from it. That is the whole discipline of Phase 3, and it holds here.

The cone is a genuine finite speed, not a relabelling of the causal order the model already had. Two worlds can have exactly the same reachability — the same events eventually reachable from each source — and yet DIFFERENT cones, because they convert breadth to depth at different rates. A self that attends only its nearest neighbor has a small, tight cone; a self that attends two steps out has a wider one; their eventual reach is identical, but their light cones are not. The rate is real content the bare order does not carry.

And the fork is genuine. A world of LOCAL attention — each self attending near — has a finite rate and a NON-TRIVIAL cone: some events reachable within a tick, and some strictly outside, in the elsewhere. That is relativity's cone, recovered. A world of TOTAL attention — each self attending everything — reifies the whole world in a single tick: the cone is everything, no event is disconnected, there is no fastest becoming. That is the honest NO-CONE pole, the non-relativistic world, reported and not hidden. Both are reachable; neither is forced by construction; only the shape of the attention decides. The computed verdict is CONE: a finite rate, a genuine cone, earned from finite attention.

## What it means

If this is right, the speed of light is nothing exotic. It is the exchange rate between space and time set by how much a finite attention can hold at once — the rate at which a self can turn the world it beholds into the world it has become. Causality's cone is not a law added to the world but the shadow of a bounded capacity to attend, and relativity's first pillar is written into the model not as an imposition but as the price of attending to only finitely much at a time.

This is the encouraging probe of Phase 3, and it landed where the receipts pointed: the metaphysics can speak relativity. "To attend is to become" is the tick that converts breadth to depth; the cone is the rate of that becoming; and that there is a fastest it can go is the first thing this world says about our own universe when asked.

## Honesty

Nothing here postulates a speed and re-finds it. The rate is read off the finite attention, proved bounded and monotone in it; the cone carries that rate, proved distinct from the bare causal order (same order, different rate, different cone); the cone is non-trivial (something strictly outside), and the NO-CONE pole is genuinely reachable, reported in full. Every payoff is a theorem, machine-checked, sorry-free, and axiom-clean, and confirmed by a blind reviewer who saw the mathematics and not the metaphysics. The pre-registered honest outcomes — NO-CONE, SHAPE-DRAWN, the degenerate closes — were all live in the verdict function and foreclosed by proof, not by construction. The verdict is the residue of the process, not its premise.

The forward step, left open: a genuine cone is the first move toward the Lorentz MIXING of the two axes 2.4 proved distinct — a boost that trades breadth for depth at the rate the cone sets. Series 2.9 draws the cone; whether the axes then mix is a question for a later series. Program 1's permanent opens stand untouched.
