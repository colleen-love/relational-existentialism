## What was built

Series 2.0 built the ground (relating is finite attending), 2.1 the tick that made it move, and 2.2 the OTHER — a
genuine second reader, non-recoverably two from the self, mutually reading, with a residue neither can close.
Series 2.2 stopped exactly at the coherence question and left it open. Series 2.3 answers it, adding just two
things on top of the pair.

1. **The orientation, typed but never evaluated.** Each perspective is given a valuation — a reading of the
   shared field drawn from a space the mathematics never fills in. Every theorem is quantified over ALL
   orientations; not one selects, constructs, or reads off a particular one. A canonical orientation anywhere in
   the core would be the central sin, and there is none — the only concrete orientations appear as anonymous
   witnesses inside existence claims.

2. **Convergence, built fresh and constrained.** `Converges₂` says: the self's orientation, carried to the
   other, agrees with the other's own. It is a genuine relation, neither always-true nor always-false — a
   converging orientation and a non-converging one both exist. Crucially it was rebuilt from scratch on the real
   self/other pair, NOT inherited from the earlier program's convergence machinery, which was excluded precisely
   because an earlier version of this exact fork turned out to be a tautology (decided by the type, not the
   structure). This series is that fork done right.

3. **The two zones.** Split the orientations by what the shared relating can SEE — those that agree wherever the
   relating cannot tell two things apart. Over that IN-SIGHT class, coherence is FORCED: the self and the other
   are indistinguishable to the plain relating, so any orientation the structure can see must value them alike,
   and they cohere. Over the FULL class, coherence is not forced: an orientation can value them differently, and
   when it does, that difference is provably an IMPORT — not recoverable from the relating, exactly the kind of
   distinction the program's first theorem says a genuine one must be. The two zones are separated by that same
   import boundary.

4. **The fork is genuine, not a tautology.** The in-sight class is a real, proper restriction: it is inhabited
   (the uniform orientation is in it) and it genuinely excludes orientations (the dissenting one is faithful yet
   out of sight). The forcing genuinely uses the fact that the self and the other are indistinguishable to the
   plain relating — on a structure where they were not, it would fail. Both outcomes are reached on actual
   witnessed orientations at the actual self/other pair.

## The verdict

**SHAPE-DRAWN.** A small function reads which way convergence falls and genuinely reaches more than one value:
SHAPE-DRAWN over the full class (the fork stays open), and the pre-registered CONVERGENCE-DECIDED over the
in-sight sub-class (the structure decides it where it can see). So the reported verdict is an earned, falsifiable
output, not the value of a constant. And the DIRECTION of convergence — whether the self and the other actually
cohere — is NEVER decided. No theorem, definition, or discharged obligation says they do, or that they do not.
Deciding it would do what the whole program has left open since it first drew the edge of the One, and naming what
the mathematics proves it cannot see.

## What stays open

draws the exact place where the answer would live and proves the structure cannot fill it. The content of the
orientation stays open (typed, never evaluated). Everything Program 1 left permanently open stands exactly as
