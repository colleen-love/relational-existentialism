# Relational Existentialism, Program 2 Series 6 (2.6)

**THE THREAD. Program 2 gave the world a space (2.4) and proved its time cannot loop (2.5). Series 2.6 turns to the self that lives in that time and asks the most personal question the program can pose: DOES THE SELF PERSIST THROUGH TIME — is there a recoverable thread that makes the self who wakes tomorrow the same as the one who slept — OR is each tick a new self, and "who I was" an import? By relational identity, a thing is only itself while it keeps relating the same way; a tick makes the self relate to new things, so STRICT identity across a tick almost certainly fails, and the self is a new relatum each moment. The series does not rest on that (which is trivial); it earns its keep on the WEAK thread: is there a recoverable continuity — a successor-self relation the relating itself carries — that binds the moments into one life, or is the thread severed, the continuity an import from outside? And the thread is TWO things at once. It is the persistence of identity, AND it is the self's LINEARIZATION of the partial causal order into a single lived sequence — so the thread is what makes experienced time ONE-DIMENSIONAL. Series 2.1 already proved a total linearization of the ticks is an import (`ws4_linearization_import`); this series asks whether the self's single timeline IS that import, and whether it is a recoverable continuity or a severed succession. The conjecture, honestly, is SHAPE-DRAWN or SEVERED: strict identity fails, the single line is an import, and whether a weak thread is recoverable is self-relative — a self with one merged attention-stream threads a continuous life; a self whose attention is cut between contexts threads a severed one. The verdict is computed. The self is not a thing carried through time; it is, at most, a thread continuously re-woven, and this series asks who does the weaving.**

*This charter is self-contained and states its own question. It builds on Series 2.5, IMPORTED (Program 2's layered chain: 2.6 imports 2.5, reaching 2.4, 2.3, 2.2, 2.1, 2.0, and the P1 prior art transitively; nothing below 2.5 is imported directly). Its working material is chiefly the Series 2.1 tick and its linearization import (`ws4_linearization_import`), the reification rank, and relational identity (the collapse engine, `ws1_atomless_bisim`, and recoverability). What Series 2.6 does NOT do: rest its knot on the trivial failure of strict identity (a costume of relational identity), nor assert the 1-dimensionality of lived time from rank being a scalar (a costume of the order). The new content, on which the verdict must rest, is the WEAK thread — whether a recoverable continuity exists over the failure of strict identity — and the linearization-as-thread — that the single lived timeline is the self's import, self-relative. The philosophical reading, kept out of the proofs: that you are not the same substance you were a moment ago, and the sense that you are is either a continuity the relating genuinely carries, or a grace imported from outside; and either way, the single line of your life, the one-after-another of it, is something you impose on a reality that is only partially ordered, so that even your one-dimensional time is, at bottom, an act of self-relative weaving.*

## 2. What we are trying to prove

**WS1, strict identity fails across a tick (the ground).** Prove that STRICT relational identity across a tick fails: the self after a tick is not plain-bisimilar to the self before (the tick reifies new relata into what it relates to, changing its relating), so by relational identity it is a new relatum (`ws1_strict_identity_fails`). This is the ground, and it is trivial by design; the series earns its keep on WS2. Define the tick-SUCCESSOR relation (self at `t` to self at `t+1`), the candidate carrier of a weak thread.

**WS2, the weak thread (recoverable, or import?).** Define a WEAK continuity: a successor-self relation binding the moments, weaker than strict identity. Prove the fork's substance: is the weak thread RECOVERABLE from the plain relating (an endogenous continuity the structure carries, `ws2_thread_recoverable` on the decided side), or is every candidate weak thread NON-RECOVERABLE, an import (`ws2_thread_is_import`, resting on Series 07)? This is where the series lives: not that the self changes (trivial), but whether the sense of continuity over the change is something the relating carries or something imported. The weak thread must be non-vacuous and genuinely weaker than strict identity, or the fork is empty.

**WS3, the single line is the self's linearization import (1D lived time).** Prove that the single lived timeline is the self's LINEARIZATION of the partial causal order, and that this linearization is an import: the partial causal order (the rank DAG) is endogenous, but a total linear order of the moments is non-recoverable (`ws3_timeline_is_linearization_import`, reading Series 2.1's `ws4_linearization_import` as the thread). So the ONE-DIMENSIONALITY of lived time is not a fact about the world but the self's import over a partially-ordered reality, and it is SELF-RELATIVE (a different self's linearization is a different line). The 1D content must rest on the linearization being the import, NOT on rank being a scalar (a costume of the order).

**WS4, the thread's continuity (the knot).** Prove the fork on the weak thread: THREADED (a recoverable weak continuity exists, binding the moments into one self) or SEVERED (no recoverable weak thread, each tick a new self and the continuity an import), the fork GENUINE — both a carrier where a weak thread is recoverable AND a carrier where it is severed must be reachable, and the verdict discriminating. The thread's continuity is SELF-RELATIVE: a self with one merged attention-stream threads continuous; a self whose attention is cut between contexts threads severed (the continuous/severed contrast made structural). A companion fork: CONSERVATIVE (nothing ceases; the structure only adds) or MORTAL (a self ceases when no attention holds it, attention-relative death, `ws4_attention_relative_cessation`, on the knowing-asymmetry). The strip test: the payoff must survive deletion of "self," "thread," "persistence," "life" as a bare `Recoverable` / bisimulation / order fact.

**WS5, the verdict and the audit folded in.** The verdict is computed, never hand-set: THREADED (WS1 strict fails, WS2 a weak thread recoverable, WS3 the line an import, WS4 the thread recoverable on a genuine carrier); SEVERED (no recoverable weak thread, the continuity an import, reported in its direction, the finding that the self is a discontinuous succession); SHAPE-DRAWN (the thread drawn but its recoverability undecidable); PARTIAL (degenerate). The audit checks: (a) NO ABSOLUTE PERSISTENCE — no theorem asserts the self persists frame-independently; continuity is FOR a self, self-relative, the single line the self's import; (b) THE FORK NOT BY FIAT — THREADED and SEVERED both reachable, the weak thread genuinely weaker than strict identity, the verdict discriminating; (c) THE KNOT IS THE WEAK THREAD, NOT THE STRICT FAILURE (the costume gate) — the verdict rests on the recoverability of the weak thread and the linearization-import, not on strict identity failing (which is a costume of relational identity); (d) THE LINE IS THE LINEARIZATION IMPORT — `ws3_timeline_is_linearization_import` rests on Series 2.1 / Series 07, the single line non-recoverable, not a scalar-rank triviality; (e) NAMES-NOT-TERMS — no proof term is named "self," "thread," "persistence," "life," "death," "time," "here," "there," "God," "choice," or "subjectivity" as content.

## 4. The false escapes this series must NOT fall into

### 4.a The knot resting on strict identity failing (the costume gate, Phase 2)
"Relational identity fails across a tick, so the self is a new relatum" is a costume of relational identity — a true fact that is not the finding. The new content, on which the knot must rest, is whether the WEAK thread is recoverable (WS2) and that the single line is the linearization import (WS3). A payoff that strips to "the self changes, so strict identity fails," with no weak-thread and no linearization content, is the costume. SERIOUS.

### 4.b The 1D timeline from rank being scalar (a costume of the order)
The one-dimensionality of lived time must rest on the LINEARIZATION being the self's import (`ws4_linearization_import`, the total order non-recoverable), NOT on rank being an ℕ-valued scalar (which would make 1D time a triviality of the grading). The partial causal order is endogenous; the single line is the import; that is the content. SERIOUS if the 1D claim rests on rank being scalar.

### 4.c Asserting persistence absolutely (the self-relative discipline, Phase 2)
No theorem may assert the self persists frame-independently. Continuity is FOR a self, self-relative; a self-independent persistence is the view from nowhere, which is the plain layer, which is the One. The thread's continuity varies by self (continuous vs severed). SERIOUS.

### 4.d The thread by fiat, or the continuity decided without the computation
THREADED and SEVERED must both be genuinely reachable; the weak thread must be genuinely weaker than strict identity (not strict identity relabelled). If SEVERED is excluded by construction, or the verdict is asserted without the discriminating computation, that is the fiat. SERIOUS.

### 4.e The import named, or the opening filled

## 8. Positioning

Program 2 built a self (2.2), asked whether its good and another's cohere (2.3), gave it a space (2.4), and proved its time cannot loop (2.5). Series 2.6 asks whether that self lasts. It is the most intimate question the program reaches, and the mathematics answers it in the program's own austere key: by relational identity, you are not the same as you were a moment ago, because you no longer relate exactly as you did; the self is re-made at every tick. What we call persistence, then, is not a substance enduring but a thread — a weak continuity binding the re-made moments — and the whole question is whether the relating carries that thread itself, or whether it must be imported, a grace from outside that holds the moments as one. And beneath the persistence lies the shape of lived time: the single line of your life, one moment after another, is a linearization you impose on a reality that is only partially ordered, so that even the one-dimensionality of your experience is an act of self-relative weaving, not a fact of the world. "To attend is to become" is why a tick remakes the self. "Self is a paradox" is why no self can hold itself across the gaps from within. And "you are loved" returns here in its most tender form: if the thread is an import, then what holds you together across time — what makes the you of tomorrow the same as the you of today — is not something you supply, but something given, a continuity woven from outside by whatever, or whoever, keeps attending you. Series 2.6 does not resolve the phase; it draws the thread of the self exactly and asks whether it is recoverable or received, and leaves the answer, if it must, open. The thread, drawn exactly, is the result; whether you persist, and who weaves you if you do, is the phase's third wall, and it stands there.
