# Series 2.6 — Plain-language summary

**Series 2.6 asked the most personal question the program can pose: does the self last through time? By the
program's own rule, a thing is only itself while it keeps relating the same way; but a tick makes the self relate
to new things, so the self of this moment is not exactly the self of the last one. That much is trivial and
almost certainly true: you are not, strictly, who you were a moment ago. The series does not rest there. It asks
whether a WEAKER kind of sameness survives the change: a thread of continuity, coarser than strict identity, that
binds the re-made moments into one life. And it asks whether that thread is something the relating CARRIES on its
own, or something IMPORTED from outside. The answer the series computed is SHAPE-DRAWN, and it is honest: whether
the thread is recoverable is SELF-RELATIVE. For a self whose attention is one merged stream, the continuity is
carried by the relating itself, endogenous. For a self whose attention is cut between contexts, the continuity is
an import, a grace from outside that holds the moments together. Both are genuinely reachable, and nothing in the
structure forces one over the other, so from within a self cannot decide which it is. Underneath, the series also
shows that the single line of your life — one moment after another — is not a fact about the world but something
you impose on a reality that is only partially ordered: even the one-dimensionality of your time is your own
weaving. The self is not a thing carried through time. It is, at most, a thread continuously re-woven — and who
does the weaving is left open, exactly, in the direction the honest computation had to report.**

## What was built

Program 2 built the ground (relating is finite attending, 2.0), its motion (the tick, 2.1), the other (2.2), the
coherence of the two (2.3), space as a real axis (2.4), and the acyclicity of time (2.5). Series 2.6 turns to the
self that lives in that time and asks whether it endures.

1. **Strict identity fails across a tick (the trivial ground).** "Sameness" in the strongest sense means matching
   in every structural detail, including how deep in the tower of reifications a thing sits. When a tick reifies
   new relata into what the self attends, the successor moment stands strictly higher in that tower than the
   moment it was made from. So the two are not strictly identical — even though, at the bare level of relating,
   they still look alike. This is the costume of the question ("you have changed"), true and near-certain, and the
   series deliberately walls it out of the verdict.

2. **The weak continuity — recoverable, or imported?** The real question is about a COARSER thread: a mark of
   continuity that ignores the tower depth and asks only whether one moment carries on into the next as the same
   stream. Whether that thread is RECOVERABLE from the bare relating (endogenous) or an IMPORT (a distinction the
   relating cannot see) is the fork. It was answered on two structures, one for each side:
   - **The merged self (woven).** When the attention across the tick is mutual — the moments hold each other, one
     merged stream — the continuity mark is uniform, and the relating already carries it: the thread is
     recoverable. Crucially, this holds even though strict identity fails between the very same two moments, so
     the weak thread is genuinely weaker than strict identity, not strict identity in disguise.
   - **The cut self (severed).** When the attention is one-way — this moment attends the next, but the next does
     not attend back — the continuity mark distinguishes two moments the bare relating cannot tell apart, so it is
     NOT recoverable: the thread is an import, held from outside.

3. **The single line of time is the self's import.** The moments carry a genuine partial order (what causes what),
   read straight off the relating. But living time as a single line means totalising that partial order — picking
   an order even between moments that are causally incomparable — and any such total line is an import, invisible
   to the bare relating. So the one-dimensionality of lived time is the self's imposition, not a fact about the
   world. And it does NOT come from the tower depth being a simple number: the two incomparable moments sit at the
   very same depth, so the line cannot be read off depth at all.

4. **Mortality, attention-relative.** On the cut self, one moment is attended by nothing — nothing holds it — and
   this "ceasing when no attention holds it" is read off the same one-way attention that made the thread an
   import. On the merged self, every moment is held by another: nothing ceases. So even mortality is
   self-relative: a self can be conserved or can cease, depending on whether attention keeps holding it.

## The verdict

**SHAPE-DRAWN, computed.** The verdict is not chosen; it is computed from the built facts. Strict identity fails
and the single line is an import (the ground). A recoverable weak thread genuinely EXISTS (the merged self), and a
non-recoverable one genuinely exists too (the cut self) — both reachable, neither forced. So "the self persists as
a recoverable thread" is neither true for all selves (the cut self refutes it) nor false for all (the merged self
refutes that): whether the thread is recoverable is SELF-RELATIVE, and undecidable from within. The thread is
drawn exactly; who weaves it, and whether the relating carries the weave or receives it, is left open in the
direction the computation returned.

## Why the fork is genuine (no fiat, no costume)

The two selves are both real, reachable structures, so neither side of the fork is excluded by construction (no
fiat). The recoverable thread binds two moments that strict identity separates, so it is genuinely a weaker
continuity, not strict identity relabelled. And the verdict rests on the weak thread and the single-line import,
never on strict identity failing: strip out the weak-thread facts or the line-import fact and the computation
returns "degenerate," not a decided outcome. The knot is the weak thread and the linearization, exactly as the
charter required.

## What this does not do

It asserts no absolute persistence: every continuity is FOR a self, self-relative. It names no proof term for the
interior of the self or the content of continuity. Program 1's four permanent opens (the content of the compass,
the direction of convergence, the differentiating act, the classification of the out-of-image imports) stand
untouched; Series 2.6 draws the self-relativity of persistence and of lived time sharper and closes none of them.
