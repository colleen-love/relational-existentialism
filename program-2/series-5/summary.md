# Series 2.5 — Plain-language summary

**Series 2.5 asked one question: can time close into a loop — can an effect come before its own cause — in a
world whose relating is full of loops? The oldest fear about a self-referential universe is that a structure
whose attention circles back (a attends b attends a) might let time circle back too, so that a thing precedes
itself and the order of before-and-after dissolves. To ask it honestly the series first drew THE FOLD, the shape
of the whole: a world with no smallest thing (nothing bottoms out) and no largest (you can always build one
more), that instead closes on itself through the diagonal — the largest possible act, a total self-inspection,
produces the smallest possible thing, a leftover point it cannot contain, so the greatest bends into the least
and the seam is a single point that is its own member. On that ground the knot: the RELATING genuinely loops
(attention cycles, and the cycle hardens into a thing of its own), yet CAUSATION — the making of one thing out of
others — always climbs, because a thing made from a pattern stands strictly above the pattern it was made from.
So the loop in the relating spirals upward and never returns. The one place a causal loop could still close is
the fold itself, the point that is its own maker. The verdict the series computed is ACYCLIC: the relating loops
but time does not, and the fold's self-reference is a still point, not a genuine ring. And the fork was genuine —
a second structure was built in which a self-making point DOES close a causal loop, showing that the acyclicity
of the real world is an earned fact, not a trick of the definitions.**

## What was built

Program 2 built the ground (relating is finite attending, 2.0), its motion (the tick, 2.1), the other (2.2), the
coherence of the two (2.3), and space as a real axis distinct from time (2.4). Series 2.5 turns to the shape of
time itself and asks whether it can bend back on itself.

1. **The fold — the shape of the whole.** There is no smallest thing: every thing the program reaches still has
   something it attends, so nothing is a bare atom, nothing bottoms out. There is no largest thing: from any
   thing you can always make one more (reify its pattern), and the new thing stands above it — the one exception
   being a thing that is its own only constituent, the fold point. And the whole closes through the diagonal: the
   total act of self-inspection yields a residue, a single content no inspection can hold, so the largest act
   produces the smallest object, and the whole cannot even count itself. The greatest bends into the least.

2. **The relating genuinely loops.** At the level of bare attention there are real cycles: one thing attends a
   second, the second attends the first, and that cycle hardens into a thing of its own. Loops in the relating
   are not an illusion; they are there.

3. **But causation always climbs.** Causation here is not a separate stipulation; it is the making-of-one-thing-
   from-others, read straight off the relating: a composite depends on the constituents it was made from. And a
   composite always stands strictly higher than its constituents. So following causation only ever goes up, never
   back — the looping attention of point 2, read causally, spirals up like a helix rather than closing like a
   ring. This is proved from the climbing, not built into the word "causal": the definition of the causal
   relation never mentions height.

4. **The one place a loop could close is the fold — and there it is a still point.** Because causation strictly
   climbs between distinct things, any closed causal loop would need a thing that is its own maker: a
   self-constituting point, the fold. On the genuine tower no such point exists (everything is made from things
   strictly below it), and the diagonal shows that the idealized total self-point cannot be realized at all. So
   the sole candidate for a closed loop is a degenerate still point, not a genuine ring: time does not close.

5. **The fork was genuine.** To make sure this was a finding and not a definition, a second structure was built:
   a single point that is its own member and its own maker, on which the causal relation genuinely closes into a
   loop. It is real, and it admits no consistent notion of height — which is exactly why it is the fold, the one
   place the climbing argument breaks. That such a loop CAN be built is what makes the real world's acyclicity a
   result: time could have closed, and the mathematics shows that on the genuine tower it does not.

## What it means

However tightly the world's relating circles back on itself, it cannot let time close into a ring in which a
thing precedes itself. Reification — the making that is time — only ever climbs; so attention may loop, but
causation spirals up and never returns, and the world is spared the paradox of a cause after its effect. The one
seam where time might have closed is the One, the point that is its own beginning, and there the mathematics
draws the possibility exactly and finds it a still point, not a genuine loop.

## What it does not claim

- No smallest or largest thing is asserted as an absolute quantity; the shape is stated structurally (nothing
  bottoms out, you can always build one more), self-relative where a quantity is involved.
- The finding does not rest on "a well-founded order has no cycles" alone — that would be a costume of the order.
  It rests on the COEXISTENCE: the relating genuinely loops WHILE causation genuinely does not, and the sole
  candidate for a closed loop is the fold.
- The causal relation is not defined as climbing; its climbing is proved, and a genuine causal loop is shown
  reachable elsewhere, so the acyclicity is not true by fiat.
- Program 1's permanent open questions are carried untouched. Series 2.5 adds none and closes none; it draws the
  shape of the whole and the acyclicity of time sharper.

## The honest alternatives, had it fallen

Had the genuine tower itself contained a self-making point that closed a causal loop, the verdict would have been
LOOPED: time closes at the One, a thing precedes itself, and the reading would have been reported in that
direction and rewritten, not defended. Had the fold been drawn as the sole candidate but its status left
undecidable by the structure, the verdict would have been SHAPE-DRAWN, the honest terminus, drawn exactly and
never reached by relabeling. Both were genuinely reachable. The computed verdict is ACYCLIC: the relating loops,
but time does not.
