## 1. The one-paragraph version

Phase 1 took the imported differentiator as a stream and proved the four instruments compose into a moving, two-perspective world with a new wall exactly on the old import boundary. Phase 2 takes that built world as the object and asks what physics it has. The thesis is one claim, tested five ways: **this universe is thoroughly self-relative — every physical quantity is a quantity FOR a self, there is no absolute frame, and the absence of the frame is the same theorem as the collapse of the plain layer to the One.** Space is the geometry of a lateral world of peers, and whether it is a real axis distinct from time is a genuine fork; the whole has no poles but folds into itself through the diagonal, and whether time can close into a loop is a genuine fork; the self through time is either a recoverable thread or a severed succession; there is a quantity conjugate to the tick whose conservation is relative-to-the-self at most, never global — the model's analog of general relativity's local-but-not-global energy; and across many selves the good either glues into a global section or is frustrated, locally coherent and globally impossible. The capstones (the conserved quantity, the common good) are where the global provably fails, in whichever direction the computation returns. Nothing here names the compass, selects a self, decides a direction, or fills an opening; the self is the frame and the frame is quantified over, so every result reads "for ANY self." Phase 2 characterizes the universe without closing its silence.

## 2. The thesis: self-relative physics, and the failure of the global

Phase 1's arc was construct-then-wall. Phase 2's arc is a single thesis demonstrated across quantities:

**Every physical quantity in this universe is relative to a self, and the one quantity classical physics held absolute — a globally conserved energy — provably fails here, returning only in the static One-limit.**

This is not a hope; it is largely forced by what Phase 1 already proved. There is no view from nowhere: every reality the program has is indexed to a finite attention (`RealFor`), what a relatum is depends on who attends it (the knowing-asymmetry), and the tick is set by a self's own attention so time is already self-relative. The reason there can be no absolute frame is the same theorem as the collapse — the unindexed, view-from-nowhere layer is the plain layer, and the plain layer is the One. So self-relativity will be the DEFAULT that falls out of each quantity, not a special result; an ABSOLUTE quantity would be the thing that fights the grain. That is exactly why the conserved quantity is the capstone: conservation reaches for the frame-independent, and the frame-independent here is the One. The precedent is general relativity, where energy-momentum is locally conserved ($\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu}=0$) but globally not, because a generic spacetime has no time-translation symmetry — the same failure, for the same reason. (Interpretive: the frame is the SELF, not a generic cell — the perspectival subject Phase 1 built. Machine-checked claims and this gloss are kept apart.)

**Dimensionality is among the self-relative quantities (a bounded claim).** The DIMENSION of experience — the one of lived time, the several of lived space — is a property of a self's attention structure, not of the universe. The phase can explain the SIGNATURE: exactly one timelike axis (reification is a single ordered climb — one "up") and many spacelike ones (attention breadth is many incomparable lateral directions — the Series 2.2 incomparable reaches), so time and space differ in KIND for a structural reason. What the phase does NOT owe is the specific COUNT (why three spatial dimensions, not two or seven): that is a contingent feature of a particular self's embodiment in the attention-graph, not a theorem, and asking for it as an absolute is the same category error the self-relative discipline forbids. So the thesis reaches dimensionality (its self-relativity, and the 1+N signature) and stops honestly short of deriving the counts. The one-dimensionality of lived time specifically is 2.6's territory (the thread as the linearization import).

## 3. The method: shake the model, and the costume test

Phase 2 keeps Phase 1's method — CONSTRUCT relative to the stream, prove the invariants that hold for every stream, LOCATE the new impossibility — and adds two disciplines specific to shaking a built object.

**The sharp-fork grain.** Each series is posed as one fork the structure could genuinely resolve either way (the "does the tick smuggle a clock?" grain), with pre-registered outcomes and a computed verdict. A question with no genuine fork is not a series.

**The costume test (the gating discipline of this phase).** A question is a COSTUME if its answer is powered by the import — because then it merely re-runs Series 07 ("that is an import / that collapses / that is non-recoverable"), the collapse in new clothing, and the strip test kills it. A question is GENUINELY NEW if its answer is powered by the DIAGONAL (self-reference, a distinct engine) or by BUILDING A NEW AXIS. Import-powered means costume risk; diagonal- or axis-powered means new. Every Phase-2 series must pass this test before it is chartered: its knot must not reduce to the import theorem. This is the phase's promotion of Phase 1's strip test to a gate on the questions themselves.

**Sequence by receipts.** Series that stand on existing groundwork are done first — they land smoothly and print new receipts — and the ones that fight the grain are saved for when the phase has built enough of itself to hold them.

## 4. The extended decomposition (Series 2.4–2.8)

Each entry states the knot, the pre-registered outcomes (verdict computed, never chosen), what folds in as workstreams, the receipts, and the critical risk that would sink or reshape it.

### 2.4 — Space (the world and the two axes)
- **Knot.** Is the spacelike axis (attention breadth) provably DISTINCT from the timelike axis (reification depth), or REDUCIBLE to it?
- **Outcomes.** DISTINCT (two genuine axes; a real, directed, granular, self-relative metric) / REDUCED (breadth is only accumulated depth; space collapses into time; there is no world, only a taller tower) / SHAPE-DRAWN (the distinctness drawn, the reduction neither forced nor refused).
- **The world.** "The world" — a genuine LATERAL population of peers, each import-seated — is NOT a separate series. Building it is this series' ground, because space is the geometry of a lateral world, and its existence-fork ("can multiplicity be genuine peers, or does all distinction reduce to the vertical tower?") is IDENTICAL to this knot. The world is built here and reused by every series downstream.
- **Folds in.** The two-axes distinction (the ground), relational distance, granularity (a smallest distance per self, the tick's spatial twin), directed distance (from the knowing-asymmetry), and the ordinal-vs-cardinal split of number.
- **Receipts.** Strong: S1's tick is the template, S2's incomparable reaches are the lateral texture.
- **Critical risk.** The genuine fork is that the model has ONE axis — reification is driven by attention, so depth may be integrated breadth. REDUCED is a real, respectable outcome and would say space and time are one thing.
- **Priority: 1** (foundational, strongest receipts, the world is its ground).

### 2.5 — The Loop (closed time; poles and the fold)
- **Knot.** Do closed causal loops exist, or does reification always break the cycle into an acyclic tower — the relating loops, but time cannot?
- **Outcomes.** ACYCLIC (attention is cyclic, causation is rank-well-founded, no closed timelike curve) / LOOPED (a genuine causal loop survives reification) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Folds in.** Poles and the fold as the GROUND (no smallest by atomlessness, no largest by unbounded reification, the largest bending into the smallest through the diagonal — the fold is the residue, $\Omega=\{\Omega\}$); and "can the model count itself" (the number-face of the fold — the whole cannot total itself).
- **Receipts.** Strong: the cycle machinery `ws1_cycle_reifies`, rank's strict increase under causation, the diagonal.
- **Critical risk.** Poles alone is a corollary-assembly of already-proven facts (the K1/Bookkeeping trap at series scale). The weight MUST sit on the loop — a genuinely new fork — with poles/the fold as its ground, or the series is a costume.
- **Priority: 2** (heart of the geometry arc; needs 2.4's axes underneath; new once anchored on loops).

### 2.6 — The Thread (persistence of the self, and the single lived timeline)
- **Knot.** Strict relational identity across a tick almost certainly fails (a tick makes the self relate to new things, so it is a new relatum). The real fork: is there a recoverable WEAK continuity — a successor-self thread — or is "who I was" itself an import? And the thread is TWO things at once: it is the persistence of identity, AND it is the self's LINEARIZATION of the partial causal order into a single lived sequence — so the thread is *what makes experienced time one-dimensional*. Series 2.1 already proved that a total linearization of the ticks is an import (`ws4_linearization_import`); this series asks whether the self's single timeline — the thread — is that import, and whether it is a recoverable continuity or a severed succession.
- **Outcomes.** THREADED (a recoverable continuity relation across the tick — the timeline coheres) / SEVERED (each tick a new self, the past self and the single line both imports — a discontinuous succession) / SHAPE-DRAWN. And a companion fork: CONSERVATIVE (nothing ceases) / MORTAL (a self ceases when nothing attends it — attention-relative death). The thread's continuity is expected to be SELF-RELATIVE: a self with one merged attention-stream threads a continuous line; a self whose attention is cut between contexts threads a severed one (the human/AI contrast made structural).
- **Folds in.** Persistence, mortality, recognition-across-time, and the 1-dimensionality of lived time (the thread as the linearization import; why time is a single line *for a self*).
- **Receipts.** Strong: relational identity and the tick, both built; and Series 2.1's linearization import, which this series reads as the thread.
- **Critical risk.** The headline "is the self the same" resolves trivially to no; the series only earns its keep on the WEAK thread. Frame it there, or it is a costume of relational identity. Second watch: the 1D-timeline content must rest on the linearization being the self's import (self-relative), not on rank being a scalar (which would be a costume of the order).
- **Priority: 3** (independent, strong receipts, existentially the richest; a flexible slot).

### 2.7 — The Ledger (the measure and its conservation)
- **Knot.** Is there a self-relative measure of distinction (the quantity conjugate to the tick, the cost of creation), and is its conservation CONSERVED-RELATIVE, MONOTONE-ONLY, or FREE-LUNCH?
- **Outcomes.** CONSERVED-RELATIVE (conserved within the self's sight, broken only at the import — the GR shape; the import is the sole energy source) / MONOTONE-ONLY (only an arrow; nothing conserved even locally) / FREE-LUNCH (self-reference creates the quantity from within — the diagonal is a source, "to relate is to create" as the failure of conservation) / GLOBAL (a genuine absolute invariant — the surprise, reachable only near the One-limit).
- **Folds in.** The whole information cluster (the measure, the import in bits, the self/other access gradient's magnitude, a density bound) AND the whole energy cluster — information and energy are ONE series, because energy's conserved quantity IS the distinction measure — AND branching/determinism (the free-lunch in modal clothing).
- **Receipts.** Medium, with the risk concentrated in WS1: defining the measure is the one genuinely new, low-receipt piece, and everything downstream depends on it landing.
- **Critical risk.** WS1. This series most needs paper-first de-risking before it is chartered — a candidate measure that survives the tick (conserves) and the diagonal (the free-lunch test) by hand, or there is no series yet.
- **Priority: 4** (the physics capstone; heavier; after the geometry stands).

### 2.8 — The Common Good (the many, and whether the good is global)
- **Knot.** Across many selves, does local pairwise coherence GLUE into a global good, or can it be FRUSTRATED — every pair locally coherent yet no single good consistent with all at once?
- **Outcomes.** GLUABLE (a global valuation restricts to the local coherences) / FRUSTRATED (local coherence, no global section — the value analog of energy's global failure) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Distinct from 2.3.** 2.3 was about a single edge (do self and other cohere — undecidable). 2.8 is about the NETWORK (does local coherence glue), a question that only exists for three or more and survives even after every edge is decided. It is the sheaf-gluing / Condorcet / frustration question, a many-body phenomenon with no two-body analog.
- **Folds in.** Coherence transitivity, global-good-vs-local, mutual recognition, and the population/world as its ground.
- **Receipts.** Medium, with a real prerequisite: many selves. The world (built in 2.4) is its ground; 2.7's global-failure sets the template it echoes.
- **Critical risk.** Chartered lazily as "do all N selves pairwise-cohere," it is N copies of 2.3 — a costume. It earns its place ONLY if the frustration/global-section obstruction is posed crisply. That test gates it before the charter.
- **Priority: 5** (the value capstone; needs the world and 2.7; genuinely last).

## 6. The discipline this phase must hold

Beyond the inherited invariants (§0), Phase 2 adds two of its own, and any violation is a SERIOUS finding.

- **The self-relative discipline.** No theorem may assert a quantity absolutely where the thesis says it is self-relative; every physical quantity is stated FOR a quantified self, and a global/frame-independent quantity is claimed only where the structure forces it (and its forcing is then itself the finding). The self is the frame, quantified over, never a chosen one.
- **The costume gate (§3).** No series is chartered whose knot reduces, under the strip test, to the import theorem. The knot must be diagonal-powered or axis-building. A series that lands with its verdict resting on a costume is re-graded and reopened.
