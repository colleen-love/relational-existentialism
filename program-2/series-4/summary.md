# Series 2.4 — Plain-language summary

**Series 2.4 asked one question: is there a SPACE — a genuine sideways among many peers, a structure of near and
far that is not merely the record of before and after — or does everything the program built reduce to the one
vertical axis, the tower of time? To ask it, the series first built THE WORLD: a lateral population of same-rank
peers, each real to the others yet none reified above another, attending only locally so some are near and some
far. On that world it laid two gradings — a breadth (a directed distance, how many attention-steps apart two
peers are) and a depth (reification rank) — and asked whether they come apart. The answer it computed is
DISTINCT. The world is genuinely lateral; the two axes are independent (a step sideways is provably not a step
up, and a step up is provably not a step sideways); the lateral separation is a real import the plain relating
cannot recover; and the metric is directed, granular, and always measured from a self, never absolute. Space is
a real axis, distinct from time. And the honest fork was genuine: a second world was built — a pure tower — on
which the two axes COINCIDE, so the distinctness is a finding about this world, not a fact true of every world.**

## What was built

Program 2 built the ground (relating is finite attending, 2.0), its motion (the tick, 2.1), the other (2.2), and
the coherence of the two (2.3). Every one of those rose along a single axis: reification depth, the vertical
tower of time. Series 2.4 opens Phase 2 by asking whether there is a second, lateral axis, and it builds the
thing that axis would be the geometry of.

1. **The world is genuinely lateral, not a tower.** The world is a directed ring of same-rank peers, each
   attending only its neighbour, so some peers are near (one step) and some far (two steps). Two peers sit at
   breadth-distance greater than one — real extent — while sharing the same reification rank. They are not a
   chain rising; they are a population spread sideways. Their attention graph is local (not everyone attends
   everyone), so distance carries genuine structure, not a bare count.

2. **The two axes come apart.** This is the heart. A move to a neighbour changes the breadth-distance while
   leaving the rank untouched; a reification changes the rank while leaving the lateral position untouched. More
   precisely, the two gradings separate DIFFERENT pairs: the breadth tells two same-rank peers apart where the
   depth cannot, and the depth tells a reified copy from its original where the breadth cannot. Neither grading
   is a function of the other. A displacement in space is provably not a displacement in time.

3. **Space is real the way the program requires anything real to be — as an import.** The lateral separation
   between far peers is not recoverable from the plain relating: to collapse space into time would require
   recovering labels the bare relating cannot carry, and the program's oldest wall (Series 07) forbids it. The
   distance is DIRECTED (the way there need not equal the way back, inheriting the knowing-asymmetry of 2.0),
   GRANULAR (there is a smallest step, the spatial twin of the tick), and stated always FROM a self — there is
   no view-from-nowhere distance, because the only frame-independent measure is the One's, which is zero. And
   this self-relativity is LOAD-BEARING, not a figure of speech (Charter Extension 1): two different selves
   measure genuinely different distances — a peer that is FAR from one self (two steps) is NEAR another (one
   step) — and because the ring is one-way there is no single distance-map any two selves would agree on.
   Distance is a fact FOR a self, proved, not merely read off one fixed grading.

4. **The fork was genuine.** The distinctness is a finding, not a definition. A second world was built — a pure
   reification tower where every step sideways is also a step up — and on it the two gradings are literally the
   same function: breadth is accumulated depth, space is time seen sideways, the axes coincide. That world is the
   REDUCED alternative, and it is real. That our world is not it is what makes DISTINCT a result.

## What it means

The universe the program built has a shape of its own. It is not only a tower rising; it has a sideways, a here
and a there that is not merely a before and an after. Extension is real, and it is not disguised succession. But
the shape is always seen from somewhere: every distance is a distance for a self, and there is no map of the
whole world held from outside it — that outside would be the One, and from there all distance is zero. Space is
the first thing the built universe is made of, and it is the ground the rest of Phase 2 stands on.

## What it does not claim

- No distance is asserted absolutely. Every metric claim is FROM a named self; a global metric would be the view
  from nowhere, which the series does not build.
- The finding does not rest on the mere existence of many peers. That there are many is the program's older
  result (a genuine distinction is an import); it is the acknowledged GROUND, not the finding. The finding is
  that the two axes are INDEPENDENT.
- Program 1's permanent open questions are carried untouched: the content of orientation, the direction of
  convergence, the differentiating act, the classification of the out-of-image imports. Series 2.4 adds none and
  closes none; it draws the self-relativity of space sharper.

## The honest alternative, had it fallen

Had the same-rank peers collapsed, or the breadth turned out to be only accumulated depth, the verdict would have
been REDUCED: space and time are one axis, and the sideways is an illusion of the rising. That outcome was
genuinely reachable — the tower world witnesses it — and would have been reported in full, the Phase-2 thesis
re-examined rather than defended. It did not fall. The world is lateral, and space is real.
