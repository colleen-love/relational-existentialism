# Series 2.13 (The Curve): summary

**Verdict: INERT. Computed, not chosen. The capstone of Phase 3 and the last series of the built arc.**

## What the series asked

General relativity's one idea is that geometry is not a fixed stage but is itself shaped by what it contains: mass-energy curves space, and gravity is the shadow of that curving. This austere relational world had already done half of that move. Series 2.4 proved its metric self-relative, varying by who measures, no absolute frame. Series 2.13 asked the other half: does the metric also vary by what is THERE? Does the measure of distinction packed into a region bend the distances around it, so that geometry is made of the same stuff as everything else in this world, distinction? That is gravity, in the model's own key.

The question had to be asked without cheating. The forbidden move, the one the whole series guards against, is by-hand coupling: define a distance that reads the measure, then "recover" that the measure curves it. That proves nothing. So the test was on the model's OWN distance, the one it already built (Series 2.4's directed path-length), never a distance rigged to curve.

## What was found

The world's own metric is decoupled from its grain. Change the measure of distinction packed into a region while holding the connections fixed, and the distances do not move at all. Geometry here reads the connections and is blind to the measure. This world built space and its own weight as two separate things, and gravity, the thread that in our universe sews them into one, is not among the things it already contains.

The test was the exact experiment the question names. Take two configurations with the SAME adjacency (the same directed reachability) but DIFFERENT grain (more of the measure packed onto one relatum). Compute the imported distance on both. Every distance is identical: packing more of the measure into the region did not bend a single distance around it. And this is not a coincidence to be tuned away. The imported distance is a function of the adjacency alone; it has no channel through which the grain could ever enter. Its invariance is structural.

This is exactly what the phase's own foundation foretold. Series 2.4 proved that space (the lateral metric) and the measure (the vertical rank) are DISTINCT axes, neither a function of the other. Gravity is precisely the coupling of those two. A world that built them apart does not contain their coupling. INERT is that fact, reported straight.

## The honest scope

The verdict is INERT, and INERT claims even less than a proportionality: no coupling of the imported distance to the grain is claimed at all, so certainly no Einstein field equation, and certainly not the area law (that a region's information is bounded by its boundary, not its volume, the Bekenstein bound and the holographic principle). The area law is the hardest, least certain piece of the whole recovery and was never this series' target; it is the disclosed forward-note, the deepest unbuilt question.

The fork was kept genuine, not decided by fiat. The metric is not a rigged constant that ignores everything: it genuinely responds to the adjacency (change the connections and the distances change), it is simply blind to the grain. And the grain-dependence test has a real positive side: a by-hand grain-weighted distance was exhibited, once, only to show that some distances DO curve with the measure, so the model's own distance being INERT is a substantive finding and not a vacuous one. That foil was fenced off and proved distinct from the distance under test; the verdict rests on the imported distance alone. The reviewer who pressed hardest on exactly this, whether the INERT result secretly leaned on the grain-weighted foil, confirmed it did not: the imported distance is structurally blind to the grain, and the foil is never the object whose verdict is reported.

The pre-registered alternative, GRAVITATIONAL (the grain genuinely sources the metric), would have been the surprise, and it did not obtain. It stays reachable in the verdict function, not excluded, but the model's own structure does not provide the coupling.

## Where it sits

"To attend is to become" built the grain (Series 2.7). "Space is real, and not time seen sideways" built the metric (Series 2.4). Series 2.13 asked whether the first curves the second, and the answer is that it does not: this world made space and its own weight two separate things. That is not a failure to hide but a precise instruction. To become our universe, where gravity sews the two into one, the next iteration must couple the axes Series 2.4 kept apart. The built arc closes here, on the deepest of its walls, with the curve drawn exactly: the grain does not bend the geometry, and the Einstein equation and the area law stand beyond it, the last things the recovery would ask for.
