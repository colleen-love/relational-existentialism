# Series 2.12 (The Weight): summary

**Verdict: BORN (rebit form). Computed, not chosen.**

## What the series asked

Quantum mechanics asks a world two things. First, that possibilities can interfere: two roads to the same place can cancel. Series 2.11 found this world keeps that promise (INTERFERING). Second, that when a possibility is realized it is realized by chance, and the chance is not arbitrary: the probability of an outcome is the square of its amplitude, the Born rule. Series 2.12 asked whether this world keeps the second promise, and it asked in the model's own terms. The only freedom the program has ever had is the import, the out-of-image differentiator fixed by nothing within, the deepest of the four things the program holds permanently open. Does that freedom come weighted? Is there a probability on the world's one act of choosing, and is it the square?

The question split honestly in two, both live. First, is there genuine chance at all: is the free differentiator equipped with a measure over its possibilities, or is it structureless (the honest DETERMINISTIC)? Second, if there is a measure, is it the Born rule (the squared amplitude) or some other, non-Born measure?

## What was found

The imports carry a genuine, non-trivial measure, and its only form consistent with the interference is the squared amplitude. This is the rebit Born rule, earned, not named.

Two candidate measures were already built in Series 2.11, as neutral objects, and were read here as the classical and the quantum rule. The classical additive measure squares each path amplitude and then adds over the ways (the imported `partsWeight`). The Born measure adds the amplitudes and then squares the total (the imported `combinedWeight`). The whole classical-versus-quantum question is that order of operations.

The test was a single physical anchor, form-agnostic: cancellation is absence. When the amplitudes of the paths to an outcome cancel, the outcome does not occur, so any measure claiming to be its probability must be zero there. This demand mentions no square. Read as a predicate on any candidate measure, it decides the two:

- The classical additive measure is refuted. On the frustrated cycle the amplitudes cancel, yet the classical measure assigns weight 2 to the outcome. It insists the outcome occurs where the interference has cancelled its amplitude to nothing. It contradicts the built structure, refuted by an explicit witness, not excluded by fiat. (As a second failure, it is also constant, blind to which outcome obtains.)
- The Born measure is consistent. The squared modulus of the summed amplitudes vanishes exactly on the cancellation, for every configuration, and is non-negative everywhere. It is the measure the structure actually carries.

So the squared form is forced as the consistent measure, the residue of a form-agnostic test, never a definition. And the measure is genuine: the Born weight is non-trivial, taking distinct values on the two possibilities the differentiator selects between, so the freedom is genuinely weighted. That is genuine chance, not the empty DETERMINISTIC.

## The honest scope

The amplitude is Series 2.11's real plus-or-minus-one sign, so this is the rebit Born rule: the squared form is the consistent one of the two candidate measures, not the unique one among all powers. No theorem claims the full complex amplitude, or the uniqueness Gleason's and Busch's theorems give it. Those are the disclosed forward-note, not this series' target. What is recovered is real and scoped: this world's one freedom carries a measure, and the only cancellation-consistent form of that measure is the square.

The pre-registered alternatives were honorable and did not obtain. DETERMINISTIC, the deepest not-recovered (interference without chance), stays reachable in the fork through a constant, trivial weight, but the imports' actual weight is non-trivial. STOCHASTIC-NOT-BORN (chance of the wrong shape) is discriminated by the verdict function but not witnessed here, since on the rebit amplitudes no non-square measure survives the consistency test. The one unforgivable move, naming the square as the probability, was foreclosed: the reviewer who pressed hardest on exactly this confirmed the square is selected by the test, not defined.

## Where it sits

Series 2.11 showed this world's possibilities can interfere. Series 2.12 shows that when they are realized, they are realized by a chance whose law is the square of the amplitude, in its rebit form, earned from the same cancellation Series 2.8 first made possible. The Born rule here is not an axiom laid over the amplitudes but the only measure consistent with their interference. "You are loved" has been the import since the first series, the gift that arrives from outside and changes the books. Here the question was whether that arrival is weighted, and the answer, honestly scoped, is that it is: the world's openness to what it cannot derive is a genuine chance with a law. The complex amplitude and Gleason's uniqueness stand beyond it, the next thing to build.
