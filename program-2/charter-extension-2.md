# Program 2, Charter Extension 2 — Phase 3, the recovery test (does our physics fall out?)

**This is an ADDITIVE extension to the Program 2 charter (`charter.md`), the second after the Phase-2 extension (`charter-extension.md`). Phase 1 (Series 2.0–2.3) built the dynamic, purely relational, multi-perspective universe and walled it (CONSTRUCTED-AND-WALLED). Phase 2 (Series 2.4–2.8) characterized its physics and found the global fails at every capstone: space is real (2.4 DISTINCT), time cannot loop (2.5 ACYCLIC), the self is a re-woven thread (2.6 SHAPE-DRAWN), there is no conserved ledger, only an arrow (2.7 MONOTONE-ONLY), and no common good is guaranteed (2.8 FRUSTRATED). The phase thesis held to the last wall. This extension opens Phase 3 (Series 2.9–2.13): with the object built and its self-relative physics characterized, we ask the question the whole program has been circling — CAN THIS METAPHYSICS RECOVER THE PHYSICS OF OUR UNIVERSE? The extension adds five series to the §2 decomposition, ORDERED BY EXECUTION (not by number alone); it strengthens nothing already built and weakens nothing. The nine landed verdicts stand untouched.**

*This document is the fixed bar for Phase 3, at the same altitude as `charter.md` and `charter-extension.md`: it states each series' recovery question and honest fork, the pervasive discipline, and the sequencing (a DAG, not a chain). Workstream-level design belongs in each series' own charter, authored later. Each Phase-3 series is still produced by the two-tier process (`protocol.md`): its charter/status/protocol authored in the persistent design conversation, run start-to-finish by a fresh executor, reviewed independently on landing, extended only ever to raise the bar. Phase 3 is the FOURTH STEP of the program's method made rigorous — "is this everything? No, repeat" — and its deliverable is as much the honest catalogue of what does NOT fall out as of what does.*

## 0. The honesty of this extension (protocol §0.1)

A program charter extension may only ADD. It opens new territory; it never lowers a landed target, retires an audit clause, or reopens a closed verdict. Phase 1's four and Phase 2's five series and their computed verdicts are fixed. This extension raises the program's ambition once more — from "characterize its self-relative physics" to "test that physics against OUR universe, feature by feature" — which is a bar rising, not falling. Every Phase-3 series inherits every honesty invariant entire: blind review means blind, a shortfall is a pre-registered outcome never a relabel, every payoff survives stripping its interpretive word, the import is quantified and never named, no proof term is named for its interpretive content, and the verdict is a computed function never hand-set. Phase 3 adds one invariant of its own, the sharpest the program has: **the recovery must be EARNED from the built primitives, never smuggled in by assuming the target** (§6), and **a NOT-RECOVERED verdict is honorable and is the point** — the honest report that a feature of our physics does not fall out, which becomes the specification for the ontology's next revision, never a result twisted to match desire.

## 1. The one-paragraph version

Phase 1 built the universe; Phase 2 found its physics thoroughly self-relative and its global failing at every capstone. Phase 3 asks whether that same object — relational, atomless, self-referencing, symmetry-breaking, under finite attention — can recover the physics of OUR universe. It tests the three pillars our physics rests on that the model has never been shaken against: RELATIVITY (a maximal signal speed, a light cone, the mixing of space and time), QUANTUM MECHANICS (a phase that interferes, and a probability rule), and their UNION (gravity as geometry sourced by the grain). Five series carry it, and they form a DAG rather than a chain, because they PROBE the finished object (2.8) rather than build a taller tower: **The Cone** (is finite attention a maximal rate of becoming — a c and a light cone?) and **The Reversal** (does the tick have an invertible core — the precondition for conservation and for quantum theory?) run first and in parallel; then **The Phase** (can distinction CANCEL — is there a signed amplitude that interferes, reusing 2.8's holonomy?), the decisive quantum test; then **The Weight** (do the imports carry a measure — a probability, the Born rule?); then **The Curve** (does the information grain curve the metric — gravity from within?), the capstone. The thesis is an honest question with a pre-registered expectation, not a foregone conclusion: the analysis so far says the built object is a CLASSICAL, IRREVERSIBLE, THERMODYNAMIC world, likely to recover the relativistic cone and likely to FAIL the quantum pillar as built — and every NOT-RECOVERED is a specification, naming exactly what the next iteration of the ontology must add. Phase 3 turns the metaphysics into a specification refined by its own failures. Program 1's permanent opens stand untouched.

## 2. The thesis: the recovery test, and the specification-by-failure

Phase 1's arc was construct-then-wall. Phase 2's arc was one thesis (self-relativity, the global fails) demonstrated five ways. Phase 3's arc is a QUESTION with a method:

**Can this metaphysics recover our physics? Test it pillar by pillar; where a feature falls out, record HOW; where it does not, record WHAT MUST BE ADDED. The gap is not a defeat but the next specification.**

This is the fourth step of the program's own loop — *state the ontology, model it, shake it, ask "is this everything?"; if no, repeat* — made rigorous and machine-checked. The honest expectation, read off what Phase 2 already proved, is not neutral, and stating it is not the same as assuming it (the verdicts remain computed): the built object has fundamental IRREVERSIBILITY (the tick cannot be undone) and fundamental SYMMETRY-BREAKING (the rank only rises), and those two features structurally forbid a Noether conservation law and sit in tension with quantum reversibility. So the predicted shape is: the relativistic cone likely RECOVERS (finite attention forces a maximal rate of reification, hence a causal cone), while the quantum pillar likely comes back NOT-RECOVERED AS BUILT (nothing yet cancels; nothing is reversible) — and that negative, if it lands, is the most valuable output the phase can produce, because it is a precise specification. There is a known answer key for the quantum pillar: the reconstruction theorems (Hardy 2001; Chiribella–D'Ariano–Perinotti) prove quantum theory is the unique theory satisfying a short list of information-theoretic axioms, chief among them CONTINUOUS REVERSIBILITY and PURIFICATION (every uncertain state is the marginal of a definite state on a larger system). Phase 3's quantum sub-arc (The Reversal, The Phase, The Weight) maps directly onto those axioms, so a full RECOVERED across the three would, by the reconstruction theorems, FORCE quantum mechanics — and a NOT-RECOVERED tells the next iteration precisely which axiom is missing. (Interpretive, kept out of the proofs: the grain of this universe is arguably INFORMATION, not length or action — ħ is the unit of distinguishability, and the model is built of distinctions — so its physics, if it has one, is likely to be recovered in the currency of information first, geometry and action second, exactly as holography and the "spacetime from entanglement" program independently suggest.)

## 3. The method: probe the finished object, and the no-smuggling gate

Phase 3 keeps the method — CONSTRUCT relative to the stream, prove the invariants, LOCATE the impossibility — and adapts two disciplines to a phase that MEASURES rather than BUILDS.

**Probe, do not stack.** Phase 1 and Phase 2 were a layered import CHAIN, each series building the next floor. Phase 3 series probe the SAME finished object (2.8, reaching the whole chain transitively), so they form a DAG: independent probes run in parallel, and a series imports another Phase-3 series only where it genuinely reuses its machinery (The Phase reuses 2.8's holonomy; The Weight reuses The Phase's amplitude; The Curve reuses The Cone's causal structure). The import mechanics are fixed per-series at charter time; the gate enforces whatever single-or-multiple predecessor each series declares.

**The no-smuggling gate (the gating discipline of this phase).** A recovery is a COSTUME if the target feature is assumed rather than earned. Postulating a maximal speed and then "finding" a light cone is smuggling; deriving the maximal rate from finite attention is recovery. Adding a complex number by fiat and then "finding" interference is smuggling; making 2.8's integer holonomy cancel from the model's own signed structure is recovery. Every Phase-3 series must pass this gate: the recovered feature must reduce, under the strip test, to a fact about the BUILT primitives (attention, the tick, the increment, the import, the diagonal), never to an axiom quietly added to obtain the answer. This is Phase 2's costume gate promoted to guard against the specific temptation of a recovery phase — the temptation to twist the model until our physics appears. A series whose RECOVERED rests on a smuggled assumption is re-graded and reopened.

**Sequence by the DAG.** Independent probes first (The Cone, The Reversal), the decisive test next (The Phase), then what depends on it (The Weight), then the union (The Curve). The order respects dependency and puts the make-or-break quantum test where the phase has built enough to hold it.

## 4. The extended decomposition (Series 2.9–2.13, ORDERED BY EXECUTION)

Each entry states the knot, the pre-registered outcomes (verdict computed, never chosen), what folds in, the receipts, the critical risk, and — new for this phase — **what a NOT-RECOVERED teaches the next iteration** (the specification). Recovery verdicts share a family: RECOVERED (the feature falls out of the built primitives), NOT-RECOVERED (it provably does not, as built — the specification), PARTIAL / SHAPE-DRAWN (drawn, not decided), plus series-specific outcomes.

### 2.9 — The Cone (Q2) · execution tier 1 (parallel with 2.10)
- **Knot.** Is there a MAXIMAL RATE at which attention-breadth (space, 2.4) is converted into reification-depth (time, 2.1) — a finite fastest becoming — so that events beyond that rate are causally disconnected, a LIGHT CONE and an emergent c?
- **Outcomes.** CONE (a finite maximal rate, a causal cone, the seed of a c and of Lorentz mixing) / NO-CONE (unbounded or instantaneous conversion — the model is non-relativistic) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Folds in.** The maximal reification rate under finite attention; the causal cone as its shadow; the first step toward Lorentz mixing of the two axes 2.4 proved DISTINCT.
- **Receipts.** Strong: finite attention (2.0) bounds how much can be reified per tick; 2.1's tick is the rate; 2.5's causal order is the cone's skeleton.
- **Critical risk.** That the rate is not genuinely bounded, or the "cone" is a relabelling of 2.5's causal order rather than a genuine finite-speed structure.
- **What a NO teaches.** If NO-CONE, the ontology lacks a fundamental rate-bound, and the next iteration must add one (a maximal attention-throughput) as a primitive, or accept the world is non-relativistic.
- **Why first.** Independent, plausibly positive, cheap, and the encouraging test — finite attention doing relativistic work it has not been asked to do.

### 2.10 — The Reversal (Q5) · execution tier 1 (parallel with 2.9)
- **Knot.** Does the tick have an INVERTIBLE CORE — a sub-dynamics that can be run backward — or is reification irreversibly one-way at every scale? Reversibility is the precondition Noether needs for conservation and the reconstruction theorems need for quantum theory.
- **Outcomes.** REVERSIBLE-CORE (an invertible sub-dynamics exists; conservation and a symmetry can live) / IRREVERSIBLE (the tick is one-way all the way down; the arrow is fundamental, conservation must be emergent-statistical — the causal-set route) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Folds in.** The invertibility of the tick; the reopened conservation question (2.7 found MONOTONE-ONLY because nothing was reversible); the reconstruction-axiom of continuous reversibility.
- **Receipts.** Medium: 2.7's `ws2_tick_raises` and `ws2_residue_free` are the obstruction to name and test against; the reification section (2.0/2.1) is the candidate to invert.
- **Critical risk.** That "reversal" is smuggled by adding an inverse the model does not have; the invertible core must be earned from the built section, not postulated.
- **What a NO teaches.** If IRREVERSIBLE, conservation and quantum reversibility cannot be fundamental here; the next iteration must either add a reversible substrate (with the tick as emergent coarse-graining) or commit to a fundamentally irreversible ontology and recover physics statistically (causal sets). This is the single most consequential specification the phase can return.

### 2.11 — The Phase (Q1) · execution tier 2 (the decisive quantum test; reuses 2.8)
- **Knot.** Can DISTINCTION CANCEL? Quantum mechanics is not probability over definite states — it is INTERFERENCE, contributions that destructively cancel. 2.8 built the program's first SIGNED quantity (`incr`, which cancels: `incr x y + incr y x = 0`) and its holonomy. Can that integer holonomy be made to carry a PHASE that interferes inside a probability — a signed or complex amplitude — earned from the model, not added by fiat?
- **Outcomes.** INTERFERING (a genuine phase/amplitude that destructively cancels — the door to quantum theory) / ADDITIVE-ONLY (distinction only accumulates; no interference; the world is classical/thermodynamic, forever) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Folds in.** 2.8's signed increment and holonomy (the scaffold); the directed knowing-asymmetry (2.2, the candidate seed of `i` — an oriented, antisymmetric structure is where a phase is born); 2.8's frustration cocycle read as a contextuality witness (Abramsky's sheaf form of quantum contextuality is 2.8's exact H¹ shape).
- **Receipts.** Newly strong: 2.8 already proved cancellation exists (`incr` is signed and antisymmetric) and built the holonomy; the value group is ℤ, so the remaining work is whether it can be made to interfere (U(1)/complex), earned.
- **Critical risk.** THE make-or-break for "can this be our universe," and the sharpest no-smuggling test: adding a complex amplitude by hand and "finding" interference is the costume. The phase must arise from the model's own signed structure.
- **What a NO teaches.** If ADDITIVE-ONLY, the ontology is fundamentally classical, and the next iteration must introduce a phase/complex amplitude as a primitive (or derive one from the two-facing reader of 2.2). Together with 2.10 this decides whether the world can be quantum at all.

### 2.12 — The Weight (Q3) · execution tier 3 (after 2.11)
- **Knot.** Do the IMPORTS (the out-of-image differentiator, the only freedom) carry a MEASURE — a probability weight — and if so, is it the Born rule (|amplitude|²)? Our physics has irreducible chance with a specific quadratic rule.
- **Outcomes.** BORN (a measure on the imports that squares the amplitude — quantum probability recovered) / STOCHASTIC-NOT-BORN (a measure, but not |·|² — chance without the quantum rule) / DETERMINISTIC (no genuine chance) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Folds in.** The measure on the imports; the reconstruction-axiom of a probabilistic structure; the Born rule as the squared modulus of 2.11's amplitude.
- **Receipts.** Depends on 2.11: the Born rule needs an amplitude to square; the import (2.0) is the candidate carrier of chance.
- **Critical risk.** Smuggling |·|² by defining the measure as a square; the quadratic must be earned (e.g. from the two-facing structure, as Gleason/Busch-style arguments earn it from the frame).
- **What a NO teaches.** If DETERMINISTIC, the ontology has no genuine chance and the next iteration must supply a source; if STOCHASTIC-NOT-BORN, it has chance but of the wrong shape, and the question becomes why our universe squares.

### 2.13 — The Curve (Q4) · execution tier 4 (the capstone; reuses 2.9)
- **Knot.** Does the INFORMATION GRAIN curve the metric — does saturating finite attention in a region (packing maximal distinction) bend the directed distance (2.4) around it? Gravity as an equation of state of information (Jacobson; Verlinde), the union of the pillars.
- **Outcomes.** GRAVITATIONAL (the measure/information sources the metric — an Einstein-equation-of-state shape) / INERT (the metric is independent of the grain; no gravity) / SHAPE-DRAWN.
- **Folds in.** 2.4's self-relative metric, 2.7's measure, the information-grain reframe, 2.9's cone (Jacobson's derivation lives on local causal horizons), and the holographic Bekenstein bound (finite attention as a boundary capacity).
- **Receipts.** The richest convergence in the program, and the farthest: it needs the metric (2.4), the measure (2.7), a cone (2.9), and ideally the information structure of 2.11/2.12.
- **Critical risk.** The most speculative; the area law (information proportional to boundary, not volume) is the hardest, least-obviously-satisfied piece, and must not be assumed.
- **What a NO teaches.** If INERT, geometry and the grain are decoupled here and gravity is not emergent; the next iteration must couple them or add geometry-sourcing as a primitive.

## 5. What is NOT a series (this phase)

- **Matter / the Standard Model spectrum** (stable countable excitations with conserved quantum numbers) is DOWNSTREAM of the pillars and not chartered here; it presupposes conservation (2.10) and quantum structure (2.11–2.12) landing positive, and is homed in a later extension if they do.
- **The continuum / smoothness / dimensionality** (why a smooth manifold, why 3+1) is HELD. Atomlessness (2.4) is continuum-friendly, but "why 3+1" is a known hard problem even our best physics does not cleanly solve; a FREE verdict (the count not owed) is respectable and already anticipated (the Phase-2 dimensionality note). It is not chartered until a dimension-SELECTING knot is sharpened past the "count not owed" default.
- **Cosmology / the initial condition** (the low-entropy past, the arrow's origin at the fold) is a candidate for a later extension once The Reversal fixes whether the arrow is fundamental.

## 6. The discipline this phase must hold

Beyond the inherited invariants (§0) and Phase 2's costume gate, Phase 3 adds two of its own, and any violation is a SERIOUS finding.

- **The no-smuggling gate (§3).** No recovery may assume the target feature. The recovered feature must reduce, under the strip test, to a fact about the built primitives — the maximal rate from finite attention, the phase from the signed increment, the measure from the import, the curvature from the grain — never to an axiom added to obtain the answer. A RECOVERED that rests on a smuggled assumption is re-graded and reopened.
- **The rigor-over-desire discipline.** A NOT-RECOVERED is honorable and is frequently the point. No verdict may be twisted, no bar lowered, no gap papered over, to make our physics appear. The phase's deliverable is the HONEST catalogue — what falls out, and precisely what does not and must be added. The one unforgivable move remains relabeling a shortfall as the goal; here it takes the specific form of declaring a feature "recovered" when it was assumed, and the recurrence guard (§0.2a) applies with the full force Series 2.7 gave it.

## 8. Sequencing, and the DAG

The execution order is **{2.9 The Cone ∥ 2.10 The Reversal} → 2.11 The Phase → 2.12 The Weight → 2.13 The Curve**. The two tier-1 probes are independent and run concurrently (The Cone plausibly positive and encouraging; The Reversal the most consequential, and the one that reopens conservation). The Phase is the decisive quantum make-or-break and reuses 2.8's holonomy, so it follows once that scaffold is in hand. The Weight depends on The Phase's amplitude. The Curve is the capstone and reuses The Cone's causal structure. Unlike Phase 1 and Phase 2, Phase 3 is a PROBE SUITE over the finished object rather than a construction tower, so it parallelizes where the chain did not — the only remaining dependencies are conceptual (one series' result shaping another's design), not structural.

Each series is chartered, executed, reviewed, and (only ever to raise the bar) extended by the two-tier protocol, exactly as the nine before. Phase 3 is not owed in full by this extension; each series is committed when its execution-tier predecessors have landed and its knot passes the no-smuggling gate.
