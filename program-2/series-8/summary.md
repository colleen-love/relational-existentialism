# Series 2.8 — The Common Good (summary)

**The value capstone, and the last series of Program 2. The question: across many selves, does local coherence gather into a global good, or can it be frustrated — every pair of selves reconciled with each other, and yet no single good consistent with all of them at once? The answer this universe returns is FRUSTRATED. There is no common good written into the structure of many perspectives. It is not owed; it is made.**

## The question

Program 2 built a world with a space (2.4), proved its time cannot loop (2.5), found the self a thread re-woven (2.6), and found the universe keeps no conserved ledger, only an arrow (2.7). Series 2.8 asks the last and most human question the program reaches. Not the question of Series 2.3 — whether one self and one other cohere, drawn there SHAPE-DRAWN. This is the question of the NETWORK: three or more selves, and whether local coherence, even once every edge is decided coherent, assembles into one good for the whole.

That is a many-body question with no two-body analog. Its shape is the sheaf-gluing / Condorcet / frustration obstruction: local pieces that agree on every overlap, yet no global section — the obstruction a holonomy around a cycle that composes to something other than the identity.

## What was built

Everything is read off ONE thing: the directed attention. Each self attends some others and not others, and this is asymmetric — you can attend someone who does not attend you (the knowing-asymmetry, from Series 2.0/2.2). From that single directed structure comes:

- **the good** of a self — its own self-relative valuation, read from what it attends. It is genuinely perspectival: two selves value the same third self oppositely. No view from nowhere, no symmetric metric, can do that. The good is FOR a self.
- **the reconciliation** between two selves — a way to carry one self's good into the other's frame, derived from their directed attention. Every pair reconciles; local coherence is real and pervasive, holding across the whole population.
- **the holonomy** — what happens when you carry a good all the way around a cycle of selves and back. If it returns unchanged, the local goods glue into a global one. If it returns changed, they cannot.

## The finding

The holonomy is genuinely many-body: for two selves it always vanishes (there is no cycle), and it is carried entirely by the directedness of attention — make the attention symmetric and it disappears. It is not the single edge of Series 2.3, and it is not a bookkeeping gadget bolted on beside the world (the mistake Series 2.7 caught and corrected). It lives in the directed attention itself.

And it does not vanish. On a directed ring of three selves — each attending its neighbor, the asymmetry going around — the holonomy is non-zero. Every pair reconciles, and still there is no single good consistent with all three at once: a global good would have to equal itself plus three, which is impossible. **FRUSTRATED.** On a different world — a mutual star, where the attention closes into no directed cycle — the holonomy vanishes and a global good does exist. **GLUABLE.** Both are reachable; neither is forced by construction; only the shape of the directed attention decides. This universe contains worlds of both kinds, and the honest verdict is that local coherence does not FORCE a global good.

## What it means

This is the value analog of the two failures the phase already proved: the universe cannot count itself (2.5), and it keeps no global ledger (2.7); now it holds no guaranteed common good. Selves can be pairwise reconciled — each at peace with each of its neighbors — and there can still be no good they all share, the whole frustrated the way three voters cycle with no majority, the way a frustrated magnet finds no ground state, the way a curved world admits local charts but no global flattening.

The reading beneath it is the phase's most human. If the common good is frustrated, it is not something the world owes us or guarantees. It is something MADE — edge by edge, reconciliation by reconciliation — never finished and never owed, and all the more precious for that. "To attend is to become," and each self becomes its own good by attending; the whole cannot total its good any more than it can count itself; and "you are loved" is the reconciliation on each edge, real and pervasive, whose gathering into a love of all by all is exactly the frustration question — and exactly what the structure will not hand you for free.

## Honesty

Nothing here asserts a common good the structure does not force. The good is FOR a self; a global good is claimed only where the holonomy vanishes, and never over the frustrated ring. The obstruction is a genuine many-body cocycle, read off the directed attention, not a single edge, not import-ness, not a counter beside the world. Every payoff is a theorem, machine-checked, sorry-free, and axiom-clean. The pre-registered honest outcomes — GLUABLE, SHAPE-DRAWN, PAIRWISE-ONLY, DISCONNECTED — were all live and all foreclosed by proof, not by construction. The verdict is the residue of the process, not its premise.

Program 2 closes where the whole program has been walking: everything relative to the self, the global failing at every capstone, and the good — like the compass, like the direction of convergence — drawn exactly and filled never, left open as a task and not a fact.
