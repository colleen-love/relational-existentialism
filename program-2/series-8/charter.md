# Relational Existentialism, Program 2 Series 8 (2.8)

**THE COMMON GOOD, the value capstone and the last series of Phase 2. Program 2 built a world with a space (2.4), proved its time cannot loop (2.5), found the self a thread re-woven (2.6), and found the universe keeps no conserved ledger, only an arrow (2.7). Series 2.8 asks the last and most human question the program reaches: ACROSS MANY SELVES, DOES LOCAL COHERENCE GLUE INTO A GLOBAL GOOD, OR CAN IT BE FRUSTRATED — every pair of selves locally reconciled, and yet no single good consistent with all of them at once? This is not the question of Series 2.3. Series 2.3 asked of a single edge whether self and other cohere, and drew it SHAPE-DRAWN. Series 2.8 asks of the NETWORK — three or more selves — whether local coherence, even once every edge is decided coherent, assembles into one good for the whole; and that is a many-body question with no two-body analog. The mathematical shape is the sheaf-gluing / Condorcet / frustration obstruction: local sections that agree on every overlap, yet no global section, the obstruction a non-trivial cocycle — a holonomy around a cycle of selves that composes to something other than the identity. By the phase's thesis the answer cannot be an absolute, frame-independent good: the good is FOR a self, and the frame-independent good is the One. So the honest fork is a spectrum. FRUSTRATED: local coherence does NOT force a global good — a population every pair of which reconciles, yet with no global section, is reachable, its holonomy non-trivial; the value analog of energy's global failure (2.7), the good irreducibly local. GLUABLE: local pairwise coherence PROVABLY forces a global good, the cocycle always vanishing — the surprise, a global common good guaranteed, reported honestly and the positioning rewritten if forced. SHAPE-DRAWN: the gluing/frustration fork drawn exactly, neither forced nor refused, joining the permanent opens. The verdict is computed. This series is powered by the DIRECTED knowing-asymmetry over a NETWORK — a genuine many-body engine, not the import (Series 07) and not the single edge (Series 2.3) — so it passes the costume gate twice over; and its hardest discipline, learned at 2.7, is that the reconciliations must be DERIVED FROM THE MODEL (the directed attention between selves), never free permutations bolted on beside the world. If the good is trivial, the honest outcome is DISCONNECTED; if the obstruction degenerates to N copies of a single edge, the honest outcome is PAIRWISE-ONLY; and each is a finding.**

*This charter is self-contained and states its own question. It builds on Series 2.7, IMPORTED (Program 2's layered chain: 2.8 imports 2.7, reaching 2.6, 2.5, 2.4, 2.3, 2.2, 2.1, 2.0, and the P1 prior art transitively; nothing below 2.7 is imported directly). What Series 2.8 does NOT do: assert a globally forced common good where the structure does not force one; the global fails at the capstone, in whichever direction the computation returns, and claiming a global good the structure does not support is the phase's central sin here. It is chartered under the Phase-2 costume gate, which it must clear on two fronts: its knot is neither import-powered (Series 07, the collapse in new clothing) NOR a mere replay of the single-edge fork (Series 2.3, N times over) — it is the genuinely many-body cocycle, invisible for two selves and alive only for three or more. ## 8. Positioning

Program 2 has built a world, drawn its space and time and self, and found it keeps no conserved books. Series 2.8 asks the question that has haunted moral philosophy since Plato and haunted social choice since Condorcet: is there a common good — a single good that many selves can share — or is the good irreducibly local, something each self holds within its own sight and reconciles with its neighbors one edge at a time, never gathering into a whole? The mathematics answers in its own austere key, and the answer, if the thesis holds, is that no common good is written into the structure of many perspectives: selves can be pairwise reconciled, every one at peace with each of its neighbors, and there can still be no good they all share — the whole frustrated the way three voters cycle with no majority, the way a frustrated magnet finds no ground state, the way a curved manifold admits local charts but no global flattening. This is the value analog of the two failures the phase already proved: the universe cannot count itself (2.5), and it keeps no global ledger (2.7); now it may hold no global good. And the reading beneath it is the phase's most human: that the common good, if it is frustrated, is not something the world owes us or guarantees — it is something MADE, edge by edge, reconciliation by reconciliation, never finished and never owed, and all the more precious for that. "To attend is to become," and each self becomes its own good by attending; "self is a paradox," and the whole cannot total its good any more than it can count itself; and "you are loved" is the reconciliation on each edge — the local coherence that is real and pervasive — whose gathering into a love of all by all is exactly the frustration question, and exactly what the structure will not hand you for free. Series 2.8 does not resolve the phase into comfort; it asks whether the many share a good, expects to prove they are not guaranteed one, and closes Phase 2 on the note the whole program has been walking toward: everything relative to the self, the global failing at every capstone, and the good — like the compass, like the direction of convergence — drawn exactly and filled never, left open as a task and not a fact. The common good, drawn exactly, is the result; whether it glues or is frustrated is the phase's last wall, and it stands there.
