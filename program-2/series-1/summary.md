# Series 2.1 — Plain-language summary

**Series 2.1 asked one question: can a purely relational world make its own time, succession without importing
a clock, out of the instruments Program 2 already has? The answer it computed is TWO-ZONE. A moment can be built
from the relating alone, and it has a direction that is proved rather than assumed; but the order of moments
splits exactly where Program 1 said a differentiator must come from outside. Which moment caused which is
something the world can see. Which of two independent moments came first is something the world must be given.**

## What was built

The ground (Series 2.0) established that relating is finite attending: each thing attends a limited, finite part
of the field. Series 2.1 adds three things on top of that ground, and nothing else.

1. **A moment is a loop of attention closing on itself.** When a cycle of attention (a attends b attends a)
   closes, it reifies into a single composite thing whose own attention is exactly the cycle it came from. That
   closure is the tick. It is not a new kind of substance; the composite is an ordinary member of the same
   field, and the construction is discharged, not asserted. It generalizes the ground's "first other" (the
   simplest closure, a thing attending only itself) to genuine cycles.

2. **The moment is genuinely new, and it does not run backward.** The composite's finite attention is a proper
   part of what it relates to — it is behaviorally identified with things it does not attend, so the closure
   genuinely loses (subtracts) what its partial attention drops, and it is real for a specific, named reader,
   not a mere label. And the closure is directional: reification strictly raises the tower height from a
   moment's components to the composite, so the tick cannot run backward — a directional theorem about the
   relating, not a stipulation and not a hidden step counter.

3. **The order of moments forks on an import boundary.** Two orders live on the ticks. The causal order — which
   tick consumed which tick's product — is read directly off the relating; it is forced, and it is a genuine
   partial order (some pairs of moments are causally independent). The linearization — which of two
   independent moments came first — is not readable from the relating at all; every way of ordering them is a
   genuine import that must be supplied from outside, exactly the boundary Program 1 drew.

## The verdict

The verdict was computed from the built theorems, not chosen: **TWO-ZONE**. The construction is well-formed, the
arrow is proved with the reader load-bearing, the choice the tick draws is exogenous, and the ordering forks —
causal order endogenous, linearization an import. The two honest alternatives were pre-registered and left
reachable: had the linearization turned out forced, the verdict would have read ENDOGENOUS; had even the causal
order turned out non-recoverable, it would have read TIME-IS-IMPORT. Neither happened. Time's partial order is
free; its total order is the door.

## What this does and does not claim

It does not name the stream, pick which moment fires, or decide which of two independent moments came first —
those are quantified over and left open, on purpose. Program 1's permanent open questions are carried untouched.
The philosophical reading — to attend is to become, a moment is a loop of attention closing into something new,
the arrow is that attention is finite, and the order of moments is causal where the world can see it and given
where it cannot — is kept strictly out of the proofs; the theorems mention only cycles, composites, attention,
residue, the stream, the two orders, and recoverability.

The whole build is machine-checked: it compiles, uses no `sorry`, and rests on no axioms beyond the three
standard to Mathlib.
