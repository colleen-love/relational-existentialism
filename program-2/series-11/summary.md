# Series 2.11 — The Phase (plain-language summary)

**Verdict: INTERFERING (scoped: real, signed `±1` interference; the complex phase is a disclosed forward-note).**

## The question

The strangest fact about quantum mechanics is that possibilities can cancel. In a classical world, two ways of reaching an outcome only ever make it more likely — probabilities add. In the quantum world, two ways can cancel, and the outcome both paths could produce becomes impossible: the dark fringes of the double slit, where light plus light makes darkness. Everything this program built before Series 2.8 lived in the classical world — distinction only ever accumulated, the rank only rose, nothing subtracted. Series 2.8, asking whether many selves share a good, built without meaning to the first thing in the whole edifice that can cancel: a signed increment and a holonomy that need not close. Series 2.11 asks the decisive question of the recovery test: **can distinction cancel into genuine interference?** Can two contributions to one outcome subtract, so the combined weight falls below either alone?

## What was built

One thing, read off Series 2.8's holonomy and nothing else: a **sign**. The sign of a path is the parity of the increment accumulated along it — `+1` if even, `-1` if odd — written with no complex number, no bolted-on phase. Within one world, two paths return to the starting self: the **direct path** (do nothing; accumulated increment `0`; sign `+1`) and the **loop path** (go around the ring; accumulated increment equal to Series 2.8's holonomy; sign the parity of that holonomy). The **weight** of a combined contribution is its squared modulus.

## What was found

- **The sign is genuinely signed.** On the frustrated ring (Series 2.8's `hol = 3`, odd) the loop sign is `-1`; on the gluable star (`hol = 0`, even) it is `+1`. There is a real sign to cancel.
- **It cancels.** In the frustrated world the direct path (`+1`) and the loop path (`-1`) sum to zero — the first genuine subtraction in the program's dynamics, carried up from Series 2.8's antisymmetry.
- **The cancellation is genuine destructive interference, not bookkeeping.** In the frustrated world the combined weight is `(1 + (-1))² = 0`, strictly below the sum of the parts `1² + (-1)² = 2`. Two ways of arriving, combined, make the arriving *less* likely than either alone — an inequality no classical mixture can satisfy. And this happens exactly when the signs cancel, which happens exactly when the holonomy is odd: the same Series 2.8 fork (frustrated vs gluable) that decided whether a common good exists now decides whether two paths interfere.
- **The sign is earned, not smuggled.** The sign is a function of the built holonomy, definitionally — no complex number was added to produce the cancellation. This was the sharpest no-smuggling test the program has posed, and the amplitude passes it.
- **The scope is honest.** The amplitude earned is a real `±1` sign. That is genuine destructive interference — strictly more than any classical probability can do — but it is not yet the full complex phase of quantum mechanics. The complex `U(1)` phase and the Born rule (Series 2.12) are the disclosed gap: this series opens the door, it does not walk through it.

Before Series 2.8, becoming could only add; here we asked whether it can subtract, and the answer is yes. As built, this world already spoke relativity (2.9 CONE) and was found irreversible (2.10 IRREVERSIBLE); now it can also interfere. What remains beyond this real interference — the complex phase, the rule of chance — is the next line of the specification.
