# Relational Existentialism, Program Review 2-1 (adversarial, Program 2 in full: P1 foundation and Series 2.0–2.13)

**Scope.** The whole built arc, read against its own bars: `program-2/formal/P1/` and all fourteen series' `formal/` sources in full (every `ws1..ws5` and `AxiomCheck`, plus `P2S7/ConservedRelativeAttempt.lean`), the program charter / protocol / charter-status, both phase charter extensions, every series charter, and the README and summaries the code is glossed by. The review is graded by the house scale (SERIOUS / REAL / COSMETIC) and held to the program's own honesty invariants (protocol §0), the series charters' strip tests and false-escape clauses, and the two Program 1 traps (PR1-S1, PR1-S2) the P1 foundation excludes by guardrail.

**Method and verification note (disclosed).** Unlike Program Review 1 (which could not recompile), this review rebuilt the full suite independently: `lake build` green over all eighteen targets, every `AxiomCheck` on Mathlib's standard three or fewer (the WS5 verdict theorems on no axioms), `scripts/gate.sh` green on all eighteen closure lines, sorry-free, no `native_decide`, no custom `axiom`, no `unsafe`/`partial` declarations. `P2S7/ConservedRelativeAttempt.lean` — which `lake build` never touches — was compiled independently for this review and is clean. The adversarial read was performed as eight independent skeptical passes over disjoint slices of the corpus (P1+S0, S1+S2, S3+S4, S5+S6, S7+S8, S9+S10, S11+S12, S13+docs), each pass blind to the others' findings and instructed to judge the Lean statements alone against the charters, with the interpretive prose treated as a claim to be checked rather than a guide. Every finding below cites the actual statement at file:line; the load-bearing falsity claims (the S9 cone/reachability divergence, the S10 periodicity, the total-bisimilarity degeneracy, the S13 faithfulness gap, the S3/S4 definitional collapses) were re-derived by hand from the sources before grading. No finding depends on a compilation question: the mechanical layer is impeccable, and everything below is about what the statements say versus what the artifacts claim they say.

**The one-sentence summary.** The mechanical claims all verify; the interpretive claims largely do not: the program's verdicts are carried by a small number of recurring structural defects — a degenerate bisimilarity that makes every "import" the same one-line fact and closes the fork the wall claims to draw, hand-set boolean literals behind every "computed" verdict, hand-painted lookup-table witnesses behind every "earned" structure, and Phase-3 "recovery" verdicts fixed by definition choice rather than proven by theorems — so that on the formal record most series land at their charters' own pre-registered PARTIAL/degenerate criterion rather than at the announced verdicts.

## Cross-cutting SERIOUS findings

### PR2-S1 (SERIOUS) — The collapse engine is the total relation on every witness carrier: every import payoff in the program is one generic fact, the recoverable side of the wall is uninhabitable, and the Import Theorem boundary the program claims to draw has only one side. Owner: the witness discipline, program-wide (S0–S7 directly; everything routed through `Recoverable` downstream.)

**The defect, mechanically.** `hneRel dest x y := SHNE dest x ∧ SHNE dest y` (`P1/Core.lean:90`) is a plain bisimulation on every coalgebra (`hneRel_isBisim`, `P1/Core.lean:95`), and `ws1_atomless_bisim` (`P1/Core.lean:105`) discharges every plain-bisimilarity obligation in the program with it. Every Program 2 witness carrier is all-SHNE by construction (`ws1_tcar_SHNE`, `ws1_rcar_SHNE`, `SHNE_M`, …), so on every witness **plain bisimilarity is the total relation**: any state is "in-sight-identical" to any other, and the conjunct carries zero information about the specific attention graph the docstrings emphasize. Two consequences, each fatal to a family of headlines:

1. **Every `AttentionDistinguishes` / `¬ Recoverable` payoff reduces to "the hand-assigned labels differ."** Since every pair is plain-bisimilar and a `rankLift` broadcasts its source's label on every edge, `AttentionDistinguishes (rankLift dest lab) x y` is extensionally `lab x ≠ lab y`, and `¬ Recoverable` holds for every non-constant labeling. The following are therefore instances of one two-line lemma (the mechanism of `rankM_sep_general`, `P2S7/ws1.lean:104`, itself sound): S0's `ws1_first_other`, `ws3_direction_not_recoverable`, `ws3_active_passive_distinct`, `ws4_import_breaks_baseline`; S1's `ws3_stream_exogenous` and `ws4_linearization_import` (which are the *same proposition* up to renaming — compare `P2S1/ws3.lean:37` with `P2S1/ws4.lean:55`); S2's `ws2_other_non_recoverable`, `ws3_facing_asymmetric`; S3's `ws3_dissent_is_import`; S4's `ws3_lateral_is_import`; S6's `ws1_strict_fails`, `ws2_cont_is_import`, `ws3_line_is_import`; S7's `ws3_change_is_source`. The program presents these as fourteen distinct walls; formally they are one wall, instanced by painting different numbers on different nodes.

2. **The fork the wall draws was never open.** On any witness built by the house template, *every* distinction whatsoever fails `Recoverable` (the only recoverable lifts are constant — exactly S6's `mergeLift`). So the "recoverable side" of the Import Theorem boundary is uninhabitable except by triviality, and the framework's own test cannot discriminate "endogenous" from "imported": had S1 applied its import test to a label derived from its *causal order* (the structure it calls endogenous), that label would come out an import too. The claim that the causal order sits on the recoverable side of the boundary is never stated, and on these carriers is arguably false under the program's own criterion. This is the program-scope recurrence of the PR1-S1 shape: a fork whose announced openness the class cannot express — here not because the constraining theory is empty but because the quotient is total.

**Why this meets the bar.** Protocol §0.4 makes the PR1-S1/PR1-S2 catches the program's central watch-point, and every series charter carries a strip test whose stated failure mode is "the payoff strips to a bare `Recoverable` fact." Stripped, the payoffs above are not merely bare `Recoverable` facts — they are the *same* bare `Recoverable` fact. The Tier-1 landing reviews ran the strip test per-series and accepted each instance as its series' local content; the degeneracy is only visible program-wide, which is exactly what a program review is for.

**Correction owed (the binary).** Build at least one witness family whose plain behavior is *not* fully collapsed (states with genuinely distinct plain observational behavior — leaves, differing branching, so `hneRel` is not total and label-free distinctions exist), prove the key payoffs there, and exhibit at least one non-constant *recoverable* structure so both sides of the boundary are inhabited and the import test discriminates. **OR** re-scope every import payoff honestly as "on a fully-collapsed carrier, any non-constant labeling is non-recoverable," record that the wall as built is one-sided, and re-grade the affected series verdicts under their own PARTIAL/degenerate clauses.

### PR2-S2 (SERIOUS) — Every WS5 verdict is `rfl` on hand-typed boolean literals; "computed, never hand-set" is false as Lean content in all fourteen series, and in at least four series the pre-registered alternative outcomes are unreachable in principle. Owner: WS5, program-wide.

**The defect, mechanically.** The pattern is identical in every series (e.g. `P2S0/ws5.lean:49`, `P2S3/ws5.lean:58`, `P2S13/ws5.lean:65`): `theorem ws5_verdict_eq : verdict true … false = Outcome.X := rfl`, where the arguments are **literals typed at the call site**. A separate `ws5_flags_justified` proves an adjacent conjunction of propositions, but no `Decidable` instance, no reflection, no defined boolean constant connects proposition *i* to literal *i*: the flag-to-theorem correspondence lives entirely in the docstring. What is machine-checked is that an honest if-chain applied to author-chosen constants evaluates as expected; the claim broadcast by every WS5 header — "the verdict is COMPUTED from the flags (never hand-set)" — is precisely what is *not* machine-checked. The `ws5_verdict_discriminates` theorems evaluate the same lookup table at other literals; that a hand-written 5-branch function reaches its 5 outcomes on 5 hand-picked rows adds no falsifiability.

**The sharper half: unreachable alternatives.** In several series the alternative outcomes are not merely un-triggered but *inexpressible or impossible*, which is the PR1-S1 tautology in verdict clothing:

- **S0:** the charter defines PARTIAL as "any obligation lands only per-instance or degenerate" — the condition the build actually satisfies (every asymmetry obligation lands on a 2–3 element hand-built witness) — but `verdict` (`P2S0/ws5.lean:44`) returns `partial'` *only* when `imp = false`. The judgment that would have produced the charter's own PARTIAL has no input through which to flip the outcome.
- **S1/S2:** the TOTALIZED arm corresponds to the negation of `ws1_no_self_total_hold` (`P1/Core.lean:265`), a Cantor diagonal true for *every* inspection on *every* coalgebra — TOTALIZED can never occur on any structure this formalization can express. The `Recoverable`-side arms are unreachable on any all-SHNE witness (PR2-S1).
- **S12:** a world whose true measure were `|amp|¹` or `amp⁴` earns the identical flag vector (see PR2-S4), so the verdict cannot distinguish BORN from STOCHASTIC-NOT-BORN; the pole the charter pre-registers is unreachable not because the world is Born but because the flags cannot express the difference.
- **S13:** `couplingEarned` is justified by a conjunct that is `rfl` (`ws3_no_by_hand_coupling.1`) and one that a purpose-built foil cannot fail; the `partial'` guard branch is dead code for the object actually under verdict.

**Correction owed (the binary).** Mechanize the flags: define each as a `Bool` computed from the structures (`decide`-able where the carriers are finite — they all are), prove `flagᵢ = true` from the WS theorems, and state `ws5_verdict_eq` as `verdict flag₁ … flagₙ = Outcome.X`, so the verdict is a function of the mathematics rather than of the author. **OR** strike "computed, never hand-set" from every WS5 header, charter, ledger entry, and README sentence, and disclose the flag-to-theorem link as editorial. Separately, for each series whose alternatives are unreachable in principle, either make the alternative expressible or re-grade under §0.2a.

### PR2-S3 (SERIOUS) — The load-bearing structure is painted, not derived: ranks, gradings, tick-hood, and reification "sections" are hand-written lookup tables whose advertised properties were put in by the author and read back out by `decide`, and every "earned by `rfl`" theorem is a definition unfolding. Owner: WS1 witness construction, program-wide.

**The defect, mechanically.** No rank function in the program is derived from the reification-tower machinery P1 provides (`towerN`/`prec`/`finTowerN` are built and used by no witness theorem): `rankU` (`P2S0/ws1.lean:148`), `rankT` (`P2S1/ws1.lean:54`), `rankR`, `rankW`/`latW` (`P2S4/ws1.lean:31-37`), `rankM` (`P2S7/ws1.lean:49`) are bare if-chains, and the "tick raises the measure" / "causal steps raise rank" / "axes cross" payoffs are `decide` on the same tables that were written to make them true. Tick-hood itself is an enumeration (`isTick x := x = kA ∨ x = kB ∨ x = kC`, `P2S1/ws4.lean:32`), not a property derived from `attendsT` or `reifyT` — and being non-recoverable by the program's own test (PR2-S1), the tick/non-tick distinction is itself an import on the series' own criterion. Total reification sections (`FinReify`) are unsatisfiable on every finite carrier (cardinality: `2^n > n`), so every "reification" in the program is a pointwise pair of equations (`attends (reify {x}) = {x}`) baked in by co-designing the two if-chains — S0's extension target "Reify is a real section on this carrier" is unsatisfiable on the chosen witness class, yet its finding closed "(Fixed)". The sharpest single instance is S4's REDUCED pole: `rankT` and `latT` (`P2S4/ws4.lean:36,40`) are *syntactically identical bodies*, so `ws4_reduced_reachable` — the theorem certifying "the collapse zone is a real structure, not a flag flip" — is `f = f` for one definition written twice; the fork's second zone is fiat in the exact sense the audit clause denies. Likewise every Phase-3 "earned" certificate (`ws3_amp_earned`, `ws3_sq_earned`, `ws3_earned_from_attention.1`, `ws5_audit_metric_is_built.1`) is a chain of `rfl`s: it certifies that definitions unfold to themselves, a test any definition passes, including the smuggled ones it is advertised to exclude.

**Why this meets the bar.** The charters demand the structure be "discharged, not posited"; the discharge order is inverted (posited, then `decide`d). Hand-built witnesses are legitimate as *consistency* certificates, and the program is entitled to them; what is not legitimate is the program-wide gloss that the properties are *found* in the structures ("the arrow is a genuine `Q`-fact, not a definitional artifact"; "a real structure, not a flag flip"; "earned, not smuggled") when each is put in by hand at definition time. A witness that cannot fail is not evidence, and none of these witnesses can fail.

**Correction owed (the binary).** For each series' central quantity, either derive it from the machinery that names it (rank from the tower; tick-hood from reification; the REDUCED pole's second grading from an independent definition — path-length on `T` — proved to coincide) **OR** relabel the witnesses as consistency models throughout and remove the "not a definitional artifact / not by fiat / earned" claims that the formal record does not support.

### PR2-S4 (SERIOUS) — The Phase-3 RECOVERED verdicts (CONE, INTERFERING, BORN) and the capstone INERT are fixed by definition choice and naming, not established by theorems; in each case the one theorem that would carry the physics is absent, and in one case the docstring claim is false in the model. Owner: Series 2.9, 2.11, 2.12, 2.13.

**S9 (CONE).** The series contains no theorem connecting `ball` to reachability in either direction. `ws2_cone` (`P2S9/ws2.lean:23`) unfolds the definition of a filter — a ball of radius `rate·depth` contains the points within `rate·depth`. The charter's WS2 target ("the cone is exactly the rate-bounded reachable set") is undelivered, and the docstring's reading "within is reachable, beyond is causally disconnected" (`ws2.lean:22`) is **false in the model**: `dist` is symmetric while the attention is one-directional, so `p0 ∈ ball attSlow p1 1` while `reachSet attSlow p1 = {1,2,3,4} ∌ p0` (re-derived by hand for this review). `ws1_rate_bounded` is `Finset.le_sup` twice — true for any function on any finite carrier, so the charter's pre-registered obstruction ("attention imposes no bound") is inexpressible and the NO-CONE fork arm is dead. No tick, becoming, or conversion exists anywhere in the series; "light cone recovered" is carried entirely by the words `rate`, `ball`, `depth`. What survives is real but modest: `ws3_rate_is_content` genuinely separates the rate from *saturated* transitive reachability — though the depth-indexed one-step order (which differs between `attSlow`/`attFast`) recovers the rate trivially, so the separation holds only against a deliberately coarsened order.

**S11 (INTERFERING).** The sign is chosen, not earned: `amp n := if n % 2 = 0 then 1 else -1` (`P2S11/ws1.lean:47`) is one function among an unconstrained space — the constant `amp' n = 1` is equally "a function of the built holonomy" and passes the identical `rfl`-shaped earned test while producing ADDITIVE-ONLY; nothing states the theorem (parity as the unique nontrivial sign character on ℤ) that would force it. There is no path type, no outcome type, no statement the two "arms" co-terminate; `directAmp` is the literal `amp 0`. Stripped, `ws3_destructive` is `(1 + (−1))² = 0 < 2`, and the advertised attention-level equivalence ("destructive exactly on odd holonomy") is claimed in a docstring but not a theorem — `ws3_destructive_iff` quantifies over ±1 integer pairs, a four-row truth table. INTERFERING is an artifact of selecting an interference-producing definition.

**S12 (BORN).** `respectsCancel μ := ∀ att, directAmp + loopAmp att = 0 → μ att = 0` (`P2S12/ws3.lean:44`) avoids the square but not the amplitude calculus: demanding weights vanish where *signed amplitudes sum to zero* is the superposition principle itself, so the classical measure's "failure" is the definition of classicality, not a refutation by the built structure. "FORCED" quantifies over a two-element candidate class, and the test provably does not force the square: `μ att := |directAmp + loopAmp att|`, or the fourth power, or any function vanishing at the cancellation points, satisfies every flag the verdict consumes — the exponent 2, which is the entire content of "Born" beyond "some cancellation-respecting weight," is exactly what is not forced. The in-code disclosure (`ws3.lean:82`: "the consistent one of the TWO candidate measures, not the unique one among all `|·|^p`") is honest and is fatal to the verdict word; no normalization, additivity, outcome, or sampling structure exists, so "the imports carry a measure" has no formal content (`ws1_measure_nontrivial` proves `0 ≠ 4`). The DETERMINISTIC pole is witnessed by "a constant function is constant" (`ws4_deterministic_reachable`, `P2S12/ws4.lean:60`).

**S13 (INERT).** The "imported distance" is not imported: no theorem connects `pathDist` (`P2S13/ws1.lean:65`) to Series 2.4's `stepsFrom` (`P2S4/ws3.lean:69`) — which is not even parametric in an adjacency (its `attendsW` is fixed inside the definition, so 2.4 never stated "the metric is a function of the adjacency," the property S13's whole test rides on), and which diverges extensionally where comparable (`stepsFrom` gives `Nat.sInf ∅ = 0` on unreachable pairs; `pathDist` gives the sentinel 4 — undisclosed). `adjDist c := pathDist c.1` is grain-blind **by definition**, so `ws3_grain_test` is the lemma that a function of the first projection is invariant in the second — a test that could not have come out GRAVITATIONAL for any witnesses whatsoever. `Config := Adj × Grain` postulates the very independence of the two axes that INERT then announces (in the imported model the grain `rankM` is a fixed function of the same entity structure as the attention; whether the imported model even contains two states with equal adjacency and different rank is never posed). The foil establishes only that `Config` has a second projection. The genuinely honest core — 2.4 built distance and rank as separate axes, so their coupling is absent — was already 2.4's theorem; 2.13 adds a restatement inside a fresh product type. The in-code "structural" disclosures are partial honesty; the framing (a pre-registered two-sided test, "de-risked on paper," a verdict "computed") presents a projection as an experiment.

**Why this meets the bar.** Charter Extension 2's no-smuggling gate says the recovered feature must be "earned from the built primitives, never assumed." In all four series the deciding structure is assumed at a definition (`ball`, `amp`, `combinedWeight`'s square, `adjDist`'s projection), the "earned" certificates are `rfl`, and the theorem that would discharge the gate non-vacuously (ball-bounds-reachability; parity forced; square forced over a class; faithfulness of the re-seat) is absent in each case. The rigor-over-desire discipline is asymmetric here: the two NOT-RECOVERED verdicts got the adversarial scrutiny (T1-S1, the S10 laziness check), while the three RECOVERED verdicts — the desired ones — rest on the thinnest formal content in the program.

**Correction owed (the binary), per series.** S9: prove `reachN att n {x} ⊆ ball att x n` (and exhibit the failure of the converse as the cone's slack), or re-scope CONE to "a rate-indexed ball family separating two carriers with equal saturated reachability." S11: state and prove the sign-forcing theorem, or re-scope INTERFERING to "a chosen parity sign produces sub-additive combination on odd holonomy." S12: prove a forcing theorem over a declared measure class (even `{|·|^p}`), or rename the verdict to the disclosure (a cancellation-respecting non-classical weight exists; the square is consistent, not forced). S13: prove the faithfulness bridge to a re-parametrized 2.4 distance (or import it literally), and pose the grain-dependence question on the imported model's own states; or re-scope INERT to "2.4's separation of axes, restated." In each case the OR-branch is honorable under §0.2a; the current middles are not.

### PR2-S5 (SERIOUS) — The two capstone NOT-RECOVERED verdicts misdescribe their own models: MONOTONE-ONLY names a monotonicity the built tick does not have (it is periodic, and lowers the measure at `e2`), and the "no conserved ledger" heart is the same total-collapse degeneracy that was rejected as a costume when it pointed the other way. Owner: Series 2.7, with 2.10's prose downstream.

**The defect, mechanically.** (i) `ws2_tick_raises` (`P2S7/ws2.lean:36`) states the rise at exactly the two table rows built to rise (`e0`, `e1`). At the other rows the same tick *falls*: `reifyM {e2}` takes the default branch to `e0`, so `rankM (tick e2) = 0 < 2 = rankM e2`, and the built dynamics is the periodic `e0 → e1 → e2 → e0` with the measure returning to 0 every three ticks — made explicit by Series 2.10's own carrier lemmas (`P2S10/ws1.lean:48-51`; re-derived by hand). The formal statements are carefully local; the verdict name MONOTONE-ONLY, the WS5 header ("the universe has an arrow and keeps no conserved ledger"), and 2.10's gloss ("the arrow is fundamental," `P2S10/ws4.lean:42`) assert a global arrow the model refutes. A recurrent dynamics whose observable returns to its initial value every three steps is a strange witness for fundamental irreversibility; what `ws4_no_core_built` actually proves (genuinely, exhaustively) is that the 3-cycle's rank-reading `0,1,2` is not *constant* — a rank-mixing fact, not an arrow. (ii) The "only conserved measures are trivial" clause (`ws3_not_conserved.2`, `P2S7/ws3.lean:39`) quantifies over plain-bisimulation-invariant `f` — but by PR2-S1 plain bisimilarity is *total* on `MCar`, so plain-invariance forces constancy for reasons independent of ticks, reification, or the measure: nothing could be conserved except constants, on any all-SHNE carrier, under any dynamics. This is the same degeneracy T1-S1 rejected when it manufactured conservation ("in-sight the atomless carrier is one class, so the bisimilarity holds for any measure"); re-signed, it now manufactures non-conservation. (iii) "Conservation-from-within is impossible" (`ws4_no_conserved_side`) conjoins a table fact (`1 ≠ 0` by `decide`) with `ws2_residue_free` — a P1 universal true for every inspection on every coalgebra, hence certifying nothing about conservation, measures, or this carrier; the connective tissue ("so a fork where the diagonal decides Q lands on creation") is prose. The on-record attempt file is real and compiles, and its requirement-1/requirement-2 structure is a genuine improvement; but requirement 2's refutation is the same structure-independent lemma.

**Correction owed (the binary).** Prove the monotonicity the verdict names for a tick that has it (or on the sub-dynamics where it holds, with the periodicity disclosed as the model's actual shape), and restate the conservation impossibility over a class the degeneracy does not decide (a carrier where plain-invariance does not force constancy — the same repair as PR2-S1). **OR** rename and re-scope: the honest verdict on the formal record is "RANK-MIXING on a periodic tick, with in-sight conservation degenerate," and the 2.10 gloss follows it down.

## Cross-cutting REAL findings

### PR2-R1 (REAL) — Structure-independent universals are counted as series content. 

`ws1_no_self_total_hold` (Cantor/Lawvere, true for every inspection on every coalgebra) is a conjunct of S2's `ws3_facing_partial` ("the facing is partial") and S5's fold; `ws2_residue_free` (same universality class) carries S7's WS4 (see PR2-S5). The universals themselves are sound and belong in P1; the defect is the accounting.

### PR2-R2 (REAL) — The audit layer is substantially vacuous, and the S13 SERIOUS is standing in ten accepted series.

`theorem ws5_audit_names_not_terms : True := trivial` appears in S1, S2, S3, S4, S5, S6, S7, S8, S9, S10, S11, S12 (twelve files; S2 and S3 carry a second `True` audit each: `ws5_audit_downstream_open`, `ws5_audit_direction_open`). Separately, the names-not-terms discipline is passed by tokenization where it matters: S2's charter forbids naming a definition "self" or "other" as content and the definitions are `slf`, `oth`, `slfReader` (`P2S2/ws1.lean:38-39`, `ws2.lean:55`); S11 defends `amp`/`directAmp`/`loopAmp` as not the whole word "amplitude" (`P2S11/ws1.lean:45`). ### PR2-R4 (REAL) — The re-seat practice bypasses the layered chain exactly where the chain matters.

The program's stated soundness argument is that importing a *built, verified* layer cannot introduce a gap. S9 re-seats 2.4's metric as a fresh symmetric `Nat.dist` on a fresh carrier (contradicting the directedness and self-relativity 2.4 proved as its content, and grounding a "same causal order" claim in fresh reachability definitions); S13 re-seats 2.4's distance and 2.7's grain as fresh objects with no bridging theorem (PR2-S4), citing S9 as precedent. A re-seat with no theorem connecting it to the built object is a fresh gadget wearing the import's name; each Phase-3 series that "reuses" a built result this way in fact re-proves nothing and inherits nothing, and the chain's guarantee does not cover it.

### PR2-R5 (REAL) — Assorted verified defects in the built objects, series-local but load-bearing.

- **S4:** `stepsFrom x y = sInf {n | reachIn n x y}` gives distance **0** to *unreachable* pairs (`sInf ∅ = 0`), conflating "unreachable" with "here"; the metric theorems silently restrict to the reachable 3 of 4 nodes.
