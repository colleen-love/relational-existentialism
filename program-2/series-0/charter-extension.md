# Program 2 Series 0 (2.0), Charter Extension 1

**A clarification of the fixed charter, not an edit of it. The charter (`charter.md`) is the fixed bar and stays as written. This extension RAISES the bar on two obligations the first build met only to the letter, never lowers it. It is triggered by an independent post-exit review that re-graded two disclosed findings (C1-S1, C3-S1) from COSMETIC to REAL: the reification the ground promised is present but INERT (discharged on an isolated counting carrier, participating in nothing), and the asymmetry of knowing is a bare conjunction whose passive conjunct does no work. Both trace to one root: reification (WS1) and the asymmetry (WS3) were built on SEPARATE witnesses that touch nothing in common, so neither is load-bearing. This extension requires them rebuilt on ONE coherent witness, where reification CREATES the first other and the asymmetry is the self against that other. The verdict may stay GROUND-ESTABLISHED only if it now rests on the stronger targets below. Everything the first build got right (the inherited collapse, direction non-recoverability, no cardinal ceiling, the quantified import, the discriminating verdict) stands unchanged and must be preserved.**

*This extension is honest under protocol §0.2a because it only strengthens: it cannot launder a shortfall, because it makes the requirement harder to meet, not easier. The findings it addresses close as (Fixed) — the originally intended target, built at its proper strength — not (Relabeled). Read it alongside `charter.md`; where they touch, the charter's intent governs and this extension says what discharging that intent requires.*

## 1. The findings this extension addresses

- **C1-S1 (re-graded COSMETIC → REAL).** `ws1_reification_exists` is discharged by a cardinality bijection `Finset ℕ ≃ ℕ` on the carrier `ℕ`, which no other theorem in the series uses. It proves `FinReify` is inhabited, but reification creates nothing that participates in the asymmetry, the collapse, or the import. "To relate is to create" is present as an existence lemma and is inert. The charter's WS1 intent, that reification "has a home" so the tick can build on it, is not discharged: the reification the dynamics needs is not de-risked, only a disconnected witness of the predicate.

- **C3-S1 (re-graded COSMETIC → REAL).** `ws3_passive_constitution` and `ws3_active_passive_distinct` are bare conjunctions: the passivity fact (`attendsD b = ∅`) and the distinction (`AttentionDistinguishes a b`) are AND-ed with no implication between them. What is genuinely proved is direction non-recoverability (`ws3_direction_not_recoverable`, which stands); the specific claim that PASSIVE constitution is load-bearing, and that active and passive are two becomings, is prose over a conjunction. The charter's audit (b) intent, that the asymmetry is genuinely load-bearing, is met only by the weaker direction-non-recoverability, not by the passive/active reading it advertises.

C4-S1 and C5-S1 stay COSMETIC and require no change (the `id` non-vacuity witness and the flag-encoding convention are the standard disclosed patterns).

## 2. The root cause

Reification (WS1) was built on `ℕ`; the asymmetry (WS3) on `Bool`. They share nothing, so reification is inert and the asymmetry has no created object to be about. The fix is not to patch each in isolation but to put them on ONE witness, because they are one story: **reifying the self-relation is what produces the first other, and the first other is what the asymmetry is between.** (User's framing, adopted as the design intent: self's relation with itself IS finite attention; reifying it produces the first other; the same operation reifies self-to-other relations in turn, which is how the many proliferates.)

## 3. The clarified requirements

**R1 — Reification must CREATE, on a witness the series uses (fixes C1).** The reification operation (`reify : Finset X → X`, `FinReify attends reify`) must be exhibited on a witness carrier that also carries the WS3 asymmetry (R2), where `reify` genuinely produces a relatum that participates. The elemental instance is the SELF-RELATION reified: the self-loop (a relatum attending itself, or its self-inspection) reified one level up into a new relatum, the FIRST OTHER. Name the target `ws1_first_other` (or equivalent): reifying the self-relation yields a relatum that is NON-RECOVERABLE from the self via the plain (symmetric) relating — the self and the first other are plain-bisimilar yet separated at the labelled/rank level. Draw the separation from the P1 prior art already imported: `P1.Reader.ws2_many_general` (a reified relatum ranks above its constituent, so it is plain-bisimilar to a base relatum yet label-separated) and `P1.Reader.ws2_attention_makes_real` (the separated relatum is real for a finite attention). Reification is general (any attention pattern reifies); the self-relation is the elemental seed, and the theorem must exhibit that elemental case producing a genuine other.

**R2 — One witness for WS1 and WS3 (fixes the root cause).** WS1's reification and WS3's asymmetry must be stated over a SINGLE witness carrier containing the self and its reified first other. No separate reification and asymmetry witnesses. Reification creates the first other; the asymmetry is the self against that same first other.

**R3 — The asymmetry stated OVER the first other (fixes C3).** Restate the passive/active obligation so the reification/attention structure DRIVES the distinction rather than sitting beside it. On the unified witness, the directional knowing between the self and the reified first other (one attends the other; the other does not, or not symmetrically) is what the plain relating cannot recover, and the passivity is load-bearing IN the separation. The target must not be a bare conjunction: either state it as an implication (the passive/directed structure entails the label-separation) or as a single theorem whose separating content IS the directional/reification structure. `ws3_direction_not_recoverable` stays as the genuine core; the passive-constitution and active-passive theorems must be rebuilt so their passive/active content is load-bearing, not conjoined.

**R4 — The verdict flag must rest on the stronger target (fixes the verdict's dependency).** `ws5_flags_justified`'s reification flag must be justified by `ws1_first_other` (R1), not by the counting-bijection `ws1_reification_exists`. GROUND-ESTABLISHED must depend on the first other being genuine, or it is not earned.

## 4. What must NOT change (the discipline preserved)

- **The collapse stays inherited, never relitigated (audit d).** The first other is NOT an escape from the One. It is a MINTED distinction: self-reference manufacturing a genuine, non-recoverable import (Series 13's mint, proved consistent with Series 07). The first other is non-recoverable precisely BECAUSE it is a genuine distinction; it does not violate "no multiplicity without an import," it IS an import, self-manufactured. Do NOT let R1 become "multiplicity from relating alone" (that would contradict WS2 and Series 07). The separation must be non-recoverable, or the first other has collapsed back into the self.
- **The first other must be non-recoverable, not recoverable.** If the executor makes the self/first-other distinction recoverable from the symmetric relating, the distinction is no distinction (Series 07) and the target fails. Non-recoverability via the rank/tower separation is the load-bearing property. This is R1's teeth.
- **No cardinal ceiling (audit a).** Finite out-attention stays the sole ontological bound; `ws1_bound_is_finite_attention` stands.
- **Names-not-terms (audit e).** "The first other," "self," "other," "the mint" are interpretive gloss. No proof term, definition, or discharged obligation is named for them; the theorems name the reified relatum, its rank/tower position, and its `¬ Recoverable` / `AttentionDistinguishes` separation.
- **Direction non-recoverability, the quantified import, and the discriminating verdict all stand** (`ws3_direction_not_recoverable`, `ws4_import_breaks_baseline` / `ws4_import_quantified`, `ws5_verdict_*`). They were built correctly; do not disturb them beyond re-pointing the WS5 reification flag (R4).
