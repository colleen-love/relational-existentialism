This is an unusually self-critical corpus, which makes reviewing it interesting: most of the easy criticisms are already in the documents. So I'll focus on where the self-correction machinery itself fails, where "Discharged" labels rest on trivial or strawman formalizations, and where the mathematics quietly refutes the philosophy. (Caveat: I can't compile the Lean; I'm taking the sorry-free/axiom-free claims at face value and evaluating statements, definitions, and the ledger.)

## 1. The ledger is stale and internally inconsistent at corpus end

The charter's revision chain stops at REV-F, which records criteria (i)–(iv) and (vi) as Discharged. But WS9 and WS10 exist in the corpus and are never registered — there is no REV-G — and WS10 materially retracts two of those discharges:

- **Criterion (iv).** REV-E moved (iv) to Discharged partly on `ws3_weak_law_canonical`. WS10's own O3 note concedes that theorem is not canonicity: it proves uniqueness of the map satisfying `dest (f t) = pkJoin (…)`, which is unique *by construction* because `dest` is a bijection. It is `destEquiv`-injectivity dressed as a canonicity result. Uniqueness *among weak distributive laws* — the thing §6.1 actually pinned — is reopened in WS10 and never done. The charter still says (iv) is Discharged.
- **Criterion (i).** This is the big one, below.

A project whose entire epistemic brand is "the registered ledger matches the proved artifact" ends with a ledger that doesn't. The §8.2 discipline was applied rigorously *within* each revision and then abandoned between REV-F and WS9/WS10.

## 2. WS10's O2 refutes the founding commitment, and the charter never reckons with it

`ws10_unlabeled_atomless_collapses` is correct mathematics (the full relation on hereditarily-nonempty states is an Aczel–Mendler bisimulation because distinguishability in unlabeled pure sets bottoms out in ∅), and it is devastating: in the built carrier, the atomless sub-universe is the single point Ω. Meanwhile the carrier at large is *full* of relational atoms — `bottomState` has empty unfolding, and every state other than Ω is distinguished from Ω precisely by reachable emptiness.

So Commitment 1 ("no object is simple; every object decomposes without termination") holds for exactly one object, and making it hold for more than one provably collapses the universe — i.e., criteria (i) and (vii) are *jointly unsatisfiable* on the unlabeled carrier. Yet the charter has carried "(i) Discharged" since REV-A, on the vacuous reading "Ω exists and there are no urelements." The honest post-WS10 status is: (i) is Impossibility-proved-except-at-a-point, with the positive object deported to an unbuilt graded core (WS10-B). That is a fundamentally different outcome than the ledger reports, and it retroactively hollows out the REV-A claim that the bounded carrier "satisfies (i), (ii), (iii) at the carrier level."

## 3. The terminal carrier is structurally hostile to the philosophy, and this is never confronted

Three features of terminal coalgebras cut directly against the metaphysics, and the documents treat them as local inconveniences rather than as findings:

**Identity is downward-only.** On νP_κ, `x` is fully determined by `str x` — by what it unfolds *into*. What contains x plays no role whatsoever in fixing its identity; terminality guarantees this. So criterion (iv)'s "bidirectional constitution" as an *identity* thesis is impossible on this carrier by design. What is actually proved (`reflects_part`, `alg`) is that a composition operator exists whose output's observation includes the parts' observations — a fact about an algebra sitting on top of the carrier, not about objects being co-constituted from above. The gap between "an object is fixed jointly by container and contained" and "there is a union operator" is never priced.

**The carrier is rigid.** `endo_eq_id` means νP_κ has no nontrivial symmetries at all. That's why `PositionFree` is vacuous (WS6) and why "standpoints" had to be invented ad hoc. But rigidity has a philosophical reading the documents avoid: every object has one absolute, canonical individuation. The framework proves a semantic God's-eye view *exists* (the terminal coalgebra itself is the absolute totality of behaviors, existing as a set); the no-outside theorems only show no *member* of the carrier is that totality. "No everything" survives only as "the everything is not an element of itself" — a much weaker claim than §3.1/§3.7 aspire to.

**One-step scope.** `IsMaximal`, `ws6_no_global_observer`, and `ws10_standpoint_proper` all quantify over the *immediate* successor set. An object could hereditarily reach the whole carrier, or an observer could enumerate it in ω steps, without violating any of these theorems. "No maximal everything" and "no view from nowhere" are discharged only for depth-1 containment/observation. The Cantor-wall rhetoric of §3.7 concerns totalization, not one-step support.

## 4. Several "Discharged" criteria rest on trivialities or strawmen

- **(vi), positive half.** `ws6_substantive_standpoints` says: if two states have different successor sets, the membership predicates of those successor sets differ. A `Standpoint` is *defined* as the membership predicate of the successor set, so this is extensionality. WS10's O5 adds real content (views are < κ, carrier ≥ κ — good), but the REV-E claim that (vi) moved to Discharged was made on the tautological pair. Nothing sheaf-like ("local sections, no global section") is ever formalized.
- **`ws6_poles_split`.** The theorem is: a faithful functor cannot send a category with two distinct parallel arrows into all-terminal objects. Two lines of category theory, and its hypothesis ("*every* image object is a zero object") is a position nobody held. The actual §3.7 aspiration — exhibit the initial-terminal coincidence realized non-trivially *with the carrier* — is never attempted; the file itself notes pointed sets host the carrier faithfully, which suggests the interesting question was answerable and dodged.
- **Incompleteness.** `ws5_carrier_incomplete` is Cantor's theorem applied to an arbitrary set; the file admits it consumes no boundedness. That's marketed as robustness, but it also means the §3.6 story — *finite* attention is "exactly the non-surjectivity" that buys consistency — is not what's proved: the theorem holds equally for an unbounded, God's-eye observer. The κ-consuming content only arrives in WS10 O4(b), which after O1 is a one-line cardinality comparison.
- **WS8's Obligation A.** Real theorem, but about a lifting (`WQRel`) with no ⊗-coupling — i.e., the sup-based lifting ignores the quantale multiplication that made the grading "genuinely quantitative." Preservation for the lifting-that-forgot-the-weights is a definitional artifact; WS8 flags this honestly, but REV-E still spends it as "criterion (iv) Discharged for the bounded carrier," and the genuinely graded question (⊗-weighted lifting, step-16 transport) stays open.

## 5. The dynamics is generic mathematics, weakly coupled to everything else

The convergence suite never touches the coalgebra. Attention is a probability vector on a *fixed* support; nothing feeds back from attention into the object's structure or identity, so Commitment 5's ontological role ("feed the attended, starve the unattended" as constitution) is unmodeled — what's modeled is weight drift over a static relation set. Within that scope:

- `ws8_attention_converges` (identity selection) is an affine map contracting at rate 1−μ — the "legitimate replicator-mutator instance" is literally the member with no selection pressure.
- The linear and exponential bands hold for μ near 1, i.e., precisely where mutation swamps selection and the map is close to constant. Convergence-where-the-interesting-term-is-suppressed is thin evidence for "well-behaved feed/starve dynamics" (criterion v).
- WS9 then shows the charter's own dynamics family contains multistable and *non-settling* (period-2) members at moderate μ. REV-F's framing — "resolved by exhibiting provably-convergent attention" — is exactly the shortfall-relabeled-as-goal move that §8.2 forbids: the exhibited dynamics were selected to converge.
- The dynamics–carrier bridge exists only at κ₀ = ℵ₀ (WS10 O6), only per-state, and only under a nonexpansive-selection hypothesis.

Relatedly, §3.8 calls locating the Goldilocks band "the program's central technical burden." What's delivered is: the carrier has ≥ 2 points (one of which is an atom), no one-step maximal state, and μ-bands for hand-picked selections. The universal richness floor was *refuted* (`ws7_general_branching_false`), and O2 shows richness and atomlessness trade off fatally. The band, as a joint-satisfiability theorem, was never located; arguably the corpus's real result is that it's much narrower than hoped.

## 6. Fragmentation of the "single object"

§7 demands "a single object (or small family)." The deliverables live on two unrelated carriers — νP_κ (identity theory, weak law, standpoints, poles) and νW_Q (grading) — with the comparison (step-16 reduction) open, plus dynamics on abstract Fintypes. The Łₙ instantiation forces `Cardinal.{0}`, universe-fragmenting the "shared (F, κ) parameter" the §6.1 machinery exists to police; the #Q ≤ κ duty for any infinite quantale (the case where grading would matter continuously) is untouched. And per O2, the (i)-object must migrate to νW_Q, where the KS no-go, the weak law, and the standpoint results have not been re-established. "Small family" is doing enormous work.

## 7. Smaller but real issues

The "solution lemma" recovers only flat/guarded systems, not AFA's full solution lemma — fine, but "the working form of AFA" oversells it. REV-F's `AxiomCheck.lean` covers WS1–WS8 and isn't in the corpus; ws9/ws10 have no machine-checked axiom audit even by the project's own standard. WS10's closing note duplicates the O3 paragraph nearly verbatim — a small thing, but symptomatic of a corpus whose revision-note apparatus has outgrown its content: the charter is now mostly ledger, and §0's poem sits atop `Cofix` of a QPF with the §8 "interpretive gap" acknowledged once and never worked. Notably, the poem's identity-through-being-seen (incoming relations) is the one direction the formalism structurally cannot represent.

## What's genuinely good

To be fair: the honesty discipline (typed open obligations, anti-laundering names, surfaced errata) is far better than typical formalization projects; the KS no-go port, O1 (`κ ≤ #νP_κ`, a clean Lambek-Cantor argument), the O2 collapse theorem, and the WS9 counterexample suite are real, correct-looking mathematics; and the impossibility results are, as the charter hoped, the most informative outputs. The problem is that the informative outputs mostly point *against* the guiding conjecture — groundlessness, plurality, bidirectional constitution, and perspectivalism resist this formal setting for structural reasons — while the positive ledger through REV-F reads as if the constitution was largely built. The honest one-line summary the project owes itself: the instruments were not "built to satisfy" these specifications; on the unlabeled terminal carrier, several specifications are provably unsatisfiable together, and the escape routes (grading, labels, non-terminal carriers) remain almost entirely unformalized.
