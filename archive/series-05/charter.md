# Relational Existentialism — Series 05

**Stratification as boundlessness: why a world that would relate to everything must be built with no first level and no last level.**

*Working draft, Revision 0. Series 05 is self-contained: it states its own question, builds its own object, and proves its own results from scratch. Series 04 is prior art that informs the design — the way Series 03 informed Series 04 — but nothing here presupposes it. A reader who has never seen Series 04 should be able to follow this charter end to end.*

---

## 1. The one-paragraph version

We want to build a world made entirely of relationships that is genuinely **boundless** — where nothing surveys the whole and no size is stapled on from outside — while still holding more than one thing. Two collapses stand in the way, and each forces a repair. The first is old: **a world of pure, gradeless relation with no atoms collapses to a single point** (Parmenides), so relations must carry a **quality** to tell things apart, and the only quality that does not smuggle an atom back in is one drawn from *inside* the relata — the part a thing turns toward another, its face. That is the inheritance. The second collapse is Series 05's own: **a world that tries to be boundless naively explodes.** If you let a single world grow until it can relate to everything, you either fix a size from outside (a wall, not the world's own grain) or you demand groundlessness everywhere and the world collapses to a point again. Naive boundlessness is not too big; it is *unbuildable* — it either needs a cap or dies. Series 05's proposal is the one repair we can find that does not cheat: do not build one world that reaches everything. Build a **stratified** world — a tower of levels, each bounded in its own grain, **with no first level and no last level.** No last level means nothing tops the world and no size caps it from outside; no first level means nothing bottoms it out into atoms. The bet of this program: stratification is what lets a relational world be boundless *without* a wall and grounded-nowhere *without* collapse, and — the deeper bet — that the layers are not sealed floors but are related across, so a thing at one level genuinely relates to things at every other, as we relate to our cells, our molecules, our quarks, and downward without end.

That is the whole story. The rest of this charter makes each step precise and lays out the work.

---

## 2. What we are trying to build

A **boundless stratified relational world**: a domain of *objects* organized into levels, where

1. every object is nothing but its pattern of relating to other objects (an object *is* how it relates — there is no further hidden substance);
2. relations are themselves objects, free to be related to in turn (no separate second kind of thing that is "just glue");
3. quality is internal: when `a` relates to `b`, the relation carries the part of `a` turned toward `b` — its **face** `a↾(a,b)` — a sub-object of the relatum, never a weight imported from outside (the Series 04 inheritance, §4);
4. there is more than one object (the world is **plural**, not a single point);
5. the world is **boundless above**: no object relates to everything, no object is the all-encompassing top, and no size is imposed from outside to make this so — the bound on each object is the grain of the level it lives at, and no level is last;
6. the world is **groundless below**: no object is a simple atom, descent through relating never bottoms out, and no level is first;
7. objects **relate across levels**: relating is not sealed within a stratum — a thing at one level relates to things at coarser and finer levels, and this cross-level relating is itself an ordinary relation carrying an ordinary face;
8. the two **poles coincide at the layer level**: the downward-open end (the ground the tower never reaches) and the upward-open end (the everything the tower never reaches) are one absence, not two — the tower is self-dual, descent and ascent the same operation read in opposite directions;
9. **self-knowledge is incomplete, in two ways and now a third**: no object wholly knows itself (the Lawvere/Cantor diagonal, carrier-independent), the self-relating point Ω is complete-in-extent yet closed at no finite depth (coinductive non-termination), and — new to a stratified world — the tower cannot be known from any level, since a total survey would have to be taken at a first or last level and there is none;
10. **attention is grade-shift**: attending more finely is moving down a grade, attending more coarsely is moving up, so attention is the connecting-map action the stratification already carries, not a dynamical system bolted onto it;
11. the world never collapses to a single undifferentiated point (**non-degeneracy**).

Points 1–3 fix the *mathematical shape*: objects defined by their relating, relations reified, quality internal. This is **coalgebra** with restriction-quality, the Series 04 carrier. The canonical inhabitant remains the self-relating object

> **Ω = {Ω}**,

whose only relation is to itself. A world satisfying points 1–2 is a fixed point `X ≅ F(X)`; Series 04 built it and proved sameness-all-the-way-down is identity there.

The tension this program lives inside is between point 5 (boundless above) and points 6–11 (groundless below, cross-level, self-dual, incomplete, attentive, non-degenerate). They pull against each other through a single fact Series 04 proved and Series 05 must respect: **on one fixed carrier, boundlessness is either imposed or fatal.** Section 3 makes this precise. Point 5 sounds like the free promise — "just don't cap it" — and Section 3 proves the opposite. Boundlessness is the expensive one, and it is at war with staying buildable at all.

## 3. The two collapses

Series 05 stands on two impossibility results. The first is inherited and sets the quality; the second is the series' own hinge and sets the stratification. Everything afterward is a response to the second.

### 3.1 The inherited collapse: gradeless relation is a single point

Take the plain world — an object is just the set of things it relates to, difference lives only in presence-or-absence of a relation — and forbid atoms honestly (every object relates to something, hereditarily). Then any two atomless objects are bisimilar, and bisimulation is identity, so they are equal: the world has exactly one object, Ω. This is the **Parmenides collapse**, proved in Series 04 (`ws2_collapse`). Its lesson: difference has to bottom out somewhere, the atom was doing the work of plurality, and forbidding atoms removes the only place difference could live — *unless* relations carry a second currency of difference. That currency is **quality**, and the only quality that does not import an external zero (whose composition would reappear as a relationless atom) is **restriction-quality**: the face, a part of the relatum itself. Series 04 established this and proved the face composes without ever draining to nothing (`ws3_faces_never_annihilate`). Series 05 inherits the faced carrier wholesale and does not re-litigate it. *When there is no quality, Parmenides collapses the world; so we relate with faces.*

### 3.2 The explosion: naive boundlessness is unbuildable

Now the new hinge. We want point 5 — no object relates to everything, and *not because we said so from outside.* Series 04 tried exactly this and reported the honest failure: on a single carrier `νP_κ`, the only thing that stops an object relating to everything is a **cardinality wall** — each object carries `< κ` successors while the world is `≥ κ`. Strip the word "face" from that proof and it goes through unchanged (`ws4_no_top_reach`, review S2). The wall is real but it is a **fiat**: κ is chosen from outside, and the M1/M2 negatives proved faces cannot supply the bound in its place (`ws5_contraction_insufficient`, `ws5_quotient_insufficient`) — faces tame *quality*, not *branching*. So on one carrier you get boundedness only by imposing κ.

Try instead to remove the cap — let the world be genuinely boundless by demanding groundlessness everywhere, every object atomless without end. Then the **inherited collapse fires globally**: Series 04's `ws5_global_groundless_collapses` proves that atomlessness *everywhere* forces a subsingleton. A single carrier that is groundless everywhere is a point.

> **The Explosion Dilemma (target of WS2).** *On any single fixed carrier, a relational world is either (a) bounded by an externally imposed cardinal — a wall, not the world's own grain — or (b) groundless everywhere and thereby collapsed to a single point. Boundless-and-plural is unsatisfiable on one carrier. Naive boundlessness does not overflow; it is unbuildable.*

### 3.3 What the explosion means

Like the Parmenides collapse, this is not a bug to be patched but a **discovery about single-carrier worlds**, and it dictates the shape of the repair:

> **Boundlessness cannot live at a level.** Any *one* world has a size, and that size is either imposed (a wall) or fatal (collapse). "No top" and "no imposed size" cannot both hold *within a single carrier*, because the carrier's cardinal is the only thing standing between the world and the global collapse.

So a genuinely boundless world — no top, no imposed size, still plural — must not *be* a single carrier at all. It must be a **structure across carriers**: a tower of levels, each with its own honest local bound, arranged so that no level is last (nothing caps the tower) and no level is first (nothing grounds it into atoms). Boundlessness stops being a property of a world and becomes a **relation between levels**. The Explosion Dilemma is a proof that stratification is not a stylistic choice; it is *forced*, the same way the Parmenides collapse forced quality.

## 4. The false repairs: two ways to stratify that cheat

The natural first moves at stratification both cheat, each echoing one horn of the Explosion Dilemma. Naming them sharpens what the real repair must avoid.

### 4.1 The tower with a top (a wall in disguise)

Build levels `W_0, W_1, W_2, …` indexed by the natural numbers and take their union. This looks boundless — infinitely many levels — but it has a **supremum cardinal**: the union sits inside one carrier of size `sup_n κ_n`, and the Explosion Dilemma fires on *that* carrier. A tower with a countable (or any set-indexed, cofinal-in-a-cardinal) top is just a bigger single world with the wall moved up one floor. *The cheat: "no last level" was faked by having a last cardinal.* The real repair needs the level index itself to be **unbounded** — cofinal in the ordinals, no supremum inside the theory — so that for any candidate cap, some level already exceeds it.

### 4.2 The tower with a bottom (an atom in disguise)

Build levels descending `…, W_2, W_1, W_0` but let `W_0` be a **first** level — a floor. Then the minimal-level objects relate to nothing finer: they are simple, relationless at the bottom, **atoms**. This is exactly the Series 04 hazard (WS5 §9, "the self-shrinking route may hide a floor"): a well-founded stratification has minimal faces that behave as the ground of distinction, betraying point 6. *The cheat: "groundless below" was faked while the bottom level quietly does the atom's old job of grounding difference.* The real repair needs the level index to be **downward-unbounded too** — no first level — so there is no minimal stratum to serve as ground.

### 4.3 What we have learned to demand

The two false repairs teach the two-sided demand precisely: the stratification must be **unbounded in both directions of its index.** No last level (or the top-cardinal wall of §4.1 returns) and no first level (or the bottom-atom of §4.2 returns). A stratification with a first or last level is a single carrier wearing a costume, and the Explosion Dilemma or the Parmenides collapse fires on it accordingly. Only a doubly-unbounded tower escapes both. That is precisely the proposal of Series 05.

## 5. The repair that does not cheat: doubly-unbounded stratification

### 5.1 The idea

Do not build a world. Build a **directed system of worlds** — a family of faced carriers `W_α`, one per level `α` drawn from an index with **no least and no greatest element**, with structure-preserving connecting maps `ι_{α,β} : W_α → W_β` for `α ≤ β` — and take the world to be its **colimit** `W_∞`, together with the cross-level structure of §5.2. Each `W_α` is a Series 04 faced carrier at its own cardinal `κ_α`; each object in `W_∞` lives at some level and there carries `< κ_α` successors, an honest **local** bound. But `W_∞` has no global cardinal: for any candidate cap, a higher level overshoots it (no last level, §4.1 defeated), and the descent has no floor (no first level, §4.2 defeated). The bound is real at every object and imposed at none — *grain, not wall*, the thesis Series 04 could not reach on one carrier, recovered by refusing to have one carrier.

### 5.2 Relating across levels

A tower of sealed floors would satisfy points 5–6 and fail point 7: if the connecting maps only re-present an object one level up as "the same object," relating stays within a stratum and a person never relates to a quark. So the stratification must carry **cross-level edges**, and Series 05 supplies them *without a new primitive* by grading the face.

An edge `x → y` carries a **depth grade** `d`, and the face `x↾(x,y)` is the part of `x` engaged at that grade. Relating to a coarser thing is a thick, shallow face; relating to a finer thing is a thinner face reached by **composing** edges down through the levels. Cross-level relating is thus a *composite* faced edge — and here the inheritance pays off exactly: Series 04 proved composition is unconditionally leak-free (`ws3_faces_never_annihilate`), so a relation reached by descending through arbitrarily many levels never drains its face to the empty object. The person-to-quark relation is a deep composite whose face is thin but never nothing. Because there is no first level, the descent never terminates in a leaf: below any grade, a finer grade of relating, without end. *You keep relating downward forever, with less and less of yourself turned toward each finer layer, the face shrinking toward the empty object it never reaches — because the empty object is the atom, and there is none.*

The philosophically load-bearing reading, which WS6 is charged to make precise: relating and being-composed-of are **one relation at two grades.** A thing is not *accompanied* by its cells; it *is* the pattern of relating that, attended to at a finer grade, is the cells relating. No level is the privileged real one — which grade is fundamental is a fact about the attender, not the world.

This is also where **attention finally fits**. In Series 03 attention was foreign machinery — a replicator dynamics with a feed/starve selection map, *bolted onto* the coalgebra and never falling out of it, which is why its convergence ("Lemma B") stayed open across two whole series. In a stratified world attention is not a dynamical system added to the structure; it is the **grade-shift the tower already carries** — attending more finely is moving down a grade along a connecting map, attending more coarsely is moving up. "To attend is to become" becomes literal and structural: the act of attention is the reindexing between levels, and the object you attend to at a finer grade is what you become when you descend to it. The bet is that attention was hard to bolt on precisely because it wanted to be this all along. *But an identity this clean is exactly what the coincidence rule (§7) exists to distrust:* WS7 must earn it, not assert it, by proving the definitional grade-shift coincides with an independently motivated notion of attention — the Series 03 feed/starve dynamics recovered as a special case, or convergence falling out of the connecting-map structure — on pain of reporting it **Trivialized**, attention-vocabulary painted on the maps.

### 5.3 Why it cannot cheat

The tower-with-a-top (§4.1) is foreclosed because the index has **no greatest element** and is cofinal in the ordinals: the colimit has no supremum cardinal for the Explosion Dilemma to fire on. The tower-with-a-bottom (§4.2) is foreclosed because the index has **no least element**: there is no minimal stratum whose objects are relationless atoms. And crucially, the **global collapse of §3.1 never gets its hypothesis.** `ws5_global_groundless_collapses` needs atomlessness *within one fixed carrier*; in the stratified world no object is asked to be hereditarily nonempty inside a level — each object is *founded relative to the levels below it* and *unfounded relative to the tower as a whole.* Groundlessness becomes a **global (tower-level) property** while local foundedness holds at each stratum. That decoupling is the whole escape, and it is available only because the tower is doubly unbounded.

> **The forced answer (framing claim of the program).** *Doubly-unbounded stratification is not one option among many for building a boundless world. It is — we conjecture, and WS2 is charged with making this precise — the essentially unique way to have no-top and no-imposed-size and plurality together, given the Explosion Dilemma that forbids all three on any single carrier. Boundlessness lives between levels; the levels are unbounded both ways; unboundedness both ways is what forecloses both the wall and the atom.*

### 5.4 What stratification buys, beyond not cheating

Because the world is a doubly-unbounded tower with graded cross-level faces, several things follow — each a response to a point of §2, and each a workstream (§6).

- **Boundlessness without a wall (point 5, vs the explosion).** No object relates to everything, and *not* by an imposed κ: the local bound is the grain of a level, and no level is last. This is the "grain, not wall" thesis Series 04 proved unreachable on one carrier, recovered by stratifying. *(WS3, WS4.)*
- **Groundlessness without collapse (point 6, vs Parmenides-global).** Descent never bottoms out — no first level — yet the world does not collapse, because groundlessness is a property of the tower, not of any carrier. Local foundedness at each level is what keeps the global collapse's hypothesis unmet. *(WS4, WS5.)*
- **Cross-level relating, leak-free (point 7).** A thing relates to coarser and finer things by graded composite faces; composition down the tower never annihilates the face (inherited `ws3_faces_never_annihilate`, transported to the graded setting). Relating-to and being-made-of are one relation at two grades. *(WS6.)*
- **The poles coincide at the layer level (point 8).** The two absences the tower approaches — the ground it never reaches by descending, the everything it never reaches by ascending — are *one* absence, if the level index is self-dual (`ℤ`-like, its own order-reversal). Then descent and ascent are the same operation read in opposite directions, the tower's limit and colimit are formally dual, and "no first" and "no last" are not two facts but one fact seen from two ends. This is the Series 03 zero-object dream (initial = terminal) relocated from *inside a carrier*, where it provably could not live (`ws6_poles_split`), to *the layer structure*, where it can. **Honest alternative:** the index may be lopsided — ascent and descent structurally different — in which case the poles are genuinely two and the self-duality is reported false. *(WS4, no-view companion.)*
- **No view from nowhere, forced at last (point 5, companion).** A view is a face, hence taken *at* a level. A view from nowhere would be a view at no level — at a level below all levels or above all levels — which is a first or last level, and there is none. So no-view-from-nowhere is *forced by no-first-no-last*, not by a cardinality cap and not by the definition of "view." This is the coincidence Series 04 could not deliver (its V2 was the cardinal wall in disguise, review R2); the tower's double-openness is a genuinely different mechanism. **The anti-laundering duty is severe here:** WS4 must show the forced form does not secretly reduce to "the index has no extremal element" (a cardinality fact relocated up a level) — the *facing* structure must be load-bearing, or this is the Series 04 failure wearing a new hat. *(WS4.)*
- **Incompleteness, inherited and extended (point 9).** Two Series 04 incompletenesses transport for free because they are carrier-independent: no object wholly knows itself (the Lawvere/Cantor diagonal, `ws6_lawvere_incomplete`), and Ω is complete-in-extent yet closed at no finite depth (`ws6_omega_nonterminating`). Stratification adds a third that the earlier series could not state: **the tower is unknowable from within** — a total self-survey would be a view at a first or last level, and there is none, so no level knows the whole. This is the epistemic face of no-view-from-nowhere: the same no-first-no-last fact, read as "no standpoint completes the world" rather than "no standpoint is unpositioned." The identity of the two is itself a coincidence duty (are they one theorem or two?). *(WS6; shares WS4's no-view mechanism.)*
- **Attention as grade-shift (point 10).** Attention is the connecting-map action, not a bolted-on dynamics — the reindexing between levels the tower already carries. If earned (§5.2), this newly fits the machinery Series 03 could only posit, and may touch the long-deferred convergence (Lemma B). **Coincidence duty:** definitional grade-shift must coincide with an independently motivated attention notion, or be reported Trivialized. *(WS6, WS7.)*
- **Plurality preserved (points 4, 11).** The faced plurality of Series 04 survives the colimit: distinct objects stay distinct in `W_∞`, so the world is more than a point. *(WS3.)*

### 5.5 The honest hazards, named at the outset

Stratification is a bet, not a theorem, and Series 05 is built to report that the bet failed. Six hazards are load-bearing and named now.

- **The colimit may not carry a coalgebra (the technical gate).** For "an object is its relating" (point 1) to survive `W_∞`, the connecting maps must be coalgebra morphisms and the transition functor must be accessible enough that the colimit carries a canonical coalgebra structure with bisimulation-is-identity intact. Terminal coalgebras do not glue into a terminal coalgebra for free. *This is the analogue of Series 04's weak-pullback gate — the single most likely place the program dies, and WS1 settles it first.* Honest outcome if it fails: Impossibility proved for the colimit carrier specifically, and the program seeks a different amalgamation or stops.
- **"No first level" may be unstatable without a floor (the index gate).** An index unbounded in both directions is not a set of ordinals (those have a least element, 0). The index theory must genuinely have no least element — a `ℤ`-grading, a dense order, or a coinductively defined level structure — and "no minimal stratum" must be a *theorem about the index*, not an assumption, or the atom of §4.2 sneaks back. WS2 owns making this precise; if it can only be *posited*, the groundlessness is fiat and must be reported so.
- **Cross-level composition may leak at some grade (the transport hazard).** The inherited leak-freeness is proved on the flat-label carrier; transporting it to the graded, cross-level setting is not automatic. If composition drains the face to empty at some grade, *that grade is behaving as a floor* — a hidden first level — and the design has a defect there, to be reported precisely. WS6 owns it.
- **Boundlessness may be only heuristic (the forced-answer risk).** "Doubly-unbounded stratification is *the* boundless construction" is the program's boldest claim. The essential-uniqueness form (WS2) may resist proof, leaving a defended-but-not-proved thesis. Reporting it as heuristic rather than theorem, if so, is required — exactly as Series 04 reported its forced-answer claim.
- **The poles may not coincide (the self-duality risk).** The layer-level pole coincidence (point 8) holds only if the index is genuinely self-dual. A `ℤ`-grading is; a dense order or a coinductive level structure may not be, and the transition functor must respect the reversal for limit and colimit to be dual. If the tower is lopsided, the poles are two, not one — a real and reportable negative, not a failure. WS4 owns it.
- **Cross-level composition may lack a coherent distributive law (the bialgebra risk).** Even if composition is leak-free edge-by-edge, the algebra (composition) and coalgebra (level-wise observation) sides must cohere via a distributive law across levels. Series 03 already proved no *strict* law exists; the graded weak law WS6 needs may fail to commute with the depth-grade, in which case cross-level relating and level-wise structure disagree — itself a hidden floor or cap. The deepest technical risk after the WS1 gate; WS6 owns it.

## 6. Workstreams

Seven workstreams. WS1–WS2 establish the object and the forced-answer claim; WS3–WS6 deliver the payoffs of §5.4, one theme each; WS7 audits the whole against the trivialization risk.

- **WS1 — The tower and its colimit.** Define the directed system: faced carriers `W_α` (the Series 04 `νLk`-style labelled coalgebra) at cardinals `κ_α`, with connecting coalgebra morphisms `ι_{α,β}`. **Gate: prove the colimit `W_∞` carries a coalgebra structure with bisimulation-is-identity preserved** — that the transition functor is accessible enough for the colimit to exist and be canonical. Deliver `W_∞`, recover a copy of Ω = {Ω} inside it, and show each object inherits an honest local `< κ_α` bound. *Blocking; settled first. Outcomes in play: Discharged, or Impossibility proved (the colimit fails to carry the structure), which redirects the amalgamation.*

- **WS2 — The explosion, and the forced answer.** Prove the **Explosion Dilemma** (§3.2): on any single fixed carrier, boundless-and-plural is unsatisfiable — either an imposed cardinal wall or the global-groundless collapse (`ws5_global_groundless_collapses`, inherited). Fix the **index theory** and prove it has no least and no greatest element (§4.1–4.2 defeated), with "no minimal stratum" a theorem, not a posit. Then state and defend the **forced-answer claim** (§5.3): doubly-unbounded stratification supplies no-top-without-imposed-size-with-plurality, the unique escape from the Dilemma. Essential-uniqueness is the target; a clean statement of *why* double-unboundedness forecloses both the wall and the atom is the floor. *This workstream is the intellectual spine; §§3–5 are its prose.*

- **WS3 — Boundlessness without a wall.** Prove **no object in `W_∞` relates to everything**, with the bound *endogenous*: each object's `< κ_α` relating is the grain of its level, and no level is last, so no global cap is imposed. **Coincidence duty:** re-prove that on any single level the bound is still the imposed cardinal wall (the inherited Series 04 result), so the endogenous boundlessness is *earned* against the single-carrier fiat, not stipulated — the tower does something one carrier provably cannot.

- **WS4 — No first, no last: the doubly-open tower, its poles, and the view from nowhere.** Prove the tower is **unbounded above** (for every level there is a higher one; no global cardinal bounds `W_∞`) and **unbounded below** (for every level a lower one; no minimal stratum, hence no atom-floor). Prove that **global groundlessness holds without collapse**: descent never bottoms out, yet `W_∞` is not a subsingleton, because the collapse hypothesis (atomlessness within a fixed carrier) is never met. Then two payoffs the double-openness makes available: **(a) the poles coincide at the layer level** — if the index is self-dual (order-isomorphic to its reverse), descent and ascent are one operation, the tower's limit and colimit are dual, and no-first ≡ no-last; the honest alternative is a lopsided index with two genuine poles, reported as such. **(b) No view from nowhere, forced** — a view is a face at a level; a view at no level is a view at a first or last level; there is none; so no-view is forced by no-first-no-last, the coincidence Series 04 lacked (its V2 was the cardinal wall, review R2). **Coincidence duties, both severe:** the non-collapse must be forced by the local/global decoupling, not read off a definition (exhibit that a *singly*-bounded tower *does* collapse or *does* wall); and the forced no-view must not secretly reduce to "the index has no extremal element" — the *facing* structure must be load-bearing, or (b) is the Series 04 failure relocated up a level and reported Partial again. **Anti-laundering check, written into every proof (for WS7):** strip "face" and "view" from the statement; if it still goes through as a bare index fact, it is not earned.

- **WS5 — The self-bounding of the world, revisited.** Make precise that the world's finitude is now **endogenous at last**: each object bounded by its own level's grain, the tower bounded by nothing. Adjudicate against the Series 04 M1/M2 negatives — show what stratification supplies that contraction and quotient-on-one-carrier could not: the bound freed from fiat by moving it from *within* a carrier to *between* levels. *Owns the "grain, not wall" thesis that Series 04 could only reach on the Ω-spine; reports honestly if the tower's bound smuggles a fiat back in through the index or the colimit cardinal.*

- **WS6 — Relating across levels, and attention as grade-shift.** Grade the face by depth and define cross-level edges as graded composite faces. Prove **cross-level relating is leak-free** — transport `ws3_faces_never_annihilate` to the graded setting, so a relation composed down through arbitrarily many levels never drains its face to the empty object. Prove the **descent never terminates** (no first level ⇒ no leaf). Deliver the identification duty: **relating-to and being-composed-of are one relation at two grades.** **Incompleteness, inherited and extended:** transport the two carrier-independent Series 04 results (`ws6_lawvere_incomplete`, the diagonal; `ws6_omega_nonterminating`, Ω complete-yet-unclosed), and prove the new stratified incompleteness — **the tower is unknowable from any level** (a total self-survey would be a view at a first or last level; none exists). **Coincidence duty on incompleteness:** prove whether the tower-unknowability is the *same* theorem as WS4's no-view-from-nowhere (the same no-first-no-last fact read epistemically) or a genuinely distinct one — one theorem or two, stated and adjudicated, not conflated. Then **attention as grade-shift**: define attention as the connecting-map action (descend a grade = attend finer, ascend = attend coarser) and prove it is a genuine structural operation of the tower, not added data. **Coincidence duties:** (i) leak-freeness must be the genuine transported theorem, not a definitional restatement — and if composition leaks at some grade, report that grade as a hidden floor (a WS4 no-first-level failure), not a success; (ii) attention-as-grade-shift must *coincide* with an independently motivated attention notion — the Series 03 feed/starve dynamics recovered as a special case of grade-shift, or convergence (the long-deferred **Lemma B**) falling out of the connecting-map structure — or it is reported **Trivialized**, vocabulary painted on the maps. **The bialgebra obligation (inherited crux):** composition is the algebra (`T`) side and level-wise unfolding the coalgebra (`F`) side, so the tower is asking for a **distributive law across levels**. Series 03 proved no *strict* law exists on `P_κ` (`ws3_no_distributive_law`) and recovered the content via a weak Egli–Milner law; WS6 must show cross-level composition satisfies a **graded weak distributive law** commuting with both observation *and* the depth-grade — or the inherited leak-freeness does not cohere with the stratification. This is the deepest technical risk in the design.

- **WS7 — The anti-trivialization audit.** Across WS3–WS6, verify the coincidence discipline: for each payoff, the easy definitional form and an independently forced form were proved *distinct statements that coincide.* Deliver the central Series 05 finding — **whether boundlessness, groundlessness, cross-level relating, and non-collapse genuinely reduce to a single fact (the double-unboundedness of the tower), or whether that unification is an artifact of the construction.** This workstream can return the verdict **Trivialized** — a sharp negative about the program's own conjecture — and returning it honestly is a success, not a failure.

## 7. Methodology and outcome vocabulary

Each point of §2 becomes a precise specification; a concrete object is exhibited; an adequacy theorem shows the object meets the specification. **Negative results are first-class outcomes** — the Explosion Dilemma is a *proved* obstacle that does the program's real motivating work, exactly as the Parmenides collapse did for Series 04, and the program counts a sharp impossibility as a success.

Each workstream obligation is classified as **Discharged** (its stated target is proved), **Impossibility proved** (a sharp negative — a success), **Partial** (part proved, with the remaining obstruction made mathematically precise), or **Failed** (not achieved, with a documented why). A Partial or Failed result triggers a **methodology note** — a correction to the plan or an explicit hand-off — never a redefinition of the target.

Series 05 inherits Series 04's signature rule against cheap success:

> **The coincidence rule.** No payoff of §5.4 may be reported **Discharged** on its definitional form alone. Discharge requires a *coincidence theorem*: the definitional form and an independently motivated forced form, separately stated and proved equal. A payoff holding only definitionally is reported **Partial — definitional-only**, with the forced form as the named open obligation.

And the outcome the ordinary vocabulary cannot express: **Trivialized**, the finding that the §5.4 unification is definitional rather than substantive. WS7 owns it; returning it honestly is a program success.

## 8. Success criteria

Success is a single object — the doubly-unbounded stratified world with graded cross-level faces — that provably:

(i) is boundless above with the bound endogenous — no object relates to everything, and not by an imposed cardinal but by the grain of its level, no level being last; (ii) is groundless below without collapse — descent never bottoms out, no level being first, yet the world is not a point; (iii) inherits internal restriction-quality and its leak-free composition, so plurality survives the colimit; (iv) relates across levels — a thing relates to coarser and finer things by graded composite faces that never drain to nothing, cohering via a graded weak distributive law; (v) identifies relating-to with being-composed-of as one relation at two grades, with attention as grade-shift earned rather than posited; (vi) coincides its poles at the layer level, or reports the index lopsided; (vii) has no view from nowhere, forced by no-first-no-last and not by a cardinality cap; (viii) carries the inherited incompleteness (diagonal, Ω non-termination) and the new tower-unknowability, with their relation to no-view adjudicated; and (ix) is non-degenerate — more than one object, and the tower does not collapse or wall.

Against the central question, success takes one of these shapes, all valid:

- **The forced answer stands (headline positive).** Doubly-unbounded stratification is shown to be the essentially unique boundless-and-plural construction (WS2), and the world's bound is endogenous at last (WS5) — grain, between levels, not wall around one.
- **A payoff is characterized honestly.** For each theme of §5.4, a reported outcome: *delivered with its coincidence theorem*, *delivered but definitional-only* (forced form open), or *obstructed* with the obstacle made precise. Each is terminal and valid.
- **The verdict, either way (WS7).** *One double-unboundedness, substantively* — the payoffs genuinely reduce to the tower's being unbounded both ways, coincidence theorems throughout — or *Trivialized* — the reduction is definitional. Both are successes; the second is a sharp negative about the conjecture.

> **The characteristic risk, stated plainly.** Series 05's danger is not that it fails but that it *succeeds too cheaply* — that stratification settles everything by making everything a feature of one construction. As in Series 04, the criteria are written so that **catching that** (WS7's *Trivialized*) counts as success, not concealment. A charter that could only succeed would prove nothing.

## 9. Open problems and honest risks

- **The colimit gate (WS1) is existential.** If `W_∞` does not carry a coalgebra with bisimulation-is-identity, "an object is its relating" (point 1 / criterion iii) breaks and is not merely weakened. Most likely single point of failure; settled first. Honest outcome if it fails: Impossibility proved for the colimit carrier specifically, and the program finds a different amalgamation or declares a different construction and pays the re-proof cost.
- **The index gate (WS2).** "No first level" must be a theorem about a genuinely least-element-free index, not a posit. If the index theory only *stipulates* no minimal stratum, the groundlessness is fiat and the §4.2 atom is hidden in the stipulation. The escape — a `ℤ`-graded or dense or coinductive index with no-minimum *proved* — is a specific, checkable structure, not a given.
- **The transport hazard (WS6).** The inherited leak-freeness may not survive grading. If cross-level composition drains a face to empty at some grade, that grade is a hidden first level, and cross-level relating fails there. Plausible, not automatic; the crux of WS6.
- **Trivialization (the signature risk).** That §5.4's payoffs are one construction restated — so the interconnection is assumed, not found. The coincidence rule (§7) and WS7 exist to catch it, but only if the forced forms are genuinely independent of the definitional ones, itself a per-payoff judgement that could be got wrong. This risk cannot be fully retired in advance; it is what the program tests.
- **The forced-answer claim may be only heuristic (WS2).** "Doubly-unbounded stratification is *the* boundless construction" is the program's boldest claim. The essential-uniqueness form may resist proof, leaving a defended-but-not-proved thesis. Reporting it as heuristic rather than theorem, if so, is required.
- **Interpretive gap (permanent).** Whether this object *is* the boundless relational world or a faithful mathematical model of it is a philosophical question to frame, not a theorem to prove. Stratification narrows the gap — the model now carries levels and cross-level relating in its very structure — but does not close it.

## 10. Positioning

Philosophically: radical ontic structural realism and the "relations without relata" objection (Ladyman & Ross, French), against which Series 04 concluded for *perspectival* structural realism — Series 05 extends this to composition, where the refusal of a privileged fundamental level (no first level) is precisely what makes radical OSR's bottom-level-of-pure-structure unavailable, consistent with Series 04's finding that radical OSR may be inconsistent; the denial of a view from nowhere (Nagel), now sharpened to the denial of a *bottom* from nowhere, a privileged finest grain; the special-sciences literature on levels, emergence, and the failure of a fundamental level (Schaffer's "metaphysical infinitism" / the no-fundamental-level debate) as a direct precedent for no-first-level; and the four tenets, with "to attend is to become" carrying literal formal weight in §5.2 as depth-grade shift. Formally: directed colimits of coalgebras and the accessibility conditions for their existence (Adámek, Rosický); non-well-founded set theory and universal coalgebra (Aczel; Rutten, Jacobs) for objects-as-relating, inherited; the theory of graded and enriched relations for the depth-graded face (WS6); and the theory of `ℤ`-indexed or dense-order towers for the doubly-unbounded index (WS2). The distinctive bet of Series 05, in one line: **a relational world becomes boundless not by growing one carrier until it reaches everything — which either walls or collapses — but by refusing to be one carrier at all, stratifying into levels unbounded above and below, so that boundlessness is a relation between levels and the finitude of each thing is the grain of the level it lives at, while relating across the levels is one relation read at many grades.**
