# Spec 2.01 — The Signature, the Dyad Reduction, and the Construction of Ω

**Series 2, document 1.** This spec fixes the formal signature realizing Spec 2.00's ontology, defines the framework's derived notions concretely, and lays out the construction of Ω with proof routes for the design-ready theorems. It is subordinate to 2.00: where the two disagree, 2.00's ontology wins and the discrepancy is reported. Read 2.00 first; this document does not restate the philosophy, only realizes it.

**Status:** normative for the signature and definitions; construction and proof routes are working plans, revisable as the Lean development teaches us. Items marked `OPEN` feed 2.02/2.03.

## 0. Summary of what this document does

1. Records three rulings from the framework's owner (§1) and derives from them the **dyad reduction** — the single mechanism that fixes arity, locates attending, and closes the order regress.
2. Fixes the **two-sorted signature** (§2): objects and relations, binary relations, unordered endpoints, patterns with sharing, contexts as sub-patterns.
3. Defines the derived notions (§3): dyad, context, aperture, direction, attending, other-knowledge, hearsay chains, observer-local identity, deployable attention.
4. Confirms at design level the **D7 simplification** (§4): the construction lands in ordinary set-based two-sorted coalgebra — no enrichment machinery. States the κ-scaffolding decision and the T2 existence route.
5. States theorems with proof routes (§5), including one new and dangerous conjecture (C3: partial derivability of A8(i)) under D15 discipline.

## 1. The three rulings and the dyad reduction

**Ruling 1 (arity).** Relations may be modeled with whatever arity is simplest, because n-ary phenomena are recoverable: a relation, having coalesced into an object with some degree of closure, can relate to a third party. → **Binary relations**, with n-arity derived.

**Ruling 2 (whose attending).** A relation's bearing can be that relation's own attending — "wouldn't it be a restriction in its own right?" — with the choice of formal realization delegated to modeling simplicity.

**Ruling 3 (the order regress).** The recursion of contexts (everything must have nonempty context, at every order) closes via ruling 1's mechanism, with **no special rules**: loops and finite cycles are ordinary.

**The dyad reduction (2.01-D1).** All three rulings are realized by one construction. For a relation r, its **dyad** dy(r) is the object that r's immediate neighborhood coalesces into — the minimal pattern centered on r, closed by the closure operator (§3.1). Then:

- **n-arity, derived:** a "relation among the three of us" is the dyad of our pairwise structure relating to the third — binary relations plus coalescence generate every arity.
- **The wound's attending, located:** the wound *does* attend — *qua* the object it has coalesced into. Attending requires an aperture; apertures are contexts; contexts live in patterns; so **to attend is to have coalesced** (2.01-D2). Nothing attends that has not coalesced, and everything that attends does so through its own pattern. The kind distinction of 2.00-D2 survives sharply: the relation does not *become* an object; it *anchors* one, and the attending belongs to the anchor. No duplicate attending-machinery enters the relation sort.
- **Higher-order relating, reduced:** "r₁ bears on r₂" is realized as an ordinary first-order relation between dy(r₁) and dy(r₂). 2.00-A6's higher-order clause is thereby *preserved as derived shorthand*: relations relate to relations *through their dyads*, uniformly at every order, and "order" remains representation-layer bookkeeping. No amendment to 2.00 is needed; this is realization, not revision.
- **The regress, closed:** context-nonemptiness recurses, and the recursion terminates in the only way this framework ever terminates anything — by closing on itself. Self-relations and finite cycles among dyads (licensed by A5, no special rules) supply the loops; the pattern of "the closing" reappears at the meta-level, and its eventual connection to ρ and T5 is noted as an aesthetic conjecture, not a claim (`OPEN`, feeds 2.03).

**2.01-D3 (recorded decision).** The signature contains binary relations between objects only. Everything 2.00 says with "higher-order" vocabulary is definable; a translation lemma (T15, §5) is owed to certify that nothing expressible in the higher-order idiom is lost.

## 2. The signature

Two sorts, mutually recursive. Presented informally here with the intended Lean shapes in parentheses; exact formalization is the next mechanization work order's job.

- **O** — objects. **R** — relations.
- **endpoints : R → Sym²(O)** — each relation has an unordered pair of endpoints, possibly equal (self-relation, A5). Unordered realizes 2.00-D6: no slots. (`Sym²` as the quotient of pairs by swap, or as sub-singleton/doubleton subsets.)
- **pat : O → P⁺κ(R)** — each object's pattern: the nonempty, κ-small set of relations that are parts of it. **Sharing is numerical identity**: r ∈ pat(x) ∧ r ∈ pat(y) is one r in two patterns — 2.00-D3 falls out of ordinary set membership, no extra structure.
- **Endpoint coherence (P3, signature form):** every relation is part of both its endpoints: endpoints(r) = {x, y} ⟹ r ∈ pat(x) ∧ r ∈ pat(y). Patterns may contain more than endpoint-relations — that surplus is exactly what composites are made of (shared joints, 2.00 §2.5).
- **No further primitives.** Qualities, apertures, directions, observations, π: all defined (§3). This is the D7 program executed: one material.

**What A8(i) adds.** The lone axiom enters as a properness condition on attending (§3.4): the witnessing of an attending is a proper part of what its aperture spans. Its exact placement — and the startling possibility that most of it is derivable — is §5's C3.

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Dyad
For r ∈ R, the **dyad** dy(r) is the object obtained by closing the minimal description centered on r (r, its endpoints' shared participation in r, and nothing else) under `close`. Existence of dy(r) for every r is proposition **P5** (§5): it should follow from T2 plus the closure of finite descriptions; if it needs a signature condition instead, that is a finding to document, since the dyad reduction (2.01-D1) leans on it.

### 3.2 Context and aperture
For x ∈ O and r ∈ pat(x), the **context** of r in x is
  ctx_x(r) := { s ∈ pat(x) | dy(r) ∈ endpoints(s) }
— the relations within x's pattern in which r's dyad participates: everything in x that bears on r, realized first-order via the reduction. The **aperture** of x at r *is* ctx_x(r), viewed as a sub-pattern of x. Comparison of apertures is inclusion, within one object only — cross-object apertures are subsets of different patterns, incomparable by type, realizing 2.00's locality (A3) with no machinery at all.

**Context-nonemptiness.** P0's recursion demands ctx nonempty wherever attending occurs. Per ruling 3, this is satisfied by loops: dy(r) always participates in *something*, if only a self-relation or a short cycle. Whether nonemptiness must be imposed on the signature or emerges from finality is a construction question flagged for the Lean work (`OPEN`; the imposition, if needed, is structural in the D4-unformulability pattern, not a new axiom).

### 3.3 Direction and attending
For r with endpoints {x, y}: the **direction of r at x** is x's **attending** to r — the pair (r, ctx_x(r)) as an act of x. A relation has one direction per endpoint (two, or one when self-related); each direction belongs to an endpoint, never to the relation itself (2.01-D2). P2 is immediate and now contentful: the two directions are (r, ctx_x(r)) and (r, ctx_y(r)) — same r, apertures made of different patterns.

### 3.4 Witnessing and the axiom
An attending **witnesses** through its aperture: what x's attending to r reveals is drawn from ctx_x(r) and the structure reachable through it. **A8(i)** enters here: the witnessing is *proper* — some aspect within the aperture's span is always missed. (Placement note: whether properness must be stated for all attendings or only for the self-referential residue is C3, §5.)

### 3.5 Other-knowledge and hearsay
**x observes y** (derived, 2.00-D10): x attends to some shared r ∈ pat(x) ∩ pat(y) through ctx_x(r). A **hearsay chain** from x is a finite sequence of shared relations x—z₁—z₂—⋯, each step an attending to a shared relation through the attender's own context; what a chain transmits at each step is bounded by the attender's aperture on the shared relation (the acquaintance bound, feeding T13).

### 3.6 Observer-local identity
For observer w: **a ≈w b** iff no attending available to w — over pat(w), through w's apertures, along finite hearsay chains from w (depth question O8 unresolved; default: all finite depths) — distinguishes a from b. Identity on Ω is the *limit* of the ≈w family (2.00 §2.6, as corrected): approached, never occupied; the representation layer alone may intersect.

### 3.7 Deployable attention (pre-registered definition, D15)
An attention A ⊆ pat(x) is **deployable by x** iff A is the aperture of some actual attending of x: A = ctx_x(r) for some r ∈ pat(x). Justification (recorded *without reference* to PR-J's desired outcome, per D15): A8 recognizes no free-floating attention — all attending is along a relation through its context — so deployability is exactly aperture-hood. Combining attentions is therefore not set-union at will: x deploys a combination only if some relation's context *is* that combination, i.e., only if the combination is already woven into x's pattern. PR-J(b) then asks: is every ctx_x(r) proper in pat(x)? See C3 — the two questions have partially merged, which neither was designed to do; D15 requires saying so out loud.

## 4. The construction of Ω

**The functor.** F acts on pairs of sets (O, R):
  F_O(O, R) = P⁺κ(R)  (an object is a pattern)
  F_R(O, R) = Sym²(O)  (a relation is an unordered endpoint pair)
with the coherence conditions of §2 imposed on coalgebras. This is a κ-accessible endofunctor on Set × Set.

**Existence (T2 route — literature-verified, three steps).** (1) *Base:* final coalgebras in many-sorted sets are settled theory — Adámek–Trnková ("Initial algebras and terminal coalgebras in many-sorted sets," MSCS 2011) prove convergence of the terminal sequence and existence for endofunctors with a fixed-point pair exceeding the number of sorts; our F is κ-accessible and mono-preserving componentwise (P⁺κ and Sym² both qualify), so νF exists on Set × Set. (2) *Coherence as coequation:* endpoint coherence is preserved under quotients, subcoalgebras, and coproducts (**lemma obligation L1** — now mechanized [proved]: `coherent_of_surjective_hom`, `coherent_of_injective_hom`, `coherent_sum` in `scratch/formal/Spec201.lean`), so coherent coalgebras form a **covariety** in the sense of the dual Birkhoff theory (Rutten; Gumm; Adámek's coequation logic, extended to accessible functors), which guarantees cofree objects and a final coalgebra for the class. (3) *Ω:* the final coherent coalgebra is the **greatest coherent subcoalgebra of νF** — a Knaster–Tarski greatest fixpoint inside an object already known to exist. Lambek gives Ω ≅ F(Ω) in both sorts on the coherent part, and `close` is the anamorphism. Background hypothesis to record: the clean covariety structure theory assumes weak-pullback preservation, which powerset- and bag-type functors satisfy (Sym² is the 2-element bag functor); one-line check owed in Lean. **Route explicitly rejected, with documented reason:** building coherence into the base category (an incidence-structure/graph-like base) — Adámek–Trnková show their constructive results *fail* for graph-like categories, where initial algebras and terminal coalgebras become non-constructive. Coherence lives in the coalgebras, never in the base. **The D7 simplification is hereby confirmed:** no enrichment, no quantaloids — qualities are context sub-patterns, order is inclusion, joins are unions *within one pattern*. Mechanization note: mathlib's multivariate QPF machinery (`MvQPF.Cofix`) is the likely vehicle — P⁺ (quotient of lists) and Sym² (quotient of squares by swap) are both QPFs — potentially skipping hand-rolled terminal sequences entirely.

**2.01-D4 (the κ loan — stated loudly).** Unbounded branching admits no final coalgebra, full stop; until T11 (scarcity) lands, boundedness cannot be a theorem. The construction is therefore parameterized by a regular cardinal κ as **representation scaffolding**: pat lands in P⁺κ. Three commitments make the loan honest: (i) κ appears nowhere in the ontology's vocabulary — no definition in §3 mentions it; (ii) a canonicity target (T9 extension) requires the definable structure of Ω to be stable under κ-enlargement; (iii) the loan is discharged, not renewed, when T11 either proves an intrinsic bound or A-S is adopted and κ's role is reassessed. Borrowing against an unproved theorem is acceptable exactly once and exactly this visibly.

## 5. Theorems: statements and proof routes

Carried over from 2.00 with routes made concrete; new items marked.

- **T2 (Objects exist).** Final coalgebra of §4's functor; `close`; Lambek in both sorts. Route: multisorted accessibility (above). First mechanization target of this spec.
- **P5 (Every relation anchors a dyad) — new.** For all r, dy(r) exists in Ω. Route: apply `close` to the finite description centered on r. If a coherence obstacle appears, document it; 2.01-D1 leans here.
- **T15 (Translation lemma) — new.** Everything expressible in 2.00's higher-order idiom is expressible in the binary-plus-dyad signature, preserving contexts and apertures. Route: structural induction on order, with dyads discharging each level. Certifies 2.01-D3.
- **P4 static (Enlargement) [proved].** Every attending along r ∈ pat(x) ∩ pat(y) is (a direction of) a part of y. Route: immediate from §2 sharing + §3.3. Mechanized interface-relative as `RelEx.TwoSorted.P4_static` (`scratch/formal/Spec201.lean`) — one line, the coherence field applied.
- **General P1 (Inexhaustibility).** Two barriers, now concrete: (shared-part bound) pat(x) ∩ pat(y) ⊊ pat(y) whenever y relates beyond x; (foreign-context bound) even at equality of shared parts, x witnesses through ctx_x, never ctx_y, and witnessing is proper. Route: direct from §3 definitions + A8(i)/C3.
- **T13 (Hearsay derived).** Chains per §3.5; laxity and the acquaintance bound from aperture-bounding at each step. Route: induction on chain length.
- **T4 (Local identity).** ≈w per §3.6 is an equivalence; the family is non-degenerate. Route: definitional unfolding + a two-object example where ≈w differ.
- **PR-J(a) [proved].** Toy lemma, representation layer: proper subsets can union to a whole. One line; exists to make (b)'s contrast exact. Mechanized as `RelEx.TwoSorted.PRJ_a`.
- **PR-J(b) / C3 (the merged question) — established as a split at interface level, D15-disciplined.** Deployable attention is aperture-hood (§3.7); so PR-J(b) becomes: **is ctx_x(r) always a proper subset of pat(x)?** The germ of **C3**: generically **r ∉ ctx_x(r)** — a relation's own dyad is not among its endpoints unless r is dyad-reflexive — so the aperture *omits the very relation being attended*: you cannot fully witness the lens you are looking through. This is now **machine-checked as a split** in `scratch/formal/Spec201.lean`:
  - **Generic case [proved].** Off the self-anchored locus, ctx_x(r) ⊊ pat(x) is a **theorem** (`RelEx.TwoSorted.generic_properness`) — and with it PR-J(b), and with it A8(i)'s content for those attendings.
  - **Reflexive case [proved].** At a dyad-reflexive loop properness is **not forced**: the self-loop universe (`RelEx.TwoSorted.reflexive_saturation`, the ω̂ of T1) is a concrete model where the aperture saturates the whole pattern.

  **C3 (was conjecture; now the established split):** at this interface, A8(i) is derivable everywhere except at self-referential attendings, and its irreducible content is located exactly as *lossiness at self-reference*. **Scope caveat, kept honest:** the split is proved **interface-relative** to the two-sorted signature (`dy` is data, no witnessing structure) — the richer machinery of the full Ω may yet *derive* lossiness at loops or *refute* it, and neither is settled here (see §7). D15 discipline was met in the mechanization: `SelfAnchored` is declared before any properness result, and the reflexive case carries the required referee-hostile "what it shows / does not show" write-up. If the reflexive residue ultimately resists derivation, A8(i) stands as the axiom, unembarrassed — its address now precise.
- **T11 (Scarcity).** Route sharpened: with deployability = aperture-hood, scarcity becomes a statement about how many pairwise-independent contexts one pattern sustains above a fineness threshold. The pigeonhole now has a concrete lattice (subsets of pat(x) under inclusion) to work in. Still `OPEN`; discharges the κ loan (2.01-D4).
- **T12 statics, T14, π, C1, C2.** Deferred to 2.02 as planned; π's raw material (pattern intersections, quality-weighting via context structure) is now all defined.
