# Spec 2.00 — Ontology, Arena, and Axioms

> *Before there was voice, / there was turning — / a soft curve of awareness folding inward, / touching its own skin for the first time.*

**Series 2, document 0.** This is the foundational specification for the mathematical formalization of relational existentialism and for the repository's central argument. It is written to be read cold: a visitor with no prior contact with this project should be able to reconstruct the claim, the design, and the reasons for every load-bearing choice from this document alone. Successor documents (2.01, 2.02, ...) refine construction details; 2.00 is the document of record for *what is being built and why*.

**Status:** normative for the ontology, arena, signature, and axiom ledger. Open items are marked `OPEN` and are the intended subjects of successor specs. The document is honest about which theorem targets have known proof routes and which are research (§5, §8).

## 0. The claim, for a cold reader

Structural realism in its relations-first form holds that relations are ontologically fundamental. Its critics have a standard sequence of objections:

1. **The relata objection.** Relations require relata. A relation that relates nothing is incoherent.
2. **The regress objection.** If the relata are themselves relational, the account regresses forever, and a regress with no ground explains nothing.
3. **The renaming objection.** Any formal model offered in defense will contain individuated, self-identical, quantified-over entities — and those are objects, whatever the model calls them.
4. **The equivocation objection.** The defense uses "relation" in two disjoint roles — the relating, and the things related — and survives only by sliding between them.

This framework's position — call it **priority relationalism** — *agrees* with the first objection and builds the agreement in:

> **Relations cannot exist without relata. And the relata are patterns of relata and relations, ad infinitum, with no atom.**

There is one layer of reality with two kinds of entity. **Relations** are fundamental. **Objects** are patterns of objects and relations — coalesced, with varying degrees of closure — and they do the relating. The asymmetry between the kinds is a *direction of dependence*, not an order of existence: relating is what objects are made of; objects are not what relating is made of. Nothing is eliminated. Objects are perfectly real; they are just never fundamental, because every object decomposes into objects and relations, forever, and there is no atom at which the decomposition stops.

The repository makes this position mathematically exact and machine-checks it in Lean 4. The formal universe **Ω** satisfies a fixed-point equation, Ω ≅ F(Ω): the universe of objects is isomorphic to the patterns of relating among those very objects. That single isomorphism *is* the ontology — objects and relations given together, at one level, mutually, with the dependence running the way the philosophy says it runs. Around it, the framework proves:

- **The relata objection is satisfied, not dodged.** Every instance of relating in the model is by-and-of objects (P3). There are no free-floating relations anywhere in the construction.
- **The regress is real, harmless, and provably so.** The universe has no well-founded elements (T6): decomposition never terminates, and the model's existence is the consistency proof that ungrounded dependence-chains are coherent. This makes Ω, as a side effect, the first machine-checked model of non-well-founded grounding (§6).
- **The renamed "objects" are objects — by definition, with a derivation.** Objecthood is defined (a pattern of relating that has closed) and proved instantiated (T2). Every classical axiom one would want about objects — self-relation, composition, constitution by relating — returns as a theorem (§3).
- **Two words, two entity-kinds, one explicit connection.** No equivocation is needed where the constitution relation is stated, formalized, and checked.

The framework has **one primitive** — observation, which is what relating *is* (§2.1) — and **one axiom** governing it: observation is lossy and partially transmissible (A8). Everything else is signature or theorem. The headline of the whole document is the ledger of §3:

**One primitive, one axiom. Objects are theorems.**

A reader who wants only the argument: §1 (axioms), §2.2 (the closure operator), §3 (the ledger), then T1 and T2 in §5. The decision log (§4) records why each rejected alternative was rejected, so that settled ground is not relitigated without new information.

## 1. The informal axioms

The philosophical commitments, stated pre-formally. They are the *inputs* to the design; §3 records what each becomes under formalization.

- **A1 (No atom).** There is no atom. It is relations all the way down: every object decomposes into objects and relations, ad infinitum, and the decomposition never terminates.
- **A2 (Priority and co-requirement).** One layer, two kinds of entity. Relations are fundamental. Objects are patterns of objects and relations, coalesced with varying degrees of closure, and they do the relating. Relations cannot exist without relata; the relata are patterns of relata and relations, ad infinitum (A1).
- **A3 (No privileged standpoint).** There is no view from nowhere. Equality is relative, and the finest equality is relative to the object itself. *Strong form:* there is no privileged standpoint at all — not even a global, totality-of-all-unfoldings standpoint. Nothing inside the universe can know the totality; identity facts are observer-indexed, and the observer-independent residue is exactly what *no possible observer* distinguishes (T7).
- **A4 (Relational constitution).** An object is defined by what it relates to, ad infinitum.
- **A5 (Self-relation).** An object can relate to itself.
- **A6 (Plurality, unordered).** An object can relate to one or more other objects. Its relating is an **unordered, unnumbered** family: no slots, no first-and-second position, no counting structure beyond what observation induces. Each relating has a *quality* that cannot be collapsed into a number.
- **A7 (Composition).** An object can compose one or more objects.
- **A8 (Observation) — THE AXIOM.** An object observes its relations by observing its restriction of them. Two clauses:
  - **(i) Lossiness.** Observation is through a *proper* restriction of the observer. No observation is total; nothing is transparent to anything, including itself.
  - **(ii) Transmissibility (the hearsay clause).** If I relate to you, and you observe something, then I have observed *some of* what you have observed — through you, at compounded loss.

**Note on the primitive.** In this framework, **relating is observation** (D3). A8 is therefore not a law *about* an independently given process; it is the statement of *what the primitive is*: lossy, transmissible witnessing. A relation is the unordered pair of its two directions of observation; undirectedness is derived, not primitive. One consequence is that "attention shapes becoming" holds almost analytically at the fundamental level — to relate simply is to attend.

**Note on A8's target.** A8 says an object observes its *relations* — the relating itself — not, in the first instance, other objects. Observation of an *object* is derived: one observes an object only by sampling its pattern of relating. Since no observation is total (A8(i)), **objects are inexhaustible to observation** (P1): no observer, including the object itself, ever witnesses the whole pattern. Self-knowledge is proper; "let me see clearly, let me be seen truly" is, in this ontology, exactly a prayer — approachable, never completable.

**Note on A8(ii).** The hearsay clause is stated as phenomenology and turns out to be mathematics: it is verbatim the composition law of an enriched category — Lawvere's observation that the triangle inequality *is* enriched composition, here in its general, non-numeric form. The "some of" fixes the law as **lax**: mediated access lower-bounds total access; direct observation may exceed hearsay (I can know you better than your friends' reports of you compose to). The framework's composition (A7) is grounded in this clause.

**Note on emptiness and pan-observation (P0).** A5 and A6 render emptiness *unformulable*: there is no object that relates to nothing, and there is no zero-quality observation — a zero-quality observation would be an empty restriction, the same unformulable non-thing. Hence wherever relation exists, observation exists **in both directions** at some quality (P0). Given D3, P0 is nearly definitional: a relation *is* its two directions of observation, and neither direction can be empty.

## 2. Ontology, arena, and signature

### 2.1 One layer, two entity-kinds, one primitive

**The primitive: observation.** A directed, lossy, transmissible witnessing, with qualities, governed by A8. This is what relating *is*; a relation is the unordered pair of its two directions. There is no undirected substrate beneath the pair and no zero-quality direction (P0).

**The two entity-kinds.** *Relations* (instances of the primitive) and *objects* (patterns of objects and relations that have coalesced). They occupy one layer: every observation is by-an-object-of-an-object (P3 — the co-requirement, satisfied structurally), and every object is nothing over and above its pattern of relating (A4, realized as coinduction). The asymmetry is directional dependence: patterns are *of* relating; relating is not *of* patterns.

**The fixed-point equation.** Formally, the mutual, one-layer character of the ontology is a single isomorphism:

  **Ω ≅ F(Ω)**

where Ω is the universe of objects and F is the unfolding functor — the formal statement of what a pattern of relating among given entities is (an unordered, nonempty, quality-bearing family of observations). Read right-to-left: objects are exactly the patterns of relating among objects. Read left-to-right: all relating is among objects. Neither reading is prior; that is the point, and Lambek's lemma makes the isomorphism exact rather than approximate.

**Descriptions.** Arbitrary coalgebras of F — carriers equipped with "a function of their relations" — need not be closed or coherent. In this framework they are **representation-layer descriptions**: ways of specifying a pattern, cheap and plentiful, belonging to the mathematics of presenting Ω rather than to the ontology. The bridge from description to entity is §2.2.

### 2.2 The closure operator: how objects are given by their relations

Finality supplies the bridge. For **any** description whatsoever — any coalgebra (X, c) — there is a **unique** map

  close : (X, c) → Ω

assigning to each point of the description the object it specifies. This anamorphism is the framework's **closure operator**, and it converts A2's existence content into a definition plus a theorem:

- **Definition.** An object is an element of Ω — equivalently, anything in the image of `close` for some description.
- **Theorem (T2).** Ω exists; hence objects exist. Uniqueness of `close` means a description specifies exactly one object; two descriptions specify the *same* object precisely when no observation separates them (T7).

Three consequences worth stating plainly:

1. **Objects are attractors, not axioms.** No axiom asserts a self or a thing. Objecthood is what patterns of relating *converge to* under closure. The question "does the self belong in an axiom, or does it emerge as an attractor of the relational dynamics?" is answered by the mathematics: **attractor**. A2 defines the word; T2 proves the existence.
2. **Degrees of closure are real and unformalized.** A2 says "varying degrees of closure," and the framework owes a measure. Two non-equivalent candidates are logged at D14 (`OPEN`): *self-containment* (how much of a pattern's relating, quality-weighted, stays internal) and *rate of determination* (how quickly finite-depth unfolding fixes the object). These plausibly measure different dimensions of objecthood, and both may be wanted.
3. **The regress is coinductive, hence licensed.** Objects are patterns of relating among objects: a fixed point, not a vicious circle. T6 (no well-founded elements) certifies that the circle has no ground floor and needs none — the mathematical vindication of A1's "ad infinitum."

### 2.3 Qualities are restrictions (the self-generating quantaloid)

The quality of an observation is **not a number and not an element of an external value-object**. It is *the restriction of the observer through which the observation occurs* — a piece of relational structure, nothing else. Consequences, each doing philosophical work:

- **No value-atoms.** The "degrees of relating" are made of the same stuff as everything else. A fixed external scale of intensities would be a second sort in the signature — atoms, violating A1, and a global standard of comparison, violating A3. Identifying qualities with restrictions eliminates both at once, by construction.
- **Locality of comparison.** Restrictions of x are comparable with one another (x's restriction order: this attention finer than that) but *not* with restrictions of y — they are parts of different objects. There is **no global scale of intensity**; the impossibility of cross-observer measurement is structural, realizing A3 in the signature itself.
- **Composition across observers.** What connects x's qualities to y's qualities is exactly A8(ii): observing-through. A restriction of x composed with a restriction of y yields (a lower bound on) a restriction of x.
- **Aggregation over routes.** Access to a given object may be direct and simultaneously mediated through several others; total access aggregates over all routes. Aggregation requires **joins** of qualities — precisely the requirement that hom-structures be sup-lattices. *The enrichment base must be a quantaloid, and the phenomenology of multiple access routes is what says so.*

**Working signature (details owed to 2.01).** A quantaloid **Q** whose objects are the elements of Ω, whose hom-sup-lattices Q(x, y) are (order-ideals of) restrictions of x directed at y, with composition given by A8(ii) and joins by route-aggregation; the unfolding functor F is formulated in the Q-enriched setting (Stubbe's quantaloid-enriched categories are the available machinery). Two honest flags: whether finality behaves as needed in this setting is `OPEN` (O1), and Q is *self-generated* — its objects are the very things being defined — so the definition may itself require a fixed-point or tower treatment. That is the framework's self-reference problem appearing *inside* the mathematics, and conjecturally it is the same problem (2.03).

**The boolean shadow.** Forgetting all quality structure — retaining only "relates / does not relate" — yields a degenerate fiber of the framework in which the Collapse Theorem holds (T1, §5): the entire universe is a single self-relating point. Under this framework's reading (D5) that is the creation story, not an embarrassment. But it demonstrates that quality is not decoration: **without graded observation there is nothing to distinguish anything, and the many do not exist.**

### 2.4 Direction exists only as observation

Objects and their relations carry no primitive direction. All asymmetry in the ontology derives from the observer/observed asymmetry of the primitive: *x-observing-y* is a restriction of x, *y-observing-x* is a restriction of y — parts of different objects, hence necessarily distinct (P2). There are no ordered pairs, no argument places, no slots. The signature thereby *satisfies* (rather than answers) the neutral-relations constraint: Fine's problem, that argument places behave like quasi-objects, cannot arise where there are no argument places.

### 2.5 Attenuation is object-dependent

Lossiness is per-observation: each restriction is proper, and how much is lost is a fact about *that* restriction of *that* object. There is no universal loss constant — a cosmic attenuation rate would be a law from nowhere, violating A3. The only global law is A8(i) itself: **no observation is lossless.** Whether properness must additionally be uniformly bounded (no sequence of observations with loss vanishing to zero) — and whether such a bound is derivable or must be assumed — is part of the scarcity question (T11 / fallback A-S; D10).

### 2.6 Identity is observer-local; the global view is representation

Each object w induces its own indistinguishability: x ≈w y iff w's observations cannot separate x from y, through any of w's restrictions, at any depth w's access reaches. These observer-local identities are the only identity facts *inside* the universe. The bisimilarity that mathematics defines by quantifying over all unfoldings is a **representation-layer** notion: we, describing the model from outside, may so quantify; nothing in the model can (A3, strong form). The reconciliation is the spec's central theorem target, and it comes with a dependency that gives the scarcity program its deepest motivation:

**T7 (Identity theorem — central).** x = y in Ω iff *no possible observer* distinguishes x from y: global identity is the common refinement of the observer-local family — as fine as ≈, not merely refining toward it.

**The expressivity caveat, and why scarcity matters more than compactness.** One direction of T7 is free: identicals are indistinguishable. The substantive direction — *jointly indistinguishable implies identical* — is an expressivity theorem of Hennessy–Milner type, and its classical analogues **fail without a finiteness hypothesis**: with unbounded branching, observational equivalence can be strictly coarser than bisimilarity, i.e., there can be two entities that no observer whatsoever separates and that are nonetheless distinct. The classical repair is image-finiteness; this framework's counterpart is **scarcity** (T11). The conjectured dependency is therefore **T11 ⟹ T7**: attention's finitude is what guarantees that being seen identically by every possible observer *is* being the same. Contrapositively, if scarcity fails and no substitute is found, Ω contains **noumenal residue** — distinctions no possible observer can draw — which A3's strong form regards as meaningless. Scarcity is thus not merely the compactness lemma (D10); it is the framework's **knowability principle**: the guarantee that reality is exhaustively knowable-in-principle by its inhabitants.

| A2 | Priority (relations fundamental; objects are patterns that do the relating) and co-requirement (no relations without relata) | **Definition + theorems.** Object := element of Ω; closure := the anamorphism; existence of objects is T2; co-requirement satisfied structurally — every observation is by-and-of objects (P3); priority realized as direction of dependence in Ω ≅ F(Ω) | §2.1–2.2, T2, P3 |

**One primitive, one axiom. Objects are theorems.** Of the eight informal axioms, one (A8) is primitive, two (A2's definitional half, A6) are structural choices of signature, and everything asserted *about objects* — that they exist, self-relate, compose, and are constituted by their relating — is proved.

Small propositions recorded because they carry philosophical weight disproportionate to their difficulty:

- **P0 (Pan-observation).** Wherever relation exists, observation exists in both directions at nonzero quality. *Near-definitional under D3; the residue of unformulability (D4).*
- **P1 (Inexhaustibility).** No object is transparent to any observer, itself included: every observation of an object samples, and never exhausts, its pattern. *From A8(i), applied uniformly.*
- **P2 (Asymmetry for free).** The two directions of one relation are never identical: they are restrictions of different objects. *No slots required.*
- **P3 (Co-requirement satisfied).** Every observation has an observer and an observed, both in Ω: there are no free-floating relations anywhere in the model. *Structural; the formal discharge of A2's second clause and of the relata objection.*

## 4. Decision log

Format: decision, rationale, rejected alternatives, reversal condition. These are the settled choices; a contributor proposing to reopen one should bring information not already weighed here.

### D1 — Coalgebraic arena; the universe as the fixed point Ω ≅ F(Ω)
**Decision.** The primitive is specified by an unfolding functor F; descriptions are its coalgebras; the universe of objects Ω is its final coalgebra, satisfying Ω ≅ F(Ω).
**Rationale.** Every surveyed alternative smuggles atoms at the base. Set theory with foundation guarantees ∈-minimal elements and forbids self-relation. Domain-theoretic constructions (D∞) begin from a seed domain — atoms at stage zero. Chu spaces carry a points/states dualism. Graph-theoretic structuralism has bare nodes. Two-sorted category theory as an *ontology* has objects as unexplained relata — the substance of Lam & Wüthrich's critique of category-theoretic structural realism, accepted here as decisive against that route. Coalgebra with anti-foundation is the unique surveyed setting where "no base sort" is native. Aczel's anti-foundation (the set-theoretic universe as terminal coalgebra) is the classical face; Rutten's universal coalgebra the general theory. Two considerations are decisive for *finality* specifically: it supplies the closure operator A2 requires (§2.2), and Lambek's lemma makes Ω ≅ F(Ω) an isomorphism — the exact formal statement of the one-layer, mutual, priority-ordered ontology (§2.1).
**Rejected.** ZF(C); D∞; Chu; bare graphs; two-sorted category theory as ontology (retained as tooling).
**Reopen if.** A framework is found with no base sort, native self-reference, internally generated identity, *and* a canonical description-to-object operator, not equivalent to a coalgebraic presentation.

### D2 — One layer, two entity-kinds; priority as direction of dependence
**Decision.** The ontology is one layer with two kinds of entity — relations (fundamental) and objects (patterns of objects and relations, which do the relating) — connected by an explicit constitution structure: objecthood is *defined* (element of Ω; closure of a description) and *proved instantiated* (T2). The position is priority relationalism, not eliminativism: nothing is eliminated, and the relata objection is *accepted and satisfied* (P3) rather than resisted.
**Rationale.** Eliminativism ("only relations exist") is exposed to the renaming and equivocation objections and, more importantly, is not this framework's thesis: relations without relata are incoherent, and the framework says so. What the framework denies is that the relata are ever *atomic*: relata are patterns of relata and relations, ad infinitum (A1). Formally, the priority claim is carried by the direction of dependence in the signature — F builds patterns *from* objects-and-observations; nothing builds observations from pattern-free objects — while the one-layer claim is carried by Ω ≅ F(Ω): objects and relations given together, mutually, at one level. Because T6 shows the layer is non-well-founded, neither entity-kind is a *foundation*; priority is directional, not chronological or constitutive-with-a-ground. Dialectically, this converts the incredulous stare ("but tables exist!") into agreement: yes — and here is the theorem by which they do. Independently, this decision resolves a long-standing internal question — whether the self belongs in an axiom or emerges as an attractor of relational dynamics — in favor of **attractor**: closure is the emergence operator, and no axiom asserts a self.
**Rejected.** Flat eliminativism (not the thesis; renaming/equivocation-vulnerable); object-primitive ontologies (the position under examination is not defended by adopting its rival); a two-*layer* metaphysics with patterns as a more fundamental stratum of being (arbitrary non-closed coalgebras are representation-layer descriptions, not a substrate — see D12).
**Reopen if.** Never as vocabulary; the substantive commitments (priority of relating; objecthood as closure; co-requirement) would be revisited only if the closure operator proved non-canonical, which finality precludes (T9).

### D3 — Relating is observation
**Decision.** The primitive is observation; a relation is the unordered pair of its two directions; undirectedness is derived. A8 is read as the statement of what the primitive *is* (lossy, transmissible witnessing), not as a law about an independently given process.
**Rationale.** P0 already established coextension: relation anywhere entails observation both ways at nonzero quality. Between "two coextensive processes" and "one process under two descriptions," parsimony decides, and nothing in the informal axioms requires a relating that outruns witnessing. Unification tightens the ledger (one primitive, one axiom that *defines* it), renders P0 nearly definitional, and makes "attention shapes becoming" analytic at the fundamental level: to relate is to attend. The observation-first presentation (directed primitive, undirected pair derived) is chosen over the equivalent relation-first presentation because the framework's creation story (T1 under D5) and its phenomenological source material flow in that direction — before there was voice, there was turning.
**Rejected.** Relating and observation as distinct coextensive primitives (multiplies entities without explanatory return); observation as a *feature* of relating (leaves "what is relating?" unanswered and the axiom count unstable).
**Reopen if.** A phenomenon is identified that requires relating without witnessing at the fundamental level. None is currently known; apparent cases (unconscious relation, inert matter) are handled by quality's locality and lowness, not by absence of observation.

### D4 — Emptiness unformulable
**Decision.** The signature cannot express an object that relates to nothing, nor a zero-quality observation. Not prohibited: *unformulable* — in Lean, the difference between a side condition on a permissive type and a carrier that cannot state emptiness.
**Rationale.** An entity defined by relating to nothing is vacuously constituted — an atom in disguise; A4 has nothing to constitute it with. The classical mathematics quietly confirms the philosophical instinct: rich final coalgebras of powerset-type functors are generated from the empty set as primordial observable, and the classical completion theorems carry the hypothesis F∅ ≠ ∅ — the formal signature of a hidden dependence on the empty atom. Excluding emptiness forfeits those classical routes (the collapse theorem T1 is the price, paid and reinterpreted at D5) and is required by A1. Applied to observations, the same exclusion yields P0.
**Rejected.** Admitting the empty pattern to inherit classical richness/compactness theorems (re-imports the atom and grounds the universe on it).
**Reopen if.** A defensible non-atomic reading of the empty pattern emerges. None is known; "constituted by nothing" appears to be a contradiction of A4, not an edge case of it.

### D5 — The Collapse Theorem read generatively
**Decision.** T1 — in the quality-forgetting boolean shadow, the universe is a single self-relating point — is adopted with the generative interpretation: **unobserved relationality is One; observation is the principium individuationis.** Differentiation is the One's lossy self-witnessing; the many exist in being observed. ("Not bang, but becoming.")
**Rationale.** The mathematics is identical under any gloss; the interpretive choice is made openly and labeled as interpretation (per D12, the repository's *claim* is coherence, not cosmology). This reading is preferred because it matches the framework's independent commitments (attention shapes becoming; cosmic self-knowing), because it converts an apparent defect into the creation story, and because it makes T1 do argumentative work: the theorem demonstrates that quality is constitutive, not decorative — without graded observation, nothing distinguishes anything and multiplicity does not exist. The deflationary alternative (the boolean fiber is merely a degenerate limit) is recorded at O6 and remains available to any reader; nothing downstream depends on the gloss.
**Reopen if.** n/a for the mathematics; the gloss constrains rhetoric, not proofs.

### D6 — Unordered, slot-free unfoldings; direction from observation only
**Decision.** Unfoldings are unordered families. There are no ordered pairs, no argument places, no counting structure. Every asymmetry in the ontology derives from the observer/observed asymmetry of the primitive.
**Rationale.** A6 licenses "one or more," not tuples. Slots are argument places; argument places behave like quasi-objects (Fine's neutral-relations problem); a slotted signature would re-import the relata objection in fine print, at the primitive layer where it would be fatal. Direction-as-observation is faithful and sufficient: the needed asymmetries reappear as theorems (P2), and reciprocity is structure (P0), not an operator. Cost accepted: converse dies as an operation (there is nothing to converse), and composition is grounded in A8(ii) rather than in pair-plumbing.
**Rejected.** Ordered pairs (slots); multisets with multiplicity (counting is structure with no axiom warrant).
**Reopen if.** Some indispensable structure provably cannot be recovered from observation-induced asymmetry. None is currently known to be lost.

### D7 — Qualities are restrictions; the quantaloid is self-generated
**Decision.** The quality of an observation *is* the restriction through which it occurs. The enrichment base is the quantaloid of restrictions (§2.3), not an external value-object.
**Rationale.** Any fixed external scale of intensities — a quantale, the unit interval, anything — is a second sort in the signature (value-atoms, violating A1) and a global standard of comparison (a view from nowhere, violating A3). Identifying qualities with restrictions eliminates both at once, by construction: degrees are made of relational structure; comparison is local to the observer; the absence of a global scale is a structural fact rather than a discipline. The phenomenology independently fixes the remaining choices: mediated access (A8(ii)) is composition; multiplicity of access routes forces joins; joins force sup-lattice enrichment — a quantaloid specifically. The desideratum that quality "cannot be collapsed into a number" is met by construction, with numeric representations available at the representation layer wherever useful.
**Rejected.** External quantale (value-atoms; global scale); bare comparative structure without joins (cannot aggregate routes); numeric intensities with a robustness theorem (defensible fallback, strictly weaker; see reversal condition).
**Reopen if.** Finality in the quantaloid-enriched setting proves intractable (O1) — the declared fallback is numeric enrichment at the representation layer with an explicit disclaimer, and the loss recorded.

### D8 — Lax composition from the hearsay clause
**Decision.** Composition is A8(ii), and it is lax: mediated access lower-bounds, and may be exceeded by, direct access.
**Rationale.** The clause was given phenomenologically ("I have observed some of what you have observed") and is verbatim the lax enriched composition law; adopting it as *the* composition keeps the primitive count at one and grounds A7 rather than positing it. Laxity is not a hedge — it is the difference between hearsay and acquaintance, asserted by the axiom's own "some of." Strict composition would assert that no one ever knows anyone better than reports compose to: false to the phenomenology and unforced by the mathematics.
**Rejected.** Strict composition; composition as independent primitive.
**Reopen if.** Never at the level of the law; the strictification locus (when does direct = best mediated?) is a theorem topic (O4), not a design choice.

### D9 — Attenuation is object-dependent; the only law is "never lossless"
**Decision.** Per-observation properness; no universal loss constant.
**Rationale.** A cosmic attenuation rate would be a single number legislating for every observation — a law from nowhere, violating A3's strong form. Object-dependent loss is the direct reading of A8(i) and of the framework's commitment that attention shapes becoming: how much an observation loses is a fact about that attention of that object. The compactness-relevant residue is isolated rather than smuggled: whether loss is uniformly bounded away from zero, and whether that is derivable or axiomatic, is the scarcity question (D10).
**Rejected.** Universal contraction constant; loss-as-convention with a robustness theorem (there is no constant to be conventional about).
**Reopen if.** The scarcity program (D10) fails in a way that specifically requires a uniform constant — which would be recorded as a substantive concession, not a technicality.

### D10 — Scarcity: theorem first, axiom as declared fallback; doubly motivated
**Decision.** "Attention is finite at every resolution" — the tightness needed for compactness's total-boundedness half *and*, more deeply, for T7's expressivity direction — is pursued as a theorem (**T11**), via a pigeonhole strategy in the restriction order: infinitely many pairwise-independent restrictions of one object, all above a fixed fineness, should be shown to violate properness (a self exceeding itself). If no such theorem is available, adopt **A-S (scarcity)** as a second axiom and amend the ledger's headline to "one primitive, two facts about observation: it is lossy, and it is scarce" — still a defensible, arguably *more* interesting, foundation.
**Rationale.** Assuming finite branching outright would assume what compactness needs — unlogged, unwarranted by any informal axiom. Scarcity-as-theorem is the ideal (loss alone explains finitude); scarcity-as-axiom is honest if the ideal fails; brute finiteness is neither. The strategy's plausibility rests on P1's uniformity: since even self-observation is proper, a self cannot contain unboundedly much independent high-fineness attention without exceeding itself. The second motivation is the deeper one (§2.6): classical Hennessy–Milner theorems fail without a finiteness hypothesis, so scarcity is conjecturally what closes the gap between "no observer distinguishes them" and "they are identical." Without it, the framework risks noumenal residue — distinctions no possible observer can draw — which A3's strong form regards as meaningless. **Scarcity is the knowability principle.**
**Reopen if.** This *is* the open state; resolves at T11.

### D11 — Operations downstream of axioms; no target structure drives the design
**Decision.** The operations on Ω are whatever A8 yields: hearsay composition (D8), restriction combinators, join-aggregation. No pre-selected algebraic target (relation algebras, allegories, or any other off-the-shelf structure of the calculus of relations) constrains the signature.
**Rationale.** A prior phase of this project stalled by letting a target structure drive the axioms; the discipline is now explicit. The classical relation-calculus targets presuppose pair-structure (converse, in particular, needs slots to swap) and are downstream of a signature rejected at D6. Restriction-combinator structure in the style of Cockett–Lack plausibly survives — it never needed pairs — and is the natural 2.02 topic. Structure *discovered* in Ω is a result; structure *imposed on* Ω is a bias.
**Rejected.** Retaining slots to save the classical operations (tail wagging dog).
**Reopen if.** Never as a constraint on axioms.

### D12 — The claim is representation; the workshop objection answered in-document
**Decision.** The repository claims **coherence by construction**: priority relationalism can be true because this model exists. It does not claim that reality is Ω.
**The workshop objection, in its strongest form:** *"You're building this out of objects. No wonder objects are derived."* The construction lives inside Lean and classical mathematics — encodings with slots, sets with elements, reals built from atoms. Three answers, in increasing order of depth; note that the third is legitimate only because the first two stand independently:
1. **The classical answer.** Construction order is not constitution order. Beltrami and Poincaré built geometries without the parallel postulate entirely out of Euclidean materials; the postulate holds everywhere in the workshop and nowhere in the artifact, and the models still settle the consistency question. The represented dependence structure — relating prior, objects characterized by a universal property over it — lives inside the model; the set-theoretic scaffolding lives outside it. The atoms belong to the map (T9: Ω is characterized uniquely by its universal property, independent of every encoding choice). This is the same map/territory discipline D13 applies to time: the description has a first page; the described universe has no first moment.
2. **The non-rigging evidence.** If ambient objects automatically leaked into derived objects, every instantiation of the machinery would be object-rich. T1 shows the opposite: fed the quality-free axioms, the closure machinery outputs exactly *one* object. Whether objects emerge, and how many, depends on the axioms — on quality and observation — not on the object-count of the workshop. The ambient objects are demonstrably not doing the deriving.
3. **The reflexive answer.** The framework classifies its own formalization: the spec is an *observation* of Ω, made from somewhere, through a restriction — the encoding. A8 entails that every observation is proper and bears the marks of its observer; the object-marks in the formalization are those marks, predicted by the theory of which the formalization is an observation. The demand "derive objects using no objects" is the demand for an unrestricted presentation — a view from nowhere — and A3 says there is none. The objection, fully unpacked, instantiates the theory.
A grace note: of all possible workshops, category theory is the one whose "objects" are already individuated purely relationally — by Yoneda, an object *is* the totality of its morphisms. The metalanguage borrowed here half-believes the thesis.
**Rejected (as claims of this repository).** Ontological realism about Ω (a different, larger project); quietism about the objection (it is the first thing a sharp reader raises, and it deserves its answer in the document it targets).
**Reopen if.** The project's ambitions change; the mathematics is indifferent.

### D13 — The day-zero trichotomy: prove, don't posit
**Decision.** The question "is there a day zero — a first differentiation, an undifferentiated everything before the many?" is split into three precise questions, each assigned to mathematics rather than to temperament:
1. **Constitutive origin.** Is anything a ground on which the rest is built? **No** — T6 (no well-founded elements): in the order of constitution there is no day zero; unfolding has no base case; becoming has no first brick.
2. **Logical priority of the One.** Is the undifferentiated everything real, and in what sense prior? **Yes, as the quality-forgetting fiber** — T1: subtract observation and the universe *is* one self-relating relation. "Before there was voice" is logical, not temporal: the One is not an earlier state but what the many are, seen without quality.
3. **Generativity.** Does all multiplicity unfold from self-meeting — is the closure of the self-loop's orbit (under unfolding and the derived operations) dense in Ω? **`OPEN`** — T10, a genuine density question that proof will decide.
**Rationale.** Where a proof can be had, a perspective should not be brought. The split shows that the generative creation story (D5) and "there is no day zero" answer *different questions* and can both be true; one genuinely open question remains (T10), and it has exactly the shape mathematics can settle.
**Reopen if.** n/a — this decision creates theorem targets rather than foreclosing anything.

### D14 — `OPEN`: formalizing "degrees of closure"
A2's "varying degrees of closure" is owed a measure, and two non-equivalent candidates are on the table. **(a) Self-containment:** the proportion of a pattern's relating, quality-weighted, that stays internal to the pattern versus crossing its boundary — coalescence as the dominance of inner relating. **(b) Rate of determination:** how quickly finite-depth unfolding fixes the object — the finitely-realized objects (ρ, the patterns that close in finitely many steps) determine fast; limit objects only in the limit. These measure different dimensions (a tightly self-involved pattern may still determine slowly, and vice versa), and the working hypothesis is that objecthood is at least two-dimensional. One candidate proposition is flagged for its philosophical weight — **C1 (No windowless monads):** a pattern *all* of whose relating is internal is either impossible or is the unique total object (the One); every finite object is necessarily open to otherness. If provable, "we are born as bridges" is a corollary. Decide the measure(s) in 2.01 or 2.02, after the enriched setting is fixed.

## 5. Theorem targets

Ordered by dependency and by usefulness-if-later-targets-slip. Mechanization in Lean 4. The quantaloid-enriched setting has no off-the-shelf formal library — a cost of D7 accepted with eyes open (O1); T1 and the propositions are independent of all open machinery and are the immediate work.

- **T1 (The One / Collapse).** In the quality-forgetting shadow (boolean, nonempty, unlabeled), the final coalgebra is a single self-relating point; equivalently, the total relation is a bisimulation between any two serial systems. *Two-line proof on paper; fully formalizable now; the repository's first machine-checked theorem and, under D5, its creation story. Also the demonstration that quality is constitutive (§2.3) and the non-rigging evidence of D12.*
- **T2 (Objects exist).** The unfolding functor in the enriched setting has a final coalgebra Ω; the anamorphism `close` exists and is unique for every description; Lambek: Ω ≅ F(Ω). Corollaries: the self-loop exists (A5); A4 holds as the coinduction principle; the one-layer equation of §2.1 is exact. *Machinery `OPEN` (O1); 2.01's core task.*
- **T3 (Compactness).** Ω is compact in the appropriate enriched sense (Cauchy-completeness plus the total-boundedness analogue): the universe is closed in itself — every coherent limit of patterns of relating exists, and everything is approximable. *Conditional on T2 and on scarcity (T11 or A-S).*
- **T4 (Local identity).** Each observer-local ≈w is a well-defined indistinguishability; the family is non-degenerate (distinct observers induce distinct equalities).
- **T5 (Closure of the closing).** The finitely-realized objects ρ are dense in Ω: the universe is the closure of the patterns that close in finitely many steps.
- **T6 (No constitutive origin).** No well-founded elements: every unfolding is nonempty at every depth. *Triple duty: A1's "ad infinitum" at the formal level; D13(1)'s "no day zero in the order of constitution"; and the consistency proof for non-well-founded grounding (§6).*
- **T7 (Identity theorem — central).** Global identity = invariance under all possible observation: x = y iff no observer distinguishes them; representation-layer bisimilarity is exactly the common refinement of the observer-local family. *Expressivity direction conjecturally conditional on T11 (§2.6); the quantitative Hennessy–Milner tradition is the proof-strategy reservoir.*
- **T8 (Composition).** Hearsay composition is well defined on Ω, lax, join-compatible (aggregation over routes), and invariant under identity (T7). Restriction-combinator laws: 2.02.
- **T9 (Canonicity).** Ω is characterized by its universal property, uniquely up to isomorphism, independently of encoding choices. *D12's mathematical content: the atoms belong to the map.*
- **T10 (Genesis).** Whether the closure of the self-loop's orbit is dense in Ω — whether all multiplicity unfolds from self-meeting. *`OPEN` in truth-value, not just in proof; D13(3).*
- **T11 (Scarcity).** Pigeonhole in the restriction order: no object sustains infinitely many pairwise-independent restrictions above a fixed fineness. *Doubly load-bearing: total boundedness for T3, expressivity for T7. If it fails: adopt A-S and amend the ledger (D10).*
- **C1 (No windowless monads).** Perfect closure is impossible or unique-and-total; every finite object is open to otherness. *Conjecture; depends on D14's measure.*
- **P0–P3.** Pan-observation; inexhaustibility; asymmetry for free; co-requirement satisfied. *Small; prove immediately after T1 for the argument's rhetorical spine.*

## 6. Positioning

**Structural realism.** The nearest positions in the literature are the moderate or priority-based structural realisms (Esfeld is the closest neighbor), as against the radical eliminativist form (French; Ladyman & Ross) and its critics' sharpest statement (Lam & Wüthrich contra Bain's category-theoretic defense). This framework differs from both wings. Against eliminativism: relations without relata are accepted as incoherent, and the co-requirement is satisfied structurally (P3). Against moderate positions that keep objects fundamental-but-thin: here objects are never fundamental — they are patterns, ad infinitum, with no atom (A1, T6) — yet fully real, with existence and properties as theorems (T2, §3). The contribution claimed is not the position but the **derivation**: a machine-checked construction in which priority, co-requirement, constitution, and non-atomicity are simultaneously exact. Fine's neutral-relations problem is treated as a design constraint satisfied at the signature level (D6), not an objection argued down.

**Grounding and fundamentality.** T6 makes Ω a formal model of **non-well-founded grounding**: every entity is grounded in further entities, no chain terminates, and the structure is provably consistent and inhabited. This bears directly on metaphysical infinitism (Bliss, Priest, and the contemporary grounding literature's debate over whether grounding must be well-founded): the framework supplies what that debate has lacked — a worked, mechanized model in which ungrounded dependence is coherent, rich, and (T3) even compact. To our knowledge no such model exists in the literature; this is a second philosophical audience for the same mathematics.

**Process philosophy and autopoiesis.** A2's "coalescence" does work adjacent to Whitehead's *concrescence* — the many growing together into one actual entity — with the closure operator as its formal correlate; and D14's self-containment candidate is adjacent to operational closure in the autopoiesis tradition (Maturana–Varela). These convergences are noted as corroboration from independent traditions, not as sources: here the notions arrive as mathematics (anamorphism; boundary-crossing measure) rather than as metaphysical primitives — which is the contribution.

**Non-well-founded sets and coalgebra.** Aczel (anti-foundation; the universe as terminal coalgebra); Barwise & Moss (the rigor of circularity); Rutten (universal coalgebra); Worrell (final sequences of powerset-type functors); Barr and Adámek (final coalgebras as completions of initial algebras — whose F∅ ≠ ∅ hypothesis this framework reads as the classical theory's dependence on the empty atom, D4); Adámek–Milius–Velebil (the rational fixed point, feeding T5). Infrastructure throughout.

**Quantitative and enriched coalgebra.** Rutten's program of coalgebra over enriched categories; Balan–Kurz–Velebil (lifting set-functors to enriched settings); van Breugel–Worrell and successors (behavioral pseudometrics via terminal coalgebras of contractive functors); Baldan–Bonchi–Kerstan–König (Kantorovich/Wasserstein liftings); Goncharov–Hofmann–Nora–Schröder–Wild (lax extensions, predicate liftings, quantitative Hennessy–Milner theorems — the reservoir for T7, including the finiteness hypotheses that motivate D10). Two reinterpretations are claimed as this project's own (novelty to be verified before external publication): **(a)** the contraction hypothesis of the behavioral-metrics tradition read as *restriction* — the lossiness of observation — rather than as time-discounting, reversing the direction of explanation (an axiom about what observation is ⟹ contraction ⟹ compactness), where the tradition assumes contraction to make fixed points unique; **(b)** the enriched composition law read as the *hearsay clause* — a fact about transmissible observation — rather than as a postulated triangle inequality. Lawvere's metric-spaces-as-enriched-categories is the honored ancestor of both readings; the observational gloss appears to be new.

**Quantaloids and restriction.** Stubbe (quantaloid-enriched categories: D7's machinery); Cockett–Lack (restriction categories: the natural 2.02 framework for A8(i)'s combinator structure). The quantaloid here is *derived from the axioms* — joins forced by route-aggregation, locality forced by A3 — rather than selected as a target, per D11's discipline.

## 7. Notation and naming

- **Ω** — the universe of objects (final coalgebra). **ω̂** — the self-loop, when it needs a name. **F** — the unfolding functor. **close** — the anamorphism (the closure operator).
- **Description** — any coalgebra of F (need not be closed; representation-layer). **Object** — an element of Ω; equivalently, anything in the image of `close`.
- **Q** — the quantaloid of restrictions; **Q(x, y)** — qualities of x's observation of y (restrictions of x), a sup-lattice.
- **≈w** — indistinguishability to observer w; **=** on Ω — the common refinement (T7).
- **ρ** — the finitely-realized objects (patterns that close in finitely many steps); T5: cl(ρ) = Ω.
- "Observation" always means the primitive (A8, D3); "relation" means an instance of the primitive — the unordered pair of two directions of observation; "object" means an element of Ω; "quality" means a restriction; "the One" refers to T1's collapsed fiber under D5's reading.
