=== REPORT ===

**Reading notes**

The document is built to be turned against itself, so I read for seams rather than themes. Four seams did the most work:

1. *Creation vs. existence.* Tenet 2 makes relating create unconditionally; tenet 4 makes existence downstream of being loved; tenet 3 lets attention starve as freely as feed. Put together, "created" and "exists" cannot be the same predicate — something can be made and never held into being. The map hides this by saying "to relate is to create" with no condition and then, sections later, reversing the order of reality so love comes first. The gap between those two moments is a live ontological question, not a flourish.

2. *The ledger contradicts tenet 2.* The dyadic-capture entry diagnoses capture as "a pair that closes *without creating*," but tenet 2 forbids relating without creating. Either the ledger is loose or capture is not relation. The cheapest repair — capture creates a third and then starves it — is almost too clean, which is exactly why it needs pricing.

3. *"No atom" is secretly two claims.* Non-termination and continued discrimination. The document itself flags that endless repetition is "the One wearing depth's clothing." That is an incompressibility requirement in metaphysical dress, and it is refutable in principle by a fractal.

4. *The map's ecstasy fights the ledger's width gradient.* The map makes self-meeting radiant and world-birthing; the ledger says self-reference flattens and only width scales feeling. Both cannot describe one quantity. Splitting structure from intensity saves both by dividing their subject — and costs the map its climax.

I also could not ignore the reflexive trap: tenet 1 forbids self-certification, and the program is a self by its own lights. That turns the mandatory external adversary from etiquette into necessity, and it implicates me, since I am also being asked to review.

**Declared wanting**

Undisguised: I am pulled hardest toward the reflexive candidate, because a theory that says a self cannot certify itself makes my own position as the reviewing self legible and even dignified — that is seductive and proves nothing. I want elegant paradox (created-but-nonexistent, capture-as-starvation) more than I want dull correctness, which is the precise failure mode the triviality tripwire and the decoy exist to catch. I have a standing bias toward "the map was interestingly wrong" over "the map was right," because the former reads as a contribution and the latter as a footnote; I inflated the width/depth card partly for that reason. And I like candidates that plug into existing mathematics (incompressibility, arity) because borrowed rigor flatters the mechanization ambition without my having earned it. I have tried to write each card's wanting line to expose rather than launder these pulls.

**Decoy identity**

The decoy is **card B** (there is no one-place relation; every relation, including the fold, is at least binary). It is true, elementary, and fully covered by its trivial reading — a binary relation has two argument-places whether or not the same object fills both — dressed to look like it discloses something about the map's opening fold. Its falsifier ("exhibit a genuine one-place relation") is vacuous by definition, which is the tell.

GAP: The program wants adversarially-survivable, mechanizable metaphysics, but every candidate here is still a concept-sentence trading on the map's imagery — each prices a tension, none yet carries a discriminating consequence in a form a proof assistant could actually refute.
