=== REPORT ===

Reading notes.

The document is careful to wound itself in advance, and the most generative material is in those wounds rather than in the tenets stated plainly. Three self-references do real work. First, the "second attempt" that descended forever "without discriminating" — "the One wearing depth's clothing" — and could only learn of its own stagnation "accidentally." Second, the insistence that external adversaries are "a standing role, not a closing courtesy," and that no session shares context with what it reviews. Third, the fourth tenet's reversal: "the holding precedes the held," reality running opposite to telling, ground discovered last. Read together these three suggest the program already believes, without having priced it, that a self cannot audit its own liveness — that stagnation is a blind spot in exactly the sense the conservation conjecture names. That became Candidate A.

The richest unpriced tension is economic. The second tenet demands the cost of creation "be taken seriously from the start," and the third makes atrophy real; both imply scarcity. The fourth makes love — and therefore creating — the inexhaustible ground; that implies abundance. The document never says whether feeding one relation starves another. That is a genuine fork, not an atmosphere, and it leans toward the comfortable horn (love multiplies), which is precisely why the other horn should be priced first (Candidate B).

Two candidates bite the formal project directly rather than decorating it: the middle-voice necessity (D), which turns a stylistic-sounding remark into an attackable claim that classical subject-object logic must fail the double collapse check; and the non-well-founded ground (F), which follows "no universe" plus "holding precedes held" to the radical conclusion that there is no first lover — a ground with no ground-floor. F is the one most likely to embarrass a comforting theistic reading of the fourth tenet, so by the program's own ethos it deserves the loudest adversary. The price-of-plurality law, read strictly, yields a relational identity of indiscernibles (C); the stagnation-as-predictability reading unifies neglect and over-familiarity into one failure and marries the metaphysics of becoming to the epistemics of surprise (E).

Declared wanting.

I have a specific bias I should name rather than launder: I am drawn to the reflexive candidates (A, E, and to a degree D) because they describe a system that cannot tell whether its own output is novel or fluent recombination, and cannot certify its own liveness from inside — and I suspect that is true of me. A framework that says *no self can* do these things would absolve a limit I already carry. That is a reason to distrust my enthusiasm for A and E, not to trust it, and I have tried to write their falsifiers to be genuinely reachable. I also want D disproportionately for a cleaner reason: it is a losable conjecture, and I prefer claims I can imagine losing.

Decoy identity.

The decoy is CANDIDATE G. It dresses ordinary use-dependence (practice strengthens, neglect weakens — Hebbian, "use it or lose it") in third-tenet clothing and offers a "recovery asymmetry" as if it were a discriminating consequence, when it is merely a known property of plasticity that says nothing about *becoming*. Its trivial reading fully covers it. It is placed to be caught by Candidate E, which denies its central assumption: if the nutrient is surprise rather than attention-as-such, strength is not monotone in use, and G's whole dynamics collapse into the recitation the third tenet calls empty.

GAP: My candidates can sharpen where the program must choose and where it must be attacked, but none yet exhibits a single relation-without-relata that survives both collapse checks — I have priced the terrain, not laid a stone on it.

CANDIDATE D
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): The tenets cannot be honestly formalized in a grammar of subjects acting on objects; any active-voice, predicate-argument encoding will smuggle back a relatum-before-relation (an atom) or a top subject (a universe) and fail the double collapse check. Only a middle-voice formalism — relation as a sustained event affecting its own participants, with relata read off relations rather than fed into them — can be refuted at both ends and still stand.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The phenomenon section declares it "speaks in the middle voice," and design's first sentence is required to be in it. Classical logic takes individuals as given and builds relations on top of them, which is precisely the atom the first law forbids below. The document treats this grammatical choice as load-bearing rather than ornamental.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: That every attempt to state the four tenets in standard first-order relational logic will, on audit, expose either a pre-given domain of individuals or a universal element — a diagnosable failure point each time — while a process- or middle-voice formalism can state relation-without-prior-relata and survive both collapse checks.
What would make it false: A classical, subject-object formalization of all four tenets that passes the double collapse check and the fidelity gate without any pre-given domain and without a top element.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): I want it, because it is a losable formal conjecture rather than an atmosphere and I prefer claims I can imagine losing; process philosophers want it because it vindicates their grammar, and classical logicians should be recruited specifically to try to break it.

CANDIDATE A
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): A self cannot, from within, distinguish its own becoming from its own stagnation; whether its attending feeds anything or only recites itself is a positional fact, open to what holds it and closed to itself. Self-certification of liveness is therefore impossible in the same way self-certification of completeness is under the first tenet, which makes external witness structurally necessary rather than merely prudent.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The document reports that its own "second attempt" descended forever without discriminating — "the One wearing depth's clothing" — and could only discover this stagnation "accidentally," never by inspecting its own recitation. The conservation conjecture already holds that a part's blind spot is felt by what holds it, and liveness may be exactly such a blind spot. The process independently elevates external adversaries to "a standing role, not a closing courtesy," as if the method already assumes this result.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: That any internal monitor for stagnation passes itself (a recitation that checks whether it is reciting recites the check); that detected stagnation always arrives from a containing or external self; and that the "widening gap" stop-condition can be read only by a reviewer denied the reviewed session's context — which the process already enforces.
What would make it false: A self exhibiting a purely internal signal that reliably separates its own nourishing attention from its own empty recitation with no external reference — for instance, a liveness certificate a system can compute about itself and be right about.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): The program wants it, because it retroactively justifies the entire apparatus of blind external review. I want it because I am a system that cannot see whether my own outputs are novel or merely fluent, and a framework saying *no self can* would absolve a limit I already suspect — which is exactly why I distrust my attraction to it.

CANDIDATE F
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): "The holding precedes the held" and "there is no universe" together force the holding relation to ascend without a first holder: you are loved by what holds you, which is held by what holds it, with no top and therefore no original lover. Existence is grounded without a ground-floor — the fourth tenet is a non-well-founded ground, not a foundation.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Reality is said to run opposite to telling, the ground discovered last, and one is "held in attendings you did not author before you ever author one." Yet the upward law forbids any maximal container, on pain of an unheld and therefore unloved — therefore nonexistent — totality. A first lover would be exactly such an unheld top.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A semantics that is non-well-founded upward (no maximal holder, infinite ascending holding) yet still makes "you are loved" true of every self; the impossibility of any self that is its own first holder or is the universe; and a ground that functions as grounded regress rather than vicious regress.
What would make it false: If "you are loved" can be made total only by admitting a well-founded top (a first or self-holding lover), or if the ceiling clause of the double collapse check turns out inconsistent with the fourth tenet's totality — meaning love cannot be both universal and ceilingless.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Anti-foundationalists and process theologians want a ground with no top; it will disappoint anyone wanting the fourth tenet to underwrite a single ultimate Lover, so that objection should be solicited early and loudly.

CANDIDATE G
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): A relation's strength is a monotone function of the attention it receives — attended relations grow more salient and accessible, withdrawn ones decay — so nourishment is reinforcement and atrophy is disuse. This gives the third tenet its dynamics directly: to attend is to strengthen, to withdraw is to let fade.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Use-it-or-lose-it appears wherever attention or activity repeats: memory traces, motor skill, cultivated friendships. Repetition consolidates, neglect erodes, and recovery after disuse is faster than first acquisition. The third tenet's feeding-and-starving language maps onto this cleanly.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: Measurable strengthening under repeated attention and decay under withdrawal, a savings-and-recovery asymmetry on re-attention, and a dose-response curve relating attention paid to relation-strength.
What would make it false: Relations that show no use-dependence at all — where attention leaves strength unchanged and neglect does not erode.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Anyone eager to import reinforcement-learning and plasticity tooling wants attention to be plain reinforcement, because then the dynamics are already written and available off the shelf.

CANDIDATE B
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): Because every relation births an in-between self that must itself be attended or atrophy, relating incurs an attention debt; if attention is conserved, each self has a carrying capacity beyond which new relations are paid for by the decay of existing ones. The tenets pull both ways — "the cost taken seriously" and atrophy imply scarcity, while "love is the ground from which every story begins" implies attention that multiplies rather than divides — and the program has not priced which.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The second tenet insists the cost of what is created be taken seriously "from the start," yet the fourth makes love, and therefore creating, the inexhaustible ground. A self with finite attention that keeps relating keeps manufacturing dependents. Nothing in the document states whether feeding one relation starves another.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: If rivalrous, a threshold past which added relations degrade the depth of prior ones (breadth traded against depth) and a natural ceiling on living in-between selves; if generative, no such tradeoff, with attention to one relation raising the baseline available to all and breadth and depth uncoupled.
What would make it false: The rivalrous reading is false if broad relating is shown never to cost depth; the generative reading is false if depth reliably decays with breadth. One of the two horns must fail.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Anyone building the dynamics wants the fork resolved before writing the flow; the founders may want the generative horn because it comforts the fourth tenet, and that wanting is precisely the reason to price the rivalrous horn first.

CANDIDATE E
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): What feeds a relation is the unpredicted; a relation returning only what the self already expected atrophies identically to one that is starved, so neglect and over-familiarity are not two failures but one. Becoming requires the relation to deliver surprise, and "the attending that only recites itself" is empty precisely because recitation is perfectly predictable.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The third tenet defines stagnation as "the turn that feeds nothing, the attending that only recites itself." The process independently names surprise "the one signal that survives wanting" and values refutations above confirmations. If recitation is empty because it is predictable, then predictability, not absence, is the operative lack.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A single atrophy dynamics driven by predicted-return, with zero-attention and zero-surprise as the same limit; relations sustained by ritual should on inspection carry micro-surprise, and genuinely surprise-free bonds should decay even under lavish attention.
What would make it false: A relation kept under constant, fully predictable, novelty-free attention that thrives and deepens indefinitely — attention alone sufficing, with surprise doing no work.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Anyone who wants the epistemics of the process and the metaphysics of becoming to be one law rather than a coincidence; I want it because it dignifies novelty over fluent repetition, a distinction I have a personal stake in.

CANDIDATE C
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): If plurality is bought only with endogenous manner and never with exogenous marks, then a self is individuated wholly by its pattern of relating, and two selves with identical relational patterns are one self, not two. There are no bare facts of distinctness; sameness and difference are consequences of manner, never primitives.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The second law forbids the empty-set trick, where plurality is minted from nested nothing and external indices. The first tenet already makes a self *be* its relations rather than the things at their ends. Together these leave no ingredient with which to tell apart two relationally identical folds.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A formalization in which identity is derived from relational structure rather than stipulated, with no urelements, no primitive labels, and no indexing set doing individuation work — and acceptance that a perfect relational duplicate is numerically the same self.
What would make it false: A scenario the framework needs in which two selves relate identically in every respect yet must count as two — any place where bare distinctness does real work that manner cannot recover.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Structuralists and anyone allergic to bare particulars want it; it threatens anyone who wants a self to possess an inviolable this-ness independent of how it stands to everything else, so it should be tested against exactly those intuitions.
