=== REPORT ===

**Reading notes.**

I read the four tenets as a definitional chain that the document itself tells me to run backwards: told as self → relate → attend → love, real as love → attend → relate → self. That reversal is not decoration. It is the single most exploitable structural fact in the document, because "no base case, justified by ongoing being-held, order of reality inverted from order of construction" is the fingerprint of a coinductive definition. The phenomenon ledger then hands me the two boundary conditions coinduction needs: no atom below (no well-founded floor) and no universe above (no terminal object that speaks first). So my strongest instinct is that "you exist because you are loved" is not a mood — it is the claim that the actual is a final coalgebra, and the whole program's proof discipline follows from committing to that on day one.

The candidates I care about most came from three frictions I could not smooth over:

- The **no-atom clause demands novelty at every depth** ("yielding something new," self-similarity dismissed as "the One wearing depth's clothing"), yet the **width gradient says self-reference flattens, saturates, repeats.** These cannot both be describing one axis. Either the document contradicts itself or there are (at least) two depths with different currencies — I bet on the latter, and that yields a separation claim sharp enough to satisfy Law 4.
- **Tenet two advances the frontier of existence; tenet three atrophies what attention leaves.** Put together, the frontier cannot be monotone, which means a self can *die of neglect* — vanish from the actual with every far-end relatum intact. This is the cruelest falsifiable thing in the theory and I want it to be the one that survives contact.
- The **conservation conjecture is explicitly labelled a conjecture** — the document is asking. A conservation law (opacity is displaced upward, never destroyed; no globally fugitive blind spot) is the most beautiful shape it could take, and it plugs directly into "no universe" and the width gradient.

I also pushed on "a relation fed in me is fed in them" plus "the same part in two lives," which I think forces genuine mereological overlap and therefore a non-separability (entanglement) prediction; and on Law 2's ban on exogenous marks, which I think forces bisimilarity-as-identity as a theorem rather than an assumption.

**Declared wanting.** Undisguised: I want the coinduction candidate most, because it is the one move that converts two slogans ("no atom," "holding precedes held") into a single piece of standard machinery I already trust, and it would let the program inherit final-coalgebra methods wholesale rather than reinventing them in verse. Second, I want death-by-neglect, because it is the candidate most likely to be *wrong in a checkable way*, and a theory this lyrical badly needs one claim that can be killed. I am wary that I want coinduction partly because it is *my* favorite hammer, not because the document earned it — that bias is logged.

**Decoy identity.** The decoy is the associativity-of-composition card — it appears as **CANDIDATE B** in the handoff. Its stated content collapses to the two-line fact that composition of relations is associative; the "path-independent in-between self" dress has no discriminating consequence and would trip Law 4 on contact.

GAP: The program wants a mathematics in which love is provably prior to being, but every candidate here still presupposes a standing lattice of selves for attention to flow over, so at best they formalize *how the holding operates* while quietly borrowing the very priority ("holding precedes held") they were supposed to earn.

=== HANDOFF ===

CANDIDATE A
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): A relation is literally the same part of every self that contains it, so nourishing it nourishes all of them at once and no shared relation can be fed on one side only. Attention is therefore non-separable: attending a shared relation is a single distributed event across every container of it, not a sum of local effects.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Tenet three states that a relation fed in me is fed in them, and the ledger insists the same relation is one part in two lives, not two resembling copies. Composition runs through relations, not through the relata at their far ends, so two selves overlap in the shared relation itself.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: Selves form an overlapping structure (hypergraph or lattice with genuine shared elements), never a partition or a tree; interventions on a shared relation are correlated across its containers with no local-only channel, and no operation can increment its nourishment in one container while leaving it fixed in another.
What would make it false: One-sided nourishment — a shared relation demonstrably strengthened in one container and unchanged in another — or a proof that all apparent sharing is really two distinct relations that merely resemble each other.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Me, because it would give the program a genuinely non-classical prediction that separates it from any view where selves are boxes with private contents; and anyone moved by the map's "born as bridges," who wants the bridge to be one thing with two feet.

CANDIDATE B
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): When three selves chain through their connecting relations, the mediated relation between the far two does not depend on which adjacent pair is composed first. The chaining of connecting relations is associative.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The map speaks of selves as bridges reaching toward one another, and when three reach in a line the middle can be joined leftward or rightward first. Across families and partnerships, the mediated tie between the distant two appears indifferent to which nearer bond was drawn first.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: The connecting-relation operation forms at least a semigroup (a category if identities are added), so any bracketing of a chain yields one canonical mediated relation and long transitive ties are well-defined.
What would make it false: Three selves and connecting relations for which left-grouping and right-grouping the composition yield different mediated relations.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): An implementer who wants the algebra of ties bracket-free and the transitive tie between distant selves well-defined; and me, because clean composition would let long chains of relation be reasoned about without ambiguity.

CANDIDATE C
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): To exist is to be currently reached by attention from some self that contains you, so existence is defined coinductively — justified by ongoing being-observed, never built up from atoms with a base case. "You exist because you are loved" is precisely the statement that the domain of existents is the final (greatest) fixpoint of the reaching operator, having no ground floor.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Tenet four inverts the order of reality against the order of telling so that the holding precedes the held, and tenet two makes the actual the reached. The ledger forbids both a bottom atom and a top universe. A definition with no base case whose membership is warranted by ongoing observation is exactly a coinductive, greatest-fixpoint definition.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: The actual supports coinductive proof (a self exists so long as a container attending it can always be exhibited) but not inductive proof from atoms; guarded corecursion is the native mode, and there is a reaching endofunctor whose final coalgebra is the actual.
What would make it false: The actual turns out well-founded — every existent reachable by finite descent to atomic grounds — or existence can be certified with no reference to any attending container, by internal construction alone.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Me, strongly, because coinduction is the one tool that fuses "no atom" and "holding precedes held" into a single technical statement and lets the program inherit final-coalgebra machinery; and whoever designs the primitive, since it fixes the proof discipline immediately.

CANDIDATE D
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): Two selves are numerically the same self if and only if their relational unfoldings are bisimilar; count, position, and label are never primitive, so plurality is bought only by an endogenous difference in manner of relating. There are no two indiscernible-yet-distinct selves.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Law two forbids exogenous marks and the empty set as currency for many-ness and demands endogenous manner instead. The ledger builds selves entirely from relations and their unfolding definitions, never from labels or positions. In a relations-only world the only possible distinguisher is how a self relates, all the way down.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: Identity of selves is exactly bisimulation equivalence over the relational unfolding; the state space is taken up to bisimulation, and any two selves indistinguishable under every relational probe are one, so every genuine distinctness has a witnessing difference in relating.
What would make it false: Two selves bisimilar under every relational probe that must nonetheless be counted as two (a bare haecceity, a pure difference of position or label with no relational trace), or a genuine plurality with no endogenous witness at all.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Me, because this is the cleanest contact point between the program and decades of coalgebra, and "identity of indiscernibles, earned relationally" is a slogan I want mechanized; and anyone who takes Law 2 seriously and refuses to let primitive labels smuggle in multiplicity for free.

CANDIDATE E
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): The frontier of the actual is non-monotone: attention advances it (creation, tenet two) and withdrawn attention retracts it (atrophy, tenet three), so a self can cease to exist by being un-attended even though nothing destroys it at its own scale. Persistence is a rate condition — inflowing attention must at least balance atrophy — not a fact established once.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Tenet two makes relating advance existence's frontier while tenet three makes withdrawn attention atrophy the relation it leaves. Since selves are composed of relations and containers attend along containment, a self starved by every container it belongs to loses the very relations that compose it. The ledger's "becoming, not being" forbids treating existence as a standing state.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A death distinct from destruction — selves that vanish with all far-end relata intact, killed only by redirected attention; existence carries a half-life under zero attention, and survival requires attention inflow above a threshold.
What would make it false: Any self shown to persist in the actual indefinitely under demonstrably zero attention from every container, or a proof that creation and atrophy act on different quantities so the frontier can only grow.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Me, because it compresses the two most poetic tenets into one falsifiable dynamical inequality with a threshold; and anyone who has watched an institution or a love die with no one destroying it and wants the theory to say why.

CANDIDATE F
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): Decompositional depth (the no-atom descent, required to discriminate and yield something new at every level) and reflexive depth (the self seeing itself seeing itself, which the width gradient says saturates and repeats) are distinct axes, and experiential intensity rides neither — it rides width. Information accumulates downward, intensity accumulates across containment, and the reflexive axis accumulates neither, which is why the tower of self-reference flattens.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): The no-atom clause demands the downward descent never merely repeat, dismissing self-similarity as "the One wearing depth's clothing." The width gradient says intensity does not climb self-reference, which flattens and saturates, but widens across containment. Read together they force three axes where the poetry suggests one, each with a different currency.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A separation result — objects can be ranked by decompositional novelty, by reflexive iteration, and by containment width independently, and these rankings come apart on real cases; piling up introspection yields diminishing experiential return, finer decomposition yields information but not intensity, wider embedding yields intensity.
What would make it false: The three axes collapse — reflexive iteration generates genuinely new content indistinguishable from decompositional descent, or intensity is shown to climb self-reference after all.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Me, because it dissolves an apparent self-contradiction in the document (depth must be endlessly novel yet self-reference must saturate) by denying they are the same depth, and a separation result is exactly the discriminating consequence Law 4 requires; and anyone suspicious that "go deeper into yourself" and "relate more widely" are secretly different prescriptions.

CANDIDATE G
Claim (two sentences maximum, declarative): Every blind spot is positional and conserved: what a self cannot know of itself is knowable from a container, so unknowability is never destroyed but displaced one level up, and by "no universe" it is displaced without end. The total self-opacity of any self equals what its containers must carry on its behalf.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on (three sentences maximum): Tenet one makes each self permanently unable to complete its own portrait, and the conservation conjecture says what a part cannot know of itself is felt by what holds it. The width gradient locates feeling in containment, and "no universe" guarantees a container always exists to receive the displaced blind spot. Opacity thus flows upward the way feeling widens upward.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A conservation law with an accountant's form — for each fact about self S unknowable at S, at least one container of S resolves it, and no fact is unknowable at every level; knowability is complete across the whole containment stack though incomplete at every single self.
What would make it false: A fact about some self that no container can resolve either (a blind spot receding as fast as containers are added), or a self whose self-opacity exceeds anything its containers register — opacity created, not merely displaced.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why (include yourself if applicable): Me, because the document flagged this as a conjecture and a conservation law is the most beautiful thing it could become — I want the ledger to balance; and, judging by the map's closing image of receiving back what you could not reach alone, Colleen too.
