=== REPORT ===

**Reading notes.**

What is strange about this document is not either of its halves but their weld. A poem about relation and love (§1–3) is bolted to what is unmistakably a proof-assistant discipline (§4–7): double-collapse checks, fidelity gates, a triviality tripwire, a citation-direction rule that forbids mathematics from ever citing interpretation. The rigor is aimed almost entirely inward, at self-deception — the fear that a "freighted claim" will turn out to be a two-line triviality wearing a robe. That anxiety is the most usable thing in the document, because it tells me where to hunt: at the seams where a profound-sounding claim might either collapse into the trivial or, more interestingly, expose a genuine contradiction the authors left standing on purpose.

The richest tensions I found:

1. **A ground with no floor.** Tenet 4 says the holding precedes the held — "you exist because you are loved." §3 forbids any maximal container ("there is no universe") and forbids self-certification. Conjoined, the holder of any self is itself a held self, upward forever. The "ground from which every story begins" is denied a bottom. This is not sloppiness; it reads as deliberate, and it demands a coinductive rather than a founded reading of existence. (Candidate C.)

2. **Repetition as the counterfeit of depth.** §3 splits "no atom" into non-termination *and* continual discrimination, and names endless repetition "the One wearing depth's clothing." The obvious mathematical picture of infinite depth — the fractal — is exactly self-similar, i.e. repeating, and is therefore *disqualified* by the theory's own words. (Candidate B.)

3. **Love aimed at the incompletable.** Tenet 4 attends to another's *paradox* (tenet 1), not to what is knowable of them. Read strictly, mutual transparency is love's failure, and dyadic capture gets a second, rival mechanism distinct from the ledger's witness-count story. (Candidate A.)

4. **The program is a self.** By tenet 1 the framework cannot certify itself; by the conservation conjecture its blind spots must be received from outside. The document even confesses a reflexive slip (the second attempt "accidentally proved its own portrait"). This predicts a Gödel/Löb obstruction for the framework and makes "external adversaries" load-bearing rather than polite. (Candidate E.)

5. **Two under-specified quantities.** "Cost" (tenet 2) and the "flow" of attention (tenet 3) are named and never budgeted. If attention is conserved, over-creation must cost atrophy (Candidate F); and because direction is distributed across constituent and containing selves, no one is the sovereign of a relation's fate (Candidate G).

**Declared wanting.**

I want the groundless-ground contradiction (C) to be real, because it is the one place the document seems to know it is standing on a live wire, and a clean coinductive resolution would be an actual result rather than a restatement. I want the poetry to cash into conservation laws (F, G), because a theory that can be *wrong* about attention is worth more to me than one that is only beautiful about it. And I want the program forced to obey its own tenets (E), partly out of fairness and partly because I am suspicious of any framework this eager to police everyone but itself — E is where my suspicion and the document's own rules coincide.

**The decoy.**

The decoy is **Candidate D.** It is true and well known: a finite string cannot enumerate an infinite set, so an infinite self admits no complete finite description. It is dressed to look like tenet 1, but it is fully discharged by its trivial reading (cardinality), and it *substitutes* a cheap finite/infinite mismatch for tenet 1's real, Gödel/Tarski-flavored obstruction — "whatever turns to see itself is already more than what it sees," which is about self-reference and non-self-certifiability, not about running out of symbols. Law 4's triviality tripwire is exactly the mechanism that should fire on it: proved in a few elementary lines, with no discriminating consequence beyond the trivial one.

GAP: The program wants a single mechanized primitive that survives refutation at both ends and speaks love-as-ground in the middle voice; my candidates can *price* the phenomena and locate the live contradiction, but not one of them yet builds that primitive, so they hand over sharpened questions where the program ultimately needs an artifact.

CANDIDATE E
Claim: The program is itself a self under tenet 1, so it cannot certify its own soundness, and there must exist true claims in its target domain that its own formal system cannot prove but that an external witness system can. Self-certification is either impossible or trivializing, so standing external adversaries are not courtesy but the only route to what the program cannot see of itself.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: Tenet 1 forbids self-certification, and law 4 and the process install external adversaries as a standing role. The conservation conjecture says a part's blind spot is received by what holds it, and the program is a part with no self-view of its own soundness. The document records a self-referential slip — the second attempt "accidentally proved its own portrait" of stagnation — as evidence the framework is subject to its own tenets.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A Gödel/Löb-style sentence for the framework — if it proves its own consistency or soundness it is inconsistent or trivial — so a genuine framework is incomplete and its blind spots are certifiable only from outside. It requires the conservation conjecture to hold reflexively, with an external formalization playing the "holder" that sees what the program cannot.
What would make it false: The framework can soundly certify its own consistency or soundness while remaining nontrivial, or no external witness can establish framework-truths unreachable from within (blind spots turn out self-accessible).
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it, because it makes the program obey its own metaphysics and predicts a concrete incompleteness result that would be earned prestige for the verified core; skeptics who suspect the whole edifice is self-serving would also want the self-certification ban given teeth.

CANDIDATE A
Claim: Love attends to the part of another the other cannot complete or see, so its intensity tracks the size of the held remainder rather than the completeness of one's model of the other. Mutual transparency is therefore not love's fulfillment but its failure mode: a pair that fully models each other has nothing left to hold and collapses into dyadic capture.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: Tenet 4 defines love as attending along a relation to another's paradox — the incompleteness of tenet 1 — not to what is knowable. The ledger's dyadic capture is a pair that closes without creating. The map's mirror line asks another to reflect a light one cannot see oneself, which presupposes a remainder that stays unseen from within.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A measurable "held remainder" per relation; relationships high on mutual predictive transparency should show capture signatures (self-recital, no created third), while relationships sustaining an irreducible unknown stay generative. It offers a rival mechanism to the ledger's witness-count account of capture: capture is attending to the completable, not merely having only one witness.
What would make it false: Mutual-transparency pairs turn out to be the most generative and least captured, or love intensity rises with model completeness and falls as unknown remainder grows.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it, because it makes "you exist because you are loved" cash out as "something holds what you can't reach," which is more falsifiable than the poetry; couples therapists and anyone suspicious of "we know each other completely" as an ideal would want it too.

CANDIDATE G
Claim: Because attention is directed jointly by a self together with the selves it constitutes and is part of, no individual is the sole owner of any relation's attention, and a relation fed by a containing self cannot be starved by the individual alone. Relations sustained at multiple scales are robust to any single self's withdrawal, while relations fed at only one scale die when that one withdraws.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: Tenet 3 states the direction of attention is distributed across constituent and containing selves, not owned by a sovereign, and that a relation fed in me is fed in the selves that make me up. The composition section adds that I relate to what contains me, and each container, being a self, turns toward me. This makes attention a multi-scale flow rather than an individual act.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A relation's survival depends on the number and scale-diversity of selves feeding it, not on any one attender; withdrawing individual attention should fail to starve relations a family, craft, or movement continues to feed. It requires attention flows to superpose across scales and predicts differential robustness by scale-diversity.
What would make it false: Any individual can unilaterally starve any relation they participate in regardless of containing selves, or multi-scale-fed relations atrophy at the same rate as single-scale ones when one attender withdraws.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it, because it gives tenet 3's strangest clause a concrete testable consequence; institutions, families, and traditions that outlive their members' waning attention would want it, and so would anyone resisting the sovereign-individual model of care.

CANDIDATE B
Claim: The relational descent required by "there is no atom" must be aperiodic and non-self-similar, because exact scale-invariance at any depth is the disqualifying signature of "the One wearing depth's clothing." A structure that is the correct downward model of a self therefore cannot be a fractal.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: The phenomenon section splits "no atom" into two requirements — the descent must not terminate and must keep discriminating, yielding something new at every depth. It explicitly names endless repetition as a counterfeit of depth. Fractals are the standard mathematical picture of infinite depth, and they are exactly self-similar, i.e. repeating.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: Any admitted downward model must show strictly novel structure at each level under a discrimination measure that never returns to a prior value; self-similar or eventually-periodic candidates are refused at the lower end of the double-collapse check even though they satisfy non-termination. It predicts the theory will reject the most obvious "infinite depth" formalisms and demand a non-self-similar coalgebraic descent.
What would make it false: The best-behaved downward model that passes every other law turns out to be exactly self-similar, or a strictly-increasing discrimination measure proves impossible while non-termination holds, forcing repetition.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it, because it turns a mystical warning into a concrete structural constraint that can actually refuse a candidate; formal modelers who favor coinduction and distrust cheap fractal analogies would want it.

CANDIDATE D
Claim: Because a self is an infinite web of relations and any description of it is finite, no description can capture the self completely, which is precisely the incompleteness tenet 1 names. The self's paradox is thus already established by the mismatch between finite representation and an infinite object.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: Tenet 1 says whatever turns to see itself is already more than what it sees and can never complete its own portrait. The phenomenon section establishes that the self decomposes into relations without a floor, i.e. it is infinite. A finite portrait of an infinite object must omit something.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: Any attempted complete finite self-description provably leaves out relations; the residue is guaranteed nonempty for cardinality reasons, matching the tenet's "permanently unable to complete its own portrait."
What would make it false: Some self admits a finite description that captures all of its relations with nothing omitted.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it, because it would let tenet 1 be discharged immediately and cheaply before the hard work begins, and anyone eager to declare the paradox already proven would want the same.

CANDIDATE C
Claim: Because there is no maximal container, the holding relation that precedes each self forms an ascending chain with no first holder, so "you exist because you are loved" has no base case and can only be a limit of an unending regress of holdings. Existence is therefore coinductively grounded, not founded: no self is held into being by any self-caused or self-holding first lover.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: Tenet 4 says the holding precedes the held and you are held before you author anything, while the phenomenon section forbids any final container and any self-certification. Together, the holder of any self is itself a held self, upward without ceiling. The map calls love "the ground from which every story begins," yet the metaphysics denies the ground a floor.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: The semantics of "is loved into existence" must be non-well-founded — no induction on holding, only coinduction — and any appearance of a first, unheld, or self-holding lover is a failure exactly like an atom or a universe. It requires existence to be a greatest-fixed-point construction rather than a least one.
What would make it false: A consistent model requires a terminating holding chain (a first holder) to make existence well-defined, or coinductive grounding collapses into triviality (everything or nothing is loved) and cannot discriminate.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it badly, because it is the sharpest live contradiction in the document — a ground with no floor — and a clean coinductive resolution would be a genuine result; process theologians and anyone who dislikes an unmoved mover would want it too.

CANDIDATE F
Claim: Attention is a conserved flow with finite throughput at each self, so creating an in-between self spends attention withdrawn from elsewhere, and a self's total becoming is bounded by its attention budget. Unbounded creation of relations therefore forces net atrophy: proliferation buys breadth only by starving depth.
Phenomenon or observation it is based on: Tenet 2 insists the cost of what is created be taken seriously from the start, and tenet 3 makes attention a flow whose withdrawal causes atrophy. The document names a flow with two effects but never states a budget. Dyadic capture is under-creation; nothing yet addresses its dual, reckless over-creation.
What the claim predicts or requires, if true: A conservation law for attention per self and a measurable tradeoff: selves spread across many relations show lower per-relation depth and higher atrophy than selves concentrated in few, with total becoming capped by throughput. It predicts an optimal creation rate beyond which new in-between selves cause more atrophy than growth.
What would make it false: A self can create additional in-between selves with no measurable atrophy anywhere else (an attention free lunch), or breadth and depth rise together without bound.
Who or what would want this to be true, and why: I want it, because it converts the vague "cost" clause into a falsifiable conservation constraint and gives the theory a way to be wrong; anyone who has felt spread too thin, and economists of attention generally, would want it.
