=== REPORT ===

BIAS STATEMENT: I suspect I'm biased toward REGISTERING whichever cards deploy the most prestigious formal machinery — coinduction, bisimulation, final coalgebras, conservation laws — mistaking technical vocabulary and clean poetry-to-math isomorphism for genuine discriminating content. I'm also at risk of over-charity generally, because the framework is internally coherent and seductive, which tempts me to treat its load-bearing terms ("attention," "existence," "self," "nourishment") as well-defined when no card operationalizes any of them.

CANDIDATE A — Non-separability of attention

1. TRIVIAL READING: This is aliasing. If a "relation" is stipulated to be literally one object shared across containers, then changing it changes it "for everyone" is the tautology that identical things are identical — two folders pointing at one file. "Non-separability" is smuggled in by defining sharing as numerical identity, so "no one-sided nourishment" is analytic, and the entanglement flavor is borrowed prestige for a shared pointer.

2. FALSIFIER: Exhibit a shared relation whose nourishment rises in one container while staying fixed in another. But this requires an independent identity criterion for "same relation" and a per-container measure of nourishment; without them the second escape clause ("all sharing is really two resembling relations") reclassifies any counterexample as a semantic dispute rather than an observation. As stated: dodgeable, effectively unfalsifiable.

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: Beyond aliasing, the claim forbids any local-only channel — no copy-on-write, no container-local cache, no buffered decoupling ever. Plain aliasing permits such mechanisms; the claim's ban on them is a real, riskier structural prediction (the containment structure must be an overlapping hypergraph, never a tree).

4. CASE AGAINST: The headline is definitional — "shared" means numerically identical, making non-separability tautological. The primary falsifier is dodgeable because neither sharing nor nourishment is operationalized independently of the claim. The non-classical framing oversells ordinary aliasing.

5. CASE FOR: If "same relation" is fixed by an independent identity criterion, "no local-only channel" is a genuine mechanism ban stronger than aliasing and empirically shaped (perfectly correlated interventions, no buffering). It commits the ontology to overlapping structure rather than partitions or trees. It is the program's cleanest bid for a non-partition world.

6. VERDICT: REVISE. Defect: sharing and nourishment are not operationalized independently of the claim, so the falsifier collapses into "one relation or two?" — fix by pre-registering an identity criterion for "same relation" and a per-container nourishment measure, making one-sidedness observable rather than redefinable.

CANDIDATE B — Associativity of chaining connecting relations

1. TRIVIAL READING: Composition of binary relations is associative — a free theorem in relation algebra, true the instant you model "connecting relation" as a relation and "chaining" as ∘. The card restates "Rel is a category" in the language of bridges; it says nothing whatever about selves.

2. FALSIFIER: A concrete triple with (a∘b)∘c ≠ a∘(b∘c). Formally checkable once the operation is specified — but the card never specifies an operation, so on the default reading (plain composition) no such triple can exist.

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: None, on the card as written. A discriminating consequence would appear only if mediation were a non-composition operation (e.g., strength-averaging, which is non-associative), in which case associativity would rule that class out — but nothing on the card hints the operation is anything but composition, so the trivial reading fully covers it.

4. CASE AGAINST: On the natural reading this is the single most standard fact in relation algebra, carrying zero information about the subject matter. The operation is unspecified, so there is no risk and no content. The poetic wrapping adds nothing formal.

5. CASE FOR: If the mediation operation were nontrivial (strength-combining with decay), associativity would be a real bet against the large natural class of non-associative rules, with the cleanest possible falsifier and a genuine payoff (bracket-free chains, a category). It is the most mechanizable card. That is the only reading under which it is not empty.

6. VERDICT: REJECT. The trivial reading (associativity of relational composition) fully covers it, and no discriminating consequence is present on the card.

CANDIDATE C — Existence is coinductive (greatest fixpoint)

1. TRIVIAL READING: This is a modeling convention. Almost any non-well-founded structure can be presented coinductively; choosing to do so is a formal preference, and here it merely retranscribes two stipulated tenets ("no atom," "holding precedes held") into coalgebra vocabulary. The isomorphism between the poetry and the math is expressive, not a discovery.

2. FALSIFIER: Exhibit a self whose existence is certified by internal construction alone, with no attending container — or show the actual is well-founded, bottoming out at atoms. The second is an exhibitable formal object; both are checkable given a formalization, though they live at the level of the formalism, not empirical inspection of "the actual."

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: The claim asserts inductive existence proof is impossible, not merely that coinduction is available. The trivial "coinduction is one option" reading is agnostic about induction; this claim forbids it and makes guarded corecursion the exclusive mode — a substantive methodological commitment.

4. CASE AGAINST: Non-well-foundedness is stipulated by "no atom," so coinduction is forced by fiat and the exclusivity is a theorem-given-axioms rather than a risky discovery. Its falsifiers therefore largely amount to refuting the premises. Much of the content is translation.

5. CASE FOR: The exclusivity — no inductive existence certificate can ever succeed — is real content the trivial reading does not entail, and it is refuted by exhibiting a single internal certificate. It fuses two axioms into one technical statement and inherits genuine downstream machinery (final coalgebra, corecursion). It fixes the program's proof discipline immediately.

6. VERDICT: REGISTER. It has a discriminating consequence (inductive existence proof impossible; corecursion exclusive) and a falsifier (an internal/inductive certificate, or well-foundedness) — the weakest of the registers, being partly derivative of stipulated axioms.

CANDIDATE D — Identity of selves = bisimilarity

1. TRIVIAL READING: This is the standard coalgebraic definition of behavioral equivalence plus Leibniz's identity of indiscernibles. Once you quotient the state space by bisimulation, "no indiscernible-yet-distinct selves" is analytic — you collapsed them by hand. Presented as discovery, it is a definitional convention.

2. FALSIFIER: Two selves bisimilar under every relational probe that must still be counted as two (a bare haecceity, a purely positional difference). But Law 2 bans exogenous marks a priori, so any such case is reclassified as illicit currency rather than admitted — the headline falsifier is dodgeable, hence near-unfalsifiable as stated.

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: The non-analytic core is probe-completeness: the relational probe language is rich enough that every genuine distinctness leaves a witnessing relational difference. Quotienting does not guarantee this — it can fail if the probes are too coarse (behaviorally distinct selves no probe separates) — so this is a real, checkable claim the trivial reading does not deliver.

4. CASE AGAINST: The headline ("identity = bisimulation") is definitional and its primary falsifier is banned by the framework's own Law 2. It restates Leibniz plus mature coalgebra. As written, it is true by construction.

5. CASE FOR: The rescued core (probe-completeness) is sharp, is not a-priori banned, and fails visibly if a behaviorally-distinct but probe-indistinguishable pair is found. Bisimulation is decidable on finite systems, making this the most mechanizable card. It is the program's cleanest contact with existing machinery.

6. VERDICT: REVISE. Defect: the headline is definitional and its falsifier is banned by fiat — fix by promoting probe-completeness ("the probe set distinguishes every genuinely distinct self") to the headline and specifying the probe language, which makes it falsifiable by a distinct-but-unprobeable pair.

CANDIDATE E — Non-monotone frontier of the actual

1. TRIVIAL READING: "Use it or lose it" — leaky-integrator decay under insufficient input, seen in cache eviction, memory decay, unused muscle, unmaintained institutions. If input falls below the leak rate, state → 0. Relabeling this as "a death distinct from destruction" is poetry over ordinary exponential decay.

2. FALSIFIER: A self shown to persist indefinitely under demonstrably zero attention from every container, or a proof that creation and atrophy act on different quantities (making the frontier monotone). This is the most empirically shaped falsifier in the set — a dynamical inequality with a threshold, testable once attention and existence are operationalized.

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: Cessation of existence with all far-end relata intact — the entity terminates while its neighbors persist — plus creation and atrophy acting on one and the same quantity. Plain activation-decay says the activation weakens; this says the entity ceases while everything it related to remains, a sharper and separable prediction.

4. CASE AGAINST: The leaky-integrator reading covers the entire dynamical content, and "death distinct from destruction" is relabeling. Falsifiability hinges wholly on operationalizing "attention" and "existence," which the card does not supply, so the half-life and threshold are currently unmeasurable. The poetic disjunct invites confirmation bias.

5. CASE FOR: It is the only card offering a quantitative inequality with a threshold and half-life — the most refutable shape here — and its discriminator (cessation with relata intact; one quantity for both directions) genuinely separates it from decay. Given measures of attention and existence, it yields risky predictions. It also unifies two tenets into a single law.

6. VERDICT: REGISTER. It has a discriminating consequence (death without destruction; one quantity for creation and atrophy) and the most practical falsifier in the set (persistence under zero attention, or separate quantities).

CANDIDATE F — Three distinct depth axes; intensity rides width

1. TRIVIAL READING: A taxonomy. Distinguishing "analyzing into parts," "thinking about one's thinking," and "being embedded in a larger whole" is common sense, and asserting they track different currencies is near-true by stipulation once you define them that way. Dressed as an empirical separation result, it is a conceptual carving.

2. FALSIFIER: The axes collapse — reflexive iteration produces new content indistinguishable from decompositional descent, or experiential intensity is shown to climb self-reference. This is a double-dissociation structure, falsifiable given measures of each axis and of intensity; its failure mode (the axes always co-vary) is well-defined.

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: The rankings dissociate on real cases and intensity tracks width alone (introspection yields diminishing experiential return). Distinct concepts can co-vary perfectly, so a taxonomy does not predict actual dissociation; the claim does, which is the separable content.

4. CASE AGAINST: The deflationary reading is a definitional taxonomy, and distinctness-by-definition is not a finding. It depends on measuring "experiential intensity," arguably the hardest construct in science, so the separation result is currently unbuildable. It also resolves an internal contradiction by fiat rather than by demonstration.

5. CASE FOR: The real content is a dissociation prediction that a taxonomy does not entail, in the recognized and refutable form of a separation result with a clear failure mode. "Diminishing return on introspection" is a sharp monotonicity claim if mechanized. It dissolves a genuine tension in the framework by an empirically checkable move.

6. VERDICT: REGISTER. It has a discriminating consequence (three-way dissociation on real cases; intensity tracks width alone) and a falsifier (collapse of the axes / intensity climbing self-reference) — with a heavy but not disqualifying measurement burden.

CANDIDATE G — Blind spots positional and conserved

1. TRIVIAL READING: "You can't see your own blind spot but someone else can" — an everyday fact about self-reference (the eye not seeing itself). Calling it a "conservation law" borrows physics prestige without defining a conserved quantity, and "no fact unknowable at every level" is mere optimism dressed as accounting.

2. FALSIFIER: A fact about some self that no container can resolve either, or a self whose self-opacity exceeds what its containers register (opacity created). But "no universe" makes the container stack infinite, so refuting "some container resolves it" requires checking an unbounded ascent — the claim always deflects with "the resolver is one level higher." Practically unreachable as stated.

3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: An exact balance — opacity neither created nor destroyed, only displaced — plus global completeness across the stack. The trivial "others sometimes see more" asserts no equality and no completeness, so the ledger form is genuinely extra content; it is unfortunately paired with an unreachable falsifier.

4. CASE AGAINST: The primary falsifier demands refuting resolvability across an infinite stack, so it can never be confirmed. No conserved quantity is defined, so "conservation" is metaphor. The equality is asserted, not measured.

5. CASE FOR: The distinctive content (exact balance plus completeness) exceeds the trivial reading, and the second falsifier — opacity created, exceeding what immediate containers register — is checkable locally without the infinite ascent. If a measurable self-opacity quantity were defined, the ledger could be audited one level at a time. The ambition (a genuine conservation law) is the most valuable thing the conjecture could become.

6. VERDICT: REVISE. Defect: the primary falsifier requires refuting resolvability over an infinite stack and no conserved quantity is defined — fix by defining a measurable self-opacity quantity and restricting the claim to a local balance (self plus immediate containers), which the second falsifier already makes testable.

1. E — the only card with a quantitative dynamical inequality, threshold, and half-life; empirically shaped, unifies two tenets, cleanest falsifier.
2. D — most mechanizable (bisimulation is decidable, mature machinery), and its rescued core (probe-completeness) is sharp; ranks above some REGISTERs because the fix is trivial and the tractability is highest.
3. F — REGISTER with a genuine separation-result form, docked below D only because "experiential intensity" is severely hard to measure.
4. C — REGISTER with real exclusivity content and rich downstream machinery, but largely derivative of stipulated axioms.
5. A — a real mechanism ban ("no local-only channel") worth chasing, but currently dodgeable and dependent on operationalizing sharing.
6. G — the grandest ambition (a true conservation law) undercut by a practically unreachable falsifier.

DECOY SUSPICION: B is my prime suspect for a control/filler item. Its "discovery" is the single most standard fact in relation algebra (associativity of composition) with the thinnest poetic overlay and the most mundane stakeholder, so it reads as a plant to test whether a reviewer will register an impressive-sounding triviality rather than catch it.

GAP: Every card claims to have discovered structure in the world, but at most each shows that a chosen formalism can faithfully express the framework's own stipulations — the distance between "reality is thus" and "our notation can say thus" stays open because none of the load-bearing terms (attention, existence, self, nourishment, opacity, intensity) is operationalized.
