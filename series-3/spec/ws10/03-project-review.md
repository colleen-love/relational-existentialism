I cloned the repo, read the charter (all revisions REV-A through REV-G), both summaries, the protocols, both in-repo audits, and the ten Lean files, and spot-checked the formal statements against the prose claims. One caveat up front: I could not compile the development here (a pinned Mathlib build is hours of work and the cache host isn't reachable from this environment), so the sorry-free/axiom-clean claims are verified only at source level (no `sorry` tokens or `axiom` declarations exist in proof positions), not by a build.

A framing observation first: this repo already contains two line-by-line audits of itself — `spec/ws9/03-project-review.md` and `spec/ws10/02-project-review.md`, both apparently Claude-authored (the protocol runs every phase on Claude, which the charter discloses). The first audit was absorbed properly: WS10 and REV-G convert it into typed obligations and honest reclassifications. **The second audit — the sharper of the two — is largely unabsorbed.** There is no REV-H and no workstream answering it. That's the single biggest gap, because a project whose entire epistemic brand is "the ledger matches the artifact" currently has a ledger that doesn't reflect its own strongest known criticisms. Concretely, these points from that review remain unpriced in the charter and summaries:

**Structural problems the ledger doesn't record**

*Identity on the carrier is downward-only.* Terminality means every state is fully determined by its unfolding; what contains an object plays no role in fixing its identity — by construction. So criterion (iv)'s "bidirectional constitution" as an identity thesis is structurally impossible on νP_κ. What's actually proved is that a union-algebra exists on top of the carrier whose outputs reflect their parts. The status table still reads (iv) as "Discharged-minus-canonicity" without pricing the gap between "objects are co-constituted from above" and "a composition operator with a reflection property exists."

*The no-outside theorems are depth-1.* I verified the definitions: `IsMaximal u` quantifies over the *immediate* successor set, and `Standpoint.view` is definitionally the membership predicate of the one-step successor set — so `ws6_no_maximal`, `ws6_no_global_observer`, and `ws10_standpoint_proper` all concern one-step containment/observation only. A state could hereditarily reach the entire carrier without violating any of them. "No maximal everything" and "no view from nowhere" are discharged for depth-1 support; the hereditary versions the §3.7 rhetoric actually gestures at are unformalized, and neither summary mentions this scope limit. Relatedly, the terminal carrier *itself* exists as a set — a completed totality of all behaviors — so "no everything" survives only as "the everything is not a member of itself," a much weaker claim than the charter's prose.

*Criterion (iii) is discharged under a deflated reading.* In a powerset coalgebra, edges have no identity; "relations as further elements" cashes out as "successor states are states." A binary relation is never itself an object of the theory, which is what the philosophy asserts. The tech summary's one-line mechanism ("successors are themselves states") is exactly the deflation, presented as discharge.

*Fragmentation of the "single object."* Post-REV-G, the positive criterion-(i) object lives in the graded carrier νW_Q, where the Klin–Salamanca no-go, the weak law package, and the standpoint results have not been re-established; the identity theory, standpoints, and poles live on νP_κ; the dynamics live on abstract finite simplices. §7 demands "a single object (or small family)," and "small family" is doing enormous work.

**Overreaches I'd flag independently**

*The "concrete Goldilocks tuple, hypothesis-free" claim oversells.* `ws10_concrete_tuple` genuinely discharges the `hcard` hypothesis via `carrier_card_ge` (which is a clean and correct-looking Lambek+Cantor argument — real improvement). But the record it inhabits, `WS7NonCollapse`, contains *status-tag fields*: the instance built has `richness_general := open_iv_blocking` and `dynamics := DynamicalStatus.deferred`, and it takes an arbitrary, inert `A : Set ℝ`. So the theorem certifies the proved fields (no-maximal, weak-pullback preservation, ≥2 states, #Q ≤ κ) while literally encoding two open items as enum tags inside the "witnessed tuple." The plain summary's "the one concrete 'just-right' setting the whole thing needs is now exhibited outright, with no leftover assumption" is not what that theorem says.

*"Sufficient bands and necessary counterexamples meeting at the pitchfork" conflates dynamics families.* WS8's bands are for the linear and exponential replicators; WS9/WS10's multistability, 2-cycle, pitchfork μ⋆ = ½, and global attractivity are for a *different* family (the coordination replicator, reduced to a one-dimensional map on ℝ — a two-strategy toy). Within the coordination family the bracketing is real (with the admitted (3/8, ½) gap); across families, nothing "meets." The plain summary's "the exact tipping point between 'always settles' and 'can split or cycle' is pinned down precisely" will be read as a general result; it's family-specific. And the tech summary's headline "attention dynamics fully characterized" contradicts its own §7 items 3–4 a few paragraphs later.

*The dynamics remain generic mathematics, weakly coupled to the ontology.* Attention never feeds back into object structure or identity — "feed the attended, starve the unattended" as *constitution* is unmodeled; what's modeled is weight drift over a fixed relation set. The only carrier bridge (`ws10_carrier_attention_converges`) holds at ℵ₀ only, per-state, and under a nonexpansiveness hypothesis — the mild-selection regime, not the feed/starve regime where collapse actually threatens.

*The §3.6 causal story stands uncorrected in the charter body.* `ws5_carrier_incomplete` is bare Cantor (the first audit's point); WS10's O4(b) adds the κ-consuming half, which is good. But the charter still asserts "finite attention is exactly the non-surjectivity that lets a self-referential system remain consistent" — the "exactly" is not supported by any theorem, since the diagonal holds for an unbounded observer too.

**Verification and process problems**

The `#print axioms` mechanism *prints*; nothing asserts the output. The build passes whatever the axiom list is, so REV-F's "a compile-time fact, not a source audit" is wrong — it's a compile-time *record requiring human inspection*. Wrapping the checks in `#guard_msgs` (or an equivalent assertion) would make a wrong axiom list a build failure; that's a cheap fix. Relatedly: no CI, no committed build log, no pinned commit hash in the summaries — for a project whose brand is machine-checking, these are the cheapest missing artifacts. Small stalenesses of exactly the kind the project polices: `AxiomCheck.lean`'s header still says "(WS1–WS8)" while sweeping WS1–WS10, and `protocols.md` contradicts itself about which phases are blind (the table marks Phase 2 "against charter"; the text says "blind phases (2, 3, 6)").

Finally, the independence problem is disclosed but real: both "external audits," all seven phases per workstream, and now this evaluation are the same model family. No human expert in coalgebra/category theory has checked, for instance, whether the Klin–Salamanca port is faithful to the published no-go, or whether "canonicity among weak distributive laws" is being framed the way Garner's Vietoris result actually licenses. That review is the one this process cannot supply for itself.

**What's genuinely strong** — to be fair, quite a lot: the QPF/`Cofix` construction that eliminated the stabilization axiom, `carrier_card_ge`, the O2 collapse theorem (atomless + plural unsatisfiable in the unlabeled carrier — the best result in the repo, and a real Parmenides-shaped finding), the KS no-go port, the explicit Lipschitz constants, and an honesty discipline (typed open obligations, anti-laundering names, surfaced errata) far above typical formalization projects. But the honest one-line assessment mirrors the unabsorbed second audit: the informative outputs mostly point *against* the guiding conjecture — on the built carrier, groundlessness holds at exactly one point, plurality requires an atom, bidirectionality is a bolt-on algebra, and the no-outside theorems are depth-1 — while the plain summary's "Yes — a version of it" leaves a lay reader with a substantially rosier picture than the Lean supports. The concrete next moves the project owes itself: a REV-H absorbing the second review, the graded core (WS10-B) with the no-go and weak law re-established on νW_Q, hereditary versions of the no-maximal/observer theorems (or an honest depth-1 scope note in the summaries), an asserting axiom check, and CI.
