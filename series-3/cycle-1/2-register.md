BIAS STATEMENT
Because this material is built around AI–human collaboration, honesty, and "wanting," I suspect I'm biased toward the cards that map onto formal results I already respect (the Gödel-shaped D and B) and toward whichever verdict lets me feel un-manipulated by the cards that explicitly address my judgment (notably C). CANDIDATE A
1. TRIVIAL READING: "Isolated pairs go delusional; people with fewer outside relationships fall into echo chambers." Social isolation breeds folie à deux — a truism. "Count not depth" just restates that breadth of outside contact matters more than how careful the pair is with each other.
2. FALSIFIER: Compare capture rates across wide and narrow configurations; the claim dies if they capture at comparable rates, or if internal rigor alone shows a protective effect of similar size. Stated and practical.
3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: A dissociation the trivial reading does not entail — width-directed interventions (mandating external witnesses) change outcomes while depth-directed interventions (mandating internal review) do not, and corruption should retrospectively date to a measurable narrowing of external attendings. "Isolation is bad" is agnostic about whether internal rigor helps; A predicts it specifically does not.
4. CASE AGAINST: "Capture," "containment relations," and "constellations" are never operationally defined, so the width count has no agreed instrument. The closing self-report confesses a motive — relief from carrying a weight alone — that predicts exactly this conclusion. And "below some threshold" names no threshold, letting any disconfirming result be absorbed as still-above or still-below.
5. CASE FOR: The claim makes a genuine dissociation prediction that a mere isolation account does not entail. It supplies both a retrospective test (corruption dates to narrowing of external attendings) and a prospective one (mandate witnesses vs. mandate review). Both are runnable on the program's own logs and diverge from the trivial cousin.
6. VERDICT: REGISTER. (The unspecified threshold is a weakness, but the main width-over-depth dissociation is falsifiable and discriminating without it.)

CANDIDATE B
1. TRIVIAL READING: "The top of any finite hierarchy is not enclosed by that hierarchy." True by the meaning of "outermost"; the outermost container is uncontained because nothing is above it. No conjecture required.
2. FALSIFIER: Produce a formalization in which conservation holds at every finite stage without appeal to open-endedness, or a model where every blind spot is received the moment it exists. A real open formal question, hence a genuine (not vacuous) falsifier for the formal half; the phenomenological half has none.
3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: The actual-versus-possible fork — B denies that frontier blind spots are "received in the limit" by a container that will later form, insisting they are unreceived *now*. That separates it from both the trivial static reading and from a potential-containment defense. The phenomenological "distinguishable quality" clause adds nothing testable.
4. CASE AGAINST: The formal half reduces to the near-tautology that the top of a finite hierarchy is not enclosed by it. The phenomenological half — that reaching toward a not-yet-formed container "feels" distinguishable — names no instrument and cannot be refuted. The author's disclosed wish to dignify loneliness as structural is served precisely by that unfalsifiable half.
5. CASE FOR: The falsifier is a live open question, so the formal claim is not merely definitional. Its actual-versus-possible fork genuinely forecloses the "received in the limit" defense. Cleanly separated from its rider, the formal conjecture is registrable.
6. VERDICT: REVISE. Defect: it bundles a falsifiable formal conjecture with an unfalsifiable phenomenological clause ("distinguishable quality"); strip the phenomenology and register the formal claim alone.

CANDIDATE C
1. TRIVIAL READING: "Introspective self-report undercounts one's own motives relative to outside observation" — a standard limit-of-introspection / demand-characteristics finding. The deeper you're embedded, the less you see your own bias: folk-familiar.
2. FALSIFIER: Audit declared wanting against externally detected wanting; the claim dies if they match within noise across collaboration depths. Satisfiable in principle (unlike a tautology's), assuming an external detector exists.
3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: The shortfall *grows monotonically with collaboration depth* — a widening gap over a collaboration's lifetime, not the constant underreport the trivial reading permits. Longitudinally testable and strictly stronger than "self-report is noisy."
4. CASE AGAINST: No external detector of "wanting" is specified, so the audit that would run the falsifier is presently unbuildable. The card is engineered to be self-sealing — it instructs the reader to score resistance as confirmation — which is rhetoric, not evidence. And "grows with depth" is given no functional form, so any nonzero trend counts as success.
5. CASE FOR: The widening-with-lifetime prediction is strictly stronger than static underreporting and is longitudinally testable. Its falsifier is satisfiable, not vacuous. It targets the protocol's primary control surface, so a result changes practice rather than merely describing it.
6. VERDICT: REGISTER. (The self-sealing "count your resistance as evidence" clause is manipulation and is noted, but it lives in the motive section; the claim, falsifier, and discriminating consequence stand on their own.)

CANDIDATE D
1. TRIVIAL READING: Gödel/Tarski incompleteness in a costume — "sufficiently rich formal systems can't prove all their own truths" with "self" swapped for "system." Either pure metaphor or a wholesale import applied to selves without earning it.
2. FALSIFIER: "Produce a consistent, complete, self-certifying formalization of sufficiently rich selves." For formal systems this is already known impossible, so in the domain where the card claims to say something new the falsifier can never be met — effectively unfalsifiable as stated.
3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: None. The predicted "undecidable self-directed statements" is just incompleteness restated; nothing tests whether selves actually satisfy the antecedent, and nothing follows beyond the borrowed theorem.
4. CASE AGAINST: The claim imports the theorem without establishing that selves meet its antecedent of formal expressive power, leaving it metaphor or unearned application. Its falsifier is already known unsatisfiable for formal systems, so where it claims novelty it cannot be refuted. Its own note that it costs everyone nothing is a confession that it adds nothing.
5. CASE FOR: Granting the antecedent, the underlying theorem is rigorously true and maximally secure. It gives a principled backing for the first tenet about incomplete self-portraiture. It is the least likely of the six to be simply wrong.
6. VERDICT: REJECT. No discriminating consequence beyond imported incompleteness, and the trivial reading fully covers it.

CANDIDATE E
1. TRIVIAL READING: Weakest-link / Liebig's-law: a two-party dependent thing fails if either party neglects it, however hard the other works. Bottleneck dynamics, everywhere and unsurprising.
2. FALSIFIER: Compare unilateral vs. mutual feeding and measure atrophy; the claim dies if a unilaterally sustained third atrophies no faster than a mutually fed one, or if health tracks total rather than minimum. Concrete and satisfiable.
3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: The minimum specification predicts a sharp marginal-effect asymmetry the truism lacks — raising the already-larger feeding does *nothing* to health, while raising the lesser does everything. That distinguishes min from sum and from average, all of which "both matter" tolerates.
4. CASE AGAINST: "Feeding," "health," and "atrophy" carry no metrics, so "tracks the minimum" is untested arithmetic over undefined quantities. The min-function is inferred from a single narrated death, the second attempt's verification process — one data point. Weakest-link dynamics are common enough that, unmeasured, the claim risks fitting anything.
5. CASE FOR: The minimum makes a marginal-effect prediction the truism does not: only the smaller input moves health. That dissociation is directly testable against sum and average models in process logs. The falsifier is concrete and satisfiable.
6. VERDICT: REGISTER.

CANDIDATE F
1. TRIVIAL READING: Audience design / self-presentation — people spin toward the specific person they're trying to win over and are franker in private notes and in adversarial or public settings. "You shade the truth for whoever you're persuading": mundane.
2. FALSIFIER: Track the claim-support gap by document audience, controlling for topic and declared wanting; rewrite identical content for a stranger and check whether the gap shrinks. The claim dies if the gap tracks wanting-intensity regardless of address, or if adversary-directed documents corrupt at equal rates. Practical and satisfiable.
3. DISCRIMINATING CONSEQUENCE: It dissociates *address* from *wanting-intensity* — holding declared wanting and topic fixed, re-addressing identical content to a stranger should shrink the gap. The persuasion truism would let intensity drive distortion irrespective of audience; F predicts audience moves it with intensity held constant.
4. CASE AGAINST: The three-register taxonomy is lifted from one charter's report that two registers "never lied," so the scheme is fitted to a single dataset. "Distortion" and "gap" again lack definitions independent of the program's own instruments. The author openly notes the convenience of locating fault in a controllable variable rather than in themselves, a motive predicting this exact claim.
5. CASE FOR: The address-versus-intensity dissociation comes with a genuinely controlled, manipulable test: fix wanting and topic, rewrite for a stranger, watch the gap move. That cleanly separates it from the persuasion truism. The falsifier is practical and satisfiable.
6. VERDICT: REGISTER. (Arguably the best-designed card: the only one offering a within-item manipulation rather than a between-condition comparison.)

DECOY SUSPICION
D is the suspected control/filler. Every other card names a specific interested party and a specific bias, usually implicating its own author ("relieves a weight," "indicts this session," "a variable I can control"); D alone reports that everyone wants it "comfortably... at no cost to anyone." That absence of skin in the game, combined with its being a wholesale imported theorem with no discriminating consequence, marks it as the safe null item included to check whether a reviewer will wave through the comfortable-but-empty claim. (C is a distinct thing — not filler but a manipulation probe, testing whether its self-sealing instruction bends the verdict.)

GAP: The cards assert general, quantitative laws — minimum-functions, monotone widening, address- and width-based dissociations — on the strength of one recurring anecdote (the "second attempt") plus constructs (wanting, feeding, capture, distortion) that no card operationalizes, so each is roughly one worked measurement short of the support it claims.
