# paper-1 — a self arises from the axioms

**The thesis: a self is _derived_, not defined.** Paper one is the foundational paper, rebuilt on the new
**quantaloid / allegory** arena where **relations are the primitive arrow** and `Q` (the hyperfinite II₁
factor) is the value-object. It takes the lived self-level datum — *I am a collection of relations
asymmetrically co-directing my attention* — **decouples** it into **relations-only** commitments that never
name a self, and shows the self **condenses out** as a fixed relation of the co-direction dynamics.

## Status — an exploration, honestly reported

This is a **derivation in progress**, explored to find what is **true**, not to produce a green checkmark. The
axioms were built to be **shaken**. Per the theorem debt:

- **Holds.** **Existence** (a fixed self-relation exists — relational Knaster–Tarski) and the **seam**
  (reflexive opacity — Lawvere, arena-independent) are proved on the arena, in
  [`formal/`](formal). Existence invokes **only the arena's completeness** — neither A1 nor A2 — a first
  finding about the minimal axiom set.
- **Open / obstructed.** The **raising dynamics (A3)**, **sparsity**, **self-differentiation**, the
  **stability dichotomy**, and the **recovery** theorem need the **quantitative** trace/modular structure of
  `Q` — exactly the arena seams that are still open. They are stated precisely and their obstructions
  recorded in [`spec/03-theorem-debt.md`](spec/03-theorem-debt.md), with `sorry`-free *statements* and no
  forced proofs.

The central structural claim — **the symmetric configuration is unstable; stable selves are asymmetric** — is
**explicitly falsifiable** and is **not** settled here; it is to be built true or false, with the honest
fallback ("stable differentiation requires asymmetry") recorded if symmetry turns out only marginal.
