# 03.1 — To relate is to create

> Part of [paper one](paper-one.md). The first of the three foundational theorems the
> basis forces. *When a relating is turned back on itself, does anything new come to be?* Status
> tags as in [paper one](paper-one.md).

## The capstone

> **Theorem 3.1 (to relate is to create).** Iterated self-relation `σ(P) = Tr(P)` ([D1](02-axioms.md))
> *has* a fixed point. Relating produces a third thing — the eigenform — that need not have
> pre-existed in either relatum.

This is the generative claim made structural: looping a relating does not merely rearrange what is
already there; it manufactures a fixed point. In the plain-language view it is *two people in real
conversation generate a third thing — the conversation itself, with its own momentum.*

## What is proved

`[proved]` In the complete-lattice setting,
[`Trace.selfTrace`](../formal/Scratch/Trace.lean)` := νP` with `selfTrace_fixed`
(`P(νP) = νP`), `le_Tr` (coinduction), `Tr_fixed`, `Tr_mono`. Footprint `propext, Quot.sound`.

The cartesian reading is sharper still: on a category with finite products, traces are in
bijection with **Conway fixed-point operators** (Hasegawa; Hyland) — so every endomorphism arising
by feedback *has* a fixed point, canonically. This is the precise sense in which "looping produces
structure" ([00 §0.4.1](00-doctrine.md)).

## Honest scope

`[reading]` What is mechanized is **Knaster–Tarski** — a greatest fixed point of a monotone map on
a complete lattice. It is *not* the Hasegawa–Hyland trace↔Conway-operator bijection (the Conway
identities are not verified in the lattice mechanization), and a greatest fixed point existing is
generic to *every* monotone operator. So "to relate is to create" rests, formally, on "monotone
maps have greatest fixed points."

The discriminating content — that the *created selves are rare*, that not every relating closes
into a self — is **not** here. It is [03.7 — Sparsity](../../archive/spec/03.7-sparsity.md). Theorem 3.1 says creation
is *possible*; sparsity says it is the *exception*. Read together they keep the theory from being a
universal solvent: relating creates fabric constantly, but a stable self only where the loop closes.

**Provenance.** `R` (rederivation). The fixed-point existence is Knaster–Tarski / the Conway-operator
theory; the contribution is the *identification* of self-relation with the trace, not new
fixed-point mathematics. See [04 — Provenance](04-provenance.md).
