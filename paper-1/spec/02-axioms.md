# 02 — The axioms: two, and a definition (relations only)

> *The whole commitment of paper one, stated over relations alone — **no self named**.* Two axioms and one
> definition. Crucially, **A3 (generativity / raising) is _not_ an axiom here**: it is a **theorem to
> derive**. The self is the **derived** fixed relation of the co-direction dynamics.

## A1 — Asymmetry

**Symmetry is not imposed.** A relation `R` and its converse `R°` are **free** — independent arrows, not
forced equal — and **each end carries its own weight**. Nothing in the arena makes a relation symmetric;
where symmetry appears it is a special, fragile case, not the default. (This is the minimal negative
commitment: we decline to assume `R = R°`.)

## A2 — Co-direction

**Attention is carried by the relation, not by a field over the relata.** The weight of `R` bears on one end;
the weight of `R°` bears on the other; and that **two-endedness _is_ co-direction**. A relation directs
attention at both of its ends at once, by being a relation with two ends — **requiring no agency and no
consciousness** at either end. There is no one who "pays" attention; co-direction is a structural fact about a
two-ended relation, read through `Q`'s weight.

## D1 — Self-relation is the trace

A **self-relation** is a relation `A ⇸ A`. **Self-relation as feedback is the trace** — the relation routed
back into itself. This is **minimal**: just the feedback of a relation on itself, with **no modular content**
(the modular flow / thermal reading of the trace is paper three's, not paper one's).

## A3 is a theorem, not an axiom

**Generativity / raising — that a self-relation grows by bounded, mutual, asymmetric raising — is _not_
assumed.** Raising is something a self *does*: a property of the **evolved** self-relation, not a premise
about bare relations. So it is **demoted to a theorem to derive** (theorem 2 of the debt), from the trace of
an asymmetric self-relation re-entering itself (D1 + A1). The exploration finds out whether it derives:

- **If it derives**, A3 was never an axiom — the minimal axiom set is just {A1, A2} + D1.
- **If it resists**, A3 is **irreducible** — and that obstruction, honestly reported, returns it to the axiom
  list. Either outcome is information.

This is the discipline of the project: we built the axioms to be **shaken**, not confirmed. We do not pad the
axiom set to make the theorems easy.

## The self is derived

The self is **never posited**. It is a **fixed relation of the co-direction dynamics** — the structure that
*arises* and sustains itself under the trace of asymmetric, co-directed self-relation. Its existence, its
shape, and whether it deserves the word "self" are **theorems** ([`03-theorem-debt.md`](03-theorem-debt.md)),
not axioms:

1. **Existence** — a fixed self-relation exists (relational Knaster–Tarski).
2. **The dynamics (A3)** — it evolves by bounded, mutual, asymmetric raising.
3. **Selectivity / sparsity** — bounded capacity makes selves *few* (else "self" is vacuous).
4. **Self-differentiation** — the stable self has internal leading/lagging asymmetry.
5. **The stability dichotomy** — the symmetric configuration is unstable; stable selves are asymmetric
   (the falsifiable headline).
6. **The seam** — reflexive opacity, three faces (a self cannot fully trace the relation that includes it).
7. **Recovery** — the derived fixed self-relation **satisfies the lived description**: it *is* "a collection
   of relations asymmetrically co-directing attention." The proof that derive-don't-define worked.

What each theorem *actually invokes* is recorded as it is explored — that is how the **true minimal axiom
set** is found, and where theorems **collapse into one another**.
