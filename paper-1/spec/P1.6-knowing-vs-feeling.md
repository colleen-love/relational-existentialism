# 03.3 — Knowing vs feeling

> Part of [paper one](paper-one.md). The third foundational theorem: the two ways relation
> can turn — *outward into an object you hold at a distance*, and *inward as presence* — are
> different **kinds of arrow**, and only one of them is Lawvere-obstructed. Status tags as in
> [paper one](paper-one.md).

---

## The capstone

> **Theorem 3.3 (knowing vs feeling).** Knowing — the σ-move, an endomap `D → D`, cartesian — is
> Lawvere-obstructed and leaves a remainder. Feeling — the `≈`-relation, one type-level up — is
> *not of the kind* that the obstruction touches.

The mirror that can't close: the moment you make yourself into something you can look at, the
looker is left out of the picture. This is not a flaw to be fixed; it is the contrapositive of a
one-line diagonal, and it lives precisely where copying (`Δ`) is available.

## What is proved

`[proved, 0 axioms]` The σ-side is genuine and fully constructive:
[`Mirror.lawvere`](../formal/RelExist/Mirror.lean), `no_complete_selfModel`,
`selfModel_remainder`, and
[`KnowingFeeling.knowing_can_fail_to_close`](../formal/Scratch/KnowingFeeling.lean),
`no_complete_boolModel`. Knowing is structurally partial — the contrapositive of Lawvere's
diagonal, which **requires** the cartesian copy and produces a residue the copy omits
([00 §0.4.2](../../theory/spec/00-doctrine.md)).

## Honest scope of the asymmetry

This is the line the formalization holds, and the plain-language view must not cross. The feeling
side is only `feeling_is_reflexive` `[proved]` — `≈` is reflexive (an equivalence). The real
asymmetry is **type-level**: knowing has type `D → D`, against which the diagonal `g a a` runs;
feeling has type `D → D → Prop`, against which there is no diagonal to run — the obstruction does
not typecheck.

We do **not** claim "feeling is whole / global / has no remainder": no absence-of-obstruction is
proved, and a universal negative cannot follow from one positive property. *Knowing is obstructed;
feeling is simply not the kind of arrow that obstruction applies to.* That — not "feeling is whole"
— is the earned claim, and every downstream reading of the knowing/feeling difference inherits its
limit.

## The residue is typed out

By Theorem 3.3, formalizing is *itself* the σ-move (the objectifying look that leaves a remainder),
so the language captures the *structure* of feeling (`≈`) and, on the doctrine's own reading, not
feeling-as-lived. Valence, the hard problem, and freedom fall outside what a structure-preserving
functor expresses — a **stance the doctrine takes, named as its boundary**, not a proved theorem
([02 — the residue](../../theory/spec/AXIOMS.md), [04 §4.7](../../archive/spec/04-functorial-semantics.md)).

The full unfolding of 3.3 — what knowing *does* to a relation (decoherence), and why the self can
never completely know itself — is [03.5 — Decoherence and the trace](P1.8-decoherence.md).
