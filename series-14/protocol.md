# Relational Existentialism, Series 14 Protocol

**The process, written for handoff to a Claude Code executor. Series 14 keeps the program's one load-bearing idea, that blind review has teeth only when the reviewer cannot see the motivation, and keeps the batching used since the middle series: each phase runs once across the whole series, because the workstreams are one coupled object. Series 14 makes ONE structural change to the phase cycle: a BLIND DESIGN REVIEW runs before any Lean is written. The evidence is in Series 13's own ledger. Both of that series' SERIOUS findings (SR1-1, SR1-2) were DESIGN errors, the orders solved for the map, and both were caught only after the build, costing a full review-address loop to fix. Series 14 carries two knots (the lift, the re-indexing action) and two signature risks (the bookkeeping re-hit, smuggled choice), all four decidable ON PAPER. This series pays for that check up front. The question: what does the defect GENERATE, along two axes. DEPTH: does the loss compound under iteration, or does it saturate (the flat answer is proved as the FLOOR in WS1, not discovered)? BREADTH: does any distinction forget its index, or is every difference in reach a difference from a self? The verdict is a PAIR, tower and index, computed independently, and no theorem may combine them. Geometry means the shape of distinction-space, never spacetime; the charter fixes this and the reviewer never sees why it matters.**

*The program's phase cycle collapsed over its history to design / build / blind review / address. Series 14 uses that shape with the design review inserted, over six workstreams. The coupling is tight: the whole series stands on whether the defect compounds and whether any distinction de-indexes, and both are only checkable across all workstreams together. Series 12 remains the terminus; Series 13 remains the fit; this series reopens neither.*

## 0. Core principle

Three things carry the program's honesty, and are unchanged:

1. **Blind means blind.** A blind review session is seeded with the artifact under review (designs at Phase C, built code at Phase F), the contracts (theorem signatures, outcome classes), and `spec/success-criteria.md`, but **not** the charter's motivating prose. No "room," no "depth/breadth," no "self/other," no "cat and plant," no "given/chosen," no metaphysics, and no statement that geometry means distinction-space. The reviewer sees the mathematical claim and judges whether it is proved. Motivation cannot launder a gap. **Series 13's SR2-4 lesson is inherited as a mechanical requirement: the success criteria live in their own prose-free file (`spec/success-criteria.md`), extracted at Phase B, and THAT is the reviewer's seed. `charter.md` is never handed to a reviewer.**

2. **Never relabel a shortfall as the goal, and reach for the pre-registered outcome BEFORE the compiler.** When a build falls short, the honest outcome is the pre-registered alternative (Broken / Rigid / Partial on the tower axis; Perspectival / Choice-gated / Partial on the index axis) with the obstruction made precise, never a quiet redefinition of the target. The charter is the fixed bar; `charter-status.md` records the miss. The pre-registered alternatives are HONORABLE, anticipated moves. **Two are more than honorable and the protocol says so up front: BROKEN closes Series 13's layer-stability open by refutation, and PERSPECTIVAL is the program's own thesis landing as a theorem.** When a finding shows a pre-registered condition has been met, reporting it is correct; constructing a theorem that avoids it, OR one that manufactures a GENERATIVE terminus past a real obstruction, is the §0.2 sin.

**2a. The recurrence guard (the anti-loop discipline), applied at Phase D and Phase G.** A SERIOUS finding closes in exactly ONE of two ways, and `charter-status.md` must name which: **(Fixed)** the charter/design's *originally specified* target was built (name the theorem), or **(Relabeled)** the target was not built, the precise obstruction is recorded, and the payoff's status is demoted to the pre-registered honest outcome (Broken / Rigid / Partial / Perspectival / Choice-gated / Refuted). "Built a different, weaker theorem adjacent to the specified target and considered the finding addressed" is NEITHER, and is prohibited. Findings carry stable IDs across passes; a finding "addressed" by a target-avoiding theorem is re-graded SERIOUS and marked RECURRING with its count.

3. **Strip the *why*, keep the *what*, and strip the *word*, keep the *theorem*.** For every payoff, delete **"room," "volume," "dimension," "geometry," "space," "overtone," "wall," "axis," "depth," "breadth," "kind," "self," "other," "given," "chosen"** and check whether the statement still goes through as a bare order, `GaloisConnection`, `Recoverable`, reification, or invariance fact. Critically: the COLLAPSE payoff SHOULD strip to "the interior operator is idempotent up to the label equivalence"; the LIFT payoff SHOULD strip to "a `GaloisConnection` between two non-trivial preorders over a once-reified carrier, with the lifted mint non-recoverable from the reified plain relating"; the STRICTNESS payoff SHOULD strip to "a layer-(n+1) non-identity witness inequivalent, up to the lifted equivalence, to the transport of the layer-n witness, separated by the lifted order"; the AXIS payoff SHOULD strip to "an order trichotomy: above, incomparable, or below the transported witness"; the DE-INDEXING payoff SHOULD strip to "a non-trivial distinction invariant under the re-indexing action, or the proof that none exists, or the exhibited hypothesis every construction consumes." A payoff that needs the deleted vocabulary is prose wearing a theorem's name.

Series 14 adds five emphases of its own, because it has two axes and each has a way to be faked:

4. **The fork stays open, on every floor and from every angle (the central sin, inherited, PROMOTED).** Reification gives more places to commit it; re-indexing gives more angles. No theorem, definition, or discharged obligation at ANY layer, and no invariance fact, may classify an out-of-image import as *given* or *chosen*. If CHOICE-GATED lands, the section the gluing consumes is likewise never classified: that is the same fork on a new face. The review greps at every layer. SERIOUS.

5. **Growth must be VISIBLE to the connection, never bookkeeping relabeled (the depth axis's signature risk, PROMOTED to first-class).** Series 10 proved the plain engine cannot see the tower grow (`ws2_growth_is_bookkeeping`); Series 11 proved the rescue re-hits the same wall. If Series 14's growth strips to a plain-layer counting fact, the series has re-proved Series 10 and called it geometry. `ws4_growth_visible` is the named obligation: the strictness witness must be separated by the LIFTED CONNECTION's own comparison, the thing Series 10 did not have. A growth fact not so separated is the re-hit, SERIOUS, and the honest verdict is RIGID.

6. **Growth must not be cheap (PROMOTED).** Strictness tested by literal equality is true by construction: reification manufactures syntactically new terms trivially. Every strictness claim is tested up to the lifted `labEquiv`, and the WS3 design must show the lifted equivalence is the honest TRANSPORT of the flat one, not a finer relation solved to make transported witnesses inequivalent. An equivalence solved for the verdict is Series 13's order-by-fiat sin in tower clothes. SERIOUS.

**6a. Exogeneity must not decay.** The line the mint must live above MOVES when the carrier reifies. A lifted mint recoverable from the ONCE-REIFIED plain relating has been smuggled below the new line even if the flat `ws2_mint_exogenous` still holds. `ws2_lift_exogenous` is a fresh per-layer proof obligation, never inherited. SERIOUS.

**6b. De-indexing must not smuggle choice (the breadth axis's signature risk, PROMOTED to first-class).** A gluing that quietly consumes a global section has performed the differentiating act off the books: picking a coordinate system IS picking, and Series 07 proves the picking cannot come from the relating. If invariant distinctions require choice, the theorem must SAY so (outcome CHOICE-GATED, the hypothesis exhibited and undischarged), never bury a `Classical.choice` in a proof term doing structural work the statement hides. The axiom check must confirm choice appears only where Mathlib's standard three already place it, and the WS5 design must argue the invariance results' choice-usage is logical bookkeeping, not the construction itself. An (a) that is secretly a (b) is this axis's sin. SERIOUS.

**6c. Names must be names, not terms (inherited).** The review greps `formal/Series14/` and confirms no proof term, definition, or discharged obligation is named "room," "volume," "dimension," "geometry," "space," "axis," "depth," "breadth," "kind," "self," "other," "given," "chosen," "consciousness," "God," "choice," or "compass" (as content). A name doing a proof's work breaches the wall from the prose side. SERIOUS.

**6d. The two verdicts stay apart, and neither is spun.** No theorem combines the tower verdict and the index verdict into a pronouncement about worlds; the combination is interpretive and lives in charter §8 only. And the relabeling guard cuts BOTH ways on the index axis (charter §4.g): PERSPECTIVAL is the program's own position and its landing is a success; DE-INDEXED would complicate the position and its landing is also a success. Neither may be softened toward the other in prose. The verdict function decides, not the summary.

## 2. The process, phases batched across the series

Eight phases. C→D loops once if the design review returns SERIOUS; F→G loops once if the series review does. The canonical run is **A → B → C → D → E → F → G → H**.

| Phase | Name | Session | Format | Batching |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Charter | 1 | normal conversation with repo | once per series |
| B | Design-all | 1 | Claude Code with repo | **all six + the two sub-artifacts + `spec/success-criteria.md` at once** |
| C | Blind design-review | 1 | delegated task, blind | **all designs at once** → writes `spec/design-review-1.md` |
| D | Address design findings | 1 | Claude Code with repo | **all designs at once** |
| E | Build-all | 1 | Claude Code with repo | **all six at once** |
| F | Blind series-review | 1 | delegated task, blind | **whole series at once** → writes `spec/series-review-N.md` |
| G | Address series findings | 1 | Claude Code with repo | **whole series at once** |
| H | Close | 1 | Claude Code with repo | summaries + root README row |

*Phases C and F are repeated at most once each, and only if the pass returned SERIOUS findings (C→D→C→D, or F→G→F→G). A second pass that returns SERIOUS runs the §0.2a recurrence check first.*

> - **The terminal fork, stated plainly.** Which TOWER verdict {Generative, Rigid, Broken, Partial} and which INDEX verdict {De-indexed, Perspectival, Choice-gated, Partial} did the code deliver? Confirm each is honestly labelled and matches its object, never assumed.
> - **The axiom-check status.** Was `#print axioms` actually run over every headline and captured to `spec/axiom-check-log.md`, or is the claim static?
> - **The scope discipline.** Does the series reopen anything in the prior terminus? Are the layer bound and the carrier-class narrowing disclosed rather than glossed?
>
> Write `spec/series-review-N.md`: findings graded SERIOUS (the verdict rests on it, growth is cheap or invisible, exogeneity decayed, choice is smuggled, the fork is closed, a name is a proof term, **or a prior SERIOUS finding RECURRING via a target-avoiding closure**), REAL (a genuine gap, correctly labelled once fixed), COSMETIC/ACCEPTABLE, plus a "what survives cleanly" section and an honest bottom line. Each finding names the owning workstream and a precise correction owed. For a RECURRING finding, the correction owed is the binary: "build the specified target [name it], OR report the pre-registered outcome, no third theorem."

If there is a SERIOUS finding, continue to Phase G. If not, conclude with Phase H.

### Phase G, Address series findings (one session)

**Prompt to seed the address session:**

> Please pull your branch, review the most recent `spec/series-review-N.md`, and address every finding. For each SERIOUS finding, run this sequence in order, and do not skip to building a theorem until you have done the first two steps:
>
> 1. **Locate the charter's pre-registered honest outcome for the owning workstream**: the Broken clause, the Rigid clause, the Perspectival clause, the Choice-gated clause, the Partial clauses. They are in the charter precisely so that falling short, or the lift failing, or the tower saturating, or no invariant existing, is an honorable anticipated move. Read it before anything else.
> 2. **Ask whether the finding shows that condition has been met.** If it does, **REPORT it**: demote to the pre-registered outcome, retract the overclaim, record the obstruction, and where possible PROVE the alternative as a theorem (that the tower saturates; that no non-vacuous lift exists; that no non-trivial invariant exists). Note the symmetric dangers: manufacturing a GENERATIVE terminus past a real obstruction is the §0.2 sin; so is closing the fork to force a cleaner story; and so is softening PERSPECTIVAL toward DE-INDEXED or the reverse (§0.6d), since both are successes and the verdict function decides.
> 3. **Only if the pre-registered alternative has NOT been met, attempt the design's originally-specified target** (name it, from the design doc). Build *that* target, not an adjacent weaker theorem that compiles.
>
> Then close each SERIOUS finding in **exactly one of two ways, and state which in `charter-status.md`**: **(Fixed)** the originally-specified target was built (name the theorem, confirm by unfolding it is that target), or **(Relabeled to Broken/Rigid/Partial/Perspectival/Choice-gated/Refuted)** the target was not built (record the precise obstruction and demote the payoff). "Built a different, weaker theorem adjacent to the specified target and considered the finding addressed" is neither and is prohibited. A Relabel to a genuinely-proved alternative is a SUCCESS. If the finding is RECURRING, the only two permitted actions are Fixed-by-building-the-named-target or Relabeled-to-the-pre-registered-outcome; a third adjacent theorem is forbidden. Record every change in `charter-status.md`; keep the charter untouched unless the finding is a genuine design error (then the charter-change log, and reopen downstream). If the series review returned SERIOUS findings, **repeat Phase F once** on the repaired build.

### Phase H, Close (one session)

When every workstream has reached a terminal state and a review pass returns no SERIOUS findings, Series 14 closes. Phase H writes `summary.md` (plain-language) and `summary-technical.md` (machine-checked account), and adds ONE row to the root `README.md` series table recording the verdict PAIR (tower × index). The close states plainly:

- **Which two verdicts landed**, each as its own sentence, neither softened toward the other, and **no synthesis of the pair into a claim about worlds**; that reading lives in the charter's positioning and the summary's closing prose, never in a theorem.
- **The two opens that CLOSED**, this series' guaranteed yield: Series 13's layer-stability question (closed by construction under GENERATIVE/RIGID, by refutation under BROKEN), and the index question (closed by the index verdict).
- **Any new open, if CHOICE-GATED landed**: whether the section the gluing consumes is given or chosen, open by the same theorem that requires it, and never to be filled.
- **The bounds, disclosed not glossed**: the carrier class, the layer depth actually reached, and whether RIGID or PERSPECTIVAL were proved generally or only at layer one / on the narrowed class (a per-instance result is PARTIAL, not RIGID).

## 3. Why the reviews are batched at series level (not per workstream)

Series 14's payoffs are tightly coupled: the collapse, the lift, the strictness, the axis, the visibility, and the invariance are all facets of two questions (does the defect compound, does any distinction de-index), so a payoff can look earned in its own file while secretly resting on a vacuous lifted order (WS2), an equivalence solved to make growth strict (WS3), a growth witness that only a plain-layer count separates (WS4), or an action that pre-decided its own trichotomy (WS5). Only reviewing the whole series at once, blind, surfaces this.

This does not weaken blindness: the review session still receives *what*, not *why*. It strengthens it: the reviewer sees the whole dependency graph and can trace a "Generative" verdict to the lifted order and the visibility theorem in other files that actually carry it.

**Disclosure, carried forward:** these reviews are Claude-reviewing-Claude, a stated limitation, not claimed independence. The objective anchors, the dependency graph, the `#print axioms` records, the strip-test results, the fork-open check, the visibility check, the exogeneity check, and the smuggled-choice check, are what keep the audit honest despite that.

## 4. Dependencies and ordering

The workstreams are one coupled object, with one deliberate independence. The load-bearing edges:

- **WS1 is blocking and is the floor.** The flat collapse. It is near-certain and its FAILURE impeaches the transcribed Series 13 WS3, halting the series to route back.
- **WS2 is the first knot and blocks the tower axis only.** The lift. WS3 and WS4 build over the lifted carrier, orders, mint, and adjoint. If WS2 lands BROKEN, the tower axis reports Broken and WS3/WS4 are not built (the obstruction is the result).
- **WS3 depends on WS1/WS2.** The tower, the strictness, the axis trichotomy, all defined over the WS2 lift and tested up to the lifted equivalence.
- **WS4 depends on WS3.** The visibility discriminator is a fact about the WS3 strictness witness; the bound is owed only if strictness landed.
- **WS5 depends on NOTHING but the transcribed Series 13 carrier.** The re-indexing action and the invariance trichotomy live on the flat family `{Lab dest h | h : Hold dest}`, which exists with no lift at all. **WS5 is built and reported even under BROKEN.** This independence is the series' insurance: the breadth question is answerable whatever the tower does.
- **WS6 owns both verdicts and consumes WS1-WS5.** Each verdict is a FUNCTION (of the lift's existence, the strictness, the visibility, the axis; and of the invariance trichotomy), never hand-set, each with a falsifiability triple and named carriers. WS6 folds in the audit and reports both terminals, plus any new open.

When a charter or design change lands upstream of built work, the affected downstream workstreams reopen in `charter-status.md`. **Upstream changes invalidate downstream builds**, and the ledger must show it.

The two review sessions are the only blind ones, and the blindness is a single checkable rule: **strip the *why*, keep the *what*** at the session level, and **strip the *word*, keep the *theorem*** at the statement level, plus, for Series 14, **confirm no theorem sorts an out-of-image import at any layer or angle**, **confirm growth is neither cheap nor invisible**, **confirm exogeneity is re-proved at the moved line**, and **confirm choice is exhibited, never smuggled**. A reviewer who can see why a theorem matters grades the ambition; a reviewer who sees only the statement and the proof grades the proof. That is the whole discipline, and it is enough. **Mechanically: the reviewer opens `spec/success-criteria.md`, not `charter.md`.**

## 6. End of series

When every workstream has reached a terminal state and a review pass returns no SERIOUS findings, Series 14 closes. Should the series surface a hard resistance worth a successor (a BROKEN lift whose obstruction a differently-constructed reification could dissolve; a GENERATIVE tower whose bound went unproved; a CHOICE-GATED invariance whose consumed section a richer carrier could exhibit without classifying), the inter-series discipline applies:

> **Each series is a response to the previous series' honest findings, not a continuation of its plan.**

The mechanics, unchanged:

1. Freeze the series under `archive/`. Readable as origin, normative for nothing.
2. Write the new charter **standalone**, its own question, its own object from scratch, prior series as informing prior art only.
3. Carry forward *machinery* freely (Lean files are reusable and get transcribed); carry forward *claims* only by re-deriving or explicitly citing. A new series does not inherit discharges; it earns or re-imports them.
4. Frame the new workstreams as responses to the prior findings, and begin the cycle (§2) again.
